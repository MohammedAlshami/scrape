# 1999 Malaysian state elections

*Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999_Malaysian_state_elections*

---

{{Short description|none}}
{{Politics of Malaysia}}

[[State legislative assemblies of Malaysia|State assembly]] elections were held in [[Malaysia]] on 29 November 1999 in all states except [[Sabah]] and [[Sarawak]]. The elections were held alongside [[1999 Malaysian general election|general elections]], and saw the opposition win a total of 113 seats, 98 of which went to the [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS), 11 to the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) and 4 for the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]]. In the states of  [[Kelantan]] and [[Terengganu]], the PAS won by a huge margin–41-2 against [[Barisan Nasional]] and 28-4 respectively–hence allowing them to form the state governments in these states. In addition, the PAS also captured one-third of the seats in [[Kedah]], with the remaining two-thirds going to Barisan Nasional ([[United Malays National Organisation]] won 16 seats and the [[Malaysian Chinese Association]] 2).

The election results were seen as a great gain for PAS, who previously had no state seats in Kedah and capturing only one seat in Terengganu in the [[1995 Malaysian state elections|1995 elections]]. Observers attributed this to the neglect by the Federal Administration in the states of Terengganu and Kelantan.<ref>{{cite book|title=Malaysia recent trends and challenges|author=Swee-Hock Saw, K. Kesavapany|publisher=Institute of Southeast Asian Studies|pages=97–8|year=2006|isbn=981-230-339-1}}</ref>

Sabah held its state assembly election earlier on 12 – 13 March 1999.
