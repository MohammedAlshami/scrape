The 15th Kelantan state election were held on 12 August 2023 to elect the State Assembly members of the 15th [[Kelantan State Legislative Assembly]], the legislature of the Malaysian state of [[Kelantan]].

Kelantan is one of the states which did not dissolve simultaneously with [[Dewan Rakyat]] on 10 October 2022. It was decided by [[Perikatan Nasional]] on 13 October 2022.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Daud |first1=Ridauddin |title=PRU15: Pas pilih PN, tidak bubar DUN Kelantan, Terengganu, Kedah |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/pru15-pas-pilih-pn-tidak-bubar-dun-kelantan-terengganu-kedah-385716 |website=Astro Awani |access-date=29 April 2023}}</ref>

The governing [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) coalition led by [[Parti Islam Se-Malaysia]] (PAS) won 43 of 45 seats in a [[landslide victory|landslide]], achieving a [[supermajority]] in the legislature, and continuing PAS' dominance in the state since 1990. The [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) – [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) electoral pact won the remaining 2 seats, with BN and PH each winning 1 seat, and becoming the main opposition in the state assembly.

This was the first Kelantan state election not held concurrently with the national general election since 1978, when Kelantan [[1978 Malaysian state elections#Kelantan|held its election]] in March that year as opposed to the general election date in July.

== Background ==

For the state elections, Perikatan Nasional has decided to use the PAS logo and name for all its candidate in Kelantan and Terengganu, regardless of the candidate party.<ref>{{cite web |title=PRN: Calon PN guna logo Pas di Kelantan, Terengganu |url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/259149/berita/politik/prn-calon-pn-guna-logo-pas-di-kelantan-terengganu |website=Sinar Harian |access-date=11 August 2023 |language=ms |date=18 May 2023}}</ref>

== Aftermath ==
Prior to the state elections, reports suggesting [[Ahmad Yakob]], two-term Menteri Besar, will not continue in the role if PN won the election.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Zaidi |first1=Hazira Ahmad |title=Semua layak jadi MB, tunggu lepas PRN - Ahmad Yakob |url=https://www.hmetro.com.my/mutakhir/2023/08/994331/semua-layak-jadi-mb-tunggu-lepas-prn-ahmad-yakob |website=Harian Metro |access-date=7 September 2023 |language=MS |date=1 August 2023}}</ref> The reports were confirmed by Ahmad, who is the PAS state commissioner and MLA for Pasir Pekan, on 12 August.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=State polls: Ahmad Yakob stepping down as Kelantan MB due to health factors |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-malaysia/state-polls-ahmad-yakob-stepping-down-kelantan-mb-due-health-factors-432524 |website=www.astroawani.com |access-date=7 September 2023 |date=12 August 2023}}</ref>

[[Mohd. Nassuruddin Daud]], elected MLA for Meranti, were sworn in as the new Menteri Besar of Kelantan on 15 August, replacing Ahmad.<ref name='ADUNMERANTIMBKELANTAN'>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=ADUN Meranti sah MB Kelantan, ADUN Temangan Timbalan MB |url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2023/08/15/adun-meranti-sah-mb-kelantan-adun-temangan-timbalan-mb/ |website=MalaysiaGazette |access-date=7 September 2023 |language=MS |date=15 August 2023}}</ref> Along with Nassuruddin, MLA for Temangan [[Mohamed Fadzli Hassan]] were sworn in as Deputy Menteri Besar, with 9 other EXCO members also sworn in on the same date.<ref name='ADUNMERANTIMBKELANTAN'/>

On 7 November 2023, MLA for Nenggiri [[Mohd Azizi Abu Naim]] declared his support for Prime Minister [[Anwar Ibrahim]]'s government.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/11/975987/gua-musang-mp-azizi-becomes-latest-bersatu-member-declare-support-anwar|title=Gua Musang MP Azizi becomes latest Bersatu member to declare support for Anwar|work=[[New Straits Times]]|date=7 November 2023|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref> Due to Azizi's action violating the party's constitution, BERSATU revoked the memberships of his and 5 other politicians who also declared support for Anwar, and seeks to vacate the seats of the 6 politicians.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/06/12/bersatu-says-six-rogue-mps-immediately-sacked-today/139414|title=Bersatu says six rogue MPs immediately sacked today|work=[[Malay Mail]]|date=12 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref> The seat of Nenggiri was declared vacant by the Speaker of the Kelantan State Legislative Assembly, [[Mohd Amar Abdullah]], on 19 June 2024, paving way for [[2024 Nenggiri by-election|by-election of the seat]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/06/19/bersatu-mans-nenggiri-seat-declared-vacant/|title=Bersatu man’s Nenggiri seat declared vacant|work=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=19 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref> The by-election were won by UMNO, turning back the seat into BN's hands a year after the state election.
