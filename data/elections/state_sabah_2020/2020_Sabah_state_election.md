The '''2020 Sabah state election''' took place on 26 September 2020<ref name="announcement">{{Cite news |url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/536739 |title=Shafie: Fresh polls within 60 days, voters can decide between Musa and me |publisher=[[Malaysiakini]] | date=30 July 2020 |access-date=30 July 2020}}</ref> to elect all 73 elected members of the [[16th Sabah State Legislative Assembly]]. The previous Assembly was dissolved on 30 July 2020.<ref>[https://www.sabah.gov.my/gazette/docs/002683.pdf Sabah e-Gazette]</ref>

The state [[snap election]] was called prematurely after a political crisis arose. Both [[Shafie Apdal]], incumbent [[Chief Minister of Sabah|Chief Minister]] and leader of [[Sabah Heritage Party]] (WARISAN) and [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition government and [[Musa Aman]], leader of [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) and [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition opposition claimed to have the majority to form the government. However, the Governor of Sabah, [[Juhar Mahiruddin]] decided to dissolve the State Legislative Assembly on the advice of Shafie.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/07/30/snap-polls-called-for-sabah-as-shafie-dissolves-state-assembly/1889488 |title=Snap polls called for Sabah as Shafie dissolves state assembly |publisher=[[Malay Mail]] | date=30 July 2020 |access-date=30 July 2020}}</ref>

The state election was conducted under the [[New normal#2020 COVID-19 pandemic|New Normal]] and special [[standard operating procedure]]s (SOP) imposed by the [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Electoral Commission]] (EC) as the country is still observing the [[2020 Malaysia movement control order#Recovery Movement Control Order (RMCO)|Recovery Movement Control Order]] (RMCO) due to [[COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/sabah-to-face-snap-polls-after-assembly-dissolved |title=Sabah to face snap polls within 60 days after state assembly is dissolved |publisher=[[The Straits Times]] | date=30 July 2020 |access-date=30 July 2020}}</ref>

[[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] (GRS) coalition won the election with a simple majority of 38 seats. [[Hajiji Noor]] from BERSATU–PN was sworn in as Chief Minister 3 days later. The alliance of Perikatan Nasional with 17 seats, Barisan Nasional with 14 seats, and [[United Sabah Party|PBS]] with 7 seats made GRS the biggest electoral coalition in Sabah since September 2020.

This was the first Sabah state election not held on the same day as the Malaysia general election since 1999, when Sabah [[1999 Sabah state election|held its election]] on March that year as opposed to the general election date in November 1999.

== Background ==
The [[2018 Malaysian general election|14th general election]] witnessed 29 seats from the government side and 31 seats from the non-government side filled the State Legislative Assembly. This count, however, did not include six seats from [[United Progressive Kinabalu Organisation]] (UPKO) and four from UMNO that switched allegiance from Musa to Shafie. The government side has 11 safe seats and four fairly safe seats, while the non-government side has two safe seats and fivefairly safe seats.{{collapse top|2018 Sabah state election}}

== Election pendulum ==
The [[2018 Malaysian general election|14th general election]] witnessed 29 seats from the government side and 31 seats from the non-government side filled the State Legislative Assembly. This count, however, did not include 6 seats from [[United Progressive Kinabalu Organisation]] (UPKO) and 4 from [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) that switched allegiance from Musa to Shafie. The government side has 11 safe seats and 4 fairly safe seats, while the non-government side has 2 safe seats and 5 fairly safe seats.{{citation needed|date=October 2022}}

== Aftermath ==
{{Main|16th Sabah State Legislative Assembly}}
The GRS governing coalition formed in September 2020 after the victory consists of
* [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) (14) :[[Barisan Nasional|Barisan National Party seat]] – '''Won 14 seats'''
* [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) (11), [[Homeland Solidarity Party]] (STAR) (6) : [[Perikatan Nasional]] – '''Won 17 seats'''
* With support from [[United Sabah Party]] (PBS) (7).

Warisan saw a gain of 2 more seats from its previous 21 seats in the 2018 election. While its ally PKR and DAP retained their number of seats with 2 and 6 respectively. Warisan also made history by becoming the first and only single party in Sabah to not govern the state despite winning the most seats overall.{{citation needed|date=October 2022}}

On the other side, STAR, led by [[Jeffrey Kitingan]] won 6 seats compared to 2 in the previous election, with most of the seats won hailing from the interior of Sabah which is dominated by the [[Kadazan-Dusun]]-[[Murut people|Murut]] (KDM) community. UPKO, even though representing the KDM community, managed only 1 seat in this election, a loss from 6 seats in the previous election.{{citation needed|date=October 2022}}

PPBM or Bersatu, despite being a Malay-based party from West Malaysia and contesting in Sabah for the first time, won 11 seats.{{citation needed|date=October 2022}}

The elected assemblywoman for [[Bugaya (state constituency)|Bugaya]], [[Manis Muka Mohd Darah]] from WARISAN later died in November 2020, triggering [[2022 Bugaya by-election|a by-election]] which was not held until November 2022, concurrently with the [[2022 Malaysian general election]]. A few elected assemblymen also changed parties after the election; see [[16th Sabah State Legislative Assembly#Current composition|List of seats that changed allegiance in Sabah after state election 2020]].
