The '''2026 Negeri Sembilan state election''', will elect members of the 16th [[Negeri Sembilan State Legislative Assembly]]. It is expected to take place on 1 August 2026.

The dissolution was announced by incumbent Mentri Besar [[Aminuddin Harun]] on 4 June 2026, signed by the Yang di-Pertua  Negeri Sembilan, [[Tunku Muhriz]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=ZULKIFLI |first=SYAMILAH |date=2026-06-04 |title=DUN Negeri Sembilan bubar secara rasmi esok |url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/782490/edisi/melaka-ns/dun-negeri-sembilan-bubar-secara-rasmi-esok |access-date=2026-06-06 |website=Sinar Harian |language=ms}}</ref>

All 36 seats in the [[Negeri Sembilan State Legislative Assembly]] will be contested. The election will determine whether the incumbent [[Pakatan Harapan]] government under Menteri Besar [[Aminuddin Harun]] retains power, or whether opposition coalitions such as [[Barisan Nasional]] or [[Perikatan Nasional]] can secure a majority.

== Background ==
=== Previous election ===
{{Main article|2023 Negeri Sembilan state election}}

The previous state election was held on 12 August 2023 following the dissolution of the 15th State Legislative Assembly. The alliance between the [[Barisan Nasional]] and [[Pakatan Harapan]] coalitions won a two-thirds supermajority, securing 31 of 36 seats. Pakatan Harapan's [[Aminuddin Harun]], serving since 2018, continued as chief minister for a second term at the head of a PH-BN coalition.

=== 2026 royal crisis ===
{{Main|:ms:Krisis perlembagaan Negeri Sembilan 2026|l1=2026 Negeri Sembilan constitutional crisis (in Malay)}}
The [[Undang Yang Empat]] are the ruling chiefs or territorial chiefs who play an important role in the election of a new [[Yang di-Pertuan Besar of Negeri Sembilan|Yang di-Pertuan Besar]] of [[Negeri Sembilan]].

On 17 April 2026, [[Menteri Besar of Negeri Sembilan|Menteri Besar]] [[Aminuddin Harun]] announced that Datuk [[Mubarak Dohak]] was removed from his office as Undang of Sungei Ujong, with effect from 13 May 2025, for 33 alleged offences by Mubarak relating to the state's traditional and customary laws.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite news |author=Mohd Amin Jalil |date=2026-04-17 |title=Dibentangkan 33 sebab, Tuanku Muhriz bersama seluruh Dewan Keadilan dan Undang terima pemberhentian Mubarak Dohak Undang Luak Sungei Ujong |trans-title=Presented with 33 reasons, Tuanku Muhriz along with the entire DKU accepted the dismissal of Mubarak Dohak Undang Luak Sungei Ujong |url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2026/04/1534902/dibentangkan-33-sebab-tuanku-muhriz-bersama-seluruh-dewan-keadilan <!--|access-date=2026-04-20--> |newspaper=Berita Harian}}{{pn|date=June 2026}}</ref> This announcement came after a special session of the ''Dewan Keadilan dan Undang'' (DKU) chaired by [[Yang di-Pertuan Besar of Negeri Sembilan|Yang di-Pertuan Besar]] [[Muhriz of Negeri Sembilan|Tuanku Muhriz]] advised the Luak of Sungei Ujong to accept Mubarak's removal, in which Klana Hulu of Sungei Ujong [[Negeri Sembilan#Culture|Clan Matriarch]] (''Ibu [[Minangkabau clans|Suku]]'') and her immediate heirs (''Anak Waris'') were also in attendance.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2">{{Cite news |author=Saadiah Ismail |date=20 April 2026 |title=Declaration to remove Negri Sembilan ruler invalid, says Telaga Undang |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2026/04/1421713/declaration-remove-negri-sembilan-ruler-invalid-says-telaga-undang <!--|access-date=20 April 2026--> |newspaper=[[New Straits Times]] }}{{pn|date=June 2026}}</ref>

On 19 April, Mubarak and the other three [[Undangs]] (Dato' Haji [[Maarof Mat Rashad|Maarof bin Mat Rashad]] of Jelebu, Dato' Haji Muhammed bin Abdullah of Johol, and Dato' Haji Abdul Rahim Yasin of Rembau) declared the removal of Tuanku Muhriz over claims of alleged misconduct and named Tunku Nadzaruddin, the ''Tunku [[Panglima]]'' of Negeri Sembilan and the third son of the 10th Yang di-Pertuan Besar [[Ja'afar of Negeri Sembilan|Tuanku Ja'afar]], as the 12th Yang di-Pertuan Besar.
 
On the 20th, Aminuddin rejected the declaration on the grounds that Mubarak, who signed and read the declaration, no longer held any authority as Undang, having not received the mandate from the Klana Hulu Matriarch and her heirs.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |author=Sarban Singh |date=17 April 2026 |title=Negri ruling council advises Sungei Ujong to accept removal of chieftain |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2026/04/17/negri-ruling-council-advises-sungei-ujong-to-accept-removal-of-chieftain <!--|access-date=20 April 2026--> |website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]}}{{pn|date=June 2026}}</ref><ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite news |author=Sarban Singh |date=20 April 2026 |title=Negri govt refuses to recognise, accept declaration to remove state Ruler, says MB |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2026/04/20/negri-govt-refuses-to-recognise-accept-declaration-to-remove-state-ruler-says-mb <!--|access-date=20 April 2026--> |newspaper=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]] }}{{pn|date=June 2026}}</ref> On the following day, the four Undang claimed that Mubarak's removal was not valid as the DKU members who were present during the special session did not make a decision on the matter and that the other three Undang did not agree with Mubarak's removal, also adding that Aminuddin was no longer fit to lead the state administration and should be replaced.<ref>{{Cite news |author=Sarban Singh |date=21 April 2026 |title=Four Undang dispute MB’s claim over Mubarak’s removal |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2026/04/21/four-undang-dispute-mbs-claim-over-mubaraks-removal <!-- |access-date=23 April 2026 --> |newspaper=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]}}{{{pn|date=June 2026}}</ref>

The standoff led to an indefinite postponement of the State Legislative Assembly on April 23 after the Undangs boycotted the opening ceremony.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web |date=27 April 2026 |title=The Negeri Sembilan political crisis: What we know so far |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2026/04/27/the-negeri-sembilan-political-crisis-what-we-know-so-far/217915 |access-date=27 April 2026 |website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=24 April 2026 |title=Negri govt refuses to recognise, accept declaration to remove state Ruler, says MB |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2026/04/20/negri-govt-refuses-to-recognise-accept-declaration-to-remove-state-ruler-says-mb |access-date=9 June 2026 |website=The Star |language=}}</ref>

=== Royal crisis becomes political crisis: UMNO withdraws from the state government ===
On 27 April, 14 UMNO assemblymen withdrew their support for Aminuddin, accusing him of “mishandling” the royal crisis and failing to respect traditional institutions. UMNO leader [[Jalaluddin Alias]] declared that Aminuddin had failed to inform UMNO party representatives in the state executive council about prior discussions about the royal dispute and were only aware after the issue escalated.<ref name=":3"/><ref name=":4">{{Cite web |date=27 April 2026 |title=The Negeri Sembilan political crisis: What we know so far |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/negeri-sembilan-umno-pulls-support-chief-minister-aminuddin-harun-royal-6083591 |access-date=27 April 2026 |website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=}}</ref> Following an emergency audience with the Tuanku Muhriz and a subsequent meeting with state DAP leaders, Aminuddin told a press conference he would remain in office for the time being.<ref name=":4"/> Aminuddin added that UMNO assemblymen would be removed from the [[Negeri Sembilan State Executive Council]] following their retraction of support.

The opposition, [[Perikatan Nasional]] said that it was “prepared” to work with all 14 UMNO lawmakers to form a new government and oust Aminuddin and Pakatan Harapan from the state government.<ref name=":3"/> Negeri Sembilan UMNO and Barisan Nasional declared that they had obtained a simple majority to form the state government with the support of the five PN assemblymen, a total of 19 seats of the 36 seat legislature, against PH's 17.

However, on 30 April, federal UMNO President and Deputy Prime Minister Datuk Seri [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] announced that UMNO would remain in the unity government with Pakatan Harapan.<ref name=":5">{{Cite web |date=30 April 2026 |title=Negeri Sembilan crisis: 14 assemblymen agree to remain in the Unity Government |url=https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/122447/negeri-sembilan-crisis-14-assemblymen-agree-to-remain-in-the-unity-government |access-date=9 June 2026 |website=The Vibes |language=}}</ref>

On 24 May, DAP secretary-general and state member for [[Chennah]] [[Anthony Loke]] said the Negeri Sembilan Legislative Assembly should be dissolved to allow voters to elect a new government as they now had a "trust issue" with UMNO and the situation was now "untenable" even though UMNO decided to remain in the state government.<ref name=":6">{{Cite web |date=24 May 2026 |title=‘We do not have a choice’: DAP sec-gen cites trust issue in fresh push for Negeri Sembilan polls |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2026/05/24/we-do-not-have-a-choice-dap-sec-gen-cites-trust-issue-in-fresh-push-for-negeri-sembilan-polls/221252 |access-date=9 June 2026 |website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=}}</ref>

A ''[[Free Malaysia Today]]'' report on 8 June revealed the existence of an alleged letter dated May 25 by Bersatu President [[Muhyiddin Yassin]] to PAS leaders, revealing that [[Perikatan Nasional]] held preliminary discussions with UMNO regarding efforts to remove Aminuddin as Menteri Besar, to form a BN-PN government. Discussions at Bersatu headquarters on April 24, involved PAS Secretary-General [[Takiyuddin Hassan]], along with three PAS assemblymen, Bersatu Secretary-General [[Azmin Ali]] and two Bersatu assemblymen to sign a statutory declaration. Discussions that night continued to took place between state UMNO chief Jalaluddin ​​and several of the party's assemblymen at a hotel. Ultimately, discussions between PAS and Bersatu ended on 28 April and Bersatu ended any support for a government with UMNO without agreement from PAS, following UMMO's announcement that it would remain in government with Pakatan Harapan. The action of the Bersatu assemblyman in Negeri Sembilan withdrawing support for Umno is among the issues cited by PAS President [[Abdul Hadi Awang]] as the reason for the party to reassess its relationship with Bersatu, culminating in PAS cutting ties with Bersatu on 9 June 2026.<ref name=":9">{{Cite web |date=8 June 2026 |title=Surat Muhyiddin kepada PAS dedah plot tumbang kerajaan PH Negeri Sembilan|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/bahasa/tempatan/2026/06/08/surat-muhyiddin-kepada-pas-dedah-plot-tumbang-kerajaan-ph-negeri-sembilan |access-date=9 June 2026 |website=[[Free Malaysia Today]] |language=ms}}</ref>

===Dissolution of the state assembly===
The next Negeri Sembilan state election was not initially due until November 2028, but in a late night press conference on 4 June, Aminuddin announced that the State Legislative Assembly would be dissolved for a fresh election. The Legislative Assembly was dissolved on the next day, 5 June<ref name=":7">{{Cite web |date=5 June 2026 |title=Dissolution of Negeri Sembilan state assembly adds heat to political environment |url=https://www.thevibes.com/index.php/articles/news/123654/dissolution-of-negeri-sembilan-state-assembly-adds-heat-to-political-environment |access-date=9 June 2026 |website=The Vibes |language=}}</ref> with the consent of Tuanku Muhriz.<ref>{{Cite web |date=5 June 2026 |title=Negeri Sembilan state assembly to be dissolved on Jun 5; polls to be held within 60 days |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/malaysia-negeri-sembilan-state-assembly-dissolved-elections-6156401 |access-date=6 June 2026 |website=[[Channel News Asia]] |language=}}</ref>

Observers see PH’s move for a snap election in Negeri Sembilan as a “calculated effort” to preempt any political momentum BN might gain in the [[2026 Johor state election]] announced one week earlier on 1 June, where BN has signalled its intent to contest independently and is widely expected to secure a decisive victory. Negeri Sembilan is considered as reflective of the national political sentiment compared to Johor, which is UMNO's stronghold.<ref name=":8">{{Cite web |date=5 June 2026 |title=Negeri Sembilan election: Move to dissolve state assembly Anwar's ‘tit-for-tat’ response to Johor snap polls |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/malaysia-negeri-sembilan-snap-election-voter-sentiment-6164586 |access-date=9 June 2026 |website=Channel NewsAsia |language=}}</ref>

== Electoral system ==
Elections in Malaysia are conducted at the federal and state levels. Federal elections elect members of the [[Dewan Rakyat]], the lower house of [[Parliament of Malaysia|Parliament]], while state elections in each of the 13 states elect members of their respective [[State legislative assemblies of Malaysia|state legislative assembly]]. As Malaysia follows the [[Westminster system]] of government, the [[head of government]] ([[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Prime Minister]] at the federal level and the [[Chief Ministers in Malaysia|Menteri Besar/Chief Ministers/Premier]] at the state level) is the person who commands the confidence of the majority of members in the respective legislature – this is normally the leader of the party or coalition with the majority of seats in the legislature.

The Legislative Assembly currently consists of 36 members, known as [[Members of the Legislative Assembly]] (MLAs), that are elected for five-year terms. Each MLA is elected from a [[single-member district|single-member constituencies]] using the [[first-past-the-post voting|first-past-the-post voting system]]; each constituency contains approximately an equal number of voters. If one party obtains a majority of seats, then that party is entitled to form the government, with its leader becoming the Premier. In the event of a [[hung parliament]], where no single party obtains the majority of seats, the government may still form through a [[coalition]] or a [[confidence and supply]] agreement with other parties. In practice, coalitions and alliances in Malaysia, and by extension, in Sarawak, generally persist between elections, and member parties do not normally contest for the same seats.

== Preparations ==
=== Political parties ===
[[Pakatan Harapan]] is expected to defend its governing mandate and aim to win a majority, while [[Barisan Nasional]] also aims to win government in its own right after losing power in 2018. Both coalitions have each decided to put up candidates in all constituencies instead of an joint electoral pact, unlike in 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2026-06-09 |title=Zahid: BN to go solo in Negeri Sembilan after Pakatan lays claim to all 36 seats |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2026/06/09/zahid-bn-to-go-solo-in-negeri-sembilan-after-pakatan-lays-claim-to-all-36-seats/223149 |access-date=2026-06-11 |website=Malay Mail}}</ref>

[[Perikatan Nasional]] may also expand its challenge in Malay-majority constituencies. However, cooperation between PAS and Bersatu was put in uncertainty after PAS's decision to sever ties with Bersatu on 9 June. Bersatu has confirmed to contest under PN,<ref>{{Cite web |last=SHAHARI |first=NAZARUDIN |last2=GHAZALI |first2=NOR SHAFIQAH MOHD |last3=GHAZALI |first3=NAZARUDIN SHAHARI dan NOR SHAFIQAH MOHD |date=2026-06-10 |title=Bersatu Negeri Sembilan tetap tanding guna platform PN |url=https://www.utusan.com.my/berita/2026/06/bersatu-negeri-sembilan-tetap-tanding-guna-platform-pn/ |access-date=2026-06-10 |website=Utusan Malaysia |language=ms-MY}}</ref> while PAS said no decision had been made on whether the party would contest the state election using the PN logo.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2026-06-10 |title=Bersatu to contest Negeri Sembilan polls under PN banner despite PAS split |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2026/06/10/bersatu-to-contest-negeri-sembilan-polls-under-pn-banner-despite-pas-split |access-date=2026-06-11 |website=Free Malaysia Today}}</ref> Bersatu breakaway Hamzah Zainuddin's party [[National Vision Party (Malaysia)|Wawasan]] would contest under PN.<ref>{{Cite web |last=WAHID |first=ADILA SHARINNI |date=2026-06-13 |title=Parti Wawasan Negara bertanding PRN Johor, Negeri Sembilan |url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/783690/berita/politik/parti-wawasan-negara-bertanding-prn-johor-negeri-sembilan |access-date=2026-06-13 |website=Sinar Harian |language=ms}}</ref> Far-right party [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Front|BERJASA]] have also announced its possible participation.<ref>{{Cite web |last=BERJASA Malaysia |date=12 June 2026 |title=MAJLIS PELANCARAN JENTERA PILIHAN RAYA BERJASA NEGERI SEMBILAN |url=https://www.facebook.com/berjasa.my/posts/pfbid0Zwm6bPvge5KV3yscrQEa73jiCS6Y6WcLDbv3RCyQR2tidbkraShjGPdpVWH5ASiml |access-date=15 June 2026 |website=Facebook}}</ref>
