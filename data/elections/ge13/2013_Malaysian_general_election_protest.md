The '''2013 Malaysian general election protest''' or '''Himpunan Black Out 2013''' ([[Malay language|Malay]]) was a series of rallies held throughout Malaysia after the [[2013 Malaysian general election|2013 general election]] to express discontent with the election result. The first rally was in [[Kelana Jaya]], [[Malaysia]] on 8 May 2013. The rally was held by various Malaysian opposition-friendly non-governmental organisations and opposition parties claiming that there had been irregularities in the polling. The main venue chosen for the rally was Kelana Jaya Stadium. Web sites and blogs favourable to the opposition claimed that the rally was attended by 120,000 people.<ref>Live Report. [http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/229564 "120,000 show of force at post-GE13 rally"], ''[[Malaysiakini]]'', Kelana Jaya, 8 May 2013. Retrieved 9 May 2013.</ref> Noting that the stadium's normal capacity is about 25,000, other sources estimated the number of people in and around it to be between 64,000 and 69,000.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/thousands-pack-kelana-jaya-stadium-for-pakatan-rally |title=Thousands pack Kelana Jaya stadium for Pakatan rally |last1=Lyn |first1=Boo Su |last2=Ding |first2=Emily |date=9 May 2013 |website=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=10 October 2015 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150919012500/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/thousands-pack-kelana-jaya-stadium-for-pakatan-rally/ |archive-date=19 September 2015 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

==Accusing the Election Commission of fraudulent conduct==
The rally was initiated by [[Anwar Ibrahim]], who claimed that the election was fraudulent. The opposition won 3 states ([[Selangor]], [[Kelantan]] and [[Penang]]), but [[Barisan Nasional]] won the elections despite losing the popular vote, thereby gaining the mandate to lead [[Malaysia]] for another 5 years. In the rally [[Anwar Ibrahim]] urged opposition supporters to wear black as a sign of protest.<ref>Razak Ahmad and Florence A. Samy. [http://www.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20130506-420474.html "Malaysia Elections: BN wins with 133, wrests back Kedah"], ''[[Asiaone]]'', Petaling Jaya, 6 May 2013. Retrieved 9 May 2013.</ref>

==Issues raised==
[[Anwar Ibrahim]] and other [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) leaders present at the rally raised issues such as phantom voters, blackouts and the usage of indelible ink by the Malaysian Election Commission.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/najib-denies-bn-link-to-phantom-voter-claims |title=Najib denies BN link to phantom voter claims |last1=Chooi |first1=Clara |date=5 May 2013 |website=The Malaysian Insider |access-date=10 October 2015 |archive-date=4 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304000527/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/najib-denies-bn-link-to-phantom-voter-claims |url-status=dead }}</ref>{{Failed verification |date=October 2015 |reason=source predates the rally, therefore does not describe discussion of these issues at the rally}}
