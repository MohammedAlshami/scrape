The eleventh Sarawak state election was held on Saturday, 7 May 2016 after nomination for candidates on Monday, 25 April 2016. The 82 members of the [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly]], were elected in single-member constituencies using [[first-past-the-post voting]]. More than 1.1 million who had their names entered or retained in an electoral register for a particular electoral district in Sarawak was eligible to vote at the time of the election. Malaysia does not practice [[compulsory voting]] and automatic [[voter registration]]. The [[voting age]] is 21 although the [[age of majority]] in the country is 18. The election was conducted by the [[Election Commission of Malaysia]].

The [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly]] would automatically dissolve on 20 June 2016, the fifth anniversary of the first sitting, and elections must be held within sixty days of the dissolution (on or before 19 August 2016, with the date to be decided by the Election Commission), unless dissolved prior to that date by the [[Yang di-Pertua Negeri of Sarawak]] on the advice of the [[Chief Minister of Sarawak]].

The previous state election was held at 2011. The state assemblymen is elected to 5 years term each. It is expected to be the most hotly contested election in Sarawak's poll history. At the previous election, the opposition coalition [[Pakatan Rakyat]] made historic gains by doubling its State Assembly seats, while the ruling coalition [[Barisan Nasional]] retained a two-thirds majority.<ref name="results">{{cite news |title=BN retains two-thirds majority but Sarawak's fixed deposit shattered |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/bn-retains-two-thirds-majority-but-sarawaks-fixed-deposit-shattered/ |publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |author=Jahabar Sadiq |date=17 April 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110418002551/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/bn-retains-two-thirds-majority-but-sarawaks-fixed-deposit-shattered|archive-date=18 April 2011 |access-date=2 April 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The incumbent Chief Minister Adenan Satem first announced the election during a party branch meeting on 29 January, speculating 18 April as the nomination day and 30 April as election day.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2016/01/30/adenan-proposes-april-30-for-sarawak-elections/|title=Adenan proposes April 30 for Sarawak elections|newspaper=The Star Online|date=30 January 2016|access-date=28 April 2016}}</ref> However, Adenan said the final decision was up to the Election Commission.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2016/03/10/ec-no-idea-on-swak-poll-dates/|title=EC: No idea on Sarawak poll dates|newspaper=The Star Online|author=Mazwan Nik Anis|date=10 March 2016|access-date=26 April 2016}}</ref>

On 14 April, the Election Commission announced that the election will be held on 7 May, with 12 days of campaigning and the nomination day set on 25 April. The legislative assembly was dissolved on Monday, 11 April.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sarawak state elections set for May 7 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160821012843/https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/sarawak-state-elections/2694650.html|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/sarawak-state-elections/2694650.html |publisher=[[Channel News Asia]] |date=14 April 2016 |archive-date=21 August 2016|access-date=16 April 2016}}</ref>

==Proposed Sarawak electoral districts==
A proposal by the Election Commission to increase the number of state seats from 71 to 82 was challenged in the High Court and nullified.  However this was overturned by the Court of Appeal reasoning that the increase in seats would not breach voters' constitutional rights and was in accordance with the 13th Schedule of the Federal Constitution.<ref>{{cite news |title=Election Commission wins appeal on Sarawak boundary redraw notice|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/election-commission-wins-appeal-on-sarawak-boundary-redraw-notice|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |author=Jennifer Gomez |date=7 August 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150909162118/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/election-commission-wins-appeal-on-sarawak-boundary-redraw-notice|archive-date=9 September 2015|access-date=2 April 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The composition of seats by ethnic majority consists of 22 Dayak-Iban majority seats, 17 Muslim-Malay majority seats, 16 Chinese-majority seats, 9 Melanau-majority seats, 8 Dayak-Bidayuh majority seats, 6 Dayak-Orang Ulu majority seats, 2 Kedayan-majority seats, and 2 Brunei-Malay majority seats.

==Campaign==
[[File:SUPP and BN direct candidate posters in Sibu.JPG|right|thumbnail|Barisan Nasional campaign posters in Sibu.]]
[[File:DAP campaign in Sibu on the night before the 2016 state election.jpg|right|thumbnail|Sarawak DAP Grand Finale campaign in Sibu.]]
The Sarawak state government was alleged to have abused their power, by banning several opposition MPs and members from entering the state during campaigning, as under Section 67 of the Immigration Act, a person cannot be denied entry into Sarawak if the visit was "for the sole purpose of engaging in legitimate political activity".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2016/04/10/adenan-has-no-power-to-ban-legitimate-activities/|title=Adenan has 'no power' to ban legitimate activities|work=Free Malaysia Today|date=10 April 2016 |access-date=9 May 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/analysts-sarawak-entry-ban-bane-to-both-bn-opposition|title=Analysts: Sarawak entry ban bane to both BN, opposition|author=A. Ruban|author2=Aizyl Azlee|newspaper=Malay Mail Online|date=23 April 2016|access-date=28 April 2016}}</ref>  Opposition leaders like [[Lim Guan Eng]] and [[Azmin Ali]] were allowed into the state on a temporary basis to campaign during the election period but were told to leave after five days.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2016/04/30/not-your-fathers-state-azmin-tells-adenan/|title=Not your father's state, Azmin tells Adenan|author=Nawar Firdaws|date=30 April 2016|access-date=2 May 2016}}</ref> Both Barisan Nasional and the opposition engaged in online Youtube videos platforms to reach out to voters. Both Barisan Nasional and the opposition conveyed the "state nationalism" message through different ways. Barisan emphasized on voting on local parties to ensure the rights of the Sarawakians are preserved from cultural influence from Peninsular Malaysia while providing a vision of better living in urban centres. Meanwhile, the opposition parties emphasized that all their candidates are sourced locally. The opposition, by showing images or videos regarding the harsh life or rural dwellers in Sarawak, alleged that the federal government has been neglecting the infrastructure development in Sarawak for years.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Foong Lian |first1=H |title="Us" Versus "Them": An Ideological Battle for Electorates on Political YouTube Videos in the 2016 Sarawak State Election |journal=Contemporary Southeast Asia |date=April 2018 |volume=40 |issue=1 |pages=27–49 |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/26539162 |access-date=27 June 2022 |publisher=ISEAS- Yusof Ishak Institute|doi=10.1355/cs40-1b |jstor=26539162 |url-access=subscription }}</ref>

===Barisan Nasional===
Breakaway parties such as TERAS and UPP, with 5 and 4 seats respectively prior to the dissolution of the state assembly, have their members contested seats under the Barisan banner as direct election candidates under a deal by Adenan Satem after their parties were prevented from joining Barisan after opposition from parties such as SUPP and SPDP.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/metro/community/2016/04/29/proxy-fights-in-limelight/ |title=Proxy fights in limelight|author=Yu Ji|date=29 April 2016 |access-date=2 May 2016}}</ref> Candidates from Barisan have been seen handing out money, foodstuffs and other election goodies to potential voters.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/338619 |title=S'wak voters admit election goodies strategy works for them|author=Kow Gah Chie|newspaper=Malaysiakini|date=21 April 2016 |access-date=28 April 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/its-not-about-vote-buying-sarawak-deserves-budget-goodies-zahid-says |title=It's not about vote-buying, Sarawak deserves budget goodies, Zahid says|author=Yiswaree Palansamy|newspaper=Malay Mail Online |date=25 October 2015 |access-date=28 April 2016}}</ref> The government is also trying to tie the election to the development of the state such as upgrading roads and building the [[Pan-Borneo Highway]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nst.com.my/news/2016/04/142625/rm5m-spent-six-years-build-roads-sarawak |title=RM5m spent in six years to build roads in Sarawak|date=30 April 2016 |access-date=2 May 2016}}</ref>

===Pakatan Harapan===
Due to disagreements in contesting several seats between both DAP and PKR, both parties would be facing each other and Barisan in 6 multi-cornered fights in Batu Kitang, Mulu, Murum, Simanggang, Ngemah and Mambong.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2016/04/28/sarawak-polls-nurul-izzah-confident-pkr-dap-can-work-together/|title=Sarawak polls: PKR, DAP can work together despite seat fiasco, says Nurul Izzah|author=P Divakaran|date=28 April 2016|access-date=2 May 2016}}</ref> A DAP candidate had brought up the issue of minimum wage being lower in Sarawak compared to Peninsula Malaysia and that many younger Sarawakians were migrating to Peninsula Malaysia and Singapore for better job opportunities.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2016/04/30/minimum-wage-no-excuse-for-adenan-says-dap-candidate/|title=Minimum wage: No excuse for Adenan, says DAP candidate|author=FMT Reporters|date=30 April 2016|access-date=2 May 2016}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
===Government formation===
[[Adenan Satem]] formed the state cabinet after being invited by [[Abdul Taib Mahmud]] to begin a new government following 7 May 2016 state election in Sarawak. To be the Chief Minister, Adenan sworn in before the Yang di-Pertua Negeri at 10.00 p.m. [[Time in Malaysia|Malaysia Standard Time]] on 7 May 2016 at [[The Astana, Sarawak]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/340674|title=Blue wave sweeps across Sarawak|newspaper=MalaysiaKini|date=7 May 2016|access-date=7 May 2016}}</ref>

{{Main|2021 Sarawak state election#Background}}
This election would be Adenan's only election as Chief Minister and his final election altogether; he died in 2017. The election would also prove to be Barisan Nasional's last election in Sarawak; in the aftermath of the 2018 Malaysian general election and the defeat of BN, the four Sarawak component parties of BN exited the coalition to form a new Sarawak-based coalition Gabungan Parti Sarawak (GPS).
