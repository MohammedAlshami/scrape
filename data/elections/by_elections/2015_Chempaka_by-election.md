A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Kelantan State Legislative Assembly|Kelantan State Assembly]] seat of '''Chempaka''' on 22 March 2015 following the nomination day on 10 March 2015.<ref>{{cite news|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|title=Chempaka by-election: Nomination day |date=10 March 2015|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2015/03/10/Chempaka-nomination/}}</ref> The seat fell vacant after the death of the incumbent five-term assemblyman and former Menteri Besar of Kelantan, [[Nik Abdul Aziz Nik Mat]] from prostate cancer in [[Kubang Kerian]], [[Kelantan]] on 12 February 2015.<ref>{{cite news|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|title=Nik Aziz, 84, dies |date=12 February 2015|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2015/02/12/PAS-Nik-Aziz-Dies/}}</ref> Niz Aziz was a member of [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]], part of the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] coalition. Nik Abdul Aziz had retained the seat in the [[2013 Malaysian general election|13th General Election]] in 2013 by beating Wan Razman Wan Abd Razak of the [[Barisan Nasional]] with a majority of 6,500 votes.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2015/03/12/pas-will-only-kick-off-campaign-on-friday-to-save-costs/ |title=PAS will only kick off campaign on Friday to save costs - the Rakyat Post - the Rakyat Post |access-date=2015-03-12 |archive-date=2016-07-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160708011127/http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2015/03/12/pas-will-only-kick-off-campaign-on-friday-to-save-costs/ |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The Chempaka by-election saw a five-way battle between [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS's]] Ahmad Fathan Mahmood and independent candidates Izat Bukhary Ismail Bukhary, Fadzillah Hussin, Aslah Mamat and Sharif Mahmood.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2015/03/12/sacked-for-contesting-as-independents-in-chempaka/ |title = Sacked for contesting as independents in Chempaka {{!}} Free Malaysia Today  |access-date=2015-03-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150401064111/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2015/03/12/sacked-for-contesting-as-independents-in-chempaka/ |archive-date=2015-04-01 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Sharif Mahmood is the vice-president of Malay rights group [[Perkasa]]<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2015/03/05/kelantan-perkasa-v-p-to-contest-chempaka-by-election-as-independent/ |title=Kelantan Perkasa V-P to contest Chempaka by-election as independent - the Rakyat Post - the Rakyat Post |access-date=2015-03-14 |archive-date=2016-07-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160707215935/http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2015/03/05/kelantan-perkasa-v-p-to-contest-chempaka-by-election-as-independent/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> and also a member of the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO).  He and Fadzillah Hussin were sacked by UMNO for contesting in the Chempaka by-election. UMNO had resolved not to contest the election, ostensibly to focus on flood relief efforts in Kelantan.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2015/03/13/sacking-2-members-for-contesting-in-by-election-will-not-smear-umnos-image/ |title='Sacking 2 members for contesting in by-election will not smear Umno's image' - the Rakyat Post - the Rakyat Post |access-date=2015-03-14 |archive-date=2016-03-04 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304222839/http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2015/03/13/sacking-2-members-for-contesting-in-by-election-will-not-smear-umnos-image/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2015/03/14/Sacked-duo-carry-on-in-byelection-Former-Umno-members-soldier-on-to-champion-the-cause-of-Malays-and/| title = Sacked duo carry on in by-election {{!}} The Star}}</ref>

== Results ==

PAS candidate Ahmad Fathan Mahmood won the election with a landslide garnering 96.4% of the votes.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2015/03/22/landslide-vote-for-pas-in-chempaka/ |title = Landslide vote for PAS in Chempaka {{!}} Free Malaysia Today  |access-date=2015-03-22 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402114106/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2015/03/22/landslide-vote-for-pas-in-chempaka/ |archive-date=2015-04-02 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pas-retains-chempaka-with-10092-majority |title = PAS retains Chempaka with 10,092 majority - The Malaysian Insider  |access-date=2015-03-22 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150323054247/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pas-retains-chempaka-with-10092-majority |archive-date=2015-03-23 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web| url = http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/ec-pas-retains-chempaka-state-seat-with-10092-vote-majority| title = PAS retains Chempaka state seat with 10,092-vote majority {{!}} Malaysia {{!}} Malay Mail}}</ref> All four independent candidates lost their election deposits for failing to garner 10% of the votes.
