A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly|Sarawak State Assembly]] seat of '''[[Balingian]]''' on 29 March 2014 following the nomination day on 17 March 2014.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |title=Balingian by-election on March 29, says Election Commission |date=6 March 2014 |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/balingian-by-election-on-march-29-says-election-commission |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140306221415/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/balingian-by-election-on-march-29-says-election-commission |archive-date=2014-03-06 }}</ref> The incumbent assemblyman and former 33-year [[Chief Minister of Sarawak|Chief Minister]], [[Abdul Taib Mahmud]] resigned from his post on 28 February 2014.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Straits Times]]|title=Taib Mahmud: From Sarawak chief minister to governor|date=2 March 2014|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/the-big-story/asia-report/malaysia/story/taib-mahmud-sarawak-chief-minister-governor-20140302}}</ref> The seat was declared vacant after he took his seat as the new [[List of Yang di-Pertua Negeri of Sarawak|Yang di-Pertua Negeri of Sarawak]], succeeding [[Abang Muhammad Salahuddin]] whose term ended on 28 February.<ref>{{cite news|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|title=Taib is Sarawak's new governor|date=1 March 2014|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2014/03/01/Taib-is-Sarawaks-new-governor-King-presents-instrument-of-appointment/}}</ref> Taib was an assemblyman from the [[Parti Pesaka Bumiputera Bersatu]], a component party of [[Barisan Nasional]]. The former chairman of the party, he won by a majority of 5,154 votes against 2 other candidates in the [[2011 Sarawak state election|2011 election]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|title=Guan Eng: Pakatan sending strong message to Taib|date=16 April 2011|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2011/04/16/guan-eng-pakatan-sending-strong-message-to-taib/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140319030620/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2011/04/16/guan-eng-pakatan-sending-strong-message-to-taib/|archive-date=19 March 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The election saw a straight fight between [[Pakatan Rakyat]] and [[Barisan Nasional]]. Yussibnosh Balo represented Parti Pesaka Bumiputera Bersatu while Abdul Jalil Bujang represented [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|Parti Keadilan Rakyat]], a component party of Pakatan Rakyat.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|title=Straight fight in Balingian|date=17 March 2014|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/03/17/straight-fight-in-balingian/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140317080547/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/03/17/straight-fight-in-balingian/|archive-date=17 March 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |title=PKR fields Muslim missionary for Balingian by-election |date=16 March 2014 |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-fields-muslim-missionary-for-balingian-by-election/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140320110035/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-fields-muslim-missionary-for-balingian-by-election |archive-date=2014-03-20 }}</ref> The by-election campaign was marred by allegations that Chief Minister [[Adenan Satem]] abused his power when 4 PKR leaders were barred from entering Sarawak during the campaign period.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |title=Now PKR Wanita chief denied entry into Sarawak |date=27 March 2014 |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/now-pkr-wanita-chief-denied-entry-into-sarawak |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140329191538/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/now-pkr-wanita-chief-denied-entry-into-sarawak |archive-date=2014-03-29 }}</ref>

Yussibnosh Balo retained the very safe BN seat by a majority of 6,911 votes.<ref>{{cite news|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|title=Barisan retains Balingian seat|date=29 March 2014|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2014/03/29/Balingian-Byelection-results/}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |title=Barisan retains Balingian with bigger majority – Bernama |date=29 March 2014 |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/barisan-retains-balingian-with-bigger-majority |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140329162625/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/barisan-retains-balingian-with-bigger-majority |archive-date=2014-03-29 }}</ref>
