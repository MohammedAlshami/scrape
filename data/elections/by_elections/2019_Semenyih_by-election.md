A '''by-election''' was held on 2 March 2019 for the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Semenyih (state constituency)|Semenyih]].'''<ref name="election date">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/460695|title=Semenyih by-election on March 2 |work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=18 January 2019|accessdate=18 January 2019}}</ref> The seat became vacant after the death of the incumbent assemblyman, Bakhtiar Mohd Nor, 57, on 11 January 2019 from [[heart attack]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/01/11/semenyih-assemblyman-bakhtiar-passes-away/|title=Semenyih assemblyman Bakhtiar Mohd Nor passes away|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=11 January 2019|accessdate=18 January 2019}}</ref> Bakhtiar was a member of the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] or ''Parti Pribumi Bersatu Malaysia'' (BERSATU), a component of [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition.

The nomination day was on 16 February 2019, with a 2-week campaigning period until election day on 2 March 2019 and 26 February 2019 for early voting. The by-election used the third-quarter 2018 electoral roll that was updated until 11 January 2019, which the constituency has 53,411 eligible voters comprising 53,520 ordinary and 109 absentee voters.<ref name="election date"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/02/28/53411-voters-eligible-to-vote-in-semenyih-by-election-this-saturday/1727979|title=53,411 voters eligible to vote in Semenyih by-election this Saturday |work=[[Malay Mail]]|date=28 February 2019|access-date=28 February 2019}}</ref>

Zakaria Hanafi from [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) won the by-election. This was the first time a seat was gained by another party after PH won the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] (GE14).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/03/02/barisan-takes-semenyih-with-a-2061-vote-majority/|title=Official: Barisan wins in Semenyih with a 1,914 vote majority|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star Online]]|date=2 March 2019|access-date=2 March 2019}}</ref>

== Nomination ==
PH had announced on 14 February that Hulu Langat BERSATU Treasurer, Muhammad Aiman Zainali who is also the son-in-law of previous Semenyih assemblyman Bakhtiar Mohd Nor who had died and sparked the by-election as their candidate for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/464014|title=Bersatu picks former rep's son-in-law for Semenyih|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Faisal Asyraf |date=14 February 2019|access-date=14 February 2019}}</ref>

The [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) had announced that they would make way for [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) to contest in the by-election as the latter had more support in the constituency.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/460550|title=PAS to make way for BN in Semenyih|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=17 January 2019|access-date=19 January 2019}}</ref>

The previous BN election candidate Datuk Johan Abd Aziz of [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO)  who was also the former Semenyih assemblyman for two terms earlier before losing in GE14 to BERSATU, said on 22 January 2019 that he will not be contesting in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/01/22/ex-semenyih-assemblyman-makes-way-for-new-blood/|title=Ex-Semenyih assemblyman makes way for new blood|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star Online]]|author= Clarissa Chung|date=22 January 2019|access-date=23 January 2019}}</ref>

BN on 14 February announced that former UKM administration officer, Zakaria Hanafi as their candidate for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/464010|title=BN fields ex-UKM official for Semenyih by-election|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Kow Gah Chie |date=14 February 2019|access-date=14 February 2019}}</ref>

[[Socialist Party of Malaysia]] (PSM) on 13 February officially announced that Youth Member, Nik Aziz Afiq Abdul as their candidate for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/463874|title=PSM announces youth member as Semenyih candidate|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=M Fakhrull Halim|date=13 February 2019|access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref>

[[Malaysian People's Movement Party]] (Gerakan) initially had announced it is highly likely to contest in the by-election to serve as an indicator of the party performance after leaving BN coalition in 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2019/01/455243/lau-gerakan-likely-contest-semenyih-election|title=Lau: Gerakan likely to contest in Semenyih by-election|work=[[New Straits Times]]|author= Rahmat Khairulrijal|date=28 January 2019|access-date=28 January 2019}}</ref> On 14 February, somehow Gerakan decided to not contest in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/464020|title=Gerakan to sit out Semenyih polls|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=15 February 2019|access-date=15 February 2019}}</ref>

Social activist Kuan Chee Heng also known as "Uncle Kentang" or even "10-sen man" announced in his [[Facebook]] page that he will contest as an independent candidate. He is the Malaysian Society of Community Policing (CP) president and also a former policeman before leaving to become a florist.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/05/10-sen-man-kuan-to-contest-semenyih-byelection/|title='10-sen man' Kuan to contest Semenyih by-election|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=5 February 2019|access-date=5 February 2019}}</ref>

On Nomination Day, candidates from PH, BN and PSM along with independent candidate Kuan Chee Heng (with telephone symbol) submitted their nomination papers. The by-election set for a four-cornered fight.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/464235|title=EC confirms four-cornered fight for Semenyih|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=16 February 2019|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref>

==Controversies and issues==
After the announcement of the by-election, a registered voter Puan Sri Sabariah Mohd Shariff, 58, has filed on 11 February 2019 for an injunction to stop the Semenyih by-election, challenging the validity of the [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) as it was then run by only the chairman [[Azhar Azizan Harun]], popularly known as Art Harun.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/09/voter-files-suit-to-stop-semenyih-by-election-says-one-man-ec-unconstitutional/|title=Voter files suit to stop Semenyih by-election, says one-man EC unconstitutional|work=[[The Star Online]]|author=Sira Habibu|date=9 February 2019|access-date=10 February 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/463575|title=Voter sues EC over upcoming Semenyih by-election|agency=[[Bernama]]|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=11 February 2019|access-date=12 February 2019}}</ref> Former EC deputy chairman Datuk Wan Ahmad Wan Omar suggested the Semenyih by-election be postponed pending a court hearing on the legitimacy of the EC currently run by only one person, the chairman and the case warranted urgent attention, as six other posts were vacant when the decision on nomination and polling dates were made but Article 114 (1) of the Federal Constitution clearly stated that the EC shall be appointed by the [[Yang di-Pertuan Agong]] after consultation with the [[Conference of Rulers]], and shall consist of a chairman, a deputy chairman, and five other members. He hope the legitimacy of the EC must be verified, or else it would set a bad precedence.<ref>{{Cite news |url= https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/11/former-ec-man--postpone-semenyih-by-election-until-after-court-hearing/|title=Former EC man: Postpone Semenyih by-election until after court hearing|work=[[The Star Online]]|author=Sira Habibu|date=11 February 2019|access-date=11 February 2019}}</ref>

EC chairman Azhar has welcomed lawsuit, calls it a 'good thing' and crucial for the development of a more transparent process.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/463666|title=EC chief welcomes lawsuit, calls it a 'good thing'|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=12 February 2019|access-date=13 February 2019}}</ref> In a statement on 14 February, Chief Secretary to the Government Datuk Seri Dr Ismail Bakar said the Yang di-Pertuan Agong, [[Abdullah of Pahang|Al-Sultan Abdullah Ri’ayatuddin Al-Mustafa Billah Shah]], had on 14 February approved [[University of Malaya]] Law Faculty lecturer Dr Azmi Sharom's appointment as Election Commission (EC) deputy chairman and also approved the appointment of four EC members; Datuk Seri Ramlan Ibrahim, Datuk Chin Phaik Yoong, Dr Faisal S. Hazis and Zoe Randhawa. The appointments will be effective from the date the appointees report themselves as EC members, until they reach 66 years old.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/king-approves-azmi-sharoms-appointment-election-commission-deputy-chairman|title=King approves Azmi Sharom's appointment as Election Commission deputy chairman|work=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Markets]]|author=Chester Tay|date=14 February 2019|access-date=15 February 2019}}</ref> On 25 February, the High Court rejected the petition filed and dismissed the bid to stop the Semenyih by-election; allowing it to continue as planned.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/25/ec-welcomes-high-court-decision-semenyih-by-election-to-go-on-as-planned/|title=EC welcomes High Court decision, Semenyih by-election to go on as planned|work=[[The Star Online]]|author=Mazwin Nik Anis|date=25 February 2019|access-date=25 February 2019}}</ref>

On Nomination Day, BN supporters had chased after Youth and Sports Minister, [[Syed Saddiq]] and made offensive remarks against him.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/16/syed-saddiq-claims-near-assault-incident-during-semenyih-nomination-day/|title=Semenyih by-election: Syed Saddiq claims near assault incident after nominations|work=[[The Star Online]] |author=Joseph Kaos Jr |author2=Jo Timbuong |author3=Clarissa Chung |author4=Tarrence Tan |date=16 February 2019|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> He alleged that UMNO Youth exco member and controversial blogger [[Wan Muhammad Azri Wan Deris]], better known as Papagomo, had cornered him from a different route and put him in a headlock. While Papagomo claimed it was just a hug. Syed Saddiq later lodged a police report at Sungai Way police station and also go for a medical check-up.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/16/i-was-headlocked-says-syed-saddiq-it-was-just-a-hug-says-papagomo/|title=I was headlocked, says Syed Saddiq; it was just a hug, says Papagomo
|work=[[The Star Online]]|author= Clarissa Chung, Farik Zolkepli and Jo Timbuong|date=16 February 2019|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref> Papagomo was arrested over the alleged attempted assault on Syed Saddiq on 17 February<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/17/papagomo-arrested-over-attempted-assault-on-syed-saddiq/|title=Papagomo arrested over alleged attempted assault on Syed Saddiq|work=[[The Star Online]]|author= Farik Zolkepli|date=17 February 2019|access-date=17 February 2019}}</ref> and he was remanded for three days for investigation.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/18/papagomo-remanded-for-three-days-over-headlock-hug/|title=Papagomo remanded for three days over ‘headlock-hug’|work=[[The Star Online]]|author= Jo Timbuong|date=18 February 2019|access-date=18 February 2019}}</ref> Papagomo was charged on 28 February 2019 with two counts of intending to insult the modesty of the minister by saying to him  '' 'ko bodoh' '' (you are stupid)  and with voluntarily causing hurt on Syed Saddiq.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/28/papagomo-pleads-not-guilty-to-hurting-syed-saddiq/ |title=Papagomo pleads not guilty to hurting Syed Saddiq|agency=[[Bernama]]|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=28 February 2019|access-date=28 February 2019}}</ref>

BN supporters were also reported to have heckled PSM members and supporters as well.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.kinitv.com/video/70613O8|title=BN supporters shout 'anjing' at PSM|work=[[Malaysiakini|KiniTV]]|date=16 February 2019|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref>

The car used to ferry BN candidate, Zakaria Hanafi was splashed with paint. His aide, Rahmad Musa found out about the issue.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/465128|title=Car used to ferry BN candidate splashed with paint|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Kow Gah Chie|date=22 February 2019|access-date=22 February 2019}}</ref> A BN supporter Mamat Awang, 59, from Kuala Terengganu, collapsed and died outside of the Nomination centre on Nomination Day.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/464244|title=BN supporter dies outside nomination centre|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Kow Gah Chie|date=16 February 2019|access-date=16 February 2019}}</ref>

During the campaigning period, independent candidate, Kuan Chee Heng has his campaign banner taken down. Furthermore, banners criticising BN and [[Rosmah Mansor]], the wife of former Prime Minister [[Najib Razak]] were hung.

On Polling Day, BN complained about two cars carrying the PH symbols drove through restricted areas.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.kinitv.com/video/71102O8|title=BN cries foul as 'Harapan' vehicles enter restricted area|work=[[Malaysiakini|KiniTV]]|date=2 March 2019|access-date=2 March 2019}}</ref>
