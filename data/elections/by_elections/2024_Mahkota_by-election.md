The '''2024 Mahkota by-election''' was a by-election for the [[Johor State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Mahkota (state constituency)|Mahkota]]''' that was held on 28 September 2024. It was called following the death of incumbent, [[Sharifah Azizah Syed Zain]] on 2 August 2024 from internal bleeding.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Umno's Mahkota assemblyman Sharifah Azizah dies|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2024/08/1085525/umnos-mahkota-assemblyman-sharifah-azizah-dies|date=2 August 2024|access-date=2 August 2024|website=[[New Straits Times]] |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Mahkota rep Sharifah Azizah passes away|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/08/02/mahkota-rep-sharifah-azizah-passes-away|date=2 August 2024|access-date=2 August 2024|website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]] |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Umno's Mahkota rep dies|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/08/02/umnos-mahkota-rep-dies/|date=2 August 2024|access-date=2 August 2024|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]] |language=en}}</ref> Sharifah Azizah served as Member of the [[Johor State Legislative Assembly]] (MLA) for [[Penawar (state constituency)|Penawar]] from 2018 to 2022 and for Mahkota from 2022.

[[Barisan Nasional]]'s [[Syed Hussien Syed Abdullah]] defeated [[Perikatan Nasional]]'s Mohamad Haizan Jaafar in a landslide, winning by a majority of 20,648 votes, leading in all polling districts, as well as early and postal votes.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mahkota by-election: Barisan Nasional retains Johor state seat with landslide win|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/mahkota-by-election-bn-retains-johor-state-seat-with-landslide-win|date=28 September 2024|access-date=28 September 2024|website=[[The Straits Times]] |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=[OFFICIAL] BN secures Mahkota seat as Syed Hussien wins by a landslide of over 20,000 votes|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/09/28/official-bn-secures-mahkota-seat-as-syed-hussien-wins-by-a-landslide-of-over-20000-votes/151854|date=28 September 2024|access-date=28 September 2024|website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=en}}</ref>

== Nomination ==
On 17 August 2024, [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) Secretary-General [[Saifuddin Nasution Ismail]] confirmed that PH would not field a candidate, and instead giving way to [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN).<ref>{{Cite web|title=PH not contesting Mahkota seat - Saifuddin Nasution|url=https://www.bernama.com/en/news.php?id=2330232|date=17 August 2024|access-date=15 September 2024|website=[[BERNAMA]] |language=en}}</ref> On 7 September 2024, BN nominated Kluang UMNO Youth chief Syed Hussien Syed Abdullah as its candidate.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BN names Syed Hussien as candidate for Mahkota by-election|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/09/07/bn-names-syed-hussein-as-candidates-for-mahkota-by-election/|date=7 September 2024|access-date=15 September 2024|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]] |language=en}}</ref> Likewise, [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) nominated former [[Johor FA]] footballer and former Kluang football head coach Mohamad Haizan Jaafar as its candidate on 10 September 2024.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Mahkota Polls: Former Johor FA footballer Mohamad Haizan Jaafar named as Perikatan’s candidate|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/09/10/mahkota-polls-former-johor-fa-footballer-mohamad-haizan-jaafar-named-as-perikatans-candidate|date=10 September 2024|access-date=15 September 2024|website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]] |language=en}}</ref>

On Nomination Day, the race was confirmed to be a straight fight between Syed Hussein of BN and Mohamad Haizan of PN after nominations closed.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Straight fight between BN, PN in Johor by-election|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/straight-fight-between-bn-perikatan-nasional-at-johor-by-election|date=14 September 2024|access-date=15 September 2024|website=[[The Straits Times]] |language=en}}</ref>
