The '''Batu Talam by-election''' was held on 28 January 2007. The seat of [[Batu Talam (state constituency)|Batu Talam]] in the state assembly of [[Pahang]], [[Malaysia]], fell vacant after the death of the incumbent, Tengku Paris Tengku Razlan of the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO), the leading party of the ruling [[Barisan Nasional]]. Nominations were held on 16 January, with two candidates filing to contest &mdash; Abdul Aziz Mat Kiram of UMNO, and independent candidate Ng Chee Pang.

==Background==
The incumbent, Tengku Paris, died in December 2006. He had won the seat in the [[2004 Malaysian general election|2004 general election]], defeating [[Malaysian Islamic Party (PAS)|PAS]] candidate Mohamed Nilam Abdul Manap with 5,414 to 2,653 votes. As of nomination day for the by-election, the constituency had 10,511 registered voters, with an ethnic composition of 73% Malay, 14% Chinese, and 13% Indian or [[Orang Asli]]. There were a total of 14 registered postal voters.<ref name="nst17">Vasudevan, V. (17 January 2007). "Straight fight between BN and independent", p. 10. ''New Straits Times''.</ref>

Batu Talam thus became the one of constituency in Malaysia to have had more than one [[by-election]] . The first was held after the 1993 death of Mazlan Idris, who was murdered by the "[[bomoh]]" (witch doctor) [[Mona Fandey]].<ref name="nst17"/> Mazlan was succeeded by Syed Ali Syed Mohamad, who held the post until 1999.

PAS and [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR) of the opposition [[Barisan Alternatif]] (Alternative Front) declined to field a candidate for the by-election, citing irregularities in the electoral roll and other alleged "dirty tactics" meant to favour the government. The [[Democratic Action Party (Malaysia)|Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) also stated that it was not interested in participating.<ref name="gambit">Wan Hamidi Hamid (17 January 2007). "Is this just a clever political gambit?", p. 10. ''New Straits Times''.</ref>

==Nominations==
Despite initial appearances of a government walkover on nomination day, Ng Chee Pang filed to contest the seat as an independent candidate. His candidacy was the subject of much interest, as although the DAP had claimed it did not intend to participate, Ng was the son of DAP Pahang treasurer, Ng Kwi Ling. Ng Kwi Ling and DAP Secretary-General [[Lim Guan Eng]] both denied allegations that Ng was a secret DAP candidate, insisting that the DAP would not campaign for him. However, state DAP leader Lee Tuck Chee said that although the DAP would not campaign for Ng, the party would open an operations base in Cheroh, a town with a high concentration of Chinese voters.<ref name="nst17"/><ref name="gambit"/>

[[Najib Tun Razak]], the Deputy Prime Minister, suggested that Ng was "supported by the opposition. They claim they are boycotting the by-election but they have instructed support for the independent candidate". Pahang Barisan Nasional (BN) Youth Chief [[Liow Tiong Lai]] told the press that the move was a ploy to attract support from a broad spectrum of opposition voters, as although PAS supporters would not vote for a DAP candidate and vice versa, both sides would have no issues with voting for an independent candidate.<ref name="nst17"/>

Ng stated that his motivation for contesting the election was "to follow in the footsteps of my father" and "fulfil his ambition of winning an election" &mdash; Ng Kwi Ling had contested in six general elections since 1982, losing in all of them.<ref>[http://malaysia-today.net/blog2006/newsncom.php?itemid=1932 Unknown youngster to take on BN] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071007181749/http://malaysia-today.net/blog2006/newsncom.php?itemid=1932 |date=October 7, 2007 }}. (17 January 2007). ''The Star''.</ref> Ng, a 22-year-old businessman, also said that he intended to help petty traders like himself if he won.<ref name="nst17"/>

==Results==
Polling took place on 28 January as scheduled. The result was an increased margin of victory for the Barisan Nasional candidate.
