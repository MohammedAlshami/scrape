The '''2009 Batang Air by-election''' is a by-election for the [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly]] state seat of '''[[Batang Ai]]''',{{efn|From 2006 to 2016, the state seat were referred in official SPR records as Batang Air.}} [[Malaysia]] that were held on 9 April 2009.<ref>{{cite web |last1=LING |first1=STEPHEN THEN and SHARON |title=BN retains Batang Ai |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2009/04/08/bn-retains-batang-ai |website=The Star |access-date=7 March 2025 |language=en |date=8 April 2009|url-access=subscription}}</ref> It was called following the death of the incumbent, Dublin Unting Ingkot on 24 February 2009.

The by election was held on the same day as the [[2009 Bukit Selambau by-election]] and [[2009 Bukit Gantang by-election]].

== Background ==
Dublin Unting Ingkot, a candidate of [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN), were first elected unopposed to the Sarawak state seat of Batang Air at the [[2001 Sarawak state election]], and defended his seat in [[2006 Sarawak state election]] defeating candidate from [[Sarawak National Party]] (SNAP). A vice-president of BN's component party [[Parti Rakyat Sarawak]] (PRS), Dublin were appointed the deputy state minister of sports and agriculture after his win in 2006.

On 24 February 2009, Dublin died at Normah Hospital, Kuching after 8 months in a comatose state, when he suffered a stroke attack while being interviewed by [[Radio Televisyen Malaysia]] as Sarawak Chef-de-Mission in [[Sukan Malaysia]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=LING |first1=JACK WONG and SHARON |title=Batang Ai rep Dublin Unting Ingkot dies (updated) |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2009/02/24/batang-ai-rep-dublin-unting-ingkot-dies-updated |website=The Star |access-date=7 March 2025 |language=en |date=24 February 2009|url-access=subscription}}</ref> His death means that his Batang Air state seat were vacated. This necessitates for a by-election to be held, as the seat were vacated more that 2 years before the expiry of the Sarawak state assembly current term. Election Commission of Malaysia (SPR) announced on 4 March 2009 that the by-election  will be held on 7 April 2009, with 29 March 2009 set as the nomination day. This means that this by election will have the same election dan nomination date as [[Bukit Selambau (state constituency)|Bukit Selambau]] state seat in Kedah and [[Bukit Gantang (federal constituency)|Bukit Gantang]] federal seat in Perak, which were announced earlier by SPR on 13 February 2009<ref>{{cite web |title=Batang Ai by-election on April 7 |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/99509 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=7 March 2025 |date=4 March 2009|url-access=subscription}}</ref>

== Nomination and campaign ==
After nomination closed, it was confirmed there will be a straight fight between BN and PKR for the Batang Air seat.<ref>{{cite web |title=#Update* Nomination day |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/article/update-nomination-day |website=The Edge Malaysia |access-date=6 March 2025 |date=29 March 2009}}</ref> BN nominated [[Malcom Mussen Lamoh]], a member of PRS.<ref>{{cite web |last1=WONG |first1=JACK |title=Batang Ai: BN S'wak picks PRS' Malcolm Mussen (Update) |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2009/03/14/batang-ai-bn-swak-picks-prs-malcolm-mussen-update/ |website=The Star |access-date=7 March 2025 |language=en |date=14 March 2009|url-access=subscription}}</ref> PKR meanwhile nominated Jawah Gerang, member of parliament for [[Lubok Antu (federal constituency)|Lubok Antu]] from 1990 to 2008, while he were still in BN as [[Parti Bansa Dayak Sarawak]] (PBDS) member until PBDS dissolution in 2004.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tawie |first1=Joseph |title=PKR names Jawah Gerang for Batang Ai |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/100750 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=7 March 2025 |date=23 March 2009|url-access=subscription}}</ref> After he were not selected to defend the Lubok Antu seat in 2008, he exits BN.

==Aftermath==
The President of the [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) [[Abdul Hadi Awang]] said that the opposition camp has been winning every by-election in Malaysia after the [[2008 Malaysian general election]] but failed in Batang Air because only Dayak people wearing "loincloth" (''cawat'') are voting for BN while those wearing "neat clothes" are voting for PAS.<ref>{{cite news |title=Of 'towel heads' and bare-assed men |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/letters/109586 |access-date=22 November 2025 |publisher=Malaysiakini |date=30 July 2009 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20251122053044/https://www.malaysiakini.com/letters/109586 |archive-date=22 November 2025}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Kumpul fakta sebelum halang Hadi ke Sarawak (Gather the facts before banning Hadi into Sarawak) |url=https://www.utusanborneo.com.my/2016/01/25/kumpul-fakta-sebelum-halang-hadi-ke-sarawak |access-date=22 November 2025 |publisher=Utusan Borneo |date=25 January 2016 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20251122053054/https://www.utusanborneo.com.my/2016/01/25/kumpul-fakta-sebelum-halang-hadi-ke-sarawak |archive-date=22 November 2025}}</ref>
