A by-election in Lamag is scheduled to be held on 24 January 2026 for the [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Lamag]]'''. It was called following the death of [[Bung Moktar Radin]] on 5 December 2025.<ref>[https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2025/12/05/sabah-bn-chairman-bung-passes-away Sabah BN chairman Bung passes away]</ref> Bung served as the Lamag MLA from 2020. The by-election is held concurrently with the [[2026 Kinabatangan by-election]], the federal seat Bung held.

The election is the first by-election since the [[2025 Sabah state election]] which was held on 29 November 2025, only 6 days before Bung's death.<ref>{{Cite news |date=5 December 2025 |title=Malaysia’s BN leader Bung Moktar dies of illness, days after retaining seat in Sabah election  |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysias-bn-leader-bung-moktar-dies-from-illness-days-after-sabah-election-win |access-date=7 December 2025 |work=The Straits Times}}</ref> Bung defended the seat and was reelected as the Lamag MLA after very narrowly defeating [[independent politician|independent]] candidate Mohd Ismail Ayub, Johainizamshah Johari of the [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] (GRS), Saifulah Lokman of the [[Heritage Party (Malaysia)|Heritage Party]] (WARISAN), Mazlin Madali of the [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) and Salahuddin Anoi of the [[Parti Impian Sabah]] (PIS) by a majority of only 153 votes.<ref>{{Cite news |date=30 November 2025 |title=Bung Moktar retains Lamag seat in Sabah state election  |url=https://thesun.my/news/malaysia-news/politics/bung-moktar-lamag-sabah |access-date=7 December 2025 |work=The Sun}}</ref> The by-election is also part of the second set of by-elections since GE15 where an incumbent death led to simultaneous by-elections in both parliamentary and state constituencies since [[2023 Pulai by-election|Pulai]] and [[2023 Simpang Jeram by-election|Simpang Jeram]] in 2023.

==Background==
Lamag is a Muslim Bumiputera-majority seat comprising predominantly 86.0% of the overall voters, followed by 13.6% non-Muslim Bumiputera and 0.40% Chinese.

Bung Moktar died on 5 December 2025 at Kota Kinabalu Gleneagles Hospital, after being hospitalized for kidney failure and a lung infection.<ref>{{Cite news |date=5 December 2025 |title=Bung Moktar hospitalised with kidney failure and severe lung infection - Jafry |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2025/12/1330057/bung-moktar-hospitalised-kidney-failure-and-severe-lung-infection-jafry |access-date=5 December 2025 |work=New Straits Times}}</ref>

==Nomination==
BN nominated Ismail Ayob, as direct party candidate. He trailed second behind Bung Mokhtar on previous state election as an independent candidate.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Sabah BN announces Ismail Ayob as candidate for Lamag|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2025/12/30/sabah-bn-announces-ismail-ayob-as-candidate-for-lamag|date=30 December 2025|newspaper=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|access-date=31 December 2025|language=en}}</ref> [[Heritage Party (Malaysia)|Warisan]] nominated previous [[Kinabatangan (federal constituency)|Kinabatangan]] candidate Mazliwati Abd Malek as their by-election candidate on 5 January 2026.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Warisan announces candidates for Kinabatangan, Lamag by-elections |date=2026-01-05 |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/node/788057 |access-date=2026-01-06 |work=[[The Edge]] |language=en}}</ref>

[[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] announced it would not contest the seat out of respect for Bung.<ref>{{Cite news|title=GRS unlikely to contest Kinabatangan, Lamag by‑elections out of respect for Bung Moktar, says Hajiji|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2025/12/16/grs-unlikely-to-contest-kinabatangan-lamag-byelections-out-of-respect-for-bung-moktar-says-hajiji/202160|last=Chan|first=Julia|date=16 December 2025|newspaper=[[The Malay Mail]]|access-date=17 December 2025|language=en}}</ref> [[Perikatan Nasional]] similarly also decided not to contest both the Kinabatangan and Lamag seats.<ref>{{Cite news|title=PN to sit out Kinabatangan, Lamag by-elections in Sabah|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/regional/2025/12/1334040/pn-sit-out-kinabatangan-lamag-elections-sabah|last=Mahsinah Abdullah|first=Sharifah|date=10 December 2025|newspaper=[[New Straits Times]]|access-date=20 December 2025|language=en}}</ref>
