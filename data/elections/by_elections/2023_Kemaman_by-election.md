The '''2023 Kemaman by-election''' was a by-election that was held on Saturday, 2 December 2023 for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of '''[[Kemaman (federal constituency)|Kemaman]]'''. It was called following the disqualification of incumbent, [[Che Alias Hamid]] on 26 September 2023.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/09/26/election-court-nullifies-che-alias-hamid039s-ge15-win-of-kemaman-parliamentary-seat|title=Election Court nullifies Che Alias Hamid's GE15 win of Kemaman Parliamentary seat|website=The Star|date=26 September 2023|accessdate=3 October 2023}}</ref>

The election was the third federal by-election since GE15 after the [[2023 Kuala Terengganu by-election|Kuala Terengganu]] and [[2023 Pulai by-election|Pulai]] by-elections that were held earlier the year.{{#tag:ref|Not counting the federal by-election for [[Padang Serai (federal constituency)|Padang Serai]] and state by-election for [[Tioman (state constituency)|Tioman]], both which were delayed to 7 December 2022 because of death of respective candidates after nomination day for GE15. Both by-elections and results are considered as part of the [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 general election]] and [[2022 Pahang state election]].|group=note}} [[Ahmad Samsuri Mokhtar]] won by the majority of 37,220 votes.

==Background==
Kemaman is a [[Malaysian Malay|Malay]]/[[Islam in Malaysia|Muslim]]-majority seat comprising predominantly 94.1% of the overall voters, followed by 5.0% [[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]], 0.5% [[Malaysian Indian|Indians]] and 0.4% of other ethnicities.

Che Alias, a member of the [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) of [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) was first elected as the [[Dewan Rakyat|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for Kemaman in [[Terengganu]] defeating [[Ahmad Said (politician)|Ahmad Said]] of the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) of [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition, Hasuni Sudin of the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) of [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition and Rosli Abd Ghani of the [[Homeland Fighters Party]] (PEJUANG) of [[Gerakan Tanah Air]] (GTA) coalition by a majority of 27,179 votes in [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 general election]] (GE15).

On 26 September 2023, the Terengganu Election Court annulled the results of the Kemaman seat in the 2022 general election. With this, Che Alias lost his seat and was disqualified as an MP with immediate effect. Judge Anselm Charles Fernandis ruled that the petitioner of BN Wan Mohamad Hisham Wan Abdul Jalil succeeded in proving that there had been elements of inducement of voters during the campaigning period of the election and corruption had taken place with the aim of influencing voters through programmes of fund distribution by the Terengganu state government in the election. He added that although the Terengganu state government was not in caretaker capacity and was allowed to work normally as the [[Terengganu State Legislative Assembly]] was dissolved and [[2023 Terengganu state election|Terengganu state election]] was held separately in 2023, it did not mean the programmes should be held so close to the election. The court also ordered Che Alias to pay RM 30,000 to Wan Mohamad Hisham. Che Alias was represented by lawyers Wan Rohimi Wan Daud and Saifuddin Othman while Wan Mohamad Hisham was by lawyers Mohd Hafarizam Harun, Amin Othman and Norhazira Abu Haiyan. Hafarizam stressed that the Anselm had called the lead counsel of both parties into his chambers and informed them that he would notify the [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) of his ruling on a week later on 3 October 2023 if there was no appeal notice, indicating a decision on whether to file a notice of appeal should be made by that day and within a week. On 3 October 2023, Secretary-General of PAS [[Takiyuddin Hassan]] announced that Che Alias and PAS had decided against filing a notice of appeal to the Federal Court. Following that, the by-election was triggered.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/10/03/pas-will-not-appeal-election-court039s-kemaman-decision-says-takiyuddin|title=PAS will not appeal Election Court's Kemaman decision, says Takiyuddin|website=The Star|date=3 October 2023|accessdate=3 October 2023}}</ref>

==Nomination==
On 3 October 2023, [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|Deputy Prime Minister]], Chairman of BN and [[President of the United Malays National Organisation|President of UMNO]] [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] stressed that it would be good for BN to test the electoral support for the coalition in Kemaman once again although the opposing PN has the advantage in the Malay-majority and rural areas like Kemaman and bring down the majority of PN in the by-election from its majority in the 2022 general election should it win.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/10/03/barisan-yet-to-decide-if-it-will-contest-kemaman-by-election|title=Barisan yet to decide if it will contest Kemaman by-election|website=The Star|date=3 October 2023|accessdate=3 October 2023}}</ref>

On 13 November 2023, BN nominated former [[Chief of Defence Forces (Malaysia)|Chief of Defence Forces]] [[Raja Mohamed Affandi]] as its candidate. Affandi stands as a direct candidate, unaffiliated with any of the component parties within BN.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/11/977838/raja-mohamed-affandi-bns-direct-candidate-kemaman-election|title=Raja Mohamed Affandi is BN's direct candidate for Kemaman by-election|website=New Straits Times|date=13 November 2023|accessdate=15 November 2023}}</ref> Affandi is the first direct member to contest a by-election since [[Ramli Mohd Nor]] in the [[2019 Cameron Highlands by-election]] although Ramli subsequently joined UMNO following his election.

On 16 November 2023, PN nominated [[Menteri Besar of Terengganu]] and Member of the [[Terengganu State Legislative Assembly]] (MLA) for [[Ru Rendang (state constituency)|Ru Rendang]] [[Ahmad Samsuri Mokhtar]] as its candidate.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2023/11/ahmad-samsuri-calon-pas-prk-parlimen-kemaman-2/|title=Ahmad Samsuri calon Pas PRK Parlimen Kemaman|website=Utusan Malaysia|language=ms|date=16 November 2023|accessdate=18 November 2023}}</ref>

On the nomination day, 18 November 2023, it was confirmed that there will be a straight fight between Affandi of BN and Samsuri of PN after nominations closed.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Kemaman by-election: Straight fight between BN and PAS |url=https://thesun.my/local-news/kemaman-by-election-straight-fight-between-bn-and-pas-OB11761942 |website=thesun.my |access-date=8 August 2024 |language=en-MY |date=18 November 2023}}</ref> As per the 2022 Malaysia general election in Terengganu and 2023 Terengganu state election, PN will use PAS' name and logo on its candidate, regardless of the candidate party.
