A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly|Sarawak State Assembly]] seat of '''Tanjong Datu''' on 18 February 2017 following the nomination day on 4 February 2017.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=The Sun Daily|access-date=5 February 2017|title=Three-cornered contest in Tanjong Datu by-election |date=5 February 2017|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/2150297}}</ref> The seat fell vacant following the death of its state assemblyman and [[List of Chief Ministers of Sarawak|Sarawak Chief Minister]] [[Adenan Satem]] from cardiac arrest on January 11, 2017.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Channel News Asia|access-date=5 February 2017|title=Sarawak Chief Minister Adenan Satem dies aged 72|date=11 January 2017|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/sarawak-chief-minister-adenan-satem-dies-aged-72/3430028.html|archive-date=6 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170206103916/http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/sarawak-chief-minister-adenan-satem-dies-aged-72/3430028.html|url-status=dead}}</ref> Adenan, who was also president of the [[Parti Pesaka Bumiputera Bersatu]] (a [[Barisan Nasional]] component party), won the Tanjong Datu seat in [[2016 Sarawak state election|2016 state elections]] when he defeated Jazolkipli Numan of [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]] by polling 6,360 votes against 468 by Jazolkipli.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Yahoo Singapore|access-date=5 February 2017|title=EC chief urges voters not brush off Tanjong Datu poll|date=4 February 2017|url=https://sg.news.yahoo.com/ec-chief-urges-voters-not-brush-off-tanjong-033500700.html|archive-date=5 February 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170205183706/https://sg.news.yahoo.com/ec-chief-urges-voters-not-brush-off-tanjong-033500700.html|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The Tanjong Datu by-election will see a three-way contest between Barisan Nasional candidate Jamilah Anu, [[State Reform Party]] candidate Johnny Aput and [[Parti Bansa Dayak Sarawak Baru]] candidate Rapelson Richard Hamit.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=The Borneo Post|access-date=5 February 2017|title=Three-cornered fight in Tg Datu|date=5 February 2017|url=http://www.theborneopost.com/2017/02/05/three-cornered-fight-in-tg-datu/}}</ref> Jamilah is the widow of Adenan and has been known for her involvement in charity and volunteer work and accompanying her late husband on constituency visits.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=The Borneo Post|access-date=5 February 2017|title=Jamilah Anu to be fielded as BN candidate for Tanjung Datu by-election|date=1 February 2017|url=http://www.theborneopost.com/2017/02/01/jamilah-anu-to-be-fielded-as-bn-candidate-for-tanjung-datu-by-election/}}</ref>

== Results ==
Jamilah Anu managed to retain the seat for Barisan Nasional with a majority of 6,443 votes beating two other candidates.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Free Malaysia Today|access-date=20 February 2017|title=Landslide win for BN in Tanjong Datu by-election|date=18 February 2017|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2017/02/18/landslide-win-for-bn-in-tanjung-datu-by-election-2/|archive-date=3 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170503103521/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2017/02/18/landslide-win-for-bn-in-tanjung-datu-by-election-2/|url-status=dead}}</ref>
