The '''2010 Hulu Selangor by-election''' is a by-election for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] federal seat of '''[[Hulu Selangor]]''', [[Malaysia]] that were held on 25 April 2010.<ref>{{cite web |title=Kamalanathan takes Hulu S’gor with 1,725-vote majority |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/article/kamalanathan-takes-hulu-s’gor-1725-vote-majority |website=The Edge Malaysia |access-date=20 March 2025 |date=25 April 2010}}</ref> It was called following the death of the incumbent, Zainal Abidin Ahmad on 25 March 2010.

== Background ==
Zainal Abidin Ahmad, a candidate of [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR) were first elected to the federal seat of Hulu Selangor at the [[2008 Malaysian general election]], defeating 3-term incumbent from [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN), [[G. Palanivel]].<ref name="MPMeninggal">{{cite web |title=MP Hulu Selangor meninggal dunia |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/127514 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=20 March 2025 |language=ms |date=25 March 2010}}</ref> A former [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) member before joining  PKR in 2005, Zainal Abidin were the assemblyman for Dusun Tua in the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly]] for 3-terms from 1990 to 2004, and were the Deputy Menteri Besar of Selangor from 1999 to 2004.<ref name="MPMeninggal"/>

On 25 March 2010, Zainal Abidin died at his house in Kajang, because of brain cancer.<ref name="MPMeninggal"/> His death means that his Hulu Selangor federal seat were vacated. This necessitates for a by-election to be held, as the seat were vacated more that 2 years before the expiry of the Dewan Rakyat current term. Election Commission of Malaysia (SPR) announced that the by-election  will be held on 25 April 2010, with 17 April 2010 set as the nomination day.

== Nomination and campaign ==
As the seat was formerly won by BN's component party [[Malaysian Indian Congress]] (MIC) since 1986, usually the candidate would come from MIC. After the news of Zainal's death,  Palanivel, the former incumbent for the seat and MIC deputy president were being announced by MIC president [[Samy Vellu]] as their candidate.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Pragalath |first1=K. |title=Sah: MIC akan calonkan Palanivel |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/127553 |website=Malaysiakini |language=ms |date=26 March 2010}}</ref> However, after several discussions involving MIC and UMNO, including naming MIC Youth vice-chief V. Mugilan, BN nominated [[P. Kamalanathan]], MIC information chief and public relations director for Serba Wangi Sdn Bhd as their candidate.<ref>{{cite web |title=#Update* BN names compromise choice for Hulu Selangor contest |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/article/update-bn-names-compromise-choice-hulu-selangor-contest |website=The Edge Malaysia |access-date=20 March 2025 |date=15 April 2010}}</ref> PKR meanwhile nominated [[Zaid Ibrahim]], a former UMNO member and member of Parliament for Kota Bharu from 2004 to 2008.<ref>{{cite news |title=Zaid Ibrahim Calon PKR Di Hulu Selangor - Semasa {{!}} mStar |url=https://www.mstar.com.my/lokal/semasa/2010/04/14/zaid-ibrahim-calon-pkr-di-hulu-selangor |access-date=20 March 2025 |work=www.mstar.com.my |date=14 April 2010 |language=ms}}</ref> A lawyer that formerly owned his own law firm Zaid Ibrahim & Co, Zaid were also Minister in the Prime Minister's Department from March 2008 before resigning only 3 months later and sacked from UMNO. He joined PKR in 2009.

After nomination closed, it was confirmed there will be a straight fight between BN and PKR for the Hulu Selangor seat.<ref name="PKRvsBN">{{cite web |title=It's PKR vs BN for Hulu Selangor |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/article/its-pkr-vs-bn-hulu-selangor |website=The Edge Malaysia |access-date=20 March 2025 |date=19 April 2010}}</ref> There were 2 independent candidate nominations that were accepted by SPR on nomination day,<ref>{{cite news |title=Two former BN men stand as independents in Hulu Selangor |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/article/two-former-bn-men-stand-independents-hulu-selangor |access-date=21 March 2025 |work=The Edge Malaysia |date=17 April 2010}}</ref> but the candidates withdrew before the nomination deadline, 2 days later.<ref name="PKRvsBN"/>
