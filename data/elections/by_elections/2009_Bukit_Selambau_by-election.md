A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Kedah State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Bukit Selambau (state constituency)|Bukit Selambau]]''' on 7 April 2009 following the nomination day on 30 March 2009.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.spr.gov.my/ms/pilihan-raya-kecil-n25-bukit-selambau |title=Pilihan raya kecil |access-date=9 December 2022 |archive-date=6 December 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211206213149/https://www.spr.gov.my/ms/pilihan-raya-kecil-n25-bukit-selambau |url-status=dead }}</ref> The by-election was held on the same day as the [[2009 Batang Ai by-election|Batang Ai state seat by-election]] and the [[2009 Bukit Gantang by-election|Bukit Gantang federal seat by-election]].

== Background ==

The seat was vacated after the incumbent assemblyman and State EXCO, [[Arumugam Vengatarakoo]] resigned of both his positions on 9 February 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thenutgraph.com/v-arumugam-resigns-as-bkt-selambau-adun/|title=Arumugam quits; by-election in Bkt Selambau, Kedah|date=February 9, 2009}}</ref> Arumugam was an assemblyman from the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR), a component party of [[Pakatan Rakyat]]. He won the seat, then as an [[Independent politicians|independent]], by a majority of 2,362 votes against [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) candidate, S. Krishnan in the [[2008 Kedah state election]]. He later joined PKR after his victory, and were the party's state deputy chairman and Merbok PKR deputy chief at the time of his resignation as an MLA.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2009/04/08/former-bkt-selambau-assemblyman-arumugam-returns|title=Former Bkt Selambau assemblyman Arumugam returns|first=EMBUN|last=MAJID|website=The Star}}</ref> Arumugam has alleged that his resignation were the result of harassment towards him and his family to leave PKR by BN, an allegation that were refuted by BN.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2009/02/10/pkrs-arumugam-quits-as-assemblyman-and-exco-member|title=PKR's Arumugam quits as assemblyman and exco member|website=The Star}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaysia-today.net/2009/02/11/bn-suspicious-over-way-bukit-selambau-rep-resigned/|title=BN suspicious over way Bukit Selambau rep resigned|date=February 11, 2009}}</ref>

Election Commission of Malaysia announced on 13 February 2009 that the by-election for both this seat and [[Bukit Gantang (federal constituency)|Bukit Gantang]] federal seat in Perak will be held on 7 April 2009, with 29 March 2009 set as the nomination day.<ref>{{cite web |title=Simultaneous by-elections on April 7 |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/98205 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=6 March 2025 |date=13 February 2009|url-access=subscription}}</ref>

== Candidates ==

S. Manikumar of PKR has been nominated as Pakatan Rakyat candidate in the by-election. BN, which forms the opposition in the Kedah Assembly, named S. Ganesan from [[Malaysian Indian Congress|MIC]] as their candidate. 13 other independent candidates were registered on nomination day, making this election one on the most number of candidates contesting ever recorded in Malaysia election history (15).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/record-15-candidates-bukit-selambau|title=Record 15 candidates in Bukit Selambau|date=March 29, 2009|website=The Edge Markets}}</ref>

== Results ==
The by-election was won by Manikumar from PKR with a majority of 2,403 votes.
