The '''Sibu by-election, 2010''' was a [[by-election]] for the seat of [[Sibu]] in the [[Parliament of Malaysia]]. 

The [[Sarawak]]-based seat fell vacant after the death of its incumbent member, [[Robert Lau Hoi Chew]], from [[liver cancer]] on 9 April 2010. 

The seat was defended for the [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition government by [[Robert Lau Hui Yew]] of the [[Sarawak United Peoples' Party]] (SUPP), while Sarawak State Assemblyman [[Wong Ho Leng]] contested the poll for the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) in the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] opposition coalition. 

Wong won the election by 398 votes, wresting the seat from the Barisan Nasional.

At the time of the election there were 54,695 registered voters, a majority of whom were ethnic [[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]].  In the previous [[2008 Malaysian general election|general election]] in 2008, the incumbent, Hoi Chew retained the seat with a 3,235-vote majority against Wong Ho Leng, and Lim Chin Chuang of the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat|People's Justice Party]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mysinchew.com/node/39107|title=DAP wins Sibu parliamentary seat|date=17 May 2010|work=mysinchew.com|publisher=[[Sin Chew Daily]]|access-date=13 June 2010}}</ref> The Sibu seat had been last won by the DAP in 1982,<ref name="history">[http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsprs.php?id=490375 Sibu By-Election To See "Battle Of The Mighty Rejang"] [[Bernama]]</ref>  but had since been won continuously by the SUPP.<ref>[http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/14/nation/20100414134958&sec=nation Sibu by-election to see "Battle of the Mighty Rejang"] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121025034300/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F4%2F14%2Fnation%2F20100414134958 |date=2012-10-25 }} The Star</ref>

In this election, known as the "Battle of the Mighty Rejang", the government and opposition went head-to-head on a number of local issues concerning Sarawak, including poverty, education and development.

==Background and history==

The Sibu constituency has been dominated by the contest between the SUPP and the DAP for approximately three decades. The DAP won the seat in the [[1982 Malaysian general election|1982 general election]] when Ling Sie Ming defeated Wong Soon Kai by 141 votes, causing Sibu to become the first DAP-held parliamentary seat in Sarawak.<ref name="history"/> The DAP lost the seat in the [[1986 Malaysian general election|1986 election]] to the [[Barisan Nasional]],<ref name="election results">{{cite web|url=http://semak.spr.gov.my/spr/laporan/5_KedudukanAkhir.php |title=Keputusan Pilihan Raya Umum Parlimen/Dewan Undangan Negeri |publisher=[[Election Commission of Malaysia]] |access-date=17 May 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110906083741/http://semak.spr.gov.my/spr/laporan/5_KedudukanAkhir.php |archive-date=September 6, 2011 }}</ref> and in the [[1990 Malaysian general election|1990 election]] won the seat through [[Robert Lau|Robert Lau Hoi Chew]].  Lau held the seat until his death from [[liver cancer]] on 9 April 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/9/nation/20100409124155&sec=nation|title=Sibu MP and Dep Transport Minister Robert Lau dies (Updated)|date=9 April 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications|access-date=17 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100410221640/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F4%2F9%2Fnation%2F20100409124155&sec=nation|archive-date=10 April 2010}}</ref> His winning margin in the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 election]] had been 3,235 votes.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mysinchew.com/node/37526|title=Sarawak CM says BN ready for Sibu by-election|date=10 April 2010|work=Sin Chew Jit Poh|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref>

Despite Lau's dominance of the federal seat from 1990, the SUPP was shocked in the [[1996 Sarawak state election|1996 state election]] when two of its State Assembly seats within Sibu town — Pelawan and Bukit Assek — fell to DAP. The Bukit Assek loss saw the defeat of their president and the then-Deputy Chief Minister of Sarawak, Wong Soon Kai. The defeat spelled the end of Wong's political career and the election of the DAP's [[Wong Ho Leng]] to the Sarawak State Assembly. Wong Ho Leng subsequently lost the Bukit Assek seat in the [[2001 Sarawak state election|2001 state election]], but regained it in [[2006 Sarawak state election|2006]].<ref name="history"/>

At the time of the by-election, 66.7% of the seat's 54,695 registered voters were ethnic Chinese; the rest of the population being [[Malaysian Malays|Malays]] or indigenous Sarawakians. Christians make up 53 per cent of the electorate while the rest include Muslims, Buddhists and other religions. The seat was largely urban.<ref name="Henry">{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/16/nation/20100416123342&sec=nation|title=Sibu by-election will be on May 16, says EC|last=Henry|first=Edward R.|date=16 April 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref>

==Nomination of candidates==

The by-election was triggered by the death of Sibu's incumbent Member of Parliament, [[Robert Lau|Robert Lau Hoi Chew]] on 9 April 2010.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsgeneral.php?id=498680|title=DAP Wins Sibu Parliamentary Seat|date=16 May 2010|work=[[Bernama]]|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref> The Election Commission subsequently set down the by-election for 16 May 2010, with candidate nominations to occur on 8 May.<ref name="Henry"/>  Within a few days, the Sibu branch of SUPP recommended [[Robert Lau Hui Yew]] to contest the by-election for the governing [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition. Hui Yew was the second cousin of Hoi Chew and the son of Lau Swee Nguong, Chairman of the KTS Group of companies.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mysinchew.com/node/37567|title=Sibu SUPP Sibu recommends Robert Lau Hui Yew for Sibu by-election|date=12 April 2010|work=Sin Chew Jit Poh|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref> On 14 April, the opposition has decided that a [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]] candidate should contest the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|title=Opposition To Field DAP Candidate In Sibu By-Election|date=14 April 2010|work=[[Bernama]]}}</ref> Opposition Leader and [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) adviser [[Anwar Ibrahim|Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim]] scheduled a visit to Sibu on 18 April.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theedgemalaysia.com/political-news/163593-dap-to-talk-to-pkr-over-pr-candidate-for-sibu-by-election.html|title=DAP to talk to PKR over PR candidate for Sibu by-election|date=11 April 2010|work=The Edge|access-date=17 May 2010}} {{Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref> On the same day, Sarawak Pakatan Rakyat was formed as an opposition coalition composed of the [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]], [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat|PKR]], and the [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) — all members of the national Pakatan Rakyat coalition — and the local [[Sarawak National Party]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/24/nation/6125445&sec=nation |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100425074915/http://www.thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F4%2F24%2Fnation%2F6125445&sec=nation |url-status=dead |archive-date=25 April 2010 |title=DAP: Sarawak Pakatan formed to promote two-party system |last=Chua |first=Andy |date=24 April 2010 |work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]] |publisher=Star Publications |access-date=17 May 2010 }}</ref> The Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia, [[Muhyiddin Yassin|Tan Sri Muhyiddin Yassin]] visited Sibu on 19 April, expressing confidence that the voters were leaning towards supporting Barisan Nasional.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bersih.org/dpm-confident-bn-can-retain-sibu/ |title=DPM confident BN can retain Sibu |work=[[New Straits Times]] |access-date=25 February 2019}}</ref>

While SUPP's candidate was confirmed only a few days after Hoi Chew's death, DAP delayed announcing their candidate until 27 April.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/20/nation/20100420152850&sec=nation|title=DAP to name candidate for Sibu by-election on April 27|last=Lee Yuk Peng|date=20 April 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref> SUPP alleged the delay was due to a split in DAP; however, DAP insisted the delay was a calculated political strategy.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/22/nation/6106660&sec=nation|title=Sarawak DAP refutes SUPP's allegation over naming of candidate|last=Chua|first=Andy|date=22 April 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref> On 27 April, the Sarawak Pakatan Rakyat announced [[Wong Ho Leng]], State Chairman of Sarawak DAP and State Assemblyman for Bukit Assek, as its candidate.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.mysinchew.com/node/38314|title=Sarawak DAP chairman picked to contest Sibu seat|date=28 April 2010|work=Sin Chew Jit Poh|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref> Wong had earlier indicated his reluctance to stand in the by-election, suggesting that the party should consider the opportunity to field a younger candidate.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/4/14/nation/6052227&sec=nation|title=DAP's Wong wants young blood to stand in Sibu polls|last=Hii|first=Philip|date=14 April 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications|access-date=17 May 2010}}</ref>

Robert Lau Hui Yew and Wong Ho Leng were joined by an independent candidate, Narawi Haron. A businessman and former member of the Army, Narawi had been a candidate for the seat in the [[1995 Malaysian general election|1995 election]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/5/nation/20100505173858&sec=nation|title=Sibu by-election: Independent Narawi Haron is the third candidate|last=Hii|first=Philip|date=5 May 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref> Narawi's campaign was dismissed by the SUPP as inconsequential, while the DAP admitted that a potential split of votes between the DAP and Narawi could hurt its chances.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/6/nation/6197139&sec=nation|title=SUPP and DAP candidates prefer to face off in a straight fight|date=6 May 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|publisher=Star Publications (Malaysia)|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref>

==Election campaign issues==
The by-election was widely known as the "Battle of the Mighty [[Rajang River|Rejang]]"<ref>[http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/129137 The battle of the mighty Rejang] Malaysiakini</ref> and was seen as a litmus test for the popularity of the opposition [[Pakatan Rakyat]] and governing Barisan Nasional coalitions on a{{clarify|date=September 2012}} including land rights, poverty, native displacement and unemployment in [[Sarawak]].<ref>[http://freemalaysiatoday.com/fmt-english/politics/sabah-and-sarawak/4454-sibu-a-high-priority-poll-for-pakatan-and-bn Sibu – a high-priority poll for Pakatan and BN] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100416174203/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/fmt-english/politics/sabah-and-sarawak/4454-sibu-a-high-priority-poll-for-pakatan-and-bn |date=2010-04-16 }} Free Malaysia Today</ref>

One of the main issues during the election was [[Titular Roman Catholic Archbishop of Kuala Lumpur v. Menteri Dalam Negeri|the ban of the use of the word 'Allah' in Christian publications]]. However, the Sarawak police has banned political parties from using the issue during campaigning in the by-election as it was deemed to be a sensitive issue. Sarawak's Officer in Charge of Criminal Investigations, Huzir Mohamed, threatened to prosecute offenders of the ban under the [[Sedition Act (Malaysia)|Sedition Act]] or the [[Internal Security Act (Malaysia)|Internal Security Act]] (ISA).<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/police-bans-use-of-allah-in-sibu-campaign/|title=Police bans use of 'Allah' in Sibu campaign|last=Adib Zalkapli|date=11 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|access-date=31 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100601101723/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/police-bans-use-of-allah-in-sibu-campaign/|archive-date=1 June 2010}}</ref>

===Barisan Nasional===

The [[Barisan Nasional]] did not initially release a policy manifesto for the by-election,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bintulu.org/news/2010/05/10/bn-has-no-manifesto-for-sibu-by-election-says-supp-publicity-secretary.php|title=BN has no manifesto for Sibu by-election says SUPP publicity secretary|date=10 May 2010|work=Bintulu.org|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref> but sought support on the basis of its claim to have caused Sibu's economic growth and development.<ref name="localissue">{{cite news|url=http://www.malaysianmirror.com/sabahsarawakdetail/12-sabahsarawak/39719-local-issues-take-centrestage|title=Local issues take centrestage|date=12 May 2010|work=Malaysian Mirror|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref> Nevertheless, late in the campaign, the BN-SUPP candidate, Robert Lau Hui Yew, himself released a manifesto in which, according to ''[[Bernama]]'', he mentioned that he would prioritise ''"getting more investment, job creation, skills training for single parents to increase their income, install more CCTVs in town to improve security and reduce street crimes rates as well as to transform Sibu into a 'wireless' township."''<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-campaign-enters-final-stretch/|title=Sibu campaign enters final stretch|date=14 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|access-date=18 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100518164206/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-campaign-enters-final-stretch|archive-date=18 May 2010}}</ref>

During the campaign period, Prime Minister Datuk Seri Najib Tun Razak had distributed [[Malaysian ringgit|RM]]18 million to 60 Chinese aided primary schools, five independent Chinese secondary schools and two missionary schools in Sibu. Najib said that the Barisan Nasional government would continue to do more for Chinese education in the country, saying that ''"the Barisan government is a true friend of Chinese education,"'' when addressing more than 3,000 Chinese educationists.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/12/sibu/20100512181629&sec=sibu|title=PM: RM18mil for Chinese schools and missionary schools (Updated)|date=12 May 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|access-date=31 May 2010}}</ref> Besides that, some 246 families living in an under-developed region in Sungai Igan within the constituency had received a total of RM147,600 as a one-off allocation from the Federal Government.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/12/sibu/6240819&sec=sibu|title=Good news for 246 families|date=12 May 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|access-date=31 May 2010}}</ref> Four [[Methodist]] churches in town had been given special grants totalling RM1.75 million from the BN federal government in the final hours of the by-election campaign.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-churches-given-federal-cash-ahead-of-vote/|title=Sibu churches given federal cash ahead of vote|date=16 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|access-date=31 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519104115/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-churches-given-federal-cash-ahead-of-vote|archive-date=19 May 2010}}</ref>

The SUPP attacked the DAP on the basis of its coalition with the Islamist PAS, arguing that "A vote for Rocket is a vote for Islamic State".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/bn-brings-islamic-state-issue-to-sibu/ |title=BN brings Islamic state issue to Sibu|last=Adib Zalkapli|date=10 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=31 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100604032031/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/bn-brings-islamic-state-issue-to-sibu/|archive-date=4 June 2010}}</ref> [[Lim Kit Siang]], the parliamentary leader of the DAP, said that the issue was a "killer weapon" which could "finish off" the chances of DAP's candidate winning by frightening off the Chinese and non-Muslim voters in Sibu as it did during the [[1999 Malaysian general election|1999 Malaysian General Election]] where both [[Karpal Singh]] and Lim himself lost their parliamentary seats.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.malaysianmirror.com/sabahsarawakdetail/12-sabahsarawak/39647-big-guns-out-to-score-brownie-points |title=Big guns out to score brownie points|last=Reubens|first=Chris|date=12 May 2010|work=Malaysian Mirror |access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref> The SUPP also claimed that if the DAP was to be voted in [[Sibu, Sarawak#Cuisine|kampua]] noodles could no longer be sold as they were mixed with pork lard; a reference to PAS's policy to establish Malaysia as an Islamic state.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dap-man-eats-kampua-to-debunk-islamic-state-fears/ |title=DAP man eats 'kampua' to debunk Islamic state fears|last=Adib Zalkapli|date=13 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=18 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100516042503/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dap-man-eats-kampua-to-debunk-islamic-state-fears/|archive-date=16 May 2010}}</ref>

[[Abdul Taib Mahmud]], the BN chief minister of Sarawak, revised new land renewal rates which took effect on June 1. He said that the Sarawak new land rates is the cheapest in Malaysia as compared to other states. In accordance with opposition proposal for the unconditioned, automatic renewal of land leases, he also challenged the opposition-led state governments to announce new land rates lower than the current one announced by himself, without risking the income of the state governments.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/taib-announces-new-rates-for-land-use-in-sarawak/ |title=Taib announces new rates for land use in Sarawak|date=14 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=13 July 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100715232837/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/taib-announces-new-rates-for-land-use-in-sarawak/|archive-date=15 July 2010}}</ref>

[[Najib Tun Razak]], the [[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Prime Minister]] and leader of BN, promised voters in Rejang Park, a DAP stronghold, 5 million ringgit to alleviate floods if SUPP's Robert Lau Hui Yew won the seat,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://beta.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/najib-dishes-out-more-money-as-race-tightens/|title=Najib dishes out more money as race tightens|last=Adib Zalkapli|date=15 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|access-date=18 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120707010722/http://beta.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/najib-dishes-out-more-money-as-race-tightens/|archive-date=7 July 2012}}</ref> stating at an election appearance: ''"Do we have a deal or not? We do! You want the RM5 million, I want Robert Lau to win.”''<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blog.limkitsiang.com/2010/05/19/najib%E2%80%99s-infamous-sibu-campaign-speech-seeing-is-believing/|title=Najib's infamous Sibu campaign speech – Seeing is Believing|last=Lim Kit Siang|date=20 May 2010|access-date=20 May 2010}}</ref>

===Democratic Action Party===

The DAP set out a seven-point manifesto with the theme "Sibu Jom Ubah" or "Sibu4change" for the by-election campaign and on the top of it is solving the town's flood woes in two years when the party and its coalition becomes the government at the federal and state levels.<ref name="dapmanifesto">{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/10/sibu/6226432&sec=sibu |title=Flood woes top manifesto|date=10 May 2010|work=The Star (Malaysia) |access-date=31 May 2010}}</ref> Another point in the manifesto stated that DAP would provide all Sarawakians aged above 60 a minimum of RM100 per annum as a gift for their contributions to the state.<ref name="dapmanifesto"/> A minimum wage of RM750 per month for workers in the peninsula and RM830 for workers in Sarawak will be introduced<ref name="dapmanifesto"/> and on education, DAP promised that all schools would be given allocations according to their needs as practised in Penang, Selangor and Kelantan.<ref name="dapmanifesto"/> The three other points in the manifesto concerned oil royalty, land reform, and competency, accountability and transparency in governance.<ref name="dapmanifesto"/>

The DAP also focused on landholding issues. [[Chong Chieng Jen]], the director of the DAP's by-election campaign, pointed out that those who decided not to renew their 60-year land leases would have their land returned to the government without compensation, and that even if the government approved requests to have leases renewed, landholders would have to pay large premiums. Chong said that the DAP on the other hand wanted the current state land code to be amended to allow for the automatic and unconditional renewal of all leased land for a period of 99 years.<ref name="localissue"/>

On SUPP's questioning DAP's association with PAS despite having different ideology, the DAP defended itself by stating that PAS has been steadfast in the rights to freedom of religion of non-Muslims and this can be seen through PAS being able to declare that there is absolutely nothing in the Quran which forbids the use of the term "Allah" in Christian publications.<ref name="allahissue"/>

The DAP sought to pressure Robert Lau Hui Yew over the government's proposed [[Goods and services tax in Malaysia|Goods and Services Tax]], arguing that the tax would increase prices for consumers in one of Malaysia's poorest states.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.malaysianmirror.com/sabahsarawakdetail/12-sabahsarawak/39497-make-a-stand-on-gst-wong-tells-lau-|title=Make a stand on GST, Wong tells Lau|date=10 May 2010|work=Malaysian Mirror|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref> The DAP, through its leader [[Lim Kit Siang]] also pressured Hui Yew to speak up against the government's education policy, claiming that Hui Yew's choice to have his children educated overseas demonstrated his lack of confidence in Malaysian education.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://blog.limkitsiang.com/2010/05/07/why-supp-candidate-lau-hui-yew-is-like-other-barisan-nasional-ministers-and-leaders-who-have-no-confidence-in-their-own-school-system-by-sending-their-children-to-international-schools-or-overseas/|title=Why SUPP candidate Lau Hui Yew is like other Barisan Nasional Ministers and leaders who have no confidence in their own school system by sending their children to international schools or overseas?|last=Lim Kit Siang|date=7 May 2010|work=Lim Kit Siang for Malaysia|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref> On education, the DAP also criticised SUPP's involvement in the sale of the Laila Taib College, previously known as United College Sarawak, to the Yayasan Sarawak (Sarawak Foundation) for the price of one [[Malaysian ringgit|ringgit]], when the local Chinese population had donated money to fund the college's building.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/131853|title=Sibu masih memihak kepada BN|date=15 May 2010|work=malaysiakini|language=ms|access-date=18 May 2010}}</ref>

==Results==

The election was won by Wong Ho Leng by a margin of 398 votes over Robert Lau Hui Yew. The result was seen as a surprise win for the DAP and the opposition, as it ended a run of by-election defeats for the Pakatan Rakyat parties and boosted the DAP ahead of the [[2011 Sarawak state election|state elections]] due to be held in Sarawak in 2011. The victorious Wong claimed his election was a "miracle". 37,919 out of 54,695 registered voters cast a ballot, signifying a 70% turnout.  There were 395 spoilt votes.<ref name="TMIresults">{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dap-takes-sibu/|title=DAP takes Sibu|date=17 May 2010|work=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|access-date=18 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519104100/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dap-takes-sibu|archive-date=19 May 2010}}</ref>

===Postal votes controversy===

The announcement of the result was marred by a two-hour-long delay because of arguments over the discrepancies of the postal votes by [[Pakatan Rakyat]] election agents.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mstar.com.my/berita/cerita.asp?file=/2010/5/17/mstar_berita/20100517114433&sec=mstar_berita |title=PRK Sibu: SPR Nafi Lengahkan Pegumuman Keputusan|publisher=[[Bernama]] |access-date=19 May 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100518080503/http://www.mstar.com.my/berita/cerita.asp?file=%2F2010%2F5%2F17%2Fmstar_berita%2F20100517114433&sec=mstar_berita|archive-date=18 May 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> Of the 2,827 ballot papers issued for postal votes, BN won 2,323 votes (82.2%), DAP won 70 (2.4%), the independent won 36 (1.2%) while 208 (7.3%) were considered spoilt.<ref name="postalvotes">{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-election-agent-claims-daps-majority-could-have-been-more/ |title=PKR election agent claims DAP's majority could have been more|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=19 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100520011356/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-election-agent-claims-daps-majority-could-have-been-more|archive-date=20 May 2010}}</ref>

Pahang PKR secretariat director Zaidi Ahmad, who was appointed by the party as the team leader in charge of monitoring the postal votes during the polls, mentioned that the DAP would not have won if the 208 postal votes were not declared spoilt and the 170 ballot papers for what he claimed were "phantom postal voters" were returned and added to the tally.<ref name="postalvotes"/>

Zaidi had claimed that the forms state the names, identification numbers and information about the voters and are supposed to be signed by the voters themselves as well as their witnesses. However, the election agents noticed that the same witness would sign differently on different forms. On some forms, the voters themselves did not even sign them. Zaidi concluded by saying that it meant that others had signed on their behalf.<ref name="postalvotes"/> He added that only after persistent complaints from the PR's election agents, Election Commission officials agreed to consider 208 ballot papers from the postal votes as spoilt votes.<ref name="postalvotes"/> He also said that they have found at least between 700 and 800 more postal votes that had discrepancies but the EC disallowed these from being considered as spoilt. Zaidi finally claimed that if the by-election had truly been run properly, DAP could have won with a much bigger majority as if at least 700 more postal votes were rejected as spoilt, DAP could have won the polls with a majority of a little over 1,000.<ref name="postalvotes"/>

As a result, the Election Commission would review the postal voting system where EC chairman Tan Sri Abdul Aziz Mohd Yusof said they would study the proposal to call for an early voting session for army personnel, policemen and EC staff instead of posting votes.<ref>{{cite web|author=Ai Fern, Ng|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/26/nation/20100526175112&sec=nation|title=EC to review postal voting system|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|access-date=26 May 2010}}</ref> Nevertheless, the Youth wing of PAS have demanded for the postal votes to be abolished altogether as it claims that the postal votes are no longer relevant and is prone to abuse and exploitation by the government to their benefit.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://idhamlim.blogspot.com/2010/05/pemuda-pas-tuntut-hapus-undi-pos.html|title=Pemuda PAS tuntut hapus undi pos|publisher=[[Harakah]]|access-date=19 May 2010}}</ref>

==Aftermath==

With the win of the DAP's candidate, there were various responses from the community. While there is fear by the local Chinese community leaders that BN's defeat in Sibu would affect the town's growth,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/18/nation/6280628&sec=nation |title=Chinese fear BN defeat would affect Sibu's growth|date=15 May 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]] |access-date=19 May 2010}}</ref> others like the opposition take it as a good omen as BN can no longer take Sarawak as its "fixed-deposit" in terms of parliamentary seats and neglect the state's development. [[Chua Soi Lek]] of the [[Malaysian Chinese Association]], a member of the BN, said that the government could no longer hide from the fact that there were issues plaguing the Chinese community and that it can no longer be swept under the carpet following the losing of Chinese-majority polling centres in the by-election.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-results-en-eye-opener-for-bn-says-soi-lek/ |title=Sibu result an eye-opener for BN, says Soi Lek|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=19 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519120504/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-results-en-eye-opener-for-bn-says-soi-lek|archive-date=19 May 2010}}</ref>

[[Lim Kit Siang]] of the DAP mentioned that the party's win in Sibu renews [[Pakatan Rakyat]]'s [[Putrajaya]] dream,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-renews-putrajaya-dream-says-kit-siang/ |title=Sibu renews Putrajaya dream, says Kit Siang|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=19 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519120459/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-renews-putrajaya-dream-says-kit-siang/|archive-date=19 May 2010}}</ref> referring to PR's ambition to form the Federal government in the next 13th general election. However, political scientists mentioned that PR would have a tough time getting support from rural areas, referring to the DAP's dismal performance in rural polling centres.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-reflects-growing-sarawak-voting-divide/ |title=Sibu reflects growing Sarawak voting divide|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=19 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100519120454/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/sibu-reflects-growing-sarawak-voting-divide|archive-date=19 May 2010}}</ref> Nevertheless, encouraged by the strong support from the urban Chinese community in the Sibu by-election, DAP is now aiming to win the four other Chinese-majority parliamentary seats (namely the Sarikei, Miri, Stampin and Lanang seats) and seven other State constituencies held by SUPP in Sarawak.<ref>{{cite web|author=Tawie, Joseph|url=http://www.malaysianmirror.com/sabahsarawakdetail/12-sabahsarawak/41047-pr-targets-more-supp-seats |title=PR targets more SUPP seats|publisher=Malaysian Mirror |access-date=26 May 2010}}</ref>

[[Abdul Taib Mahmud]], the BN Chief Minister of Sarawak, said that the BN lost mainly due to the influx of PR campaigners from Peninsular Malaysia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/taib-new-campaign-tactics-by-opposition-cost-bn-sibu-seat/ |title=Taib: New campaign tactics by opposition cost BN Sibu seat|publisher=[[Bernama]] |access-date=19 May 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100521092252/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/taib-new-campaign-tactics-by-opposition-cost-bn-sibu-seat|archive-date=21 May 2010}}</ref> Abang Johari Openg, Sarawak state Minister for Housing and Urban Development, blamed the "provocative tactics" allegedly employed by Pakatan Rakyat leaders for causing the defeat of the SUPP candidate, referring to the raising of the 'Allah' issue.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.limkitsiang.com/2010/05/30/sibu-by-election-the-incredible-hulk-speech-after-the-barbarians-at-the-gate-warning/ |title=S'wak BN shell-shocked by Sibu defeat|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]] |access-date=31 May 2010}}</ref> President of [[Parti Rakyat Sarawak]] (a component party of BN in Sarawak) and state Minister of Land Development James Masing had warned Peninsular Malaysia-based opposition parties to refrain from practising "rowdy" politics in Sarawak saying that it might break down social order in the country, citing the [[May 13 incident]] where a racial riot occurred when BN lost its two-thirds [[supermajority]] in parliament during the [[1969 Malaysian general election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theborneopost.com/?p=32441 |title=Don't make Incredible Hulk angry, opposition told|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]] |access-date=30 May 2010}}</ref>

As for the SUPP, its president [[George Chan Hong Nam]] who congratulated its winning candidate Wong immediately after the election result was announced, said that his party was completely overwhelmed by DAP's streetfighter style of politics<ref>{{cite web|author=Tan, Joceline|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/18/nation/6280114&sec=nation|title=Defeat a bitter pill to swallow|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|access-date=19 May 2010}}</ref> and said, "''these outsiders with their street culture have come to spoil the good nature of Sarawakians,"'' and that ''"SUPP would do all it could to ensure that the "outsiders" do not gain a foothold here''".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blog.limkitsiang.com/2010/05/24/najib-should-haul-up-and-reprimand-supp-president-george-chan-for-his-%E2%80%9Cbarbarians-at-the-gate%E2%80%9D-speech-which-makes-a-total-mockery-of-the-prime-minister%E2%80%99s-year-old-1malaysia-po/|title=Sibu by-election – the "Incredible Hulk" warning after the "Barbarians at the gate" speech|publisher=Lim Kit Siang|access-date=31 May 2010}}</ref> Wong Soon Koh, the SUPP state assemblyman for the Bawang Assan state seat within Sibu and a minister in the Sarawak state cabinet, claimed that the DAP was now extremely rich and could fly over campaigners from [[Peninsula Malaysia]] to Sibu for the by-election.<ref name="soonkoh">{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/fmt-english/politics/sabah-and-sarawak/6056-clueless-supp-comes-under-heavy-dap-fire|title='Clueless' SUPP comes under heavy DAP fire|publisher=Free Malaysia Today|access-date=27 May 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100530070038/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/fmt-english/politics/sabah-and-sarawak/6056-clueless-supp-comes-under-heavy-dap-fire|archive-date=30 May 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> Soon Koh's remarks came under fire by [[Chong Chieng Jen]] of the DAP where he defended the party by saying that nearly all DAP campaigners including Members of Parliament, state assemblymen or ordinary members, took care of their own expenses and stayed either with party members in Sibu or in low-budget hotels to save expenses. Chong also said that unlike BN leaders, they do not fly first class, nor stay at five-star hotels which are paid for by the federal or state government under the pretext of carrying out their government duties while on the other hand obviously campaigning for the SUPP candidate.<ref name="soonkoh"/>

[[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]], the president of Malay rights group [[Perkasa]] labelled the Chinese in Sibu as ungrateful for not voting for the BN. He claimed that the Chinese in Sibu had not appreciated the development projects and financial allocations offered by BN while the [[Bumiputera (Malaysia)|Bumiputera]] had to endure and compromise with the wish of Chinese community.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/18/sibu/6279918&sec=sibu|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100523045649/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F5%2F18%2Fsibu%2F6279918&sec=sibu#|url-status=dead|archive-date=2010-05-23 |title=Ibrahim labels Chinese as ungrateful|date=18 May 2010|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]] |access-date=29 July 2010}}</ref> His statement however, was met with strong opposition from the local community, including the Bumiputera community, and he was dubbed as 'ignoramus' upon his statement.<ref>{{cite news|author=Loh, Deborah|url=http://www.thenutgraph.com/who-supports-perkasa/ |title=Who supports Perkasa?|date=19 May 2010|work=The Nut Graph |access-date=28 April 2012}}</ref>

Nevertheless, the losing candidate, Lau Hui Yew, pledged that he will continue to serve the people as a councilor of [[Sibu Municipal Council]] (SMC) and as a party leader.<ref>{{cite web|author=Tan, Joceline|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/18/nation/6280887&sec=nation|title=Lau pledges to serve the people|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|access-date=19 May 2010}}</ref> On the other hand, [[Muhyiddin Yassin]], the [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia]] and BN's campaign director in Sibu, said that the BN will keep all promises and commitments made during the by-election even though the party lost the by-election.<ref>{{cite web|author=Ng, Si Hooi|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/5/18/nation/6281482&sec=nation|title=Muhyiddin: Promises made during Sibu by-election to be fulfilled|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|access-date=19 May 2010}}</ref>
