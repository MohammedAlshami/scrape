The [[Tenang]] state by-election was a state by-election that was held on 30 January 2011 in the state of [[Johor]], Malaysia.  The nomination of candidates was done on 22 January 2011.  The Tenang parliamentary seat fell vacant when its state assemblyman Datuk [[Sulaiman Taha]] of [[United Malays National Organisation]] died due to a blood infection and other complications due to diabetes.<ref>[http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/fmt-english/politics/barisan-nasional/14634-storm-clouds-may-roll-into-tenang-skies Storm clouds may roll into Tenang skies] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101227042611/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/fmt-english/politics/barisan-nasional/14634-storm-clouds-may-roll-into-tenang-skies |date=27 December 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thesundaily.com/article.cfm?id=55528 |title=Tenang state by-election Jan 30, nomination Jan 22 |publisher=Thesundaily.com |date=6 November 2011 |accessdate=10 November 2011 }}{{Dead link|date=April 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Previously Sulaiman won the Tenang seat with a 2,492-vote majority, beating [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]]' Mohd Saim Siran at the [[2008 Malaysian general election]]s. The state assembly seat has some 14,511 voters consisting of 48.9% Malays, 38.3% Chinese, 12% Indians and 0.9% other races.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v5/newspolitic.php?id=552353 |title=Tenang is BN's fort, says DPM |publisher=Bernama.com.my |date=24 December 2010 |accessdate=10 November 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110629034240/http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v5/newspolitic.php?id=552353 |archive-date=29 June 2011 |url-status=dead  }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|author=Hafiz Yatim |url=http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/151573 |title=Tenang by-election on Jan 30 |publisher=Malaysiakini.com |accessdate=10 November 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.malaysianmirror.com/media-buzz-detail/6-nation/51256-tenang-by-election-to-be-held-on-jan-30 |title=Tenang by-election to be held on Jan 30 |publisher=Malaysianmirror.com |date=24 December 2010 |accessdate=10 November 2011}}</ref> PAS has picked as its candidate Normala Sudirman, a school teacher,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thesundaily.com/article.cfm?id=56412 |title=PR picks woman candidate for Tenang |publisher=Thesundaily.com |date=6 November 2011 |accessdate=10 November 2011 }}{{Dead link|date=April 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Letters from readers |url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/2011/01/17/fighting-hard-for-womens-vote/ |title=Fighting hard for women’s vote |publisher=Freemalaysiatoday.com |date=17 January 2011 |accessdate=10 November 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110721075508/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/2011/01/17/fighting-hard-for-womens-vote/ |archive-date=21 July 2011 |url-status=dead  }}</ref>  while Barisan Nasional has picked Mohd Azahar.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mysinchew.com/node/51775 |title=Mohd Azahar pledges to serve all races |publisher=Mysinchew.com |accessdate=10 November 2011}}</ref>
