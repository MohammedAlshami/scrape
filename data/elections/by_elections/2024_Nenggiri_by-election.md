The '''2024 Nenggiri by-election''' was held on 17 August 2024 for the [[Kelantan State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Nenggiri]]'''. The by-election was called following the disqualification of the incumbent, [[Mohd Azizi Abu Naim]], on 19 June 2024 due to his support for the [[Anwar Ibrahim]] government.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kelantan declares Nenggiri seat as vacant|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2024/06/1065476/kelantan-declares-nenggiri-seat-vacant|date=19 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024|website=[[New Straits Times]] |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/06/19/kelantan-speaker-declares-vacancy-in-nenggiri-seat-held-by-rogue-bersatu-man-who-declared-support-for-pm-anwar/140438|title=Kelantan Speaker declares vacancy in Nenggiri seat held by rogue Bersatu man who declared support for PM Anwar|website=[[Malay Mail]]|language=en|date=19 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/06/19/kelantan-speaker-declares-rogue-bersatu-rep039s-nenggiri-state-seat-vacant|title=Kelantan Speaker declares rogue Bersatu rep's Nenggiri state seat vacant|website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|language=en|date=19 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref> Mohd Azizi had served as a Member of the Kelantan State Legislative Assembly for Nenggiri since 2023, and also as the [[Member of Parliament (Malaysia)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Gua Musang (federal constituency)|Gua Musang]].

In this by-election, [[Barisan Nasional]]'s [[Mohd Azmawi Fikri Abdul Ghani]] defeated [[Perikatan Nasional]]'s Mohd Rizwadi Ismail by a majority of 3,352 votes. This marked the first by-election since the [[2022 Malaysian general election]] where a seat flipped to another party.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/08/17/unofficial-in-blow-to-pas-bn-takes-nenggiri-in-heartland-of-kelantan/147333|title=In blow to PAS, BN scores massive win to take back Nenggiri|website=[[Malay Mail]]|language=en|date=17 August 2024|access-date=17 August 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/08/17/nenggiri-polls-live-updates-ec-says-voter-turnout-nearly-72-as-of-4pm|title=Official: Barisan wins Nenggiri by 3,352 vote majority, says EC|website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|language=en|date=17 August 2024|access-date=17 August 2024}}</ref>

== Background ==
[[Mohd Azizi Abu Naim]] was first elected to the [[Kelantan State Legislative Assembly]] seat of [[Nenggiri]] at the [[2023 Kelantan state election]]. Prior to this, he contested and won the federal seat of [[Gua Musang (federal constituency)|Gua Musang]] at the [[2022 Malaysian general election]], defeating longtime incumbent [[Tengku Razaleigh Hamzah]] of [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN). He contested under the ticket of [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party|Parti Pribumi Bersatu Malaysia]] (BERSATU), a component party of [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN). On 7 November 2023, Mohd Azizi declared his support for Prime Minister [[Anwar Ibrahim]]'s government.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/11/975987/gua-musang-mp-azizi-becomes-latest-bersatu-member-declare-support-anwar|title=Gua Musang MP Azizi becomes latest Bersatu member to declare support for Anwar|work=[[New Straits Times]]|date=7 November 2023|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref> As a result of violating the party's constitution, BERSATU revoked the memberships of Mohd Azizi and five other politicians who also declared their support for Anwar. PN sought to vacate the seats of these six politicians.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/06/12/bersatu-says-six-rogue-mps-immediately-sacked-today/139414|title=Bersatu says six rogue MPs immediately sacked today|work=[[Malay Mail]]|date=12 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref>

The seat of Nenggiri was declared vacant by the Speaker of the Kelantan State Legislative Assembly, [[Mohd Amar Abdullah]], on 19 June 2024, making it the first seat to be declared vacant following the defections.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/06/19/bersatu-mans-nenggiri-seat-declared-vacant/|title=Bersatu man’s Nenggiri seat declared vacant|work=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=19 June 2024|access-date=19 June 2024}}</ref>

== Nomination ==
Mohd Azizi announced that he would not contest in the election, paving the way for others to take over the seat.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Former Nenggiri assemblyman Mohd Azizi steps aside for unity govt candidates in upcoming by-election|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/06/30/former-nenggiri-assemblyman-mohd-azizi-steps-aside-for-unity-govt-candidates-in-upcoming-by-election/142280|date=30 June 2024|access-date=15 July 2024|website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=en}}</ref> On 11 July 2024, BN, expected to represent the state opposition and the federal government, confirmed that it would nominate a candidate to contest in the election.<ref>{{Cite web|title=BN to contest Nenggiri by-election, unity govt secretariat confirms|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/07/11/bn-to-contest-nenggiri-by-election-unity-govt-secretariat-confirms/|date=11 July 2024|access-date=15 July 2024|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|language=en}}</ref> On 18 July 2024, BN nominated Kelantan [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) youth chief [[Mohd Azmawi Fikri Abdul Ghani]] as its candidate.<ref>{{Cite web|title=PRK Nenggiri: Mohd Azmawi diumum calon BN|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/675748/berita/politik/prk-nenggiri-mohd-azmawi-diumum-calon-bn|date=18 July 2024|access-date=29 July 2024|website=[[Sinar Harian]]|language=ms}}</ref> On 29 July 2024, PN nominated former Gua Musang PAS deputy youth chief Mohd Rizwadi Ismail as its candidate. Rizwadi was previously a member of PAS before joining BERSATU.<ref>{{Cite web|title=PN names ex-PAS man for Nenggiri by-election|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/07/29/pn-names-ex-pas-man-for-nenggiri-by-election/|date=29 July 2024|access-date=29 July 2024|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=PRK Nenggiri: Bekas timbalan Ketua Pemuda PAS Gua Musang diumum calon BERSATU|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2024/07/1277908/prk-nenggiri-bekas-timbalan-ketua-pemuda-pas-gua-musang-diumum-calon|date=29 July 2024|access-date=29 July 2024|website=[[Berita Harian]]|language=ms}}</ref>

On nomination day, it was confirmed that there would be a straight fight between Azmawi of BN and Rizwadi of PN after nominations closed. As in the 2022 Malaysian general election in Kelantan and the 2023 Kelantan state election, PN decided to use PAS' name and logo for its candidate, regardless of the candidate's party affiliation.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Nenggiri by-election a straight fight between BN, PAS |url=https://thesun.my/local-news/nenggiri-by-election-a-straight-fight-between-bn-pas-JK12811190 |website=thesun.my |access-date=8 August 2024 |language=en-MY |date=3 August 2024}}</ref>

== Controversies ==
During a [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) campaign event, the event's host Zamri Zahari from the [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) was criticised for using the term ''"kafir harbi"'' (belligerent infidels) against the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) in his speech. Muslim DAP leaders demanded an apology for the remarks and made a police report regarding the incident.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kempen PN di Nenggiri gelar DAP ‘kafir harbi’|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/714968|date=9 August 2024|access-date=31 August 2024|website=[[Malaysiakini]]|language=ms}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=DAP demands apology for ‘kafir harbi’ remark at Nenggiri ceramah|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/08/09/dap-demands-apology-for-kafir-harbi-remark-at-nenggiri-ceramah/|date=9 August 2024|access-date=31 August 2024|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|language=en}}</ref> The office of the Mufti of the Federal Territories described the usage of the term as "extreme" against non-Muslims and even many moderate Muslims in the country.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kafir harbi term extreme for non-muslims in Malaysia: FT Mufti office|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2024/08/1089464/kafir-harbi-term-extreme-non-muslims-malaysia-ft-mufti-office|date=10 August 2024|access-date=31 August 2024|website=[[New Straits Times]]|language=en}}</ref> In response to the incident, [[Annuar Musa]] announced that Zamri would be placed on break for three days but refused to apologise on behalf of PN as he claimed it was Zamri's personal remarks.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Perikatan says PAS man who called DAP ‘kafir harbi’ in Nenggiri campaign ‘misspoke’, won’t apologise since not its official stance|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/08/10/perikatan-says-pas-man-who-called-dap-kafir-harbi-in-nenggiri-campaign-misspoke-wont-apologise-since-not-its-official-stance/146553|date=10 August 2024|access-date=31 August 2024|website=[[Malay Mail]]|language=en}}</ref>

In another PN campaign event in Felda Perasu, former Prime Minister [[Muhyiddin Yassin]] claimed in a speech that he was not invited by the then 16th [[Yang di-Pertuan Agong]], [[Abdullah of Pahang|Al-Sultan Abdullah]], to form a government following the 2022 general election despite having the alleged support of 115 MPs. This led the Crown Prince of Pahang, [[Tengku Hassanal Ibrahim Alam Shah]], to call for police action against Muhyiddin for what he described as "deliberately disparaging" his father.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Muhyiddin defends ‘factual’ Nenggiri speech|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/08/19/muhyiddin-defends-factual-nenggiri-speech/|date=19 August 2024|access-date=31 August 2024|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|language=en}}</ref> On 27 August 2024, Muhyiddin plead not guilty on a charge of insulting the monarchy under the [[Sedition Act 1948]] at the Gua Musang Sessions Court.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Former Malaysian PM Muhyiddin charged with sedition over monarchy comments|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/8/27/former-malaysian-pm-muhyiddin-charged-with-sedition-over-monarchy-comments|date=27 August 2024|access-date=31 August 2024|website=[[Al Jazeera]]|language=en}}</ref>
