A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly|Selangor State Assembly]] seat of '''[[Kajang]]''' on 23 March 2014 following the nomination day on 11 March 2014.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|title=Kajang polls fixed for March 23|date=5 February 2014|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/02/05/kajang-polls-fixed-for-march-23/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171026163144/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/02/05/kajang-polls-fixed-for-march-23/|archive-date=26 October 2017|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=Kajang by-election to be held on March 23, nominations on March 11, says polls body|date=5 February 2014|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/kajang-by-election-to-be-held-on-march-23-nominations-set-for-march-11-says|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006144706/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/kajang-by-election-to-be-held-on-march-23-nominations-set-for-march-11-says|archive-date=6 October 2014}}</ref> The seat was vacated after the incumbent assemblyman, [[Lee Chin Cheh]] resigned on 27 January 2014.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=Kajang rep quits for by-election, Anwar or Rafizi slated to stand|date=27 January 2014|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/kajang-rep-quits-for-by-election-anwar-slated-to-stand|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006081212/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/kajang-rep-quits-for-by-election-anwar-slated-to-stand|archive-date=6 October 2014}}</ref> Lee was an assemblyman from the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]], a component party of [[Pakatan Rakyat]]. He won by a majority of 6,824 votes against 5 other candidates in the [[2013 Malaysian general election|general elections in 2013]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Channel News Asia]]|title=Kajang by-election set for March 23|date=5 February 2014|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/kajang-by-election-set/981466.html|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140409025417/http://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asiapacific/kajang-by-election-set/981466.html|archive-date=9 April 2014}}</ref>

Federal parliamentary opposition leader [[Anwar Ibrahim]] had confirmed to stand as the Pakatan Rakyat candidate in the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=PKR confirms Anwar to stand in Kajang|date=28 January 2014|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/prk-confirms-anwar-as-candidate-for-kajang-state-assembly-seat|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006100221/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/prk-confirms-anwar-as-candidate-for-kajang-state-assembly-seat|archive-date=6 October 2014}}</ref> However, due to the unusually swift conviction by the [[Court of Appeal of Malaysia|Court of Appeal]] in his [[Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials|sodomy case]], his wife [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]] replaced him as the PR candidate.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|title=Wan Azizah for Kajang|date=9 March 2014|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/03/09/wan-azizah-for-kajang|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170703015822/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/03/09/wan-azizah-for-kajang/|archive-date=3 July 2017|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=Wan Azizah to contest Kajang seat, replaces Anwar|date=9 March 2014|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/wan-azizah-to-contest-kajang-seat-replaces-anwar|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006112718/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/wan-azizah-to-contest-kajang-seat-replaces-anwar|archive-date=6 October 2014}}</ref> [[Barisan Nasional]], which forms the opposition in the Selangor Assembly, named [[Malaysian Chinese Association|MCA]] vice president and former Petaling Jaya Utara MP [[Chew Mei Fun]] as their candidate.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=MCA’s Chew Mei Fun to contest Kajang by-election|date=21 February 2014|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/mcas-chew-mei-fun-to-contest-against-anwar-in-kajang-by-election|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006132622/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/mcas-chew-mei-fun-to-contest-against-anwar-in-kajang-by-election|archive-date=6 October 2014}}</ref>

The by-election was won by Dr Wan Azizah with a majority of 5,379 votes.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|title=PKR retains Kajang with reduced majority|date=23 March 2014|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/257966}}</ref> The turnout in this by-election decreased by 16% from the general election in 2013.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|title=PKR retains Kajang with lower majority|date=23 March 2014|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/03/23/unofficial-pkr-retains-kajang-with-lower-majority/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171026163447/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/03/23/unofficial-pkr-retains-kajang-with-lower-majority/|archive-date=26 October 2017|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=PKR retains Kajang in low-key by-election|date=23 March 2014|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-retains-kajang-in-low-key-by-election/|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141008045658/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-retains-kajang-in-low-key-by-election|archive-date=8 October 2014}}</ref>
