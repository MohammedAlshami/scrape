A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Terengganu State Legislative Assembly|Terengganu State Assembly]] seat of '''[[Kuala Besut]]''' on 24 July 2013 with nomination day on 12 July 2013. The seat of [[Kuala Besut]] fell vacant after the death of incumbent assemblyman, A. Rahman Mokhtar on 26 June 2013 from lung cancer.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|title=Umno's Kuala Besut state rep dies|date=26 June 2013|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/06/26/umnos-kuala-besut-state-rep-dies/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130629212953/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/06/26/umnos-kuala-besut-state-rep-dies/|archive-date=29 June 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Dr. Mokhtar was an assemblyman from the [[United Malays National Organisation]], a component party of the ruling [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition, which holds a 1-seat majority into the by-election. The [[by-election]] was contested by Tengku Zaihan Che Ku Abdul Rahman from [[UMNO]] and Azlan Yusof, who hails from the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] component [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|accessdate=10 July 2013|title=In Kuala Besut, PAS, Umno settle on new faces|date=10 July 2013|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/in-kuala-besut-pas-umno-settle-on-new-faces/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130712002145/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/in-kuala-besut-pas-umno-settle-on-new-faces/|archive-date=12 July 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

This by-election is viewed as critical by both the government and opposition. A win by [[Pakatan Rakyat]] would have resulted in a [[hung parliament]] with both coalition holding 16 seats, and potentially a fresh state election.<ref>{{cite news|work=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun]]|title=Odd numbers and hung assemblies|date=1 July 2013|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/757900|access-date=12 July 2013|archive-date=15 September 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130915143805/http://www.thesundaily.my/news/757900|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|title=Impact of Kuala Besut by-election|date=11 July 2013|url=http://www.nst.com.my/latest/impact-of-kuala-besut-by-election-1.317423}}</ref>

The by-election was won by BN's Tengku Zaihan with a slightly increased majority, thus keeping the government's wafer-thin 2-seat majority.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|accessdate=26 July 2013|title=Kuala Besut defeat an opportunity for PAS to take stock|date=25 July 2013|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/kuala-besut-defeat-an-opportunity-for-pas-to-take-stock|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130727124909/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/kuala-besut-defeat-an-opportunity-for-pas-to-take-stock|archive-date=27 July 2013|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Turnout in this by-election dropped by 7% compared to the turnout during the [[2013 Malaysian general election|general election two months before]].
