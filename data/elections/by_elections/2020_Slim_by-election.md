A [[by-election]] was held on 29 August 2020 for the seat of [[Slim (state constituency)|Slim]] in the [[Perak State Legislative Assembly]].<ref name="announcement">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/07/23/ec-sets-slim-by-election-on-aug-29/1887284|title=EC sets Slim by-election on Aug 29|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=23 July 2020|accessdate=23 July 2020}}</ref> The seat fell vacant following the sudden death of the incumbent [[Member of the Legislative Assembly]] (MLA) from [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) of the [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition, [[Mohd. Khusairi Abdul Talib]], 59, due to a heart attack on 15 July 2020. He had held the seat for four terms since [[2004 Malaysian general election|2004]].<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/07/15/slim-assemblyman-mohd-khusairi-abdul-talib-dies-at-59/1884791 |title=Slim assemblyman Mohd Khusairi Abdul Talib dies at 59|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=15 July 2020|access-date=23 July 2020}}</ref>

The [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) had set the nomination day on 15 August 2020, early voting on 25 August and polling day for 29 August with a 14-day campaign period.<ref name="announcement"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2020/07/610929/ec-slim-election-aug-29|title=EC: Slim by-election on Aug 29 |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|work=[[Bernama]]|date=23 July 2020|access-date=23 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://english.astroawani.com/malaysia-news/ec-slim-election-aug-29-252466|title=EC: Slim by-election on Aug 29 |publisher=[[Astro Awani]]|work=[[Bernama]]|date=23 July 2020|access-date=23 July 2020}}</ref>

==Background==
{{Pie chart|thumb=left|caption=Ethnic breakdown of Slim's electorate as of [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018]]<br /><small>''Source: [[Malaysiakini]]<ref name="undi.info">{{cite web|title=undi.info-SLIM (P77-N58)|date=2020|publisher=MalaysiaKini|accessdate=7 May 2020|url=https://undi.info/perak/r18/N58|archive-date=19 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200719035705/https://undi.info/perak/r18/N58|url-status=dead}}</ref>''</small>|label1=[[Malaysian Malay|Malay]]|value1=75|color1=green|label2=[[Malaysian Indian|Indian]]|value2=13|color2=orange |label3=[[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]]|value3=10|color3=red|label4=Other|value4=2|color4=grey}}
The small town of [[Slim River]] in Perak has a population of 27,139 residents comprising [[Malaysian Malay|Malay]]s (74 per cent), [[Malaysian Indian|Indian]]s (12 per cent), [[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]] (10 per cent), [[Orang Asli]] (1.4 per cent) and others.<ref>{{Cite news |url= https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/08/16/in-slim-by-election-voters-want-a-rep-who-can-help-small-businesses-grow-br/1894345 |title=In Slim by-election, voters want a rep who can help small businesses grow, bring in new jobs |date=16 August 2020|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=16 August 2020}}</ref> The corresponding Slim state constituency has 23,094 registered voters comprising 22,815 normal voters, 277 early voters as well as 2 absentee voters overseas.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bernama.com/en/general/news.php?id=1869162 |title=Slim By-election: EC sends cards to 23,094 registered voters|publisher=[[Bernama]]|date=11 August 2020|access-date=11 August 2020}}</ref> The by-election to be conducted under the special guidelines and [[standard operating procedure]]s (SOPs) imposed by EC due to the ''[[New Normal (business)#Usage related to COVID-19|New Normal]]'' as the country is still observing the recovery phase of [[2020 Malaysia movement control order#Recovery Movement Control Order (RMCO)|Movement Control Order]] (MCO) for [[COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bernama.com/en/news.php?id=1867742 |title=COVID-19: Improved Chini by-election SOP to be used in Slim - Ismail Sabri|publisher=[[Bernama]] |date=7 July 2020|access-date=7 July 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2020/08/616536/ec-issues-slim-election-guidelines |title=EC issues Slim by-election guidelines|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=August 14, 2020|access-date=August 14, 2020}}</ref>

The by-election is unlikely to affect the Perak state political scene, as the results of by-election would has no bearing on the position of the state government amidst the looming talks of snap elections following the [[2020 Malaysian political crisis|2020 political crisis]]. There are 59 state seats in Perak in which 32 formed the [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) coalition comprising 25 BN (UMNO), 4 [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party|BERSATU]] and 3 [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]]; compare to 24 seats holds by [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition comprising 16 [[Democratic Action Party (Malaysia)|DAP]], 5 [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|AMANAH]], 3 [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]]; meanwhile  2 [[Independent politician|Independent]] and 1 [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia|GERAKAN]]; as just upon the seat vacancy.<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://english.astroawani.com/politics-news/slim-election-no-clash-between-pn-parties-perak-mb-252655|title=Slim by-election: No clash between PN parties - Perak MB|publisher=[[Astro Awani]]|work=[[Bernama]] |date=24 July 2020 |access-date=24 July 2020}}</ref> One of the independent assemblyman, [[Hasnul Zulkarnain Abdul Munaim]], 2 weeks later joined PN's component BERSATU on 29 July 2020.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.msn.com/en-my/news/national/ex-amanah-rep-in-perak-joins-ppbm/ar-BB17iVLi?li=BBr8Hnu |title=Ex-Amanah rep in Perak joins PPBM|date=29 July 2020 |work=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|publisher= [[MSN]] |accessdate=29 July 2020}}</ref>

==Nominations==
Perak Menteri Besar and BERSATU chief, [[Ahmad Faizal Azumu]] has said that Perikatan Nasional (PN) will field a candidate from UMNO to defend the Slim state seat in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/07/612380/perikatan-field-umno-candidate-slim-election|title=Perikatan to field Umno candidate in Slim by-election |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|work=[[Bernama]]|date=28 July 2020|access-date=28 July 2020}}</ref> Barisan Nasional (BN) secretary-general [[Annuar Musa]] said UMNO will pick the candidate for the Slim by-election, in which BN with [[Muafakat Nasional]] (MN)'s UMNO-PAS and BERSATU together will set up a joined committee named Muafakat Nasional 'Plus'.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/07/613031/umno-decide-slim-election-candidate-sunday|title=Umno to decide Slim by-election candidate on Sunday|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|work=[[Bernama]]|date=28 July 2020|access-date=28 July 2020}}</ref> BN has named acting Tanjung Malim UMNO chief [[Mohd Zaidi Aziz]], 43, as its candidate for the Slim state by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://english.astroawani.com/politics-news/bn-named-mohd-zaidi-aziz-candidate-slim-election-254971 |title=BN named Mohd Zaidi Aziz as candidate for Slim by-election |publisher=[[Astro Awani]]|work=[[Bernama]]|date=12 August 2020|access-date=12 August 2020}}</ref>

[[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) secretariat secretary [[Saifuddin Nasution Ismail]] had confirmed that PH will not be contesting the by-election for the Slim state seat.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/536903|title=Slim polls - Harapan not contesting, makes way for Dr M's faction |author=B Nantha Kumar |publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=30 July 2020|access-date=30 July 2020}}</ref> PH is to make way to former BERSATU chairman Dr [[Mahathir Mohamad]]’s  faction to contest instead.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.themalaysianinsight.com/s/263615|title=Dr Mahathir's faction to contest Slim seat, says source |author=Chan Kok Leong |publisher=The Malaysian Insight|date=29 July 2020|access-date=29 July 2020}}</ref> Ahead of the by-election, Dr Mahathir has announces the formation by the initially dubbed "BERSATU Blackout" faction's new party, [[Party of Homeland's Fighters|Parti Pejuang Tanah Air]] (Pejuang) or the Fighters of the Nation Party.<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/08/12/ahead-of-slim-poll-dr-mahathir-announces-new-partys-name-parti-pejuang-tana/1893410 |title=Ahead of Slim poll, Dr Mahathir announces new party's name: Parti Pejuang Tanah Air |publisher=[[Malay Mail]] |date=12 August 2020|access-date=12 August 2020}}</ref> The yet-to-be registered Pejuang party has also names [[Syariah]] lawyer, Amir Khusyairi Mohamad Tanusi, 38, as their candidate with PH endorsement for the Slim poll, who will be contesting as an [[Independent (politician)|Independent]].<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/08/12/lawyer-amir-khusyairi-gets-dr-mahathirs-endorsement-as-slim-independent-can/1893407 |title=Dr Mahathir's new party Pejuang names lawyer Amir Khusyairi as candidate for Slim by-election |publisher=[[Malay Mail]] |date=12 August 2020|access-date=12 August 2020}}</ref>

On nomination day, BN's Mohd Zaidi and Pejuang's Amir Khusyairi (tree symbol) along with another independent, former teacher Santharasekaran [[Malaysian names#Indian names|a/l]] Subramaniam (book symbol), 44, filed their nomination papers to set for a three-cornered fight in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/threecornered-fight-slim-byelection|title=Three-cornered fight in Slim by-election|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Markets]]|date=August 15, 2020|access-date=August 15, 2020}}{{Dead link|date=January 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2020/08/616726/three-cornered-fight-slim|title=Three-cornered fight in Slim |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=August 15, 2020|access-date=August 15, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/08/15/nominations-for-slim-by-election-open/1894105|title=Three-cornered fight in Slim by-election|date=15 August 2020|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=15 August 2020}}</ref> There were also two other individuals who turned up that morning but didn't submit their nomination papers as independent candidates due to personal technical reasons.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/538719|title=Pertembungan tiga penjuru di PRK Slim|last1=Kumar|first1=B Nantha|last2=Faisal Asyraf|date=15 August 2020|language=ms|website=[[Malaysiakini.com]] |others=Photo by Mukhriz Hazim|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200815072629/https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/538719|archive-date=15 August 2020 |access-date=15 August 2020}}</ref>

==Controversies and issues==
Members of the BERSATU Youth (ARMADA) were "booed" during the launching ceremony of the BN Youth Campaign Machinery. Supporters of BN started "booing" the ARMADA members after BN Chairman, [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] announced that BERSATU will join the [[Muafakat Nasional]] pact.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/538764|title=Bersatu Youth booed at BN event, told to prove their worth to Umno's Slim candidate|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Faisal Asyraf|date=15 August 2020 |access-date=15 August 2020}}</ref>

On the morning of poling day, several voters were prevented from entering a polling centre merely for wearing shorts which led to a protest when the affected voters unable to cast their ballots. EC chairman Abdul Ghani Salleh had later explained it was a miscommunication as there is no Act that forbids a voter donning shorts from entering a polling centre.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2020/08/620442/slim-election-brouhaha-over-shorts-misunderstanding |title=Slim by-election: Brouhaha over shorts a 'misunderstanding' |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=August 29, 2020|access-date=August 29, 2020}}</ref>
