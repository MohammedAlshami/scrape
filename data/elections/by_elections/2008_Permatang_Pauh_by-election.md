== Background of the by-election ==
On 27 July 2008, Anwar said that he aimed to return to parliament for the first time in a decade later that year if a court ordered a by-election near his home town.  The seat mentioned would probably be Kulim-Bandar Baharu constituency next to his hometown of [[Permatang Pauh]] in Penang.<ref>[https://news.yahoo.com/s/afp/20080727/wl_asia_afp/malaysiapoliticsoppositionanwar_080727050217 Malaysia's Anwar eyes return to parliament in by-election] 27 July 2008, Associated Press</ref> Anwar Ibrahim on 31 July 2008 said he would contest a by-election for the parliamentary seat of [[Permatang Pauh]] vacated by his wife to expedite his return to political office.  His wife Wan Azizah said she handed her letter of resignation to the parliament speaker on Thursday 31 July. Party officials said the by-election must be held within 60 days.<ref>[https://news.yahoo.com/s/afp/20080731/wl_afp/malaysiapoliticsanwar_080731101401 Malaysia's Anwar to contest election for wife's seat] 31 July 2008, Associated Press</ref> The Election Commission (EC) had fixed the nomination day for the Permatang Pauh by-election on 16 August, with polling to be held ten days later on 26 August.  Some 58,459 voters in the Permatang Pauh constituency would be eligible to vote on 26 August, a working Tuesday, adding that the figure also include 490 postal voters.<ref name="TheEdgeDaily"/>

Anwar Ibrahim was charged on 7 August 2008 for [[sodomy]] under Section 377B of the Penal Code, fueling speculations over a possible [[Conspiracy (crime)|conspiracy]] on the part of the Malaysian government to derail his election campaign.<ref>{{cite news|agency=Agence France-Presse|url=http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5iFah-DQkKWexR0zrfHk-ult8Rw6g|title=Malaysian opposition leader Anwar charged with sodomy|date=7 August 2008|access-date=8 August 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110520154142/http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5iFah-DQkKWexR0zrfHk-ult8Rw6g|archive-date=20 May 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Background of Permatang Pauh==
Parliamentary seat '''P.44 [[Permatang Pauh]]''' lies in the federal state of '''[[Penang]]'''. In the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 election]], [[Malay titles|Datuk Seri]] Wan Azizah Wan Ismail of the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (a component party of the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] coalition), garnered 30,338 votes, defeating [[Firdaus Ismail]] of the [[United Malay National Organization]] (UMNO) (a component party of the [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition) (16,950 votes) .<ref name="GE2008">{{cite web| title = MYPolitician| publisher = mypolitician.com.my| url = http://mypolitician.com.my/indexpru.php?page=map2/pulaupinang#P44b| access-date = 6 August 2008| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20090919113759/http://mypolitician.com.my/indexpru.php?page=map2%2Fpulaupinang#P44b| archive-date = 19 September 2009}}</ref>

== By-election results ==
Early results indicated a landslide victory for Anwar. Reuters reported "Anwar Ibrahim has won with a majority of 16,210 votes.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20080828193153/http://uk.reuters.com/article/worldNews/idUKKLR31057520080826 uk.reuters.com, Website says Anwar wins Malaysia vote with big majority]. Uk.reuters.com (26 August 2008).</ref> According to news website [https://www.malaysiakini.com Malaysiakini]: Anwar won 26,646 votes, while the government's Arif Omar won 10,436 votes. Anwar's [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] spokeswoman Ginie Lim told [[BBC]]: "We won already. We are far ahead."<ref>[http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/7581446.stm news.bbc.co.uk, Malaysia's Anwar claims vote win]. BBC News (26 August 2008).</ref>

Malaysia's government and ruling coalition declared defeat in a landslide victory in by-election by Anwar Ibrahim. [[Muhammad Muhammad Taib]], information chief of the [[United Malays National Organisation]] which leads the [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition stated: "Yes of course we have lost . . . we were the underdogs going into this race."<ref>[http://www.thestandard.com.hk/breaking_news_detail.asp?id=4470&icid=4&d_str=20080826 thestandard.com.hk, Malaysian government declares by-election defeat to Anwar] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111220200743/http://www.thestandard.com.hk/breaking_news_detail.asp?id=4470&icid=4&d_str=20080826 |date=20 December 2011 }}. ''The Standard'' (Hong Kong).</ref> Malaysia's Election Commission officials announced Anwar won by an astounding majority against [[Arif Shah Omar Shah]] of [[United Malays National Organisation]].<ref>[https://www.bangkokpost.com/breaking_news/breakingnews.php?id=129976 bangkokpost.com, Malaysia's Anwar wins by landslide in election]. ''Bangkok Post''.</ref><ref>{{cite news| title = Malaysia's Anwar heads for parliament after election victory| agency = Agence France-Presse| date = 26 August 2008| url = http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5jacv7L2zK6_pmxcWx_USPCrqXDAA| access-date = 26 August 2008| url-status = dead| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20080828213445/http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5jacv7L2zK6_pmxcWx_USPCrqXDAA| archive-date = 28 August 2008}}</ref>

Final results announced by the Election Commission reveals [[Anwar Ibrahim]] won 31,195 of the estimated 47,000 votes cast in the district in the northern state of [[Penang]], while rival Arif Shah Omar Shah got only 15,524 votes and a third candidate had 92 votes.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.pr-inside.com/malaysia-s-anwar-wins-parliament-seat-r773685.htm |title=pr-inside.com, Malaysia's Anwar wins Parliament seat |access-date=26 August 2008 |archive-date=17 February 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120217035221/http://www.pr-inside.com/malaysia-s-anwar-wins-parliament-seat-r773685.htm |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5gvk18e1IzUJ9V-VNzo1_wg-4mkWgD92Q0O300 ap.google.com, Malaysia's Anwar wins seat in parliament] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080829072524/http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5gvk18e1IzUJ9V-VNzo1_wg-4mkWgD92Q0O300 |date=29 August 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Anwar wins seat; calls it 1st step to toppling Malaysian government |agency=The Canadian Press |date=26 August 2008 |url=http://canadianpress.google.com/article/ALeqM5hiapfbaXE6_paRxiWNFkA6yDJ2cQ |access-date=26 August 2008 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080828220243/http://canadianpress.google.com/article/ALeqM5hiapfbaXE6_paRxiWNFkA6yDJ2cQ |archive-date=28 August 2008 }}</ref>
