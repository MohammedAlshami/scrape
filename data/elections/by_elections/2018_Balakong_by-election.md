A '''by-election''' was held on 8 September 2018 for the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Balakong (state constituency)|Balakong]].'''<ref name="electionday">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/436512|title=21-day campaign period for Sept 8 Balakong by-election|first=Alyaa|last=Alhadjri|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=30 July 2018|access-date=30 July 2018}}</ref> The seat became vacant after the death of the incumbent assemblyman, Eddie Ng Tien Chee on 20 July 2018 in a traffic accident on the [[Cheras–Kajang Expressway]].<ref name="accident">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/435050|title=DAP Balakong rep dies in accident, second by-election for S'gor|author=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=20 July 2018|access-date=20 July 2018}}</ref> Eddie Ng was a member of the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP), a component party of the state ruling [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition. This was the second [[casual vacancy]] in the Assembly since the [[2018 Malaysian general election|May 2018 general election]] after [[Sungai Kandis (state constituency)|Sungai Kandis]], which held a [[2018 Sungai Kandis by-election|by-election]] in August 2018. The by-election will be held on the same day as the [[2018 Seri Setia by-election|Seri Setia by-election]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/437959|title=Seri Setia and Balakong by-elections to run concurrently|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=8 August 2018|access-date=8 August 2018}}</ref> In the last [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]], Ng defeated [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN)'s [[Malaysian Chinese Association]] (MCA) candidate, Lim Chin Wah and Mohamad Ibrahim Ghazali of [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS), with a vote majority of 35,538.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/2018/08/26/balakong-election-candidate-shares-secret-maintaining-good-stamina-while-campaigning|title=Balakong by-election candidate shares secret to maintaining good stamina while campaigning|publisher=The Sun Daily|date=26 August 2018|access-date=8 September 2018}}</ref>

The nomination day was on 18 August 2018.<ref name="electionday"/> The by-election set for a straight fight between PH's DAP and BN's MCA. While PAS had decided not to contest to make way for the BN candidate, just liked in the case of the Sungai Kandis by-election a month earlier.<ref name="nomday">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/439416|title=Balakong straight fight: DAP adopts Harapan logo, MCA drops BN's for first time|first=Kai Hui|last=Wong|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=18 August 2018|access-date=18 August 2018}}</ref>

Pakatan Harapan retained the seat with a larger share of vote.<ref name="results">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442349|title=Official: Harapan retains Balakong with increased vote share|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=8 September 2018|access-date=9 September 2018}}</ref>

== Candidates ==
'''[[Pakatan Harapan]] ([[Democratic Action Party]])'''
Wong Siew Ki was serving as the Seri Kembangan assemblyman and Selangor state executive councillor [[Ean Yong Hian Wah]]’s political secretary and she was also Subang Jaya municipal councillor since 2016 then.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/08/17/sgor-mb-grassroots-leader-wong-siew-ki-a-good-choice-for-balakong/|title=S'gor MB: 'Grassroots leader' Wong Siew Ki a good choice for Balakong|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=17 August 2018|access-date=18 August 2018}}</ref>
She graduated in 2009 from the [[Universiti Putra Malaysia]] (UPM).

'''[[Barisan Nasional]] ([[Malaysian Chinese Association]])''' 
Tan Chee Teong is a committee member in the Serdang [[Malaysian Chinese Association]] (MCA). He is a local of Balakong.

== Electoral logo quirk ==
This by-election marks the first time Pakatan Harapan had used their just approved common logo in an election. The then-federal opposition coalition was unable to contest the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] with their logo due to the Registrar of Societies not approving their registration in time for the election.<ref name="nomday"/> The registration was finally approved only after the election, which delivered the coalition to power.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/s/1660855/ec-finally-approves-pakatan-harapans-logo|title=EC finally approves Pakatan Harapan's logo|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=9 August 2018|access-date=18 August 2018}}</ref>

Conversely, the MCA will be using their own party logo for the first time since they joined Barisan Nasional in 1973.<ref name="nomday"/> MCA candidates have normally used the BN common logo in elections up to the 2018 general election.

== Result ==
DAP and Pakatan Harapan's Wong Siew Ki polled 22,058 votes to MCA and Barisan Nasional's Tan Chee Teong's 3,975 votes retaining the seat for DAP and Pakatan Harapan.<ref name="results"/>
