The '''2019 Sandakan by-election''' was a by-election held on 11 May 2019 for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of '''[[Sandakan (federal constituency)|Sandakan]]'''.<ref name="announcement">{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/04/05/sandakan-by-election-falls-on-may-11/|title=Sandakan by-election falls on May 11|author= Mazwin Nik Anis|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star Online]]|date=5 April 2019|access-date=5 April 2019}}</ref> It was called following the death of incumbent, [[Wong Tien Fatt]] on 28 March 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/469893|title=Sandakan MP Stephen Wong dies of heart attack|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=28 March 2019|access-date=28 March 2019}}</ref> Wong was a 2-term Member of Parliament for Sandakan since 2013. He was a member and Sabah state party chairman (2015-2019) of the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP), a component party of the [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2017/04/28/jimmy-claims-dap-sabah-chief-broke-shanghai-promise/|title=Jimmy claims DAP Sabah chief broke 'Shanghai' promise|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=28 April 2017|access-date=25 May 2018}}</ref>

[[Vivian Wong Shir Yee]] of DAP won the by-election by 16,012 votes out of the 21,595 votes cast with a bigger majority of 11,521 votes despite at a lower turnout rate at 54.44%.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/05/11/pakatan-retains-sandakan-by-a-landslide-snaps-run-of-three-by-election-losses/|title=Pakatan retains Sandakan by a landslide, snaps run of three by-election losses|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=11 May 2019|access-date=11 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/05/11/early-results-show-dap-heading-for-big-majority/|title=DAP wins bigger majority in Sandakan|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|author=Tracy Patrick|date=11 May 2019|access-date=11 May 2019|archive-date=11 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190511142731/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/05/11/early-results-show-dap-heading-for-big-majority/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/475772|title=OFFICIAL: DAP retains Sandakan, Harapan breaks losing streak|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=11 May 2019|access-date=11 May 2019}}</ref>

== Background ==
Wong Tien Fatt had died of heart attack on 28 March 2019.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/03/28/sandakan-mp-stephen-yong-dies-of-heart-attack/|title=Sandakan MP Stephen Wong dies of heart attack|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=28 March 2019|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref> Causing the Sandakan seat vacant and a by-election was called. The [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) had set the polling day for 11 May 2019. Early voting was held on 7 May 2019 and Nomination Day on 27 April 2019.

The electoral roll last updated on March 28 with a total of 40,131 voters was used. Of that figure, 39,856 are regular voters, 270 qualify as early voters and five voters who live abroad and qualify as absentee voters.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/04/05/ec-sandakan-by-election-on-may-11-nomination-on-april-27/1740118|title=EC: Sandakan by-election on May 11, nomination on April 27|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|author=Lazareen Thaveethu Moses|date=5 April 2019|access-date=5 April 2019}}</ref>

Sandakan's registered voters are 49% Chinese, 44% Muslim-Bumiputra, 6% non-Muslim Bumiputra, and 1% Indian and others.<ref name="sandakan">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/04/18/sabah-opposition-still-torn-over-unified-front-for-sandakan-race/1744424|title=Sabah Opposition still torn over unified front for Sandakan race|author=Julia Chan|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=18 April 2019 |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref>

==Nominations==
On 25 April 2019, Sabah Chief Minister [[Shafie Apdal]] announced that Sabah DAP's Vivian Wong Shir Yee, the daughter of the late incumbent, as the candidate for the alliance of [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH), [[Sabah Heritage Party]] (WARISAN) and [[United Pasokmomogun Kadazandusun Murut Organisation]] (UPKO; later renamed United Progressive Kinabalu Organisation and now component party of PH) for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/04/04/sabah-dap-pushes-for-stephen-wongs-daughter-as-sandakan-candidate/|title=Sabah DAP pushes for Stephen Wong's daughter as Sandakan candidate|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=4 April 2019|access-date=5 April 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/473783|title=Harapan to field ex-MP's daughter in Sandakan|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=25 April 2019|access-date=25 April 2019}}</ref> On 26 April, Shafie Apdal said that the alliance would use DAP's logo for the by-election as DAP won by a vast majority using their logo in the [[2018 Malaysian general election|14th General Elections]] (GE14).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/473783|title=DAP party logo to be used in Sandakan by-election, says Shafie|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=26 April 2019|access-date=26 April 2019}}</ref>

[[United Sabah Party]] or ''Parti Bersatu Sabah'' (PBS); of the [[United Alliance (Sabah)|United Alliance]] or ''Gabungan Bersatu Sabah'' (GBS; later rebranded as [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] or Sabah People's Coalition; GRS) had earlier mulls over their decision fielding a candidate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2019/04/477400/pbs-mulls-fielding-candidate-sandakan-election|title=PBS mulls fielding candidate for Sandakan by-election|author= Mohd Izham Unnip Abdullah |date=8 April 2019 |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|access-date=9 April 2019}}</ref> On 22 April 2019, the PBS finally announced their decision to contest the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/04/22/pbs-to-contest-sandakan-by-election/1745638|title=PBS to contest Sandakan by-election|author=Julia Chan|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=22 April 2019 |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref> On 26 April, former [[Batu Sapi (federal constituency)|Batu Sapi]] MP, [[Linda Tsen Thau Lin]] was announced as the PBS candidate.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/473825|title=PBS names Linda Tsen for Sandakan, banks on ex-MP's popularity for upset|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Lu Wei Hoong|date=26 April 2019|access-date=26 April 2019}}</ref> PBS would also use its own logo as GBS coalition is not registered while it had quit [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) after GE14.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/04/23/pbs-to-contest-sandakan-byelection/|title=PBS to contest Sandakan by-election|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=23 April 2019|access-date=25 April 2019}}</ref>

[[Homeland Solidarity Party]] or ''Parti Solidariti Tanah Airku'' (STAR); one of PBS's two allies beside [[United Sabah People's Party]] (PBRS) within GBS alliance had earlier announced its intention to field economist Jenny Liew Wean Chee, 50, who is its Tanjung Papat division chief to contest the Sandakan by-election.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news/133452/star-announces-intention-to-contest-sandakan-by-poll/|title=Star announces intention to contest Sandakan by-poll |author=Leonard Alaza|publisher=[[Daily Express (Malaysia)|Daily Express]]|date=April 6, 2019|access-date=April 6, 2019}}</ref> Somehow STAR had later retracted earlier decision to contest in the by-election in order to support their GBS counterpart and ally, PBS instead.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/04/22/star-bows-out-of-sandakan-by-election-pledges-support-for-pbs-candidate/1745817|title=STAR bows out of Sandakan by-election, pledges support for PBS candidate|author=Julia Chan|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=22 April 2019 |access-date=22 April 2019}}</ref>

[[Sabah Progressive Party]] (SAPP) president [[Yong Teck Lee]] on 25 April had conveyed their backing for the party's former foe, PBS to its president [[Maximus Ongkili]] in the by-election; for the interest of Sabah rights.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2019/04/483236/sandakan-election-sapp-declares-support-pbs|title=Sandakan by-election: SAPP declares support for PBS|author= Olivia Miwil|date=26 April 2019 |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|access-date=29 April 2019}}</ref>

Sabah [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) chief [[Bung Mokhtar Radin]] said Sabah BN or UMNO would not contest the by-election but would support a single candidate from the Opposition front.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/03/31/field-one-candidate-for-sandakan/|title=Field one candidate for Sandakan|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=31 March 2019|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref> There was speculation that [[Malaysian Chinese Association]] (MCA) might contest the by-election and Batu Sapi MCA Division Chief, Chew Kok Wah was touted as the candidate.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2019/04/477704/sandakan-election-gaining-interest|title=Sandakan by-election gaining interest|author=Poliana Ronnie Sidom|date=9 April 2019 |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|access-date=9 April 2019}}</ref> On 21 April 2019, BN announced their intention to contest the by-election and candidate would likely come from the MCA or a direct member.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/473089|title=Amid palm oil price slump, BN mulls contesting in Sandakan|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Lu Wei Hoong|date=21 April 2019|access-date=21 April 2019}}</ref> On 22 April 2019, BN acting chairman, [[Mohamad Hasan (politician)|Mohamad Hasan]] made the final decision for BN to not contest the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/473313|title=BN will not contest Sandakan by-election - Mat Hasan|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=22 April 2019|access-date=23 April 2019}}</ref>

[[Sabah Native Co-operation Party]] (Anak Negeri) intended to contest in the by-election since BN opted out of it and wished BN would support them instead.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.msn.com/en-my/news/national/were-in-if-youre-out-anak-negeri-tells-bn-on-sandakan-by-election/ar-BBVXe9a|title=We're in if you're out, Anak Negeri tells BN on Sandakan by-election|date=15 April 2019|work=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|publisher=[[MSN]]|access-date=24 April 2019}}</ref> However the party did not contest.

[[Liberal Democratic Party (Malaysia)|Liberal Democratic Party]] (LDP), of which previous candidate Lim Ming Hoo contested the seat under BN ticket in the last [[2018 Malaysian general election|GE14]], announced that they would not contest this by-election.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/472016|title=Star mulls contesting Sandakan by-election|author=Lu Wei Hoong |publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=12 April 2019|access-date=12 April 2019}}</ref> They also had earlier denied they had met their previous BN coalition partner, UMNO, and declared they would not work with them in the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/highlight/2019/03/31/we-wont-work-with-umno-in-sandakan-polls-says-ldp/|title=We won't work with Umno in Sandakan polls, says LDP|author=Durie Rainer Fong|date=March 31, 2019|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|access-date=March 31, 2019}}</ref>

Another former BN component; [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia]] (Gerakan) cancelled their plan to contest by fielding the ex-Sabah deputy chief minister, Raymond Tan Shu Kiah, for the by-election after he resigned abruptly from the party.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/473500|title=Ex-DCM quits Gerakan after offered to contest Sandakan seat|date=24 April 2019|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|access-date=24 April 2019}}</ref> He joined WARISAN later.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/474791|title=Ex-Tanjung Papat assemblyperson Raymond Tan joins Warisan|date=3 May 2019|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|access-date=3 May 2019}}</ref>

The Sabah [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) announced on 2 April that they were ready to field a candidate in the by-election and Sabah PAS commissioner Mohd Aminuddin Aling said the decision would be confirmed in a meeting later on.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thesundaily.my/local/pas-ready-to-contest-in-sandakan-BI749517|title=PAS ready to contest in Sandakan|work=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun]]|date=2 April 2019|access-date=2 April 2019}}</ref> On 17 April 2019, PAS announced that they would not contest the seat and would support a candidate from the Opposition.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thesundaily.my/local/pas-to-skip-sandakan-by-election-support-opposition-candidate-BJ795407|title=PAS to skip Sandakan by-election, support opposition candidate|work=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun]]|date=17 April 2019|access-date=17 April 2019}}</ref>

[[United Sabah National Organisation (New)|United Sabah National Organisation]] (USNO) also declared they would not contest and would support the PBS candidate in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/politik/2019/04/556633/prk-sandakan-usno-sokong-calon-pbs|title=PRK Sandakan: USNO sokong calon PBS|date=24 April 2019|language=ms|publisher=[[Berita Harian]]|access-date=24 April 2019}}</ref>

Businessman and former oil palm company manager Chia Siew Yung, 46, announced he would be contesting in the by-election as an independent.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news/133738/ex-manager-contesting-sandakan-by-election-as-independent-/|title=Ex-manager contesting Sandakan by-election as independent |author=Mardinah Jikur|publisher=[[Daily Express (Malaysia)|Daily Express]]|date=12 April 2019 |access-date=13 April 2019}}</ref> Other than Chia, at least two other independents, ex-Sabah [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|National Trust Party]] (AMANAH) chairman / ex-Sabah PAS commissioner Hamzah Abdullah and youth activist Jufazli Shi Ahmad, also stated their wish to contest for the by-election.<ref name="sandakan"/> Sulaiman Abdul Samat, 36, the ex-Assistant Administrator of DAP Sandakan Parliament Service Centre, had also intended to join the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/politik/2019/04/554375/mahu-bertanding-kerana-rasa-bertanggungjawab|title=Mahu bertanding kerana rasa bertanggungjawab|author=Poliana Ronnie Sidom|date=18 April 2019|language=ms|publisher=[[Berita Harian]]|access-date=24 April 2019}}</ref>

On 27 April Nomination Day, Vivian Wong of DAP and Linda Tsen of PBS along with 3 independents, Chia Siew Yung (key), Hamzah Abdullah (tree) and Sulaiman Abdul Samat (chair) filed their nomination papers to set for a five-cornered fight in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/04/27/five-cornered-fight-in-sandakan-by-election/1747470|title=Five-cornered fight in Sandakan by-election|author=Raynore Mering|date=27 April 2019|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=27 April 2019}}</ref>

== Controversies and issues ==
Finance Minister, [[Lim Guan Eng]] announced a RM2.28 billion development project to be held in Sandakan. This was widely criticised by social media for launching a project when the by-election was near.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/473356|title=Ahead of by-election, gov't announces RM2.28b development projects in Sandakan|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=23 April 2019 |access-date=23 April 2019}}</ref>

DAP candidate Vivian Wong was criticised for cleaning a Muslim cemetery. Social media users cited that it was a political gimmick to gain Muslim Bumiputera votes.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/04/28/sandakan-polls-dap-criticised-for-cleaning-up-muslim-cemetery-on-campaign-trail/|title=Sandakan polls: DAP criticised for cleaning up Muslim cemetery on campaign trail|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=28 April 2019 |access-date=28 April 2019}}</ref>

PBS put up a controversial banner that claimed Finance Minister Lim Guan Eng had threatened Sandakan voters by saying he could not solve the issue of insufficient parking lots at the Sandakan Hospital in the event that DAP's candidate Vivian Wong lost in this by-election. DAP dismissed the claim and explained that Lim's remark was taken out of context. The banner was subsequently removed by the Election Commission.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/474562|title=EC tears down PBS banner critical of Guan Eng|author=Lu Wei Hoong|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=2 May 2019 |access-date=9 May 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/05/03/sandakan-cops-we-only-advised-ec-to-take-down-controversial-billboard/|title=Sandakan cops: We only advised EC to take down controversial billboard - Nation {{!}} The Star Online|last=Tan|first=Tarrence|website=www.thestar.com.my|access-date=2019-05-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/474562|title=EC tears down PBS banner critical of Guan Eng|website=Malaysiakini|date=2 May 2019 |language=en|access-date=2019-05-12}}</ref>
