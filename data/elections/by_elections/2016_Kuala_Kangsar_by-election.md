A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of P67 '''[[Kuala Kangsar]]''', a parliamentary seat located in the state of [[Perak]], [[Malaysia]] on 18 June 2016 following the nomination day on 5 June 2016.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Malaysiakini|accessdate=27 May 2016|title=Sungai Besar, K Kangsar by-elections on June 18|date=13 May 2016|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/341368}}</ref> The seat fell vacant after death of member of parliament [[Wan Mohammad Khair-il Anuar Wan Ahmad]], who died in a helicopter crash on May 5, 2016 while campaigning in the [[2016 Sarawak state election|Sarawak state election]].<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Malaysiakini|accessdate=27 May 2016|title=Sungai Besar, Kuala Kangsar set for by-elections|date=10 May 2016|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/340934}}</ref> In the [[2013 Malaysian general election|2013 general election]], Wan Mohammad Khair-il Anuar Wan Ahmad won the seat with a majority of 1,082 votes beating Khalil Idham Lim Abdullah of [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) and independent candidate Kamilia Ibrahim.<ref>{{cite news| publisher=Malaysiakini|accessdate=3 June 2016|title=Hisham: BN doesn't take Sungai Besar, Kuala Kangsar by-elections lightly|date=16 May 2016| url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/341727}}</ref> This by-election was held concurrently with the [[2016 Sungai Besar by-election|Sungai Besar by-election]] for the same reason.

The Barisan Nasional candidate for [[Kuala Kangsar (federal constituency)|Kuala Kangsar]] was [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) member [[Mastura Mohd Yazid]] who is the widow of the former MP Wan Mohammad Khair-il Anuar Wan Ahmad.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Malay Mail Online|accessdate=28 May 2016|title=BN says candidate for Kuala Kangsar has the right criteria|date=28 May 2016| url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/bn-says-candidate-for-kuala-kangsar-has-the-right-criteria}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/bn-eyes-sympathy-votes-for-widow|title=BN eyes sympathy votes for widow|website=The Straits Times|date=11 June 2016|access-date=23 June 2016}}</ref> The [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]] candidate chosen was Najihatussalehah Ahmad.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Malay Mail Online|accessdate=28 May 2016|title=PAS hopes for a straight fight with BN in Kuala Kangsar|date=28 May 2016| url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/pas-hopes-for-a-straight-fight-with-bn-in-kuala-kangsar}}</ref> The opposition coalition [[Pakatan Harapan]] chose [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|National Trust Party]] (AMANAH) vice chairman Ahmad Termizi Ramli as their candidate.<ref>{{cite news|work=The Star Online|accessdate=3 June 2016|title=Pakatan Harapan picks ex-scientist as candidate in Kuala Kangsar by-election|date=2 June 2016| url=http://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2016/06/02/amanah-picks-exnuclear-scientist-to-contest-in-kuala-kangsar-byelection/}}</ref> Izat Bukhary Ismail Bukhary joined the race as an Independent on the nomination day.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=Malaysiakini|accessdate=5 June 2016|title=Three-cornered fight in Sg Besar, four-way tussle in K Kangsar|date=5 June 2016| url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/344130}}</ref>

== Results ==
Mastura Mohd Yazid managed to retain the seat for Barisan Nasional with a majority of 6,969 votes beating 3 other candidates.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=The Straits Times|accessdate=18 June 2016|title=Malaysia's Sungai Besar, Kuala Kangsar by-elections: Barisan Nasional sweeps to victory|date=18 June 2016| url=http://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysias-sungai-besar-kuala-kangsar-by-elections-barisan-nasional-sweeps-to-victory}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2016/06/23/masturas-first-day-at-work-as-kuala-kangsar-mp|title=Mastura’s first day at work as Kuala Kangsar MP|website=The Star Online|date=23 June 2016|access-date=23 June 2016}}</ref>
