A [[by-election]] was held on 4 July 2020 for the seat of [[Chini (state constituency)|Chini]] in the [[Pahang State Legislative Assembly]].<ref name="announcement">{{cite news|url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/chini-byelection-july-4|title=Chini by-election on July 4|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Market]]|date=15 May 2020|access-date=15 May 2020}}{{Dead link|date=January 2022 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> The seat became vacant following the death of the incumbent [[Member of the Legislative Assembly]] (MLA) from [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) of [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition, Abu Bakar Harun on 7 May 2020 who had held the seat since [[2004 Malaysian general election|2004]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/82349/BERITA/Nasional/ADUN-Chini-meninggal-dunia |title=ADUN Chini meninggal dunia|publisher=[[Sinar Harian]]|date=15 May 2020|access-date=15 May 2020}}</ref> [[Mohd Sharim Md Zain]] from BN won the by-election with a 12,650 majority to retain the seat again.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.spr.gov.my/sites/default/files/Rasmi.PNG|title=Pilihan Raya Kecil N.23 Chini - Keputusan Penuh PRK|publisher=[[Election Commission of Malaysia|Suruhanjaya Pilihanraya Malaysia]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200706034030/https://www.spr.gov.my/sites/default/files/Rasmi.PNG|archive-date=2020-07-06}}</ref>

The [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) had set the nomination day on 20 June 2020, early voting on 30 June and polling day for 4 July with a 14-day campaign period.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/05/592799/ec-sets-july-4-chini-election|title=EC sets July 4 for Chini by-election|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=15 May 2020|access-date=15 May 2020}}</ref>

Chini, one of the four state constituencies within the [[Pekan (federal constituency)|Pekan]] federal constituency which is currently held by BN's adviser and former Prime Minister [[Najib Razak]], is a majority [[Malaysian Malay|Malay]] seat with 91% Malays. There are 20,990 registered voters for the Chini by-election comprising 20,972 ordinary voters and 18 early voters. The voters comprise 10,269 men and 10,721 women. This will be the first election in the country following the 2020 [[2020 Malaysian political crisis|political crisis]] and [[COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia|COVID-19 pandemic]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/05/592478/covid-19-ushers-new-normal-chini-election|title=Covid-19 ushers in the new normal for Chini by-election|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=15 May 2020|access-date=15 May 2020}}</ref> The by-election to be conducted under the special [[standard operating procedure]] (SOP) imposed by EC due to the ''[[New Normal (business)#Usage related to COVID-19|New Normal]]'' as the country is still observing the recovery phase of [[2020 Malaysia movement control order#Recovery Movement Control Order (RMCO)|Movement Control Order]] (MCO) for [[COVID-19]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thesundaily.my/local/special-sop-for-chini-by-election-a-benchmark-for-future-elections-ec-DJ2469731|title=Special SOP for Chini by-election a benchmark for future elections: EC|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun Daily]]|date=30 May 2020|access-date=30 May 2020}}</ref>

==Nominations==
Secretary-General of UMNO and BN, [[Annuar Musa]] confirmed that BN will place its candidate in the by-election<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2020/05/688655/bn-akan-letak-calon-di-prk-dun-chini|title=BN akan letak calon di PRK DUN Chini|publisher=[[Berita Harian]]|date=14 May 2020|access-date=15 May 2020}}</ref> and continue to work with [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) on the concept of [[Muafakat Nasional]] (MN) as in the previous by-elections.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/06/603065/chini-election-bn-candidate-hyped-challenge|title=Chini by-election: BN candidate hyped up for challenge|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|author= TN Alagesh|date=June 24, 2020|access-date=June 24, 2020}}</ref> UMNO also confirmed it will represent BN to defend the seat.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/umno-contest-chini-byelection-wan-rosdy|title=UMNO to contest Chini by-election - Wan Rosdy|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Market]]|date=14 May 2020|access-date=15 May 2020|archive-date=28 May 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200528013325/https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/umno-contest-chini-byelection-wan-rosdy|url-status=dead}}</ref> Najib Razak's eldest son, [[Nizar Najib|Mohd Nizar Najib]] who is the Pekan UMNO Youth chief has been coined as a potential party candidate of the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://mystar.newspaperdirect.com/epaper/viewer.aspx |title=Support for Najib's son to join Chini by-election|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=30 May 2020|access-date=1 June 2020}}</ref> On 18 June, Barisan Nasional decided to nominate [[Mohd Sharim Md Zain]], a [[Federal Land Development Authority|FELDA]] resident as its candidate for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/amp/news/politics/2020/06/601566/bn-rests-hope-felda-settler-retain-chini?|title=BN rests hope on Felda settler to retain Chini|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|author=Arfa Yunus|date=18 June 2020|access-date=18 June 2020}}</ref>

[[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) had also declared its full support to the  chosen BN candidate who will be representing the new [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) government.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/06/601282/chini-election-bersatu-will-support-bn-candidate|title=Chini by-election: Bersatu will support BN candidate|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=17 June 2020|access-date=18 June 2020}}</ref> Pekan BERSATU deputy chief, Tengku Zainul Hisham Tengku Hussin, however announced his candidacy for the by-election as an [[Independent politician|Independent]] candidate on 18 June.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/ampArticle/88403?|title=Timbalan Ketua Bersatu Bahagian Pekan umum bertanding Bebas|publisher=[[Sinar Harian]]|author=Nik Amirulmumin Nik Min|date=18 June 2020|access-date=18 June 2020}}</ref>

[[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) Secretary-general, [[Saifuddin Nasution Ismail]] announced that [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) will not contest the by-election. Saifuddin cited that the COVID-19 pandemic may affect the safety of voters, therefore abandoning the race.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/amp/news/malaysia/2020/06/06/pakatan-wont-field-candidate-in-chini-by-election-cites-covid-19-threat-as/1872953?|title=Pakatan won't field candidate in Chini by-election, cites Covid-19 threat as main reason|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|author=Emmanuel Santa Maria Chin|date=6 June 2020|access-date=6 June 2020}}</ref> Conceding the coalition's presidential council decision, PH might back independent candidate instead at the polls in order to fight against UMNO and BN.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/06/06/ph-might-back-independent-candidate-at-chini-polls-says-fuziah/|title=PH might back independent candidate at Chini polls, says Fuziah|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|author=Faiz Zainudin|date=6 June 2020|access-date=6 June 2020|archive-date=6 June 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200606132857/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/06/06/ph-might-back-independent-candidate-at-chini-polls-says-fuziah/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

On Nomination Day, the BN candidate Mohd Sharim, alongside two independents; later-got-sacked BERSATU's Tengku Zainul (house logo) and social activist Mohd Shukri Mohd Ramli (key logo), filed their nomination papers for a three-way race for the state assembly seat.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/06/20/bns-sharim-vs-2-independents-in-chini-by-election/|title=BN's Sharim vs 2 independents in Chini by-election|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=20 June 2020|access-date=20 June 2020|archive-date=8 July 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200708201557/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/06/20/bns-sharim-vs-2-independents-in-chini-by-election/|url-status=dead}}</ref>
