The '''2024 Kuala Kubu Baharu by-election''' was a by-election for the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Kuala Kubu Baharu (state constituency)|Kuala Kubu Baharu]]''' that was held on 11 May 2024. It was called following the death of incumbent, [[Lee Kee Hiong]] on 21 March 2024 of cancer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=ADUN Kuala Kubu Bharu, Lee Kee Hiong meninggal dunia|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/amp/berita/nasional/2024/03/1226204/adun-kuala-kubu-bharu-lee-kee-hiong-meninggal-dunia|date=21 March 2024|access-date=21 March 2024|website=[[Berita Harian]] |language=ms}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2024/03/1028618/kkb-rep-lee-kee-hiong-dies|title=KKB rep Lee Kee Hiong dies|website=[[New Straits Times]]|date=21 March 2024|access-date=21 March 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2024/03/21/kuala-kubu-baharu-assemblyman-dies/|title=Kuala Kubu Bharu assemblyman dies|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=21 March 2024|access-date=21 March 2024}}</ref> Lee had served as Member of the Selangor State Legislative Assembly for Kuala Kubu Baharu, serving since 2013 for three terms. It was the first [[List of state by-elections in Malaysia|Selangor state by-election]] since the [[2023 Selangor state election]]. The last time Selangor had a by-election was during the [[2019 Semenyih by-election]]. 

[[Pang Sock Tao]] of [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) won the by-election after defeating [[Khairul Azhari Saut]] of [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN), [[independent politician|independent]] candidate Eris Nyau Ke Xin and Hafizah Zainuddin of [[Parti Rakyat Malaysia]] (PRM) by a majority of 3,869 votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/05/11/kkb-polls-ec-declares-win-for-pakatan039s-pang-sock-tao|title=KKB polls: EC declares win for Pakatan's Pang Sock Tao|website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=11 May 2024|access-date=11 May 2024}}</ref>

== Events ==
On 7 April 2024, State Secretary of PH of Selangor [[Mohd Khairuddin Othman]] announced the appointment of Member of the [[Selangor State Executive Council]] (EXCO), MLA for [[Kinrara (state constituency)|Kinrara]] and State Secretary of DAP of Selangor [[Ng Sze Han]] as Election Director of the by-election. Mohd Khairuddin also said that he himself, alongside [[Dewan Rakyat|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Puchong (federal constituency)|Puchong]] [[Yeo Bee Yin]] and MP for [[Shah Alam (federal constituency)|Shah Alam]] [[Azli Yusof]] would assist Ng in his role. Mohd Khairuddin also added that Selangor PH would draw up, prepare and coordinate the work and tasks of the campaign with the central committee of PH and parties of the Selangor [[coalition government|coalition state government]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/04/07/kkb-by-election-pakatan-appoints-ng-sze-han-as-election-director|title=KKB by-election: Pakatan appoints Ng Sze Han as election director|website=The Star|date=7 April 2024|accessdate=7 April 2024}}</ref> 

On 10 April 2024, Deputy Chairman of [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) and Deputy President of the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) [[Ahmad Faizal Azumu]] revealed that PN had identified the candidate representing the coalition and would soon officially announce the name. He then acknowledged that the coalition was contesting and fighting in the by-election as an underdog but declared confidence in garnering support of the [[Malaysian Malays|Malays]] while also significantly attracting the votes of the [[Malaysian Indian|Indian]] and [[Orang Asli]] voters in the Malay-majority Kuala Kubu Baharu. He also dismissed concerns about which PN component party would the candidate be of, be it BERSATU, [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) or [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia]] (GERAKAN), stressing that the candidate would be the one who represents the PN coalition instead of any of the parties.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/04/10/perikatan-picks-candidate-for-kuala-kubu-baharu-set-for-039underdog039-fight|title=Perikatan picks candidate for Kuala Kubu Baharu, set for 'underdog' fight|website=The Star|date=10 April 2024|accessdate=10 April 2024}}</ref>

The by-election was supposed to be maiden electoral participation of [[Malaysian Orang Asli Party|Parti Orang Asli Malaysia]] (ASLI), but its candidate failed to file its nomination on Nomination Day citing "technical issues".<ref>{{Cite web |last=Osman |first=Muhammad Azizul |date=2024-04-26 |title=PRK KKB: Parti Asli tarik diri |url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2024/04/27/prk-kkb-parti-asli-tarik-diri/ |access-date=2026-06-13 |website=MalaysiaGazette |language=en-US}}</ref>
