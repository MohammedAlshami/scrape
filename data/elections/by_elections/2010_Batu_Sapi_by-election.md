}}The '''2010 Batu Sapi by-election''' in the [[Malaysia]]n state of [[Sabah]] was held on 4 November 2010. The nomination of candidates was done on 26 October 2010. The [[Batu Sapi (federal constituency)|Batu Sapi]] parliamentary seat fell vacant when its [[Dewan Rakyat|Member of Parliament]]; Datuk [[Edmund Chong Ket Wah]] of [[United Sabah Party]] or ''Parti Bersatu Sabah'' (PBS), was killed after his 750cc motorcycle accident in Sabah on 9 October 2010. Previously PBS won the seat during the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 General Election]] by a 3,708-vote majority over [[Independent politician|Independent]] candidate Chung Kwong Wing.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20101023211521/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/10/16/nation/7236479&sec=nation] Batu Sapi polls on Nov 4, too</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20101023210932/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/10/16/nation/7237178&sec=nation] DAP: Same polling date not to our advantage</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mmail.com.my/content/52392-batu-sapi-byelection-same-day-galas |title = Batu Sapi by-election same day as Galas' {{!}} Malay Mail Online  |accessdate=2010-10-19 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110102172050/http://www.mmail.com.my/content/52392-batu-sapi-byelection-same-day-galas |archivedate=2011-01-02 |df= }} Batu Sapi by-election same day as Galas'</ref> A total 25,720 registered voters eligible to vote in this by-election. The electorate is made of 50.61% Bumiputera, 40.22% Chinese and 3% others.<ref>http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v5/newspolitic.php?id=535541 {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110622084617/http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v5/newspolitic.php?id=535541 |date=2011-06-22 }} SAPP likely to put candidate for Batu Sapi by-election</ref><ref>http://www.mysinchew.com/node/46538 Simultaneous by-elections for Batu Sapi, Galas</ref>

The by-election was held on the same day as the [[2010 Galas by-election]].
