The '''2004 Kuala Berang by-election''' is a by-election for the [[Terengganu State Legislative Assembly]] state seat of [[Kuala Berang]], [[Malaysia]] that were held on 28 August 2004.<ref>{{cite web |title=Polling in Kuala Berang smooth and brisk |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/29545 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=12 March 2025 |date=28 August 2004}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=BN kekalkan kerusi DUN Kuala Berang |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/29555 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=12 March 2025 |language=ms |date=28 August 2004}}</ref> It was called following the death of the incumbent, Komaruddin Abdul Rahman on 30 July 2004.<ref name='ADUNKualaBerangMeninggal'>{{cite web |title=Adun Kuala Berang meninggal dunia akibat sakit jantung |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/28815 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=12 March 2025 |language=ms |date=30 July 2004}}</ref>

== Background ==
Komaruddin Abdul Rahman, from [[United Malays National Organization]] (UMNO), were elected to the Terengganu State Legislative Assembly state seat of Kuala Berang at the 2004 Terengganu state election, winning the seat in his first attempt as a [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) candidate and helped BN regaining the seat from [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) candidate. A vice-chief of UMNO Hulu Terengganu division, he was BN's development officer of Kuala Berang seat, when the seat and also the state was under PAS' control.<ref name='ADUNKualaBerangMeninggal'/>

On 30 July 2004, Komaruddin died at Awana Hospital, Genting Highlands after suffering a heart attack.<ref name='ADUNKualaBerangMeninggal'/> He was on a holiday trip with his family and several Terengganu assemblymen, before going to Kuantan to participate in a charity football friendly match between Pahang Menteri Besar team and Terengganu State Legislative assembly team.<ref name='ADUNKualaBerangMeninggal'/> Komaruddin's death means that Kuala Berang state seat were vacated. This necessitates for by-election to be held, as the seat were vacated more that 2 years before the expiry of Terengganu assembly current term. Election Commission of Malaysia (SPR) announced that the by-election for the seat will be held on 28 August 2004, with 16 August 2009 set as the nomination day.

== Nomination and campaign ==
After nomination closed, it was confirmed that BN will face PAS in a straight fight for the Kuala Berang seat.<ref name="BNPASCalon">{{cite web |title=BN, Pas letak calon di Kuala Berang |url=https://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=2004&dt=0815&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Dalam_Negeri&pg=dn_02.htm |website=Utusan Online |access-date=12 March 2025 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612150203/https://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=2004&dt=0815&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Dalam_Negeri&pg=dn_02.htm#ixzz5I0Qazibv |archive-date=12 June 2018 |date=14 August 2004}}</ref> BN nominated [[Mohd Zawawi Ismail]], an Assistant Education Officer of Hulu Terengganu district, and a diploma holder from Raja Melewar Education College.<ref name="BNPASCalon"/> PAS meanwhile nominated Muhyidin Rashid, who lost for Hulu Terengganu parliamentary seat in the [[2004 Malaysian general election]].<ref name="BNPASCalon"/> 

Observers predicted that BN will retain the seat as it has been among the safest BN seats in the state, and only changed hand 2 times (1974 and 1999), both to PAS.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Rais |first1=Aman |title=Is PAS moon waning in Kuala Berang? |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/columns/29057 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=12 March 2025 |date=10 August 2004}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Rais |first1=Aman |title=Can PAS redeem its pride in Kuala Berang? |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/columns/28907 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=12 March 2025 |date=4 August 2004}}</ref>
