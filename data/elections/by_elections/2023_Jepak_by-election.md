The '''2023 Jepak by-election''' was a by-election that was held on 4 November 2023 for the [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly]] seat of [[Jepak (state constituency)|Jepak]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/09/15/deputy-dun-speaker-ec-to-be-notified-of-vacant-jepak-state-seat-for-by-election|title=Deputy DUN Speaker: EC to be notified of vacant Jepak state seat for by-election|first=Nur Shazreena|last=Ali|date=15 September 2023|accessdate=16 September 2023|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]}}</ref><ref name="JepakByElec4Nov">{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Jepak state by-election on Nov 4: EC |url=https://www.thesundaily.my/local/jepak-state-by-election-on-nov-4-ec-CG11534475 |website=www.thesundaily.my |access-date=28 September 2023 |language=en-MY |date=25 September 2023}}</ref> It was called following the death of its incumbent, [[Talib Zulpilip]] from kidney complications on 15 September 2023.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/09/15/talib-zulpilip-passes-away-at-72|title=Talib Zulpilip passes away at 72|date=15 September 2023|accessdate=15 September 2023|website=[[The Borneo Post]]}}</ref>

The by-election was the latest in Sarawak after the [[2017 Tanjong Datu by-election]].

==Background==
Jepak is a state constituency in the Malaysian state of [[Sarawak]], and one of the four constituencies in the federal constituency of [[Bintulu (federal constituency)|Bintulu]]. As of 15 September 2023, Supplementary Electoral Roll used by Election Commission (EC) for this by-election, Jepak has 22,804 eligible registered voters, including 43 voters from the police force.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Jepak by-election falls on Nov 4 |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/09/25/jepak-by-election-falls-on-nov-4 |website=The Star |access-date=30 September 2023 |language=en |date=25 September 2023}}</ref> The significant increase of voters from 14,643 during the 2021 state election to 22,804 is due to the previous election has not implemented the [[UNDI18]].

Talib was a member of the Sarawak State Legislative Assembly, representing Jepak for six terms since winning the seat in [[1996 Sarawak state election|1996]]. He was a member of the supreme council appointed by [[Parti Pesaka Bumiputera Bersatu]] (PBB), which is a component party of [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS), the current ruling party of Sarawak. He is also the chairman of PBB's Jepak branch.<ref>{{cite web |last1=YUSSOP |first1=YUNUS |title=Talib Zulpilip remains PBB Jepak chairman, Pengiran Daud replaces Saidi as deputy chairman |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2022/03/31/talib-zulpilip-remains-pbb-jepak-chairman-pengiran-daud-replaces-saidi-as-deputy-chairman/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=30 September 2023 |date=31 March 2022}}</ref> In the Sarawak cabinet, he has held the position of Junior Minister of Tourism and Heritage, and Minister in the Premier Office (Integrity and Ombudsman).<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Talib Zulpilip pemimpin berintegriti tinggi, sentiasa perangi rasuah - Abang Johari |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-malaysia/talib-zulpilip-pemimpin-berintegriti-tinggi-sentiasa-perangi-rasuah-abang-johari-437605 |website=www.astroawani.com |access-date=30 September 2023 |date=16 September 2023}}</ref> Talib's poor health condition previously raised concerns among his party members in his branch, who urged him to step down and give way to younger candidates before the [[2021 Sarawak state election]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=YUSSOP |first1=YUNUS |title=PBB Jepak: Time for Talib to give way |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2021/11/22/pbb-jepak-time-for-talib-to-give-way/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=30 September 2023 |date=22 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=YUSSOP |first1=YUNUS |title=PBB Jepak deputy chief in about turn after initially backing Talib to be retained |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2021/11/08/pbb-jepak-deputy-chief-in-about-turn-after-initially-backing-talib-to-be-retained/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=30 September 2023 |date=8 November 2021}}</ref> However he was retained as candidate by GPS-PBB in the election, successfully defending his seat in a 4-corner contest by 4,243 votes majority over his nearest rival. On 15 September 2023, Talib died of kidney complications at Normah Medical Specialist Centre in [[Kuching]], leaving his constituency vacated at the next day.

== Nomination and campaign ==
On 18 October 2023, GPS through [[Premier of Sarawak|Premier]] and its Chairman [[Abang Abdul Rahman Johari Abang Openg]], announced Iskandar Turkee, former state chairman of National Anti-Drugs Agency (NADA) as the candidate representing the coalition in the by-election.<ref>{{cite web|title=Iskandar Turkee calon GPS PRK Jepak|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2023/10/1166744/iskandar-turkee-calon-gps-prk-jepak |website=Berita Harian|language=ms|access-date=4 November 2023 |date=18 October 2023}}</ref> A local candidate from Kampung Sebiew who previously was not a member of any component party in GPS including PBB, Iskandar was chosen by consensus within PBB and support from other GPS parties.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ling |first1=Sharon |title=Jepak by-election: Iskandar Turkee named as GPS candidate |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/10/18/jepak-by-election-iskandar-turkee-named-as-gps-candidate |website=The Star |access-date=19 November 2023 |language=en |date=18 October 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=PBB Women chief backs Iskandar Turkee as GPS candidate for Jepak by-election|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/10/23/pbb-women-chief-backs-iskandar-turkee-as-gps-candidate-for-jepak-by-election/97893 |access-date=19 November 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Yussop |first1=Yunus |title=Former PBB Jepak deputy chairman says no split in party, members to ensure bigger majority for Iskandar Turkee |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/10/19/former-pbb-jepak-deputy-chairman-says-no-split-in-party-members-to-ensure-bigger-majority-for-iskandar-turkee/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=19 November 2023 |date=19 October 2023}}</ref>

Parti Sedar Rakyat Sarawak (SEDAR) president, Othman Abdillah has said on 20 September that the party may consider fielding a candidate in the by-election,<ref>{{cite web |last1=TEN |first1=MARILYN |title=Jepak by-election: Sedar may field candidate, says party president |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/09/20/jepak-by-election-sedar-may-field-candidate-says-party-president/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=22 September 2023 |date=20 September 2023}}</ref> but later on 23 September clarified his statement, and proposing a unified opposition to face GPS.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Edward |first1=Churchill |title=Sedar proposes opposition pact for Jepak by-election to face GPS |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/09/23/sedar-proposes-opposition-pact-for-jepak-by-election-to-face-gps/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=28 September 2023 |date=23 September 2023}}</ref>

Meanwhile, [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR) announced on 25 September that they will not enter as a sign of solidarity to GPS, who are one of the partners in the [[Pakatan Harapan]]-[[Barisan Nasional]] led federal government.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Cheng |first1=Lian |title=PKR Sarawak to sit out Jepak by-election for sake of maintaining unity govt |url=https://dayakdaily.com/pkr-sarawak-to-sit-out-jepak-by-election-for-sake-of-maintaining-unity-govt/ |website=DayakDaily |access-date=28 September 2023 |date=26 September 2023}}</ref> Although urged to compete in the election by fellow PH component party [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP), who is in opposition in Sarawak Assembly,<ref>{{cite web |last1=ALI |first1=NUR SHAZREENA |title=Jepak by-election: DAP man tells PKR to field candidate, challenge GPS |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/09/28/jepak-by-election-dap-man-tells-pkr-to-field-candidate-challenge-gps/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=30 September 2023 |date=28 September 2023}}</ref> PKR stood on its decision.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ahmad |first1=Shafik |title=PRK Jepak: PKR Sarawak tidak bertanding |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/prk-jepak-pkr-sarawak-tidak-bertanding-439656 |website=www.astroawani.com |access-date=30 September 2023 |language=MS |date=30 September 2023}}</ref>

On 28 September, [[Parti Aspirasi Rakyat Sarawak]] (ASPIRASI) announced through its president that the party will contest the by-election.<ref>{{cite web |title=Aspirasi to contest in Jepak by-election |url=https://dayakdaily.com/aspirasi-to-contest-in-jepak-by-election/ |website=DayakDaily |access-date=28 September 2023 |date=28 September 2023}}</ref> The party later confirmed the statement and fills Chieng Lea Ping, a businessman by trade, as their candidate on 17 October.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Wong |first1=Philip |title=Aspirasi names businessman as its candidate for Jepak |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/10/17/aspirasi-names-businessman-as-its-candidate-for-jepak/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=19 November 2023 |date=17 October 2023}}</ref>

[[Parti Bumi Kenyalang]] (PBK) also confirmed its intention to contest in this by-election, having pick Stevenson Joseph Sumbang as its candidate.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Yussop |first1=Yunus |title=PBK names Stevenson Joseph Sumbang as its Jepak by-election candidate |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/10/05/pbk-names-stevenson-joseph-sumbang-as-its-jepak-by-election-candidate/ |website=Borneo Post Online |access-date=19 November 2023 |date=5 October 2023}}</ref> The party was the first among all the contesting parties to announce its candidate, on 5 October. A former [[Royal Malaysian Police]] personnel, Stevenson will contest in Jepak for the second time, having lost to Talib in the 2021 state election while garnering only 587 votes.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tawie |first1=Sulok |title=Sarawak opposition selects former cop as candidate for Jepak by-election set for November 4 |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/10/05/sarawak-opposition-selects-former-cop-as-candidate-for-jepak-by-election-set-for-november-4/94605 |website=Malay Mail |access-date=19 November 2023 |language=en |date=5 October 2023}}</ref>

Candidates nomination day was announced by Election Commission (EC) to be on 21 October 2023.<ref name="JepakByElec4Nov"/> On the nomination day, it was confirmed that there will be a 3-corner fight between Iskandar (GPS-PBB), Chieng (ASPIRASI) and Stevenson (PBK) after nominations closed.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Chua |first1=Andy |title=Jepak polls: Three-way fight for Sarawak state seat |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/10/21/jepak-polls-three-way-fight-for-sarawak-state-seat#:~:text=The%20three%20are%20Iskandar%20Turkee,Chieng%20Lea%20Ping%20of%20Aspirasi.&text=The%20by-election%20is%20being,72%20due%20to%20health%20complications. |website=The Star |access-date=19 November 2023 |language=en |date=21 October 2023}}</ref>

== Aftermath ==
Iskandar, the winner of the by-election, were sworn in as the new MLA for Jepak on 20 November at the start of the Sarawak State Assembly sitting.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Borneo Post Online |title=Iskandar Turkee sworn in as Jepak assemblyman |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/11/20/iskandar-turkee-sworn-in-as-jepak-assemblyman/102998 |website=Malay Mail |access-date=10 June 2024 |language=en |date=20 November 2023}}</ref> He subsequently joined PBB, and was appointed its chairman for Jepak branch on 4 May 2024, filling in the vacancy left by the late Talib.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Meraw |first1=Marlynda |title=Iskandar Turkee appointed PBB Jepak branch chief |url=https://dayakdaily.com/iskandar-turkee-appointed-pbb-jepak-branch-chief/ |website=DayakDaily |access-date=10 June 2024 |date=4 May 2024}}</ref>
