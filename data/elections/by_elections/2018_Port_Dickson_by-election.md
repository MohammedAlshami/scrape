A '''by-election''' was held on 13 October 2018, for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of '''[[Port Dickson (federal constituency)|Port Dickson]].'''<ref name="announcement">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442734|title=PKR executes 'PD Move' to pave way for Anwar's return|first=Zikri|last=Kamarulzaman|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=12 September 2018|access-date=13 September 2018}}</ref> The seat became vacant after the resignation of the incumbent member Danyal Balagopal Abdullah, a member of [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR) and the governing [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442808|title=Speaker confirms Port Dickson MP's resignation|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=12 September 2018|access-date=13 September 2018}}</ref> This was the first federal by-election since the 9 May [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] (GE14), which saw the first-ever change of federal government in Malaysia's history.

Danyal Balagopal resigned his seat to pave a way for PKR leader [[Anwar Ibrahim]] to return to Parliament, it was dubbed as the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)#PD Move|'PD Move']].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/10/13/anwar-ibrahim-wins-pd-byelection-with-23560-vote-majority/|title=Anwar takes 'PD Move' critics to task|author=Mazwin Nik Anis|author2=Sarban Singh|author3=RSN Murali|author4=Hemananthani Sivanandam|author5=R Sekaran|author6=Tarrence Tan|name-list-style=amp|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=15 September 2018|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref> Anwar was unable to contest the earlier GE14 due to his [[Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials|imprisonment on sodomy charges]], which were widely accepted as politically motivated. He received a royal pardon after PH defeated the then-ruling [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and came into power, restoring his political rights and his eligibility to contest in elections.<ref name="announcement"/>

The Port Dickson parliamentary constituency had 75,770 registered voters in this by-election. 43% were Malays, 33% Chinese, 22% Indian and 2% other races.

Anwar won the by-election with an increased majority, returning to Parliament for the first time in three years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/10/13/anwar-ibrahim-wins-pd-byelection-with-23560-vote-majority/|title=EC: Anwar wins PD polls with 23,560-vote majority|author=Sarban Singh|author2=Hemananthani Sivanandam|author3=Rahimy Rahim|author4=Tarrence Tan|name-list-style=amp|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=13 October 2018|access-date=14 October 2018}}</ref>

== Nomination ==
The nomination day for the by-election was 29 September 2018.

While PH confirmed early that it would field Anwar in the by-election, the oppositions did not announce its candidate until close to the nomination day. The [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) was initially expected not to contest the by-election and lend its support to the BN, like they did in the [[2018 Sungai Kandis by-election|Sungai Kandis]] and [[2018 Balakong by-election|Balakong by-elections]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442900|title=Port Dickson polls: PAS to 'make way' for Umno|first=Hariz|last=Mohd|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=13 September 2018|access-date=13 September 2018}}</ref>

Criticisms were landed at PH for engineering the by-election too soon after the general election. BN politicians lambasted PH's move as an attempted coronation of Anwar.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/443471|title=Khairy: Coronations are for kings and Anwar isn't one|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=17 September 2018|access-date=17 September 2018}}</ref> This was repudiated by prominent PH leaders such as [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) secretary-general and [[Minister of Finance (Malaysia)|Finance Minister]] [[Lim Guan Eng]]. Lim said Anwar's return to Parliament was overdue and criticised BN's role in jailing Anwar when they were in government, thus preventing him to contest in the first place.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/443271|title='Anwar's return to Parliament is overdue'|first=Guan Eng|last=Lim|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=17 September 2018|access-date=17 September 2018}}</ref>

A candidate from the [[United Malays National Organization]] (UMNO) was initially expected to contest for BN. Another BN component party, the [[Malaysian Indian Congress]] (MIC), announced that it would not contest the by-election for the coalition. The MIC said that their decision was a protest against UMNO's exclusion of their party from the candidacy consultations. They have previously contested the seat and its predecessor seat [[Telok Kemang (federal constituency)|Telok Kemang]] for BN as recently as the 2018 federal elections.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442911|title=MIC to sit out Port Dickson polls this time around|first=B Nantha|last=Kumar|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=13 September 2018|access-date=13 September 2018}}</ref> Another BN component party, the [[Malaysian Chinese Association]] (MCA), also criticised UMNO's sidelining of other BN component parties and its cooperation with PAS.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442965|title='BN can kiss seats goodbye' - Wee says of Umno-PAS hook-up|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=13 September 2018|access-date=13 September 2018}}</ref>

UMNO itself was divided on what approach it will be taking in the by-election. While most leaders were saying that the party would contest the seat, some prominent leaders were suggesting that the party should not contest and let Anwar win in a [[Walkover#Use in elections|walkover]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/442871|title=Umno Youth leaders: Give Anwar a walkover, enjoy the fireworks later|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=12 September 2018|access-date=17 September 2018}}</ref> Several UMNO politicians were even planning to support Anwar, therefore bucking their party's official stance. The most notable politician in the last category was former [[Minister of Tourism, Arts and Culture (Malaysia)|Tourism Minister]] [[Nazri Aziz]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/443008|title=Nazri says will campaign for Anwar in PD by-election|first=Zikri|last=Kamarulzaman|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=12 September 2018|access-date=17 September 2018}}</ref> BN ended up deciding not to contest, as a boycott and protest against the government's forcing of the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/443008|title=BN boycotts 'forced, wasteful' PD by-election|first=Alyaa|last=Alhadjri|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=24 September 2018|access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref>

To prevent the absence of opposition candidates, PAS moved to field their own candidate, a retired [[Lieutenant colonel|Lt Col]] ranked [[Royal Malaysian Air Force|air force]] officer, Mohd Nazari Mokhtar.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/pas-picks-former-air-force-officer-in-port-dickson-by-election-against-anwar-ibrahim|title=PAS picks former air force officer in Port Dickson by-election against Anwar Ibrahim|first=Nadirah|last=H. Rodzi|publisher=[[Straits Times]]|date=27 September 2018|access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref> Close to the nomination day, the field became more crowded with the surprise entry of two independent candidates, namely [[Mohd Isa Abdul Samad]] the former [[Menteri Besar of Negeri Sembilan]] and [[Saiful Bukhari Azlan]] the former personal aide of Anwar who was also the accuser in his [[Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials#2008 allegation|second sodomy trial]].<ref name="candidates">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/s/1679073/pundits-isa-samads-umno-backing-in-pd-shows-party-cracks-strongman-appeal/|title=PRM to contest in Port Dickson by-election|first1=Ida|last1=Lim|first2=Yiswaree|last2=Palanisamy|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=4 October 2018|access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/445153|title=Anwar's ex-aide Saiful to join the fray|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=28 September 2018|access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref>

The other independent candidates contested were social media activist Stevie Chan Keng Leong, former lecturer Lau Seck Yan and management consultant Kan Chee Yuen.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/2018/10/09/voters-want-port-dickson-be-developed-langkawi|title=Voters want Port Dickson to be developed like Langkawi|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun Daily]]|date=9 October 2018|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

An eight and last independent candidate A. Rajendra who had filed his nomination papers, was disqualified because his proposer was not a registered voter in the constituency.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/pd-byelection-one-disqualified-seven-fight-seat|title=PD by-election: One disqualified, seven to fight for seat|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Markets]]|date=29 September 2018|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

[[Parti Rakyat Malaysia]] (PRM) originally stated that they would contest this by-election, but ended up not fielding a candidate.<ref name="candidates"/><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2018/09/21/prm-to-contest-in-port-dickson-by-election/|title=PRM to contest in Port Dickson by-election|first=Predeep|last=Nambiar|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=21 September 2018|access-date=23 September 2018|archive-date=16 October 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181016162103/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2018/09/21/prm-to-contest-in-port-dickson-by-election/|url-status=dead}}</ref> PRM eventually sacked its designated candidate, Ahmad Kamarudin, an ex-PKR member for withdrawing from the by-election claiming insufficient funds for the electoral deposit only at the eleventh hour.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/s/1678126/prm-urged-to-sack-would-be-pd-candidate-for-dropping-out-of-race|title=PRM urged to sack would-be PD candidate for dropping out of race|author=Sharina Ahmad|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=1 October 2018|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

== Controversies ==
A voter in Port Dickson, Rosmadi Mohd Kassim filed a judicial review at the High Court on 27 September 2018, seeking the court to declare that the resignation of incumbent MP Danyal Balagopal Abdullah unconstitutional, thus rendering the move by [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) to hold the by-election as null and void.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/2018/09/27/man-files-judicial-review-stop-pd-election|title=Man files judicial review to stop PD by-election|author=Vathani Panirchellvum|publisher=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun Daily]]|date=27 September 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> The High Court dismissed the application on 2 October 2018.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2018/10/416998/port-dickson-election-court-dismisses-application-declare-resignation-mp|title=Port Dickson by-election: Court dismisses application to declare resignation of MP null and void|author= Rahmat Khairulrijal|publisher=[[The New Straits Times]]|date=2 October 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref>

Another Port Dickson voter, Noraziah Mohd Shariff filed on 28 September 2018 for a court declaration to invalidate the royal pardon received by Anwar on the grounds that it was a "full pardon" and not a "free pardon" under the Federal Constitution, which allows a former convict to contest.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/445146|title=PD voter seeks to disqualify Anwar from by-election|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=28 September 2018|access-date=30 September 2018}}</ref> PAS had hoped that the court would disqualify Anwar after he won and if their candidate finished second, he would instead be declared as the new constituency MP.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/10/07/pas-hopes-to-win-port-dickson-seat-via-back-door-if-anwar-is-disqualified/|title=PAS hopes to win Port Dickson seat via back door if Anwar is disqualified|author= Tarrence Tan|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=7 October 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> EC chairman [[Azhar Azizan Harun]] affirmed the agency's stand that Anwar was free and eligible to contest and proceed in the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/2018/10/09/anwar-free-and-eligible-contest-pd-ec-chairman|title=Anwar is free and eligible to contest in PD: EC chairman|author=Amar Shah Mohsen|publisher=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun Daily]]|date=9 October 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> The High Court dismissed the application finally when the applicant and her counsel failed to show up in court on 22 November 2018.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/court-dismisses-pd-voters-suit-against-anwar-ibrahim|title=Court dismisses PD voter's suit against Anwar Ibrahim|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Markets]]|date=23 November 2018|access-date=23 November 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181123034627/http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/court-dismisses-pd-voters-suit-against-anwar-ibrahim|archive-date=23 November 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Campaigning==
On 8 October 2018, [[Mahathir Mohamad]] broke a prime ministerial tradition by coming to Port Dickson to campaign in the by-election. He also spoke on the same stage with Anwar for the first time in twenty years.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/10/07/port-dickson-byelection-dr-ms-presence-will-boost-anwars-support-says-wan-azizah/|title=Port Dickson by-election: Dr M's presence will boost Anwar's support, says Wan Azizah|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=7 October 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref>

Some UMNO members defied their party's official boycott and supported Mohd Isa including his own son.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2018/09/416556/son-campaigns-isa-samad-port-dickson|title=Son campaigns for Isa Samad in Port Dickson|author1=Syed Umar Ariff|author2= Ifwan Tun Tuah|publisher=[[The New Straits Times]]|date=30 September 2018|access-date=11 October 2018}}</ref> The Indian-based political party [[All Malaysian Indian Progressive Front|Indian Progressive Front]] (IPF) expressed support for PAS in the by-election.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/446780 |title=Indian-based party pledges support for PAS candidate|author=B Nantha Kumar |publisher=[[Malaysiakini]] |date=10 October 2018|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref> The Negeri Sembilan [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia]] (GERAKAN) announced their support for Anwar.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/446737 |title=Gerakan Negeri Sembilan isytihar sokong Anwar di PD|language=ms |publisher=[[Malaysiakini]] |date=10 October 2018|access-date=10 October 2018}}</ref>

==Result==

Anwar won the Port Dickson by-election with 31,016 votes securing a 23,560-vote majority, with runner-up Mohd Nazari garnering 7,456 votes.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/447208|title=Official: Anwar wins PD by-election with bigger majority than GE14|work=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=13 October 2018|access-date=13 October 2018}}</ref>
