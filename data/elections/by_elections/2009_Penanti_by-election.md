An election to fill the state assembly seat for [[Penanti]] was held on 31 May 2009. Penanti has 15,384 registered voters, with 73% of them [[Ethnic Malays|Malays]], 24% [[Chinese people|Chinese]], 2% [[India]]ns and 0.7% of other communities.<ref>{{cite news |title= Penanti by-election likely to be 4-cornered fight| url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2009/5/22/nation/20090522124024&sec=nation| publisher=TheStar Online| date= 22 April 2009 |accessdate=22 April 2009}}</ref> Dr [[Mansor Othman]] of the [[People's Justice Party, Malaysia|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) party won the election.

== Election background ==
[[Penang]] Deputy Chief Minister and  state assemblyperson Mohammad Fairus Khairuddin quit the state assembly seat of Penanti, Penang regarding allegations over his non-performance in public office and corruption allegations.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mohammad Fairus quits Penanti seat| url=http://thenutgraph.com/article-3220.html| publisher=The Nut Graph | date= 16 April 2009 |accessdate=16 April 2009}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> Fairus was however cleared of the alleged corruption links with quarry operations in the state by the [[Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission]] (MACC).<ref>{{cite news| title=MACC clears Fairus of corruption charges| url=http://www.thesundaily.com/article.cfm?id=32669| publisher=Sun2Surf| date=23 April 2009| accessdate=23 April 2009}}{{Dead link|date=May 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

According to the mainstream media, Opposition leader [[Anwar Ibrahim]] decided to force a by-election to allow his former political secretary Dr Mansor Othman to contest, win and automatically become Penang deputy Chief Minister.<ref>{{cite news |title=BN's dilemma: To fight or not to fight| url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2009/4/21/courts/3732750&sec=courts| publisher=TheStar Online | date= 21 April 2009 |accessdate=21 April 2009}}</ref> The Election Commission has set the nominations day for 23 May and polling day on 31 May for the Penanti state by-election in Penang.<ref>{{cite news |title=Penanti vote is on |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/24661-penanti-vote-is-on |publisher=Malaysian Insider |date=27 April 2009 |accessdate=27 April 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090428145513/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/24661-penanti-vote-is-on |archive-date=28 April 2009 |url-status=dead |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Former Prime Minister Tun Dr [[Mahathir Mohamad]] urged Barisan Nasional to contest the by-election.,<ref>{{cite news| title=Dr M says BN must contest Penanti by-election| url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/23812-dr-m-says-bn-must-contest-penanti-by-election| publisher=Malaysian Insider| date=22 April 2009| accessdate=22 April 2009| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090422142945/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/23812-dr-m-says-bn-must-contest-penanti-by-election| archive-date=22 April 2009| url-status=dead| df=dmy-all}}</ref> but Barisan Nasional supreme council announced on 18 May 2009 that the party would not contest the Penanti state assembly seat in Penang.

== Nominations ==
Penang PKR deputy chief Mansor Othman, a 54-year-old party veteran, was named the party's candidate.<ref>{{cite news| title=Mansor Othman is PKR candidate for Penanti by-election| url=http://www.thesundaily.com/article.cfm?id=32870| publisher=Sun2Surf| date=28 April 2009| accessdate=28 April 2009}}{{Dead link|date=May 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>  Three candidates ran as independents: former Penang PKR Wanita chief Aminah Abdullah, company manager Nai-Khan Ari and motivational expert Kamarul Ramizu Idris.<ref>{{cite news |title=Penanti by-election| url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2009/5/23/nation/20090523113820&sec=nation| publisher=TheStar Online| date= 23 May 2009|accessdate=23 May 2009}}</ref>

== By-election results ==
PKR's Dr Mansor Othman won 6,052 of the 7,100 votes cast in the Penanti by-election on 31 May 2009. Independents Nai Khan Ari, Aminah Abdullah and Kamarul Ramizu Idris obtained 494, 392 and 56 votes respectively. The polling ended with a low turnout of 46.1% or 7,100 voters despite fair weather.<ref>{{cite news|title=Penanti: Dr Mansor wins with 6,052 votes out of 7,100|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2009/5/31/nation/20090531091732&sec=nation|publisher=TheStar Online|date=31 May 2009|accessdate=31 May 2009|archive-date=1 June 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090601064405/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2009%2F5%2F31%2Fnation%2F20090531091732&sec=nation|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Penanti:Mansor wins, heads for DCM office|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/28144-lower-turnout-missing-names-as-penanti-vote-closes|publisher=Malaysian Insider|date=31 May 2009|accessdate=31 May 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090602080211/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/28144-lower-turnout-missing-names-as-penanti-vote-closes|archive-date=2 June 2009|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
