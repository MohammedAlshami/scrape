The '''2002 Ketari by-election''' was a by-election that was held on 1 April 2002 for the [[Pahang State Legislative Assembly]] seat of [[Ketari (state constituency)|Ketari]]. It was called following the death of its [[Barisan Nasional]] assemblyman Loke Koon Kam on 3 March 2002.<ref>{{Cite news |title='Dont' recruit members from other BN parties' |date=2002-03-11 |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=x8G803Bi31IC&dat=20020311&printsec=frontpage&hl=en |access-date=2024-12-19 |work=[[New Straits Times]] |pages=4 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |title='Kiler blows' tactics  backfire on DAP |date=2002-04-01 |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=x8G803Bi31IC&dat=20020401&printsec=frontpage&hl=en |access-date=2024-12-19 |work=[[New Straits Times]] |pages=4 |language=en}}</ref> Loke won the seat on [[1999 Malaysian state elections]] against Choong Siew Onn  of [[Democratic Action Party]] with a majority of 231.<ref>{{Cite report |title=Laporan pilihanraya umum Malaysia 2002 |date=2002 |trans-title=Report of the general election Malaysia 2002 |publisher=[[Election Commission of Malaysia|Suruhanjaya Pilihan Raya Malaysia]] |language=ms}}</ref>

Yum Ah Ha of [[Barisan Nasional]] retain the seat against [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]] Choong Siew Onn with a 10 times increase in majority of 2,204 votes.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Barisan Nasional retains Ketari with 10-fold increase in majority |date=2002-04-01 |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=x8G803Bi31IC&dat=20020401&printsec=frontpage&hl=en |access-date=2024-12-19 |work=[[New Straits Times]] |pages=1 |language=en}}</ref>

==Nomination==
On nomination day, [[Barisan Nasional]] nominated chairmen of [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia|GERAKAN]] Ketari division, Yum Ah Ha.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Keng Yaik expects tough fight in Ketari by-election |date=2002-03-11 |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=x8G803Bi31IC&dat=20020311&printsec=frontpage&hl=en |access-date=2024-12-19 |work=[[New Straits Times]] |pages=4 |language=en}}</ref> [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]] nominated Choong Siew Onn for the third straight time.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Choong bitter about suffering third straight loss |date=2002-04-01 |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=x8G803Bi31IC&dat=20020401&printsec=frontpage&hl=en |access-date=2024-12-19 |work=[[New Straits Times]] |pages=4 |language=en}}</ref>
