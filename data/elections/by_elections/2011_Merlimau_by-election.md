The [[Merlimau]] state by-election is a state by-election that was scheduled to be held on 6 March 2011 in the state of [[Malacca]], [[Malaysia]]. The nomination of candidates was scheduled on 26 February 2011.  The Merlimau seat fell vacant following the death of its state assemblyman Datuk Mohamad Hidhir Abu Hasaan of [[United Malays National Organisation]] from a heart attack at the [[Putra Specialist Hospital]] in Malacca.<ref>https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/154801 March 6 polls for Merlimau by-election</ref><ref>http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2011/1/28/nation/20110128230445&sec=nation {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110204113726/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2011%2F1%2F28%2Fnation%2F20110128230445&sec=nation |date=2011-02-04 }} Merlimau by-election: Nomination on Feb 26, polling on Mar 6</ref> Previously Mohamad Hidhir won the Merlimau seat with a 2,154 vote majority, beating [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]]' Jasme Tompang at the [[2008 Malaysian general election]]s.<ref>http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2011/1/25/nation/20110125124711&sec=nation {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110629062817/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2011%2F1%2F25%2Fnation%2F20110125124711&sec=nation |date=2011-06-29 }} Merlimau by-election: EC meets on Jan 28 to decide on dates</ref> The state assembly seat has around 10,400 voters, comprising mainly 64% Malays, 21% Chinese and 14% Indians.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/2011/02/12/cards-are-staked-against-pas/ |title = Cards are staked against PAS {{!}} Free Malaysia Today  |access-date=2011-03-03 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110318174337/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/2011/02/12/cards-are-staked-against-pas/ |archive-date=2011-03-18 }} Cards are staked against PAS</ref> For the by-election PAS picked as its candidate, Yuhaizad Abdullah while Barisan Nasional picked Roslan Ahmad.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20110628191549/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2011/2/27/merlimau_kerdau/8148704&sec=merlimau_kerdau] Straight fights in Merlimau and Kerdau by-elections</ref>
