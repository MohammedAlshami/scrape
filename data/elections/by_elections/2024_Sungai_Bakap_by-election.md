The '''2024 Sungai Bakap by-election''' was a by-election for the [[Penang State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Sungai Bakap (state constituency)|Sungai Bakap]]''' that was held on 6 July 2024. It was called following the death of incumbent, [[Nor Zamri Latiff]] on 24 May 2024 due to [[gastritis]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=ADUN Sungai Bakap meninggal dunia|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/666477/berita/semasa/adun-sungai-bakap-meninggal-dunia|date=24 May 2024|access-date=24 May 2024|website=[[Sinar Harian]] |language=ms}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2024/05/1054788/pas-sungai-bakap-assemblyman-dies|title=Pas' Sungai Bakap assemblyman dies|website=[[New Straits Times]]|language=en|date=24 May 2024|access-date=24 May 2024}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/05/24/sungai-bakap-rep-dies-in-hospital/136243|title=Sungai Bakap rep dies in hospital|website=[[Malay Mail]]|language=en|date=24 May 2024|access-date=24 May 2024}}</ref> Nor Zamri has served as Member of the Penang State Legislative Assembly for Sungai Bakap since 2023. It is the first [[List of state by-elections in Malaysia|Penang state by-election]] since the [[2023 Penang state election]]. 

[[Abidin Ismail]] of [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) defeated Joohari Ariffin of [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) by a significantly increased majority of 4,267 votes compared to the one won by Nor Zamri in the 2023 state election.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://mysprsemak.spr.gov.my/semakan/keputusan/terkini|title=MySPRSemak|website=SPR|accessdate=6 July 2024}}</ref>

== Nomination ==
On 12 June 2024, [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) and its component party the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR), representing the government, announced its candidate, Joohari Ariffin, who was making his electoral debut in the election. [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN), one of the major political coalitions working with PH to form the [[coalition governments]] at the [[Government of Malaysia|federal]] and [[Government of Penang|Penang state]] levels, agreed to actively campaign for the PH candidate.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/06/13/pkr-names-retired-educator-joohari-ariffin-as-sungai-bakap-by-election-candidate/139516|title=PKR names retired educator, Joohari Ariffin, as Sungai Bakap by-election candidate|website=[[Malay Mail]]|language=en|date=13 June 2024|access-date=17 June 2024}}</ref>  

Three days later on 15 June 2024, [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) and its component party the [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS), defending the seat in the election, named Nibong Tebal PAS vice chairman Abidin Ismail, who was also contesting in an election for the first time, as its candidate.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2024/06/15/sg-bakap-polls-abidin-ismail-named-as-perikatan-candidate|title=Sg Bakap polls: Abidin Ismail named as Perikatan candidate|website=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|language=en|date=15 June 2024|access-date=17 June 2024}}</ref>

Both Joohari and Abidin were officially nominated on Nomination Day on 22 June 2024. The election saw a straight fight between them as no other candidates were nominated.
