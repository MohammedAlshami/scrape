The '''2007 Ijok by-election''' was held on 28 April 2007. The seat of [[Ijok]] in the State Legislative Assembly of [[Selangor]], [[Malaysia]], fell vacant after the death of the incumbent, [[K. Sivalingam]], on 4 April 2007 from a heart attack. Sivalingam was a member of the state executive council as well as the deputy chairman of the [[Selangor]] state [[Malaysian Indian Congress]] (MIC), a component party of the ruling [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition.<ref>Vijian, P. (4 April 2007). [http://bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=254966 Selangor Exco Member Sivalingam Dies Of Heart Attack In Chennai] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929091930/http://bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=254966 |date=29 September 2007 }}. [[Bernama]].</ref> The by-election was won by the BN candidate [[K. Parthiban]], with a 1,850-vote majority over the opposition candidate, [[Khalid Ibrahim]].<ref name="results">{{cite web|title=Big turnout cheer for Barisan |work=The Star|location=Malaysia |date=29 April 2007 |url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2007/4/29/nation/17587039&sec=nation |accessdate=30 April 2007 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110521055053/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2007%2F4%2F29%2Fnation%2F17587039&sec=nation |archivedate=21 May 2011 }}</ref>

== Background ==
State assembly seat '''N.08 [[Ijok]]''' lies in parliamentary seat '''P.096 [[Kuala Selangor]]'''. As of the [[2004 Malaysian general election|2004 General Elections]], Ijok was a [[Malays (ethnic group)|Malay]]-majority seat (50.6%), followed by [[Malaysian Indian|Indians]] (28.3%), [[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]] (20.9%) and others (0.2%). In the 2004 election, the late K. Sivalingam of the [[Malaysian Indian Congress|MIC]] (a component party of the [[Barisan Nasional|BN]] coalition), garnered 5,213 votes, defeating [[Abdul Rahman Moharam]] of the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) (3,564 votes) and independent candidate [[Mohamed Shariff Nagoorkani]] (313 votes).<ref name="GE2004">{{cite web|title=Nominee Details for the state assembly – IJOK |publisher=pilihanraya.com.my |url=http://www.pilihanraya.com.my/English/assemblyNominees.asp?assemblyId=364 |accessdate=13 April 2007 }}{{dead link|date=April 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

== Pre-nomination speculation ==
=== Barisan Nasional ===
There was no shortage of potential candidates from the BN coalition; the English daily ''[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]'' had quoted MIC president [[S. Samy Vellu]] that 23 MIC members have expressed interest to contest the by-election.<ref>{{cite news|title=MIC has 23 potential candidates for Ijok by-election |work=The Star|location=Malaysia|date=10 April 2007 |url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2007/4/10/nation/20070410191200&sec=nation |accessdate=15 April 2007 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110521052753/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2007%2F4%2F10%2Fnation%2F20070410191200&sec=nation |archivedate=21 May 2011 }}</ref> However, the two leading favourites were said to be MIC central working committee members [[S. Murugesan (Malaysian politician)|S. Murugesan]] and [[T. Mohan]].<ref name="MICIjok">{{cite news |last=Ganesh |first=V.S |date=11 April 2007 |title=Lawyer Murugesan heads shortlist |url=http://www.nst.com.my/Article/vArt?did=20070411075748 |accessdate=15 April 2007 |work=New Straits Times}} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot|fix-attempted=yes}}</ref> Other names bandied by the media included teacher and MIC [[Kuala Selangor]] division chief [[P. Thirumoorthy]], [[Shah Alam]] division vice-chairman [[S. Muruga Vellu]] and [[Kota Raja]] deputy chief [[R. Subramaniam]].<ref name = "MICIjok"/> However, on 16 April 2007, the BN picked a relative unknown, former teacher [[K. Parthiban]] as its by-election candidate.<ref>{{cite news|last=Singh |first=D. |title=Parthiban BN's pick for Ijok |work=The Star|location=Malaysia |date=17 April 2007 |url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2007/4/17/nation/17460150&sec=nation |accessdate=20 April 2007 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070519162150/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2007%2F4%2F17%2Fnation%2F17460150&sec=nation |archivedate=19 May 2007 }}</ref> The selection prompted the MIC [[Kuala Selangor]] division chief [[P. Thirumoorthy]], a relative party [[heavyweight]] on the candidate shortlist to quit the MIC, only to backtrack and declare his support for Parthiban two days later.<ref>{{cite news| title = Thirumoorthy expresses support for BN candidate in Ijok| agency = Bernama| date = 18 April 2007| url = http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=257507| accessdate = 20 April 2007}}{{Dead link|date=September 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

=== Opposition ===
[[Image:Khalid Ibrahim announcing Ijok candidacy.jpg|thumb|150px|The opposition candidate was [[Khalid Ibrahim]] from [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR).]]

Meanwhile, the two major [[opposition parties]], the [[Islamic Party of Malaysia|Pan Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) and the [[Democratic Action Party (Malaysia)|Democratic Action Party]] (DAP), in a rare display of opposition solidarity, both threw their support behind the PKR for the by-election.<ref name = "Opposition">{{cite news | title = PAS, DAP ready to back PKR in Ijok (subscription required)| publisher = Malaysiakini| date = 15 April 2007| url = http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/65958| accessdate = 15 April 2007}}</ref> PKR's campaign will be led by party adviser and former Deputy Prime Minister [[Anwar Ibrahim]].<ref name = "Opposition"/> The leading potential candidate was current party treasurer and former [[Guthrie (Malaysia)|Kumpulan Guthrie Bhd]] and [[Pemodalan Nasional Berhad|Permodalan Nasional Bhd]] CEO [[Khalid Ibrahim]], although some PKR members lobbied for an [[Malaysian Indian|Indian]] candidate.<ref>{{cite news | last = Ong| first =A | title = Khalid likely to be PKR choice for Ijok (subscription required)| publisher = Malaysiakini| date = 14 April 2007| url = http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/65947| accessdate = 15 April 2007}}</ref>

Eventually, the party announced that it had shortlisted Khalid and Indian bureau head [[K.S. Nallakarupan]] for its ticket. Later on 17 April 2007, it was announced that, as expected, Khalid would be the PKR nominee. Nallakarupan said that although PKR President Wan Azizah Wan Ismail had offered him the nomination, he declined in favour of Khalid. Azizah denied allegations of racial bias in the decision, stating: "We picked the candidate not on racial lines but on the basis of the most appropriate choice to be the people's voice in the state assembly".<ref>{{cite news| title = Khalid Ibrahim Keadilan's Pick For Ijok| agency = Bernama| date = 17 April 2007| url = http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=257358| access-date = 18 April 2007| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070929111245/http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=257358| archive-date = 29 September 2007| url-status = dead}}</ref>

=== Independent candidates ===
On 15 April 2007, [[Klang, Malaysia|Klang]]-based businessman [[K. Loganathan]] announced he would contest as an independent candidate in the by-election, citing the lack of [[rural development|development]] and progress under the previous assemblyman and his desire to serve the rural folk of [[Ijok]] as reasons.<ref>{{cite news| title = Businessman To Contest As Independent in Ijok By-election| agency = Bernama| date = 15 April 2007| url = http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=256891| accessdate = 15 April 2007| archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070929091700/http://www.bernama.com.my/bernama/v3/news.php?id=256891| archive-date = 29 September 2007| url-status = dead}}</ref>

By 18 April 2007, rumours were rife that a plethora of independent candidates will be contesting the by-election. Independent [[online]] [[news]] daily ''[[Malaysiakini]]'' reported as many as four independent candidates, including Loganathan, have expressed interest to contest.<ref>{{cite news | last = Kabilan| first =K. | title = Six-way battle in Ijok? (subscription required)| publisher = Malaysiakini| date = 18 April 2007| url = http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/65947| accessdate = 20 April 2007}}</ref>

==Nominations==

[[Image:Khairy Jamaluddin on nomination day at Ijok.jpg|thumb|150px|BN Deputy Youth Chief [[Khairy Jamaluddin]] was alleged to have been involved in provoking a nomination day scuffle.]]

On 19 April 2007, the candidates from BN and PKR filed their nominations at the [[Batang Berjuntai]] community hall, which was converted into a temporary nomination centre. Neither Loganathan nor any of the purported independent candidates appeared, and thus after the nomination period ended at 11.00 am, returning officer Haris Kasim announced that the by-election would be a straight fight between [[K. Parthiban]] of the MIC and Khalid of PKR.<ref name="straight_fight">{{cite news|title=BN And PKR in Straight Fight in Ijok |agency=Bernama|date=19 April 2007 |url=http://web5.bernama.com/events/ijok/news.php?id=257617 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20070810220021/http://web5.bernama.com/events/ijok/news.php?id=257617 |url-status=dead |archive-date=10 August 2007 }}</ref>

The nomination was marred by a clash between BN and PKR supporters when their processions to the nomination centre inadvertently met. Eventually, the police intervened and separated the two groups.<ref name="straight_fight"/> PKR later accused that [[Khairy Jamaluddin]], Deputy BN Youth Chief, had been involved in provoking the fight, and also that BN Youth Chief and Education Minister [[Hishamuddin Hussein]] had thrown bottles at PKR supporters. However, no valid reports was actually made.<ref>{{cite web|language=Malay |title=Tan Sri Khalid dilantik sebagai calon, penyokong dan pemimpin BN bertindak liar |publisher=Parti Keadilan Rakyat |date=19 April 2007 |url=http://www.keadilanrakyat.org/spm/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=1072&Itemid=31 |accessdate=19 April 2007 }}{{dead link|date=June 2016|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref>

The Prime Minister had later warned both PKR and BN supporters of their behaviour during the by-election of Ijok.

== By-election results ==
The BN candidate, [[K. Parthiban]], won with a majority of 1,850 votes. Parthiban secured 5,884 votes while Keadilan's Abdul Khalid Ibrahim got 4,034 votes. A total of 10,052 voters, or 81.9%, of the 12,272 eligible voters had cast their ballots, a record turnout for the constituency. In the 2004 General Elections, BN defeated PKR by 1,649 votes in the constituency.<ref name="results"/>
