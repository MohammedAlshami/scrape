The '''2020 Kimanis by-election''' is a by-election held on 18 January 2020 for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of '''[[Kimanis (federal constituency)|Kimanis]]'''.<ref name="announcement">{{cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/16/kimanis-by-election-on-jan-18/|title=Kimanis by-election on Jan 18|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=16 December 2019|accessdate=16 December 2019|archive-date=19 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191219114437/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/16/kimanis-by-election-on-jan-18/|url-status=dead}}</ref> The seat was declared vacant after the Federal Court on 2 December 2019 upheld the Election Court's ruling earlier on 16 August, nullifying the sitting Member of Parliament (MP), [[Anifah Aman]]'s victory in the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] (GE14).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/12/16/kimanis-by-election-set-for-january-18|title=Kimanis by-election set for January 18|work=[[The Star Online]]|author=JOSEPH KAOS Jr|date=16 December 2019|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref> Anifah was the former [[Minister of Foreign Affairs (Malaysia)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] from 2009 to 2018 and formerly a member of [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) part of the [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition until he left to become  [[Independent politician|independent]], despite the fact that he had been a 4-term MP for Kimanis since 2004 and had just retained the seat under BN in the 2018 election a few months prior.

The [[Election Commission of Malaysia|Election Commission]] (EC) had set the nomination day on 4 January, early voting on 14 January and polling day for 18 January 2020 with a 14-day campaign period.<ref name="announcement"/> The electoral roll in the parliamentary constituency to be used would be the one up to the third quarter of 2019 which was last updated on 9 December 2019 with a total of 29,664 voters made up of 52,698 ordinary voters, 9 early voters while 1 absentee voters (who are abroad).<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.bernama.com/en/news.php?id=1799931|title=Kimanis parliamentary by-election on Jan 18|publisher=[[Bernama]]|date=16 December 2019|access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref> [[Kimanis]] which consists of the [[Bongawan (state constituency)|Bongawan]] and [[Membakut (state constituency)|Membakut]] state seats, has about 68% Muslim-bumiputras voters of mainly [[Bruneian Malay people|Bruneian Malay]], [[Bisaya (Borneo)|Bisaya]] and [[Bajau people|Bajau]] communities, some 25% non-Muslim bumiputras voters of mainly ethnic [[Kadazan-Dusun]] and [[Murut people|Murut]], while the remaining 7% were [[Malaysian Chinese|Chinese]] and others.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/12/17/will-anifah-bid-for-kimanis|title=Will Anifah bid for Kimanis?|work=[[The Star Online]]|author= MUGUNTAN VANAR and JOSEPH KAOS Jr|date=17 December 2019|access-date=18 December 2019}}</ref>

==Nomination==
On 11 December 2019, Sabah [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) Chairman and Deputy Chief Minister [[Christina Liew]] said that PH will pave way and support ally, [[Sabah Heritage Party]] (WARISAN) to field its candidate.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/11/warisan-to-contest-in-kimanis-by-election-after-all/|title=Warisan to contest in Kimanis by-election after all|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=11 December 2019|access-date=16 December 2019|archive-date=15 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191215205754/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/11/warisan-to-contest-in-kimanis-by-election-after-all/|url-status=dead}}</ref> Previously, the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) had offered to contest the by-election but decided to support the WARISAN candidate instead.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/12/08/sabah-bersatu-offers-to-contest-in-kimanis-by-election/1817314|title=Sabah Bersatu offers to contest in Kimanis by-election|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=8 December 2019|access-date=16 December 2019}}</ref> Karim Bujang, the WARISAN candidate for Kimanis in GE14 who had lost and filed the election petition, was once again nominated as WARISAN's candidate for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2020/01/02/kimanis-by-election-warisan039s-karim-bujang-gets-second-chance-to-contest-for-seat|title=Kimanis by-election: Warisan's Karim Bujang gets second chance to contest for seat|work=[[The Star Online]]|author=Kristy Inus|date=2 January 2020|access-date=2 January 2020}}</ref>

Sabah UMNO chief, [[Bung Mokhtar Radin]] confirmed that BN will contest the by-election by fielding a candidate from UMNO.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/14/sabah-umno-confirms-taking-part-in-kimanis-by-election/|title=Sabah Umno confirms taking part in Kimanis by-election|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=14 December 2019|access-date=16 December 2019|archive-date=17 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191217174826/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/14/sabah-umno-confirms-taking-part-in-kimanis-by-election/|url-status=dead}}</ref> BN decided to field UMNO Kimanis division chief and former Bongawan assemblyman, [[Mohamad Alamin]] as their candidate for the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/01/01/umno-to-field-former-bongawan-rep-alamin-in-kimanis-polls/|title=Umno to field former Bongawan rep Alamin in Kimanis polls|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|author=Jason Santos|date=1 January 2020|access-date=1 January 2020|archive-date=1 January 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200101050443/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/01/01/umno-to-field-former-bongawan-rep-alamin-in-kimanis-polls/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

The [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) has announced its support for the BN candidate in the by-election. PAS secretary-general, [[Takiyuddin Hassan]], said that the party will mobilise its election campaign machinery to support the BN candidate.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/504693|title=PAS backs Umno in Kimanis, will help with campaign|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=23 December 2019|access-date=27 December 2019}}</ref>

[[Anifah Aman]], the incumbent prior to the disqualification, denied that he will contest the seat again. Anifah also said that he will return in the coming 15th General Election instead.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/amp/news/malaysia/2019/12/19/anifah-confirms-wont-contest-kimanis-by-election-but-to-return-in-ge15/1820690?|title=Anifah confirms won't contest Kimanis by-election but to return in GE15|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=19 December 2019|access-date=19 December 2019}}</ref> Anifah has since declared his support for the BN candidate in the by-election.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/12/29/anifah-backs-barisan-in-kimanis-stresses-immigrant-issue|title=Anifah backs Barisan in Kimanis, stresses immigrant issue|work=[[The Star Online]]|author=Muguntan Vanar|date=29 December 2019|access-date=1 January 2020}}</ref>

The [[Liberal Democratic Party (Malaysia)|Liberal Democratic Party]] (LDP), [[Homeland Solidarity Party]] (STAR), [[United Sabah National Organisation (New)|United Sabah National Organisation]] (USNO Baru), [[Sabah Progressive Party]] (SAPP) and the [[Sabah United Party]] (PBS) have declined to contest the by-election,  paving the way for a possible straight fight between BN and Warisan.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/23/warisan-umno-straight-fight-in-kimanis-on-the-cards/|title=Warisan, Umno straight fight in Kimanis on the cards|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|author=Jason Santos|date=23 December 2019|access-date=23 December 2019|archive-date=23 December 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191223112334/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/12/23/warisan-umno-straight-fight-in-kimanis-on-the-cards/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/505030|title=Sabah BN, PBS unofficially discuss Kimanis cooperation|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|author=Kow Gah Chie|date=27 December 2019|access-date=27 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/505764|title=PBS opts out of Kimanis race in order not to split votes|author=Kow Gah Chie|date=3 January 2020|access-date=4 January 2020|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]}}</ref>

On Nomination Day, BN's [[Mohamad Alamin]] and WARISAN's Karim Bujang filed their nomination papers, setting the stage for a two-cornered fight for the parliamentary seat.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/01/04/straight-fight-between-bn-warisan-in-kimanis/#|title=Straight fight between BN, Warisan in Kimanis|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=4 January 2020|access-date=4 January 2020|archive-date=4 January 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200104074742/https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2020/01/04/straight-fight-between-bn-warisan-in-kimanis/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Controversies and issues==
The main issues in the by-election are not national matters but local issues such as the Sabah Temporary Pass or ''Pas Sementara Sabah'' (PSS) for [[Refugees of the Philippines#Malaysia|Filipino refugees immigrants]], [[Livelihood|bread-and-butter]] and job opportunities issues.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2020/01/18/final-dash-to-the-kimanis-finish-line|title=Final dash to the Kimanis finish line|work=[[The Star Online]]|date=18 January 2019|access-date=19 January 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.therakyatpost.com/2020/01/17/pas-sementara-sabah-why-is-this-an-issue/|title=Pas Sementara Sabah: Why Is This An Issue?|publisher=[[The Rakyat Post]]|author=Hakim Hassan|date=17 January 2020|access-date=4 January 2020}}</ref>

== Aftermath ==
After the [[2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis|Sheraton Move]], Mohamad was appointed as [[Minister of Education (Malaysia)|Deputy Minister of Education II]] in the [[Ismail Sabri cabinet|Ismail Sabri Yaakob cabinet]]. He eventually successfully retained his parliamentary seat in the [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 general election]].
