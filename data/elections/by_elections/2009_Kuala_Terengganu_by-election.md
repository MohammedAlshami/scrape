'''[[Kuala Terengganu]] by-election of 2009''' was held on 17 January 2009 after the death of the incumbent Member of Parliament, [[Razali Ismail (MP)|Razali Ismail]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Kuala Terengganu By-election On Jan 17   |url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsgeneral.php?id=376536|publisher=Bernama |date=5 December 2008 |accessdate=5 December 2008}}</ref> In the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 election]], Datuk Razali won by a slim 628 vote majority, defeating [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) heavyweight [[Mohamad Sabu]] and 89-year-old independent candidate, Maimun Yusuf.<ref>{{cite news |title=PAS Candidate To Contest In K. Terengganu By-Election | url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsindex.php?id=377248|publisher=Bernama |date=9 December 2008 |accessdate=9 December 2008}}</ref> Prime Minister Datuk Seri Abdullah Ahmad Badawi and [[Barisan Nasional]] were confident that Barisan Nasional would retain the Kuala Terengganu parliamentary seat. The Prime Minister had rejected the assumption that there would be a big swing by the voters to the Opposition.<ref>{{cite news |title=Abdullah confident of BN's victory in Kuala Terengganu by-election| url= http://www.nst.com.my/Current_News/NST/Tuesday/NewsBreak/20081209213142/Article/index_html| publisher=NST|date=9 December 2008 |accessdate=9 December 2008}}</ref> Opposition leader [[Anwar Ibrahim]] stated that [[People's Justice Party, Malaysia|Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR) and the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) would support any candidate that PAS picked. He was optimistic that [[Pakatan Rakyat]] would win in Kuala Terengganu if all its coalition parties worked hard against Barisan Nasional.<ref>{{cite news |title=Not the time to point fingers, says Anwar| url=http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20081210-106738.html| archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081211183040/http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20081210-106738.html| url-status=dead| archive-date=December 11, 2008| publisher=AsiaOne|date=10 December 2008 |accessdate=10 December 2008}}</ref>  In the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 election]] Razali, a government minister of the [[UMNO]] party, defeated a PAS opposition candidate by 628 votes.<ref>{{cite news |title=PAS Candidate To Contest In K. Terengganu By-Election | url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsindex.php?id=377248|publisher=Bernama |date=9 December 2008 |accessdate=9 December 2008}}</ref> For the by-election PAS nominated state assemblyman [[Mohd Abdul Wahid Endut]], while Barisan Nasional nominated Wan Ahmad Farid Wan Salleh. Mohd Abdul won the by-election by 2,631 votes.{{Citation needed|date=April 2010}}
