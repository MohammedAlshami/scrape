The '''2023 Pulai by-election''' was a by-election held on 9 September 2023 for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of [[Pulai (federal constituency)|Pulai]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-08-03 |title=PRK Parlimen Pulai, Dun Simpang Jeram ditetapkan 9 September - SPR |url=https://www.hmetro.com.my/utama/2023/08/995027/prk-parlimen-pulai-dun-simpang-jeram-ditetapkan-9-september-spr |access-date=2023-08-03 |website=Harian Metro |language=en}}</ref> It was called following the death of incumbent, [[Salahuddin Ayub]] on 23 July 2023 of [[brain haemorrhage]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-23 |title=Salahuddin Ayub meninggal dunia |url=https://www.hmetro.com.my/utama/2023/07/991011/salahuddin-ayub-meninggal-dunia |access-date=2023-08-03 |website=Harian Metro |language=en}}</ref> The by-election was held on the same day as the [[2023 Simpang Jeram by-election|Simpang Jeram by-election]].

== Background ==
Pulai is a federal constituency in Johor. The state constituencies of Perling and Kempas are within Pulai. Pulai has a total of 165,313 electors, of which 44.5% are Malays, 40.2% Chinese, 12.2% Indians and 3.1% of other ethnicities.

Salahuddin was a 2-term Member of Parliament for Pulai since [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018]] and Member of Johor State Assembly for Simpang Jeram since [[2018 Johor state election|2018]]. He was a member and Deputy Chairman (2015-2023) of the [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|National Trust Party]] (AMANAH), a component party of the [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition. He was the [[Minister of Agriculture and Food Security (Malaysia)|Minister of Agriculture and Agro-Based Industry]] from 2018 until the collapse of the Pakatan Harapan government in 2020. After the [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 elections]], he was appointed as [[Minister of Domestic Trade and Costs of Living (Malaysia)|Minister of Domestic Trade and Costs of Living]] until his death. By his death in 2023, he became the fourth federal minister to die in office after [[Ismail Abdul Rahman|Tun Dr. Ismail]] ([[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|Deputy Prime Minister]], [[Minister of Home Affairs (Malaysia)|Minister of Home Affairs]] and [[Minister of Investment, Trade and Industry (Malaysia)|Minister of Trade and Industry]] in 1973), [[Abdul Razak Hussein|Tun Abdul Razak]] ([[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Prime Minister]] and [[Minister of Defence (Malaysia)|Minister of Defence]] in 1976) and [[Ali Ahmad (politician)|Ali Ahmad]] ([[Minister of Agriculture and Food Security (Malaysia)|Minister of Agriculture and Rural Development]] in [[Malaysian Airline System Flight 653|1977]]). This also the first elections with incumbent death still hold both parliamentary and state constituencies since [[Fadzil Noor]] in [[2002 Pendang by-election|2002]].

==Nomination and campaign==
Nomination day were announced by Election Commission to be on 26 August 2023.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Pulai, Simpang Jeram by-elections begin with nomination tomorrow |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-malaysia/pulai-simpang-jeram-elections-begin-nomination-tomorrow-434472 |website=www.astroawani.com |access-date=8 September 2023 |date=25 August 2023}}</ref> On the nomination day, 3 candidates were accepted: [[Suhaizan Kayat]] from PH, Zulkifli Jaafar from [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN), and Samsudin Fauzi as an independent.<ref>{{cite web |last1=SHADIQE |first1=JASSMINE |title=3-cornered fight in Pulai, Simpang Jeram by-elections [Updated] |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/08/947524/3-cornered-fight-pulai-simpang-jeram-elections-updated |website=nst.com.my |access-date=8 September 2023 |date=26 August 2023}}</ref>

Suhaizan were announced as PH candidate in a media conference by AMANAH President, [[Mohamad Sabu]] on 19 August.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Amin |first1=Manzaidi Mohd |title=Suhaizan, Ir. Nazri calon Amanah PRK Parlimen Pulai, DUN Simpang Jeram |url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2023/08/19/suhaizan-ir-nazri-calon-amanah-prk-parlimen-pulai-dun-simpang-jeram/ |website=MalaysiaGazette |access-date=8 September 2023 |language=MS |date=19 August 2023}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Amanah names Suhaizan, Nazri candidates for Pulai, Simpang Jeram |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/08/944806/amanah-names-suhaizan-nazri-candidates-pulai-simpang-jeram |website=nst.com.my |access-date=9 September 2023 |date=19 August 2023}}</ref> A former political secretary to the late Salahuddin, he also had served as Speaker for the Johor Legislative Assembly from 2018 to 2022 under 3 different Menteri Besar ([[Osman Sapian]], [[Hasni Mohammad]] and [[Onn Hafiz Ghazi]]), including when PH is in opposition from 2020 to 2022. He also is the current AMANAH Johor deputy chairman.<ref name="SuhaizanYakin">{{cite web |last1=Alhadjri |first1=Alyaa |title=Suhaizan yakin menang di Pulai, tapi bimbang majoriti merosot |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/677631 |website=Malaysiakini |access-date=9 September 2023 |language=MS |date=1 September 2023}}</ref> This is Suhaizan's seventh election as a candidate, having lost his previous six attempts.<ref name="SuhaizanYakin"/> Suhaizan's campaign includes introducing more 'Rahmah Sales' affordable necessities locations, a project initiated by his mentor Salahuddin.<ref name="SuhaizanRahmah">{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=By-election: Suhaizan proposes more Rahmah sales locations |url=https://theedgemalaysia.com/node/681581 |website=The Edge Malaysia |access-date=9 September 2023 |date=7 September 2023}}</ref> His campaign also targets increasing job opportunities, and providing more recreational and sports facilities in the area if he wins the election.<ref name="SuhaizanRahmah"/>

Zulkifli, from [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU), were announced as PN's candidate by PN chairman [[Muhyiddin Yassin]] on 23 August.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Xiang Yun |first1=Yee |title=Perikatan to field Zulkifli Jaafar for Pulai by-election, Dr Mohd Mazri Yahya for Simpang Jeram |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/08/23/perikatan-to-field-zulkifli-jaafar-for-pulai-by-election-dr-mohd-mazri-yahya-for-simpang-jeram |website=The Star |access-date=8 September 2023 |language=en |date=23 August 2023}}</ref> An entrepreneur based in Tampoi by trade who were listed in directorship at six different companies, he is the BERSATU deputy chief for Pulai branch.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Mohamed Farid Noh |author2=Omar Ahmad |title=Kelebihan Zulkifli calon anak jati di Parlimen Pulai |url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2023/08/1145106/kelebihan-zulkifli-calon-anak-jati-di-parlimen-pulai |website=Berita Harian |access-date=9 September 2023 |language=MS |date=26 August 2023}}</ref> In his maiden campaign as candidate, Zulkifli is focused on helping to restore economic balance.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tan |first1=Ben |title=Pulai's Perikatan candidate says won't promise ‘the moon and the stars’ but will prioritise economic balance |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/08/29/pulais-perikatan-candidate-says-wont-promise-the-moon-and-the-stars-but-will-prioritise-economic-balance/87986 |website=Malay Mail |access-date=9 September 2023 |language=en |date=29 August 2023}}</ref>

Samsudin announced his candidacy as independent on 22 August.<ref name='SamsudinIndependent'>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Pulai by-election: Samsudin to stand as independent candidate |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/08/22/pulai-by-election-samsudin-to-stand-as-independent-candidate |website=The Star |access-date=9 September 2023 |language=en |date=22 August 2023}}</ref> He is the president of the Kuala Lumpur Consumer Safety Association (PKPKL), a non governmental organization (NGO) based in Kuala Lumpur, and aims to bring the voice of NGO to Parliament if elected.<ref name='SamsudinIndependent'/> Samsudin has contested before in the Putrajaya federal seat at the last general election in 2022, losing his deposit. Samsudin claims most people see him as an outsider as he was based in Kuala Lumpur, even though he were born in the state, at Kluang.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Tan |first1=Ben |title=Independent in Pulai says being outsider not hurdle to representing constituents |url=https://malaysia.news.yahoo.com/independent-pulai-says-being-outsider-123310213.html |website=Malay Mail via Yahoo News |access-date=9 September 2023 |language=en-MY |date=29 August 2023}}</ref> For his campaign, Samsudin has been assisted by a group of NGOs based in Johor Bahru, and introduced his manifesto to tackle poverty in the area.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Nordin |first1=Remar |title=Independent Samsudin Mohamad ropes in NGOs to help win Pulai by-election |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/09/04/independent-samsudin-mohamad-ropes-in-ngos-to-help-win-pulai-by-election |website=The Star |access-date=9 September 2023 |language=en |date=4 September 2023}}</ref>

Notably, this was the first federal election in Johor without candidates from [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN), traditionally the dominant party in the state and the current Johor government party. After formation of federal government consisting of the alliance between PH and BN in the aftermath of the [[2022 Malaysian general election|general election in November 2022]], both Pulai and Simpang Jeram by-election saw the first collaboration between BN and PH at Johor state level, as BN acceded to PH to put its candidate there,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Abu Yamin |first1=Essa |title=BN beri laluan AMANAH tanding di Parlimen Pulai, DUN Simpang Jeram |url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2023/08/1135522/bn-beri-laluan-amanah-tanding-di-parlimen-pulai-dun-simpang-jeram |website=Berita Harian |access-date=8 September 2023 |language=MS |date=3 August 2023}}</ref> as well as campaigning together.<ref>{{cite web |author1=[[Bernama]] |title=Pulai, Simpang Jeram by-elections, test for PH-BN pact |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/08/26/pulai-simpang-jeram-by-elections-test-for-ph-bn-pact/87428 |website=Malay Mail |access-date=8 September 2023 |language=en |date=26 August 2023}}</ref>

Think tank Nusantara Academy for Strategic Research data analyst Azlan Musyabri suggests that PN could win both seats, if the voter turnout trend is the same as the 2022 state election, but also suggested PH would retain both seats if BN voters support the PH candidate as well as increase of support from non-Malay voters from the 2022 election.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Kasinathan |first1=Shathana |title=Analysis suggests Perikatan has chance to win Pulai, Simpang Jeram by-elections if voter turnout same as Johor state polls |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/09/05/analysis-suggests-perikatan-has-chance-to-win-pulai-simpang-jeram-by-elections-if-voter-turnout-same-as-johor-state-polls/89242 |website=Malay Mail |access-date=8 September 2023 |language=en |date=5 September 2023}}</ref> Bridget Welsh, another political analyst based in Kuala Lumpur, predicted the result will hinge on either voters respect for Salahuddin legacy, or voters response to BN and UMNO president [[Zahid Hamidi]] discharge not amounting to an acquittal (DNAA) of his 47 court cases just days before the election.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Welsh |first1=Bridget |title=Changing tide? A look at Pulai and Simpang Jeram - Bridget Welsh |url=https://bridgetwelsh.com/articles/changing-tide-a-look-at-pulai-and-simpang-jeram/ |website=Bridget Welsh {{!}} Educator, Scholar and Political Analyst |access-date=8 September 2023 |date=6 September 2023}}</ref> Other analysts, such as Dr Azmi Hassan also from Nusantara Academy, and Dr Ainul Adzellie Hasnul, believe that Zahid's DNAA, and also another UMNO leader [[Bung Mokhtar Radin]] acquittal of his own court case a day after, will not affect PH-BN negatively in the by-elections.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Qistina Sallehuddin |author2=Mandy Leong |author3=Hazween Hassan |title=Zahid's DNAA will not affect outcome of Pulai, Simpang Jeram by-election [UPDATED] |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/09/952783/zahids-dnaa-will-not-affect-outcome-pulai-simpang-jeram-election |website=nst.com.my |access-date=8 September 2023 |date=8 September 2023}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
