A '''by-election''' was held on 4 August 2018 for the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly]] seat of '''[[Sungai Kandis (state constituency)|Sungai Kandis]].'''<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/433334|title=Sungai Kandis by-election on Aug 4|first=Annabelle|last=Lee|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=9 July 2018|accessdate=9 July 2018}}</ref> The seat became vacant after the death of the former assemblyman, [[Shuhaimi Shafiei|Mat Shuhaimi Shafiei]] from [[lymphatic cancer]] on 2 July 2018.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/07/09/sg-kandis-byelection-nomination-on-july-21-polls-on-aug-4/|title=Sg Kandis by-election: Nomination on July 21, polls on Aug 4|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=9 July 2018|accessdate=9 July 2018}}</ref> Mat Shuhaimi was a member of the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]], a component party of the state ruling [[Pakatan Harapan]] coalition. He won his seat after defeating BN, PAS and PRM candidates with a majority of 12,480 votes.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/sg-kandis-byelection-six-years-mohd-zawawi-finds-himself-front-line|title=Sg Kandis by-election: Six years on, Mohd Zawawi finds himself on the front line|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge]]|date=20 July 2018|accessdate=20 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180720165626/http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/sg-kandis-byelection-six-years-mohd-zawawi-finds-himself-front-line|archive-date=20 July 2018|url-status=dead}}</ref> It is the first by-election since the historic [[2018 Malaysian general election|May 2018 general election]] that saw the first-ever change of federal government since independence.

The by-election was won by PKR's Zawawi Mughni amidst a drop in turnout.<ref name="result">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/437413|title=Official: PKR retains Sg Kandis with majority of 5,842 votes|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=4 August 2018|accessdate=4 August 2018}}</ref>

== Background ==
The Sungai Kandis constituency contained the polling districts of Seksyen 34 Shah Alam, Sungai Kandis, Teluk Menegun, LLN Connought Bridge, Kampung Pandan, Kampung Jawa, Kota Raja, Taman Seri Andalas 1, Taman Seri Andalas 2, Taman Klang Jaya 1, Sentosa Dato Yusof Shahbuddin, Bukit Jati, Taman Seri Andalas 3, Taman Desawan, Sentosa Dato Abdul Hamid 1, Sentosa Dato Dagang, Jalan Raya Timur, Taman Klang Jaya 2, Sentosa Dato Abdul Hamid 2, Taman Maznah, Taman Menara Maju.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.federalgazette.agc.gov.my/outputp/pub_20160429_P.U.(B)197.pdf|title=Federal Government Gazette - Notice of Polling Districts and Polling Centres for the Federal Constituencies and State Constituencies of the States of Malaya [P.U. (B) 197/2016]|date=29 April 2016|publisher=Attorney General's Chambers of Malaysia|accessdate=2016-06-19|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190328131558/http://www.federalgazette.agc.gov.my/outputp/pub_20160429_P.U.(B)197.pdf|archive-date=28 March 2019|url-status=dead}}</ref> It encompassed the southern part of [[Shah Alam]] and part of [[Klang (city)|Klang City]] around [[Jalan Kebun]].

The electoral roll to be used for this by-election was from the first quarter of 2018, last updated on 2 July 2018. The electoral roll contained 51,230 registered voters comprising 51,217 ordinary voters and 13 overseas voters. There was no early voters in the by-election, while the overseas and domestic postal voting applications was opened from 8 to 21 July. The Tanjung Hall of the [[Shah Alam City Council]] was used as the nomination centre and the official vote tallying centre.<ref name="bernama">{{cite web|url=https://www.bernama.com/en/news.php?id=1478580|title=Sg Kandis by-election: nomination day on July 21, polling day on Aug 4|publisher=[[Bernama]]|date=9 July 2018|accessdate=22 July 2018}}</ref>

It was estimated that the by-election would cost RM1.4 million. The EC also made several improvements for this by-election, including setting up two polling lanes for voters aged 60 and above, and adding more polling lanes at polling centres that had many voters. A total of 21 new lanes were created to avoid overcrowding of the voting lanes, making it a total of 109 lanes compared to 88 during the [[2018 Malaysian general election]].<ref name="bernama"/>

The polling hours was extended from 8 am to 5.30 pm instead of 5pm in the general election. Any non-governmental organisations meeting the conditions were also allowed to observe the election process from the nomination day until the election results were announced.<ref name="bernama"/>

==Nomination==
The nomination day for the elections was on 21 July 2018.<ref name="candidates">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/435232|title=Three-cornered fight in Sg Kandis by-election|first=Alyaa|last=Alhadjri|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=21 July 2018|accessdate=21 July 2018}}</ref> The nomination for the by-election begun at 9am at Tanjung Hall in Section 19.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/07/21/sungai-kandis-by-election-nomination-day/|title=Sg Kandis by-election: Nomination day|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=21 July 2018|accessdate=22 July 2018}}</ref> As initially expected, the by-election was a straight contest between the Pakatan Harapan and [[Barisan Nasional]], with [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]] bowing out to make way for BN.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/434962|title=Sungai Kandis: PAS bows out, clear path for BN-Harapan clash|first=Shakira|last=Buang|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=19 July 2018|accessdate=20 July 2018}}</ref> Three candidates were contesting the seat. PKR chose Zawawi Mughni as their candidate for the by-election.<ref name="PKRcand">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/435036|title=PKR picks religious teacher to contest Sg Kandis seat|first=Chee Hong|last=Lum|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=19 July 2018|accessdate=20 July 2018}}</ref> Barisan Nasional chose Lokman Noor Adam, an [[UMNO]] Supreme Council member as their candidate.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/s/1654319/umno-names-lokman-adam-as-sungai-kandis-candidate|title=Umno names Lokman Adam as Sungai Kandis candidate|first=Syed|last=Jaymal Zahiid|publisher=Malay Mail Online|date=20 July 2018|accessdate=20 July 2018}}</ref> Independent Murthy Krishnasamy was the third candidate for the by-election.<ref name="candidates"/>

==Campaign==

The Pakatan Harapan candidate used the [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat|PKR]] logo as part of their campaign instead of the Pakatan Harapan logo as this logo was more recognised by the electorate of this constituency.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/07/07/ph-to-use-pkr-logo-in-sungai-kandis-byelection/|title=PH to use PKR logo in Sungai Kandis by-election|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=7 July 2018|accessdate=21 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2018/07/13/ph-stays-with-pkr-logo-for-sg-kandis-by-election/|title=PH stays with PKR logo for Sg Kandis by-election|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=13 July 2018|accessdate=22 July 2018}}</ref>
