The 14th Johor State election was held on 9 May 2018, concurrently with the [[2018 Malaysian general election]]. The previous state election was held on 5 May 2013. The state assemblymen is elected to 5 years term each.

The [[Johor State Legislative Assembly]] would automatically dissolve on 20 June 2018, the fifth anniversary of the first sitting, and elections must be held within sixty days (two months) of the dissolution (on or before 20 August 2018, with the date to be decided by the Election Commission), unless dissolved prior to that date by the Head of State ([[Sultan of Johor]]) on the advice of the Head of Government ([[List of Menteris Besar of Johor|Menteri Besar of Johor]]).

The results of the election saw a historic win for [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH), winning 36 seats (a majority but 1 seat short of [[supermajority]]) and ousting [[Barisan Nasional]] from the state government, the first time since the first Johor state elections in 1955 that BN or its predecessor [[Alliance Party (Malaysia)|Alliance]] were defeated.
BERSATU's [[Osman Sapian]] was sworn in as Menteri Besar on 12 May 2018,<ref>{{cite web |author1=Zazali Musa |title=Osman Sapian is the new Johor MB, exco to be sworn in on May 15 (updated) |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/05/12/osman-sapian-is-the-new-johor-mb/ |publisher=The Star (Malaysia) |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=12 May 2018}}</ref> while the state EXCO members were sworn in on 16 May 2018.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Ben Tan |title=Johor’s 10-member exco sworn in |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2018/05/16/johors-10-member-exco-sworn-in/1631494 |publisher=Malay Mail |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=16 May 2018}}</ref>

==Contenders==

[[Barisan Nasional|Barisan Nasional (BN)]] is set to contest all 56 seats in [[Johor State Legislative Assembly]]. [[Barisan Nasional|Barisan Nasional (BN)]] linchpin party [[United Malays National Organisation|United Malays National Organisation (UNMO)]] is to set to contest major share of [[Barisan Nasional|Barisan Nasional (BN)]] seats.

[[Pakatan Harapan]] have decided to contest all 56 seats in [[Johor State Legislative Assembly|Johor]]. [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (Bersatu) will contest in 18 seats while the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) will have 14 seats. [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) and the [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|National Trust Party]] (Amanah) will contest 12 seats each.<ref>[https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/409412 Analisis agihan kerusi HARAPAN di Johor]</ref>

[[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) will compete for 40 seats.<ref>[http://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/pas-johor-bakal-tanding-17-kerusi-parlimen-40-dun-144921 Pas Johor bakal tanding 17 kerusi Parlimen, 40 DUN]</ref>

==Aftermath==
The results in Johor were seen as shocking to many, since the state was the birthplace of UMNO, and were viewed as 'fortress' with no defeats by BN or Alliance since 1955, the start of Johor state elections.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Tashny Sukumaran |title=Malaysian election: Malay fortress Johor falls to Pakatan Harapan as ‘warlords are toppled by kids’ |url=https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/politics/article/2145441/malaysian-election-malay-fortress-johor-falls-pakatan-harapan |website=scmp.com |publisher=[[South China Morning Post]] |access-date=30 December 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180906161739/https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/politics/article/2145441/malaysian-election-malay-fortress-johor-falls-pakatan-harapan |archive-date=6 September 2018 |date=10 May 2018}}</ref>
{{See also|2020 Malaysian political crisis#Johor|2022 Johor state election}}
Osman had only led the state government for 11 months, before resigning as Menteri Besar in April 2019.<ref>{{cite web |title=Osman Sapian resigns as Johor Menteri Besar: Mahathir |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/osman-sapian-has-tendered-resignation-as-johor-menteri-besar-mahathir-says |publisher=The Straits Times |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=9 April 2019}}</ref> He were replaced by another BERSATU MLA, [[Sahruddin Jamal]], as Menteri Besar, who then led the state government for another 11 months, until the wake of 2020 Malaysian political crisis, which saw the exit of BERSATU and most of its MLAs from PH, which resulted in Sahruddin's resignation.

A new state government formed under a coalition of BN and [[Perikatan Nasional]] (BERSATU and PAS), with BN's [[Hasni Mohammad]] sworn in as Menteri Besar in February 2020. That government, in turn, lasted another 23 months until the death of Osman in December 2021 that reduced the majority of the government into a [[minority government]], which subsequently resulted in a snap election being called by Hasni in January 2022 and took place in March 2022.
