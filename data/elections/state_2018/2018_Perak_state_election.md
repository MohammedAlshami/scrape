The 14th Perak State election was held on 9 May 2018. The previous state election was held on 5 May 2013. Each of the state assemblymen are elected to a five-year term each.

The [[Perak State Legislative Assembly]] automatically dissolved on 28 June 2018, the fifth anniversary of the first sitting, and elections were required to be held within sixty days (two months) of the dissolution (on or before 28 August 2018, with the date to be decided by the Election Commission), unless dissolved prior to that date by the Head of State ([[Sultan of Perak]]) on the advice of the Head of Government ([[List of Menteris Besar of Perak|Menteri Besar of Perak]]).

[[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) gained a [[Plurality (voting)|plurality]] in the election, winning 29 seats but still short of 1 seat for a simple majority win,<ref>{{cite web |title=Malaysia GE: Formation of state govt in Perak Kedah and Sabah up to parties, says election commission |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysia-election-formation-of-state-govt-in-perak-kedah-and-sabah-up-to-parties-says |publisher=The Straits Times |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=10 May 2018}}</ref> until 2 MLAs from [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) quit their parties to support PH, enabling it to form the state government.<ref>{{cite web |title=Perak BN accepts defeat |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2018/05/13/perak-bn-accepts-defeat/ |website=BorneoPostOnline |publisher=The Borneo Post |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=13 May 2018}}</ref> [[Ahmad Faizal Azumu]], from [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party|BERSATU]], was sworn in as Menteri Besar on 12 May 2018, while the state EXCO members were sworn in on 19 May 2018.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Ivan Loh |title=Perak state exco sworn in |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/05/19/perak-state-exco-sworn-in/ |work=The Star (Malaysia) |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=19 May 2018}}</ref>

==Contenders==
[[Barisan Nasional|Barisan Nasional (BN)]] contested all 59 seats in [[Perak State Legislative Assembly]]. [[Barisan Nasional|Barisan Nasional (BN)]] linchpin party [[United Malays National Organisation|United Malays National Organisation (UNMO)]] contested a major share of [[Barisan Nasional|Barisan Nasional (BN)]] seats.

[[Pakatan Harapan]] also contested all 59 seats in [[Perak State Legislative Assembly|Perak]] with [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) was largest contested 18 seats while [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|National Trust Party]] (Amanah) contested 13 seats and both [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) and the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (Bersatu) contested 14 seats each.<ref>[https://www.facebook.com/permalink.php?story_fbid=1989214024422861&id=1644910628853204 AGIHAN KERUSI DEWAN UNDANGAN NEGERI PERAK: DAP MENDOMINASI PAKATAN HARAPAN PERAK, AMANAH PULA DI TEMPAT TERCOROT]</ref> [[Pakatan Harapan]] will use PKR logo as its logo for election.

[[Gagasan Sejahtera]] contested all 56 seats in [[Perak State Legislative Assembly|Perak]]. [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) competed for 43 seats. The remaining 16 seats were distributed to [[Love Malaysia Party|Parti Cinta Malaysia]] (PCM).<ref>[https://www.hmetro.com.my/mutakhir/2018/03/321217/pas-perak-tandingi-43-kerusi-dun PAS Perak tandingi 43 kerusi DUN]</ref>

[[Socialist Party of Malaysia|Parti Sosialis Malaysia]] (PSM) will contest 5 seats in [[Buntong (state constituency)|Buntong]], [[Jelapang (state constituency)|Jelapang]], [[Menglembu (state constituency)|Menglembu]], [[Tronoh (state constituency)|Tronoh]] and [[Malim Nawar (state constituency)|Malim Nawar]].<ref>[https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/411800 PSM umum calon 5 kerusi parlimen, 12 DUN]</ref>

==Aftermath==
Two MLAs from BN who stated their support for PH, [[Zainol Fadzi Paharudin]] and [[Nolee Ashilin Mohammed Radzi]], despite their insistence they are not quitting the party for supporting PH, were automatically sacked by UMNO and BN on 14 May 2018.<ref>{{cite web |author1=John Bunyan |title=Perak rep sacked from Umno says not joining Pakatan |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2018/05/15/perak-rep-sacked-from-umno-says-not-joining-pakatan/1631045 |publisher=Malay Mail |access-date=30 December 2022 |date=15 May 2018}}</ref> They both later joined Bersatu.

{{See also|2020 Malaysian political crisis#Perak}}
The Pakatan Harapan state government led by Ahmad Faizal only lasts 22 months, when in the wake of 2020 Malaysian political crisis and exit of Bersatu MLAs from PH resulted in a new state government under a coalition between BN and [[Perikatan Nasional]] (Bersatu and PAS) in March 2020, with Ahmad Faizal re-appointed as Menteri Besar. That government, in turn only lasted another 9 months before Ahmad Faizal [[2020 vote of no confidence in the Faizal Azumu ministry|lost a vote of confidence motion tabled by BN]], resulting in his resignation and another new government formed and led by BN's [[Saarani Mohammad]] in December 2020.
