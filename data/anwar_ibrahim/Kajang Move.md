# Kajang Move

*Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kajang Move*

---

{{Short description|2014 Malaysian political crisis}}
{{Kajang Move |expanded=all}}

The "'''Kajang Move'''" ({{langx|ms|Langkah Kajang, also known as Selangor's political crisis, Selangor's MB crisis or Khalid Move}}) was a political manoeuvre attempted in Malaysia in 2014. The manoeuvre sought to topple the 14th [[Menteri Besar of Selangor]] in [[Selangor]], [[Menteri Besar]]  [[Khalid Ibrahim]], and install the national opposition leader [[Anwar Ibrahim]] as his replacement. The move was initiated by [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|Parti Keadilan Rakyat (PKR)]] Strategy Director, then Vice President cum Secretary General, [[Rafizi Ramli]]. The attempt resulted in a nine-month political crisis within the state of Selangor and the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] coalition, that also involved [[Sultan of Selangor|the palace of Selangor]]. The crisis concluded with the appointment of PKR's Deputy President, [[Azmin Ali]], as the 15th Menteri Besar of Selangor.

==Introduction==
[[Khalid Ibrahim]] had been the Menteri Besar of Selangor, Malaysia's wealthiest and most populous state, since the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] coalition consisting of PKR, the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) and the [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) won a majority in the Selangor State Assembly in the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 election]]. Pakatan Rakyat, led by PKR's [[Anwar Ibrahim]] were in opposition at a federal level; the [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition had governed Malaysia since its independence.

Rafizi Ramli, in a blog post to justify the "Kajang Move", stated that the manoeuvre would be the "game changer" in the quest of the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] to win government nationally.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://rafiziramli.com/2014/01/the-kajang-move-game-changer-in-putrajaya-quest/|title=The Kajang Move: Game Changer in Putrajaya Quest|work=Rafizi Ramli|date=29 January 2014|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> It was triggered by the signing of a water deal between the Selangor state government, headed by Khalid, and the federal government. Anwar and his allies within PKR disapproved of the deal; many of the party's leaders had not been informed of it in advance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.malaysia-today.net/the-kajang-move-explained/|title=The Kajang Move explained|work=Malaysia Today|access-date=11 December 2014|archive-date=30 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141230092735/http://www.malaysia-today.net/the-kajang-move-explained/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Khalid Ibrahim sacked from PKR===
On 22 July, it was claimed that PKR president [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail|Datuk Seri Dr Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]] had received the official endorsement from party leaders to replace Khalid the Selangor Menteri Besar. On 4 August, Party secretary-general Datuk [[Saifuddin Nasution Ismail]] released a 40-page document highlighting some court cases which questioned Khalid's personal integrity. According to Saifuddin, the court cases involved an out of court settlement with [[Bank Islam]] over the RM66.67mil Kumpulan [[Guthrie (company)|Guthrie]] Bhd's shares when Khalid was the group's chief executive officer.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysiantimes.com.my/pkr-to-release-expose-on-khalids-hanky-panky/|title=PKR to 'expose' Khalid's hanky-panky - The Malaysian Times|publisher=Themalaysiantimes.com.my|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> Khalid however, hit back by forwarding the issue to [[Malaysian Anti-Corruption Commission]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/1134021|title=Khalid hits back|publisher=Thesunday.my|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nst.com.my/node/16020|title=Wan Azizah next Selangor MB?|work=NST Online|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> On 9 August, Abdul Khalid was sacked from PKR, as announced by the party's disciplinary board during a press conference at the party's headquarters. Abdul Khalid said that despite the sacking being “flawed and illegal”, he accepted it.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2014/08/12/timeline-kajang-move-khalid-move/#ixzz3FBmXUJXT|title=Timeline: From 'Kajang Move' to 'Khalid Move' - The Rakyat Post|work=The Rakyat Post|access-date=11 December 2014|archive-date=4 March 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304124917/http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2014/08/12/timeline-kajang-move-khalid-move/#ixzz3FBmXUJXT|url-status=dead}}</ref>

===Conflict over legislative support===
<!-- Deleted image removed: [[File:Wan Azizah 30 assemblymen.jpg|right|thumbnail|250px|[[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]] during press conference claiming she is the one that commence major support]] -->
In a special media conference made by Khalid on 11 August 2014, he announced that the [[Sharafuddin of Selangor|Sultan of Selangor]] had given his consent for him to continue as the Menteri Besar of Selangor and claimed that he still maintained the majority support. PKR and DAP later asked Khalid to prove his claim.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/08/11/khalid-perlu-buktikan-dapat-sokongan-majoriti-daripada-dun-gesa-pkr-dan-dap/|title=Khalid perlu buktikan dapat sokongan majoriti daripada DUN, gesa PKR dan DAP|work=Free Malaysia Today|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> Khalid also suggested that state executive councillors (Excos) who did not support his leadership should step down. He also said that he would meet the Excos individually to know their stand on his leadership. Four Excos from PAS state that they would continue to serve under the administration of Abdul Khalid as directed by their party leadership while six Excos from DAP and PKR were discharged by Khalid.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kwiknews.my/news/from-kajang-move-to-selangor-mb-crisis-chronology-of-events|title=From Kajang Move to Selangor MB crisis: Chronology of events|publisher=Kwiknews.my|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> On 14 August, Wan Azizah claimed that most Selangor assembly-members supported her.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.abnxcess.com/2014/08/sgor-mb-crisis-wan-azizah-claims-30-assemblymen-support-her-inc-two-pas-reps/|title=S'gor MB crisis: Wan Azizah claims 30 assemblymen support her inc. two PAS reps ABN News - Eng|work=ABN News - Eng|access-date=11 December 2014|archive-date=4 October 2014|archive-url=https://archive.today/20141004152910/http://news.abnxcess.com/2014/08/sgor-mb-crisis-wan-azizah-claims-30-assemblymen-support-her-inc-two-pas-reps/|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.malaysia-today.net/pkr-reiterates-wan-azizah-has-majority-support-and-should-be-mb/|title=PKR reiterates Wan Azizah has majority support and should be MB|work=Malaysia Today|access-date=11 December 2014|archive-date=29 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141229031617/http://www.malaysia-today.net/pkr-reiterates-wan-azizah-has-majority-support-and-should-be-mb/|url-status=dead}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
Many analysts viewed the Kajang Move as a failure. [[Wan Saiful Wan Jan]], head of the think tank [[Institute for Democracy and Economic Affairs]] (Ideas) deemed the move a "failure" as Pakatan Rakyat failed to install their preferred candidates as Menteri Besar. Awang Selamat, the pseudonym for [[Utusan Malaysia]]'s editors expressed similar sentiments.<ref name="Utusan">{{cite web|url=http://www.utusan.com.my/utusan/Rencana/20140928/re_04/ANALISIS-MINGGUAN:-BISIK-BISIK-AWANG-SELAMAT|title=ANALISIS MINGGUAN: BISIK-BISIK AWANG SELAMAT|publisher=Utusan.com.my|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> Dr Oh Ei Sun, of Singapore's [[S. Rajaratnam School of International Studies]], rated the Kajang Move a paltry success rate of 33%, saying it only succeeded in removing Khalid, and that too after a long impasse. The head of the International Relations and Politics department in [[Universiti Malaysia Sarawak]], Dr Arnold Puyok, said the Kajang Move had taken a toll on both Pakatan Rakyat and the Selangor people. Independent analyst Khoo Kay Peng, on the other hand, said the architect of the Kajang Move, PKR vice-president Rafizi Ramli, would still insist the strategy's success in removing Khalid.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/with-azmin-as-mb-the-failure-of-pkrs-kajang-move-say-analysts#sthash.ukVgAB9Z.dpuf |title=With Azmin as MB, the failure of PKR's Kajang Move, say analysts |publisher=Themalaysianinsider.com |access-date=11 December 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140925073208/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/with-azmin-as-mb-the-failure-of-pkrs-kajang-move-say-analysts |archive-date=25 September 2014 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/no-clear-winner-in-selangor-mb-crisis|title=No clear winner in Selangor MB crisis|publisher=Themalaysianinsider.com|access-date=11 December 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006123222/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/no-clear-winner-in-selangor-mb-crisis|archive-date=6 October 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> Bridget Welsh, Associate Professor of Political Science at [[Singapore Management University]] described the move as "childish in-fighting".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2014/11/01/the-economist-anwar-no-longer-crucial/|title=The Economist: Anwar no longer crucial|work=Free Malaysia Today|access-date=11 December 2014}}</ref> [[Monash University]] political scientist Prof James Chin said the Kajang Move to remove Khalid failed as bickering among the PR leaders continued.
