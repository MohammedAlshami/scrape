The '''Kuala Lumpur Peace Accord''' (officially the '''Joint Declaration by the Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Cambodia and the Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Thailand on the Outcomes of Their Meeting in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia''') was a [[peace agreement]] signed on 26 October 2025, on the sidelines of the [[2025 ASEAN Summits#47th ASEAN Summit|47th ASEAN Summit]] at the [[Kuala Lumpur Convention Centre]] in [[Kuala Lumpur]], [[Malaysia]].

The accord was reached in the aftermath of an escalating [[2025 Cambodian–Thai border crisis|border crisis]] along the [[Cambodia–Thailand border]], which had seen armed clashes, [[landmine]] incidents, and heightened military deployments on both sides. The crisis raised regional and international concerns about stability in [[Southeast Asia]] and underscored the urgency of diplomatic intervention.

In the declaration, Cambodian prime minister [[Hun Manet]] and Thai prime minister [[Anutin Charnvirakul]] affirmed their "unwavering commitment to peace and security between our two countries" and reiterated a pledge to "refrain from the threat or use of force, and to seek peaceful settlement of disputes."<ref name="Cambodia-Thailand joint peace declaration">{{cite press release|url=https://image.mfa.go.th/mfa/0/mkKfL2iULZ/joint_declaration_261025/Thailand_Cambodia_Joint_Declaration.pdf|title=Joint Declaration by the Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Cambodia and the Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Thailand on the Outcomes of Their Meeting in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia|via=Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Thailand|date=26 October 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030064057/https://image.mfa.go.th/mfa/0/mkKfL2iULZ/joint_declaration_261025/Thailand_Cambodia_Joint_Declaration.pdf|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

The accord also outlined several concrete measures aimed at stabilizing the Cambodia–Thailand border region, including the establishment of an [[ASEAN Observer Team]] (AOT) to monitor the implementation of a [[ceasefire]], the withdrawal of heavy weapons from border areas under observation, joint humanitarian [[demining]] operations, the restoration of [[confidence-building measures]] and diplomatic relations, and the prompt release of [[prisoners of war]] (POWs) by [[Thailand]] as a demonstration of goodwill.<ref name="Cambodia-Thailand joint peace declaration"/>

The declaration was witnessed by Malaysian prime minister [[Anwar Ibrahim]] and United States president [[Donald Trump]], underscoring regional and international support for the agreement. The accord marked a critical step toward de-escalating the border crisis and promoting long-term peace and stability between Thailand and Cambodia. However, clashes resumed between the two countries just two months later on 8 December.

== Background ==
[[File:Leaders of Malaysia US Thailand Cambodia Oct 2025.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Malaysian prime minister [[Anwar Ibrahim]], U.S. president [[Donald Trump]], Thai prime minister [[Anutin Charnvirakul]] and Cambodian prime minister [[Hun Manet]] after the signing of the Kuala Lumpur Peace Accords, 26 October 2025.]]
{{Anwar Ibrahim sidebar}}Tensions along the [[Cambodia–Thailand border]] [[2025 Cambodian–Thai border crisis|escalated sharply in July 2025]], resulting in the heaviest fighting between the two countries in over a decade. The conflict involved heavy artillery exchanges and airstrikes, which displaced an estimated 130,000 people and caused at least three dozen civilian deaths.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/thailand-cambodia-exchange-heavy-artillery-fire-border-battle-expands-2025-07-25/|title=Thailand and Cambodia exchange heavy artillery fire as border battle expands|last1=Naing|first1=Shoon|last2=Pookasook|first2=Artorn|work=Reuters|date=25 July 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030064346/https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/thailand-cambodia-exchange-heavy-artillery-fire-border-battle-expands-2025-07-25/|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="ceasefire CAM-THA">{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/china/ceasefire-takes-effect-between-thailand-cambodia-after-five-day-border-battle-2025-07-28/|title=Ceasefire takes effect between Thailand and Cambodia after five-day border battle|last1=Setboonsarng|first1=Chayut|last2=Azhar|first2=Danial|last3=Naing|first3=Shoon|work=Reuters|date=29 July 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030064628/https://www.reuters.com/world/china/ceasefire-takes-effect-between-thailand-cambodia-after-five-day-border-battle-2025-07-28/|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

After several days of hostilities, Thailand and Cambodia agreed to an immediate ceasefire on 28 July 2025 during emergency talks in [[Putrajaya]], Malaysia.<ref name="ceasefire CAM-THA"/> This truce, sometimes referred to as the Putrajaya Agreement, was brokered with the support of [[ASEAN]], Malaysia (then serving as ASEAN chair), and international representatives from the [[United States]] and [[China]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/guns-fall-silent-thai-cambodia-border-commanders-seek-uphold-truce-2025-07-29/|title=Guns fall silent on Thai-Cambodia border as commanders seek to uphold truce|last1=Wongcha-um|first1=Panu|last2=Thepgumpanat|first2=Panarat|work=Reuters|date=29 July 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030064843/https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/guns-fall-silent-thai-cambodia-border-commanders-seek-uphold-truce-2025-07-29/|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

Following the ceasefire, the two countries convened a General Border Committee (GBC) and a Regional Border Committee (RBC) to consolidate the truce, restore communication channels, and address ongoing security and humanitarian issues along the border.<ref name="CM-TH joint peace">{{cite press release|url=https://www.mfa.go.th/en/content/joint-press-statement-special-gbc-100925|title=Joint Press Statement of the Special General Border Committee (GBC) Meeting, Koh Kong, Cambodia|work=Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Thailand|date=10 September 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030065037/https://www.mfa.go.th/en/content/joint-press-statement-special-gbc-100925|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nationthailand.com/news/asean/40054588|title=Second Army outlines 11-point RBC, Cambodia defers demining and scams to GBC|work=The Nation (Thailand)|date=27 August 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030065329/https://www.nationthailand.com/news/asean/40054588|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

The [[Secretary-General of the United Nations|United Nations secretary-general]], [[António Guterres]], welcomed the ceasefire and later commended ASEAN's ongoing mediation efforts to maintain peace and stability in the border region.<ref>{{cite press release|url=https://press.un.org/en/2025/sgsm22746.doc.htm|title=Secretary-General Welcomes Ceasefire between Cambodia, Thailand|work=United Nations Meetings Coverage and Press Releases|date=28 July 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030065501/https://press.un.org/en/2025/sgsm22746.doc.htm|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

The accord followed months of technical consultations between the [[Ministry of National Defence (Cambodia)|Cambodian]] and [[Ministry of Defence (Thailand)|Thai defence ministries]] regarding verification procedures for an observer mission and the modalities of troop withdrawal.<ref name="GBC meeting"/><ref>{{cite press release|url=https://www.mfa.go.th/en/content/press-briefing-thailand-cambodia-situ-19-sep-25-en?menu=5d5bd3cb15e39c306002a9b0&page=5d5bd3da15e39c306002aaf9|title=Summary of Press Briefing on the Thailand – Cambodia Border Situation|via=Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Thailand|date=19 September 2025|access-date=26 October 2025|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20251030072605/https://www.mfa.go.th/en/content/press-briefing-thailand-cambodia-situ-19-sep-25-en?menu=5d5bd3cb15e39c306002a9b0&page=5d5bd3da15e39c306002aaf9|archive-date=30 October 2025|url-status=live}}</ref>

== Terms of the accord ==
The Joint Declaration included political, military, and humanitarian commitments:<ref name="Cambodia-Thailand joint peace declaration"/>
* '''Principles of peace and non-use of force:''' Both sides reaffirmed their adherence to the principles of the [[Charter of the United Nations|UN Charter]] and the [[ASEAN Charter]], undertaking to resolve disputes peacefully and respect existing boundaries.
* '''Implementation of prior border agreements:''' The parties reconfirmed the validity of mechanisms such as the General Border Committee, the Regional Border Committee, and the Joint Boundary Commission as frameworks for dispute settlement.
* '''[[ASEAN Observer Team]] (AOT):''' A ''Terms of Reference'' for an ASEAN Observer Team was signed concurrently with the declaration. The AOT, composed of personnel from ASEAN member states, is mandated to "ensure the full and effective implementation of the ceasefire" and to report its findings to ASEAN.
* '''Military de-escalation:''' Under AOT supervision, both sides agreed to withdraw heavy and destructive weapons from border areas and return them to regular bases.  Military liaison teams were tasked with drafting a detailed action plan and timeline.
* '''Information restraint:''' The parties committed to avoid "[[false information]], accusations, allegations, and harmful rhetoric," whether through official or unofficial channels, in order to reduce tensions.
* '''Confidence-building and diplomatic restoration:''' Immediate implementation of joint civil-military programmes and border coordination was mandated, with a stated goal of restoring full [[Diplomacy|diplomatic relations]] between the two countries.
* '''Humanitarian de-mining:''' Joint operations are to be conducted to remove landmines and unexploded ordnance "to protect civilian lives and contribute to socio-economic development."
* '''Cessation of hostilities and prisoner release:''' The declaration provides that, upon completion of de-escalation measures, both sides will recognise the cessation of active hostilities. Thailand undertook to "promptly release the prisoners of war" captured during the July conflict as a confidence-building step.
* '''Transnational-crime cooperation:''' The governments agreed to strengthen coordination on issues such as trafficking, illegal arms trade, and smuggling across the border region.

== Peace agreement suspension ==
On November 10, 2025, several Thai soldiers on a routine patrol near the border in Sisaket province were injured from a landmine. This event led Thailand to accuse Cambodia that this was a newly placed landmine and it suspended all progress on the peace agreement until Cambodia proves it will not show hostility. Cambodia denied the accusations and reaffirmed its commitment to the peace accord, but border tensions remain unresolved.<ref>{{Cite web |author1=Kocha Olarn |author2=Helen Regan |date=2025-11-13 |title=Trump’s Thailand-Cambodia peace agreement is falling apart after Cambodian villager killed in fresh clashes |url=https://www.cnn.com/2025/11/12/asia/thailand-cambodia-border-clashes-intl-hnk |access-date=2025-11-13 |website=CNN |language=en}}</ref> On 8 December 2025, Thailand and Cambodia resumed fighting after both sides accused each other of breaking the ceasefire.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Mogul |first=Kocha Olarn, Rhea |date=2025-12-08 |title=Thailand launches airstrikes on Cambodia as Trump’s peace agreement hangs in balance |url=https://www.cnn.com/2025/12/07/asia/thailand-cambodia-border-clashes-december-intl-hnk |access-date=2025-12-09 |website=CNN |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Thailand attacks Cambodia in blow to Trump-backed ceasefire deal |url=https://www.irishtimes.com/world/asia-pacific/2025/12/08/thailand-attacks-cambodia-in-blow-to-trump-backed-ceasefire-deal/ |access-date=2025-12-09 |website=The Irish Times |language=en}}</ref>
