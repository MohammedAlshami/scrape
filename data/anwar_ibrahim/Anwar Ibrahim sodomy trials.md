The '''Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials''' are a source of considerable political controversy in [[Malaysia]]. The first trial was held in 1998, and resulted in former [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|Deputy Prime Minister]] [[Anwar Ibrahim]] being convicted, and given a nine-year prison sentence. This verdict was overturned in 2004, resulting in Anwar's release from prison.

While being the leader of the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] opposition, Anwar was charged in 2008 with [[sodomy|sodomising]] a male aide. He was tried in 2010 and 2011 and, in January 2012, was acquitted. In March 2014, the acquittal was overturned by the [[Court of Appeal of Malaysia|Court of Appeal]], which convicted him and imposed a sentence of five years' imprisonment. Anwar appealed to the [[Federal Court of Malaysia|Federal Court]], which in February 2015 re-affirmed the conviction and sentence. Anwar served the sentence in [[Sungai Buloh Prison]] in [[Selangor]] before being pardoned on 16 May 2018.

==1998 allegation==
===Corruption accusations===
In 1998, a book, [[50 Dalil Mengapa Anwar Tidak Boleh Jadi PM|''50 dalil mengapa Anwar tidak boleh jadi PM'']] ("50 Arguments Why Anwar Shouldn't Be Prime Minister") was circulated among members of the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) General Assembly, containing graphic allegations as well as accusations of corruption against Anwar.<ref>[http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news.cfm?NewsID=37269 Dr M: Up to Anwar to sue over 'gay' remark] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110621232043/http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news.cfm?NewsID=37269 |date=21 June 2011 }}, ''Daily Express'', 22 September 2005.</ref>

===Anwar's sacking from office===
Anwar was fired from the [[Cabinet of Malaysia|Cabinet]] on 2 September 1998, amid police reports that he was under investigation. The following day, he was expelled from UMNO. Munawar Anees, Anwar's former speechwriter, and Sukma Darmawan Sasmita Atmadja, Anwar's adoptive brother, were arrested under suspicion of engaging in homosexual acts. Five days later, they were given a jail sentence of six months after pleading guilty to "unnatural sex" with Anwar. They later recanted their confessions, and appealed the sentence, claiming to have been [[coercion|coerced]] into pleading guilty. Anees made a statutory declaration to that effect.<ref>[https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/59852 Statutory Declaration from Dr. Munawar Ahmad Anees] 7 November 1998</ref> However, Munawar's appeal was rejected by the High Court on 29 October 2008 upholding his guilty sentence.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20081102040105/http://www.nst.com.my/Current_News/NST/Thursday/Frontpage/20081030134210/Article/index_html Dr. Munawar fails in bid to remit appeal to High Court]. nst.com.my. 29 October 2008</ref> Two of Anwar's secretaries, Ezam Mohamad and Mohamad Azmin Ali, were both held separately as part of police investigations into the "50 Arguments" book. Both were later released.

===Black eye incident===
<!-- [[Image:Anwar Ibrahim and police 20020207.jpg|thumb|right|Anwar Ibrahim being escorted by the police to the [[Federal Court of Malaysia]] on 4 February 2002, he was released from prison on 2 September 2004 after his conviction on sodomy was reversed.]] -->
On 29 September 1998, Anwar appeared in court and pleaded innocent to charges of corruption and [[sodomy]]. A photo of Anwar with a [[black eye]] (incurred from a beating by then [[Inspector-general of police|Inspector General of Police]] [[Rahim Noor]]) and one hand raised became a symbol of the political opposition in many ''reformasi'' posters.

The black eye was explained by [[Mahathir Mohamad]] and Rahim Noor as being "self-inflicted" and caused by "pressing a glass over his eyes". Only after a Royal Commission was convened did Rahim Noor admit that he had administered the beating to Anwar.<ref name="Aliran Black Eye">{{cite news |title=Rahim Noor's Confession: Black Eye and Blacker Deeds |work=Aliran |date=1 March 1999 |url=http://www.aliran.com/oldsite/ms990301.html |access-date=30 August 2008| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20080725005620/http://www.aliran.com/oldsite/ms990301.html| archive-date= 25 July 2008 | url-status= live}}</ref><ref name="DAP Black Eye">{{cite news |title=Anwar vindicated but Attorney-General should give full accounting of the compensation government is paying for Rahim Noor's "black-eye" assault |work=Democratic Action Party |date=4 August 2005 |url=http://www.dapmalaysia.org/all-archive/English/2005/Aug05/lks/lks3607.htm |access-date=30 August 2008}}</ref> Rahim apologised for the incident in August 2005.<ref name="BBC Black Eye">{{cite news |title=Anwar wins apology for beating |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/4740937.stm |work=BBC News|date=3 August 2005 |access-date=7 September 2008 }}</ref> Rahim Noor was eventually charged for the assault and was given a two-month prison sentence and fined 2,000 ringgit.<ref>{{cite news |title=Black eye for justice|url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/678217.stm|work=BBC News|date=15 March 2000 |access-date=4 February 2010 }}</ref>

===DNA evidence===
During the trial, a mattress was presented to court, supposedly stained with Anwar's semen. This was submitted as [[DNA profiling|DNA evidence]] of Anwar's sexual acts. Anwar denied having anything to do with the mattress, although the DNA tests came out positive. Lim Kong Boon, a DNA chemist, testified during the trial that DNA taken from 10 of 13 semen stains on the mattress matched Anwar's DNA.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=1998&dt=1231&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Muka_Hadapan&pg=fp_03.htm |title=Saksi: Kesan di tilam milik Anwar, Shamsidar |access-date=21 August 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180820075412/http://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=1998&dt=1231&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Muka_Hadapan&pg=fp_03.htm |archive-date=20 August 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="CBS89">Alvin Ung [https://www.cbsnews.com/news/a-stain-upon-anwars-defense/ CBS-489A Stain Upon Anwar's Defense], ''The Associated Press'' (via ''CBS News''). 30 December 1998</ref> The defense team implied that DNA samples may have been taken from Anwar, while unconscious, after his beating in police custody, to create false forensics evidence to frame Anwar; however, High Court Judge Augustine Paul accepted that DNA evidence.<ref name=CBS89/><ref>[https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/244570.stm Anwar trial hears DNA evidence] 30 December 1998, ''BBC News''</ref>

===Alleged government interference===
During the trial, Mahathir appeared on Malaysian television in a special appearance to explain the arrest of his deputy. This was one of several occasions in which Mahathir declared Anwar guilty of sodomy and homosexual acts, even as the trial still was underway. The government included the statements of the purported victims of Anwar's sodomy attacks, evidence that was widely considered to be tainted.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sodomy II: Hinging on court review |url=http://malaysianmirror.com/homedetail/45-home/28865-sodomy-ii-hinging-on-court-review |date=3 February 2010 |work=Malaysian Mirror |access-date=9 February 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100205110941/http://malaysianmirror.com/homedetail/45-home/28865-sodomy-ii-hinging-on-court-review |archive-date=5 February 2010 }}</ref> Furthermore, the prosecution was unable to accurately decide on a date that the alleged acts of anal sex had occurred—the government originally alleged that the sodomy had occurred inside a building that had not been constructed at the time of the alleged event.{{citation needed|date=October 2014}}

Furthermore, the Attorney General's prosecution team (headed by [[Abdul Gani Patail]]) also submitted a scenario in which Munawar Anees took an overnight flight from Britain all the way to Malaysia to be sodomised by Anwar, and flew back to Britain after that. The infamous "stained mattress" that was taken in and out of court over 20 times throughout the duration of the trial, was supposedly the same one that Anwar did his homosexual acts and had extramarital sex on.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mattress shown in Anwar trial |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/world/asia-pacific/235255.stm|work=BBC News|date=15 December 1998|access-date=17 February 2010}}</ref> However, according to the evidence, the sexual acts had taken place on the mattress a few years ago, and yet the DNA evidence obtained from it were rather recent in age.<ref>{{cite news |title=Anwar's indignity of soiled bedding case rests on bedding|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/anwars-indignity-of-soiled-bedding-case-rests-on-bedding-1191555.html |archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/archive/20220507/https://www.independent.co.uk/news/anwars-indignity-of-soiled-bedding-case-rests-on-bedding-1191555.html |archive-date=7 May 2022 |url-access=subscription |url-status=live|work=The Independent|location=London|date=15 December 1998|access-date=17 February 2010 }}</ref>

===Sentencing===
On 14 April 1999, Anwar was sentenced to six years in prison for corruption and, on 8 August 2000, nine years in prison for sodomy. The sentences were to be served consecutively, and Anwar was given no credit for the six months he spent in jail during the trial. The following year, Anwar's corruption conviction was upheld by Malaysia's Court of Appeal. In July 2002, Anwar lost his final appeal against the corruption conviction in the Federal Court. In September 2004 the Federal Court overturned his sodomy conviction.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.abc.net.au/cgi-bin/common/printfriendly.pl?http://www.abc.net.au/pm/content/2004/s1190999.htm|title=Anwar Ibrahim freed after sodomy sentence overturned|last=Colvin|first=Mark|date=2 September 2004|work=ABC|location=Australia|access-date=31 March 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110203172614/http://www.abc.net.au/cgi-bin/common/printfriendly.pl?http%3A%2F%2Fwww.abc.net.au%2Fpm%2Fcontent%2F2004%2Fs1190999.htm|archive-date=3 February 2011|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

Both [[Amnesty International]] and [[Human Rights Watch]] expressed doubts about the fairness of the trials.<ref>[https://www.amnesty.org/en/library/info/ASA28/015/2003/en Malaysia: Double injustice heaped on Anwar Ibrahim] 18 April 2003, ''Amnesty International''</ref><ref>[https://www.hrw.org/press/1999/jun/anwar0610.htm Human Rights Watch Monitors Second Anwar Trial] 11 June 1999, ''Human Rights Watch''</ref>

Anwar's wife, [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail|Wan Azizah Ismail]], subsequently formed Parti Keadilan Nasional (the [[National Justice Party (Malaysia)|National Justice Party]], later known as [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] or the People's Justice Party), which based its platform on campaigning for Anwar's release and ''reformasi''. At the [[2004 Malaysian general election|following general election]], the then-National Justice Party performed poorly in the election, only winning five parliamentary seats.

====International reaction====
The sodomy charges sparked numerous criticisms and expressions of concern from overseas. Political leaders from the United States, Australia, and other governments commented on the case, damaging Malaysia's reputation as a progressive, Islamic democracy.<ref>{{cite news |title=Anwar Sodomy Trial Hurts Malaysia's Reputation|url=https://www.voanews.com/a/anwar-sodomy-trial-hurts-malaysias-reputation-84594087/112633.html|work=Voice of America|date=18 February 2010 |access-date=18 February 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100219180707/http://www1.voanews.com/english/news/Anwar-Sodomy-Trial-Hurts-Malaysias-Reputation-84594087.html| archive-date= 19 February 2010 | url-status= live}}</ref> Amnesty International and other human rights groups condemned the charges against Anwar.

Over 50 Australian Parliamentarians from the Government and the Opposition signed a letter formally requesting that Malaysia cease the trial of Anwar.<ref>{{cite news |title= Malaysian lawmakers object to Aussie MPs' call on Anwar|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/2/13/nation/20100213165216&sec=nation|work=The Star|date=13 February 2010 |access-date=12 February 2010}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= Australia lawmakers urge Malaysia to drop Anwar sodomy case |url=http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20100212-198392.html|work=AsiaOne|date=12 February 2010 |access-date=12 February 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100214071406/http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20100212-198392.html| archive-date= 14 February 2010 | url-status= dead}}</ref> Prime Minister Najib stated that the Australian lawmakers were likely not familiar with the details of the case.<ref>{{cite news|title=Najib: We'll help Australian reps better understand Anwar's trial|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/53141-najib-well-help-australian-reps-better-understand-anwars-trial|work=Malaysian Insider|date=14 February 2010|access-date=15 February 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100216150802/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/53141-najib-well-help-australian-reps-better-understand-anwars-trial|archive-date=16 February 2010|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Wisma Putra Will Explain Facts Of Anwar's Case To Australian Lawmakers – Najib|url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsindex.php?id=475666 |work= Bernama|date=14 February 2010 |access-date=15 February 2010}}</ref>

However, [[People's Union for Civil Liberties]] and [[International Commission of Jurists]] have stated that the appeal process was going smoothly and fair on both sides.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.therakyatpost.com/news/2014/10/31/international-observers-call-anwars-appeal-hearing-fair-far/#ixzz3ITrlIcJb|title=International observers call Anwar's appeal hearing fair so far - The Rakyat Post|work=The Rakyat Post |date=31 October 2014 }}</ref>

====Local reaction====
Local reaction to the trial was split between supporters of different governmental factions. Opposition supporters believed there was a conspiracy to discredit him. Prominent opposition MP [[Karpal Singh]] suggested that Prime Minister Najib Tun Razak and his wife were behind the initiation of the sodomy charge. However, the police strongly disagreed, indicating that Anwar's arrest was supported by the evidence.<ref>{{cite news |title=Anwar's Sodomy Charge Complicates Political Struggle |url=http://www.asiasentinel.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=1374&Itemid=178 |work=Asian Sentinel| date=7 August 2008|access-date=10 February 2010| archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100116045312/http://asiasentinel.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=1374&Itemid=178| archive-date= 16 January 2010 | url-status= live}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5iEg6QO7zxNvZtTZnZAWchWpt6q_w |title=afp.google.com, Malaysian opposition leader Anwar charged with sodomy |work=AFP Google |date=6 August 2008 |access-date=10 January 2012}}</ref> A shared concern, such as that expressed by veteran UMNO leader [[Tengku Razaleigh Hamzah]], was that the trial will "shame the country the world over".<ref>{{cite news|title= Anwar's Sodomy II trial starts today|url= http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/51635-anwars-sodomy-ii-trial-starts-today-|work= Malaysian Insider|date= 2 February 2010|access-date= 9 February 2010|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100205104210/http://themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/malaysia/51635-anwars-sodomy-ii-trial-starts-today-|archive-date= 5 February 2010|url-status= dead}}</ref> Malaysia's Foreign Ministry on 8 February 2010 cautioned foreign governments against involving themselves in the trial by commenting on or criticising the Malaysian legal system.<ref>{{cite news|title=Stop meddling in Anwar trial |url=http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20100208-197394.html |work=AsiaOne |date=8 February 2010 |access-date=9 February 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100211184636/http://news.asiaone.com/News/AsiaOne%2BNews/Malaysia/Story/A1Story20100208-197394.html |archive-date=11 February 2010 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

====Acquittal====
On 9 January 2012, almost two years after the trial started, Judge Zabidin Mohamad Diah found Anwar not guilty of sodomy, after finding the DNA evidence submitted by the prosecution unreliable. His oral verdict concluded: "The court is always reluctant to convict on sexual offences without corroborative evidence. Therefore, the accused is acquitted and discharged." Anwar expressed surprise at the verdict and said: "Thank God justice has prevailed I have been vindicated." Information Minister [[Rais Yatim]] responded by claiming the verdict demonstrated the independence of Malaysia's courts.<ref>{{cite web|title=Malaysia court finds Anwar Ibrahim not guilty of sodomy|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-16463989|work=BBC News Asia|date=9 January 2011|access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref> [[Mahathir Mohamad]], the prime minister at the time of Anwar's first sodomy trial, said that the verdict vindicated the government and proved there had never been a conspiracy between the government and the judiciary to imprison Anwar.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dr-m-acquittal-proves-no-conspiracy-against-anwar/ |title=Dr M: Acquittal proves no conspiracy against Anwar |date=9 January 2012 |work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=9 January 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120111035302/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dr-m-acquittal-proves-no-conspiracy-against-anwar |archive-date=11 January 2012 }}</ref> Saiful, the complainant, said after the verdict was delivered that he hoped that the prosecution would appeal against Anwar's acquittal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/anwar-accuser-wants-appeal/ |title=Anwar accuser wants appeal |last=Teoh |first=Shannon |date=9 January 2012 |work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=9 January 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120111035252/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/anwar-accuser-wants-appeal |archive-date=11 January 2012 }}</ref>

Anwar had spent the days before the verdict travelling Malaysia to gain support for the opposition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/asia/malaysia/9001514/Malaysian-opposition-leader-Anwar-Ibrahim-acquitted-timeline.html|title=Malaysian opposition leader Anwar Ibrahim acquitted: timeline|date=9 January 2012|work=[[The Daily Telegraph]]|access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref> Approximately 5,000 opposition supporters rallied outside the court on the day the court delivered the verdict.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2012/jan/09/anwar-ibrahim-sodomy-dismissed-malaysian?newsfeed=true|title=Anwar Ibrahim sodomy charge dismissed by Malaysian judge|date=9 January 2012|work=The Guardian |location=UK|access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref> Five people were injured in explosions in the area around the court complex, the cause of which could not be confirmed by police at the time.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v6/newsgeneral.php?id=638799|title="Free Anwar Campaign" Marred By Explosions, Five Injured|date=9 January 2012|work=[[Bernama]]|access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref>

On 20 January 2012, the prosecution filed an appeal against Anwar's acquittal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/prosecution-appeals-anwar-sodomy-acquittal/ |title=Prosecution appeals Anwar sodomy acquittal |last=Shazwan Mustafa Kamal |date=20 January 2012 |work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=20 January 2012 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120122223637/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/prosecution-appeals-anwar-sodomy-acquittal |archive-date=22 January 2012 }}</ref>

===Acquittal overturned===
The Court of Appeal overturned the acquittal of Anwar, upholding a government appeal. The court also convicted Anwar and sentenced him to five years' imprisonment.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-26479642|title=Malaysia: Anwar Ibrahim sodomy acquittal overturned|date=7 March 2014|work=[[BBC News]]|access-date=7 March 2014}}</ref> The decision came late on 7 March 2014 as Anwar prepared to contest a [[Kajang Move|state by-election]] on 23 March he was expected to win, opening the way for him to become the chief minister of [[Selangor]] state, the country's main economic hub surrounding Kuala Lumpur. Human rights groups accused Malaysia's government of using an "anachronistic colonial-era law" that criminalises "carnal intercourse against the order of nature" to persecute 66-year-old Anwar, who leads a three-party opposition that is increasingly popular in the predominantly Muslim nation.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.smh.com.au/world/anwar-ibrahim-sodomy-acquittal-overturned-20140307-34cxi.html|title=Anwar Ibrahim sodomy acquittal overturned|date=7 March 2014|work=[[The Sydney Morning Herald]]|access-date=7 March 2014}}</ref>

===Federal Court appeal and imprisonment===
Anwar lodged an appeal against the Court of Appeal's decision and, on 10 February 2015, the [[Federal Court of Malaysia|Federal Court]] re-affirmed the conviction and five-year sentence. The conviction was by a unanimous decision of the Federal Court.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kamal |first=Shazwan Mustafa |date=2015-02-10 |title=Anwar gets five years as Federal Court maintains sodomy conviction |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2015/02/10/anwar-jailed-as-federal-court-maintains-sodomy-conviction/838015 |access-date=2022-08-28 |website=Malay Mail |language=en}}</ref> The lead prosecutor had called for the prison sentence to be increased, given that the maximum sentence is 20 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/anwar-jailed-as-federal-court-maintains-sodomy-conviction|title=Anwar gets five years as Federal Court maintains sodomy conviction|work=Malaymail online|author=Shazwan Mustafa Kamal|date=10 February 2015|quote=Tan Sri Muhd Shafee Abdullah, the private lawyer appointed to lead the prosecution, argued that this was not the first time Anwar was convicted of sodomy and pressed for a heavier penalty as the offence permits for jail of up to 20 years. He contended that the sentence should not be lesser than the six years Anwar had received during his first sodomy conviction that was later reversed.}}</ref> As this was Anwar's final right of appeal, he was incarcerated immediately in [[Sungai Buloh Prison]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nst.com.my/node/72464|title=Anwar's arrives at Sungai Buloh prison|last=Farezza Hanum Rashid|date=10 February 2015|work=New Straits Times|access-date=10 February 2015}}</ref> Before leaving the court, Anwar criticised the presiding judges for "bowing to the dictates of the political masters". During the hearing he had praised the judges as "patient and attentive".<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.nst.com.my/node/49012?m=1 |title=Archived copy |access-date=17 February 2015 |archive-date=3 June 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190603074112/https://www.nst.com.my/node/49012?m=1 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name="auto">{{cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-malaysia-anwar-idUSKBN0LD2F520150210|title=Malaysia's Anwar jailed for five years after losing appeal in sodomy trial|date=10 February 2015|work=Reuters|access-date=10 February 2015}}</ref> His statement was supported by one of his lawyers, [[Ramkarpal Singh]], who applauded the bench for hearing both submissions without fail and "the fact that this appeal is one of the longest in Federal Court history proved that we will obtain fair judgement."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/2015/09/anwar%E2%80%99s-appeal-judges-praised-anwar?m=1|title=Anwar's Appeal: Judges praised by Anwar|date=3 November 2014|work=New Straits Times|access-date=17 May 2017}}</ref>

[[Human Rights Watch]] described the ruling as a "major setback for human rights in Malaysia".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.hrw.org/news/2015/02/10/malaysia-anwar-s-conviction-sets-back-rights|title=Malaysia: Anwar's Conviction Sets Back Rights|date=10 February 2015|publisher=Human Rights Watch}}</ref> In an official response to the decision, the United States government stated, "The United States is deeply disappointed and concerned by the rejection of Anwar Ibrahim's final appeal and his conviction&nbsp;... The decision to prosecute Mr Anwar, and his trial, have raised serious concerns regarding the rule of law and the independence of the courts".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.straitstimes.com/news/asia/south-east-asia/story/us-deeply-disappointed-jailing-malaysias-anwar-20150210|title=US 'deeply disappointed' by jailing of Malaysia's Anwar|date=10 February 2015|work=The Straits Times|access-date=13 February 2015}}</ref> The office of Malaysia's Prime Minister [[Najib Razak]] responded to the ruling by stating that "Malaysia has an independent judiciary, and there have been many rulings against senior government figures".<ref name="auto"/>

On 16 March 2015, [[Nurul Izzah Anwar]], daughter of Anwar and a member of parliament, was arrested by the police for a speech she read in parliament that referred to the February 2015 ruling which upheld Anwar's sodomy conviction.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-malaysia-sedition-anwar-idUSKBN0MC12820150316|title=Malaysia arrests Anwar's daughter for sedition: opposition party|date=16 March 2015|work=[[Reuters]]|access-date=17 May 2017}}</ref>

===Royal pardon===
On 16 May 2018, [[Yang di-Pertuan Agong|Malaysia's King]], [[Muhammad V of Kelantan|Sultan Muhammad V]], officially pardoned Anwar after meeting with members of the pardons board and Prime Minister [[Mahathir Mohamad]].

=== Witnesses ===
Ummi Hafilda Ali, a Malaysian businesswoman, also became a witness in the Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials in 1998.<ref name=MSTAR>{{cite web|title=Umi Hafilda Pula Cabar Anwar, Azmin Debat Kes Liwat 1998|url=http://www.mstar.com.my/berita/cerita.asp?file=/2011/1/13/mstar_berita/20110113162617&sec=mstar_berita|publisher=Star Publications (Malaysia) Bhd|accessdate=13 January 2011|author=MOHD AL QAYUM AZIZI}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Anwar's Defense Nixed |url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/anwars-defense-nixed/ |access-date=27 July 2021 |work=CBS News |agency=The Associated Press |date=February 9, 1999}}</ref> After accusing Anwar of adultery and sodomy, she later retracted her allegations.<ref>{{cite news |title=World: Asia-Pacific Anwar sex claims 'were false' |url=https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/208972.stm |access-date=27 July 2021 |work=BBC News |date=November 6, 1998}}</ref>
