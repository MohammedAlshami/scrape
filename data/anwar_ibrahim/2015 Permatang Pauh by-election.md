}}A '''by-election''' was held for the [[Dewan Rakyat]] seat of '''[[Permatang Pauh (federal constituency)|Permatang Pauh]]''' on 7 May following the nomination day on 25 April 2015.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Star (Malaysia)]]|title=Four cornered fight in Permatang Pauh by-election  |date=25 April 2015|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2015/04/25/Permatang-Pauh-by-election-candidates/}}</ref> The seat fell vacant after member of parliament [[Anwar Ibrahim]] was disqualified from holding his seat after being [[Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials|found guilty of sodomy]] in a highly controversial trial.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=Anwar no longer Permatang Pauh MP|date=1 April 2015|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/anwar-has-lost-his-eligibility-as-mp-says-speaker|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518082900/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/anwar-has-lost-his-eligibility-as-mp-says-speaker|archive-date=18 May 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Anwar Ibrahim was a member of the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] and leader of the opposition alliance [[Pakatan Rakyat]]. In the [[2013 Malaysian general election|2013 general election]], Anwar won the seat with a majority of 11,721 votes beating Mazlan Ismail of [[Barisan Nasional]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.asiaone.com/news/malaysia/wan-azizah-pkr-candidate-permatang-pauh-election|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150503133206/http://news.asiaone.com/news/malaysia/wan-azizah-pkr-candidate-permatang-pauh-election|url-status=dead|archive-date=May 3, 2015|title=Wan Azizah is PKR candidate for Permatang Pauh by-election}}</ref>

The Permatang Pauh by-election was contested by 4 candidates, consisting of former opposition leader, Anwar's wife [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]] from PKR, [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO's]] Suhaimi Sabudin, [[Parti Rakyat Malaysia|PRM's]] Azman Shah Othman and independent candidate Salleh Isahak.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/election-commission-expects-permatang-pauh-by-election-results-by-9pm-polli |title = Election Commission expects Permatang Pauh by-election results by 9pm polling day - The Malaysian Insider  |access-date=2015-04-25 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150518082855/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/election-commission-expects-permatang-pauh-by-election-results-by-9pm-polli |archive-date=2015-05-18 }}</ref> There were reports saying that PRM has denied that Azman Shah Othman is a member of their party.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nst.com.my/node/81696|title=Permatang Pauh by-election: PRM says Azman not from their party|date=25 April 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thesundaily.my/news/1395450|title=PRM denies Azman Shah is its member|website=www.thesundaily.my}}</ref>

== Campaign ==
Several issues dominate the by-election campaign, with the recently introduced [[Goods and Services Tax (Malaysia)|GST]] being the main issue.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=GST main issue among Permatang Pauh voters|date=27 April 2015|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/gst-main-issue-among-permatang-pauh-voters|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150429222109/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/gst-main-issue-among-permatang-pauh-voters|archive-date=29 April 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

An UMNO branch in Permatang Pauh dissolved during the campaign period, citing neglect by party leadership.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=Umno branch in Permatang Pauh dissolved after years of neglect|date=1 May 2015|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/umno-branch-in-permatang-pauh-dissolved-after-years-of-neglect|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150503221857/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/umno-branch-in-permatang-pauh-dissolved-after-years-of-neglect|archive-date=3 May 2015|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

== Results ==
Wan Azizah retained the seat for PR with a majority of just below 9 thousand votes.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|title=Official: PKR retains Pmtg Pauh, smaller majority |date=7 May 2015|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/297557}}</ref> Mirroring the trend shown in the [[2015 Rompin by-election|Rompin by-election]] two days before, turnout dropped by more than 10% in Permatang Pauh. The lower turnout also means while PKR's majority is lower, their share of popular vote is practically unchanged.<ref>{{cite news|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|title=Wan Azizah wins Permatang Pauh|date=7 May 2015|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-leads-with-3555-majority-in-permatang-pauh-as-counting-under-way|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150508151807/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pkr-leads-with-3555-majority-in-permatang-pauh-as-counting-under-way|archive-date=8 May 2015}}</ref>
