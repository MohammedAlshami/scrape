The '''Anwar Ibrahim cabinet''' is the current [[federation|federal]] [[Cabinet (government)|cabinet]] of [[Malaysia]], formed on 3 December 2022,<ref>{{cite news |date=24 November 2022 |title=Anwar Ibrahim appointed as Malaysia's 10th Prime Minister |work=[[Bernama]] |url=https://pru15.bernama.com/news-en.php?id=2141779 |access-date=24 November 2022}}</ref> nine days after [[Anwar Ibrahim]] took office as the 10th [[Prime Minister of Malaysia]]. The composition of the cabinet was announced by Anwar a day earlier on 2 December 2022, consisting of most of the political coalitions and parties represented in the 15th [[Parliament of Malaysia|Parliament]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Anwar's Malaysia Cabinet Announcement Delayed Until Later Friday|newspaper=Bloomberg.com |date=2 December 2022 |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2022-12-02/anwar-s-malaysia-cabinet-announcement-delayed-until-later-friday|publisher=Bloomberg|access-date=3 December 2022}}</ref> It was the 23rd [[Cabinet of Malaysia#List of cabinets|cabinet of Malaysia]] formed since [[Independence Day (Malaysia)|independence]]. Although [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) was also invited to join the government, it decided to decline the invitation and instead formed the opposition. The government is often referred to as "[[unity government]]" ([[Malay language|Malay]]: ''Kerajaan Perpaduan'') or informally, ''Madani'' government ([[Malay language|Malay]]: ''[[Malaysia Madani|Kerajaan Madani]]'').

== Changes ==

=== Changes in result of the new incoming cabinet ===
From the appointment of Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim to 12 December 2023:
* The number of [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|Deputy Prime Ministers]] increased from one to two.
* The number of Deputy Ministers of several ministries decreased from two to one.
* No Deputy Ministers for Ministry of National Unity and for the Minister in the Prime Minister' Department (Religious Affairs).
* [[Ministry of Agriculture and Food Industries (Malaysia)|Ministry of Agriculture and Food Industries]] was renamed to ''[[Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security]]''.
* [[Ministry of Communications and Multimedia (Malaysia)|Ministry of Communications and Multimedia]] was renamed to ''[[Ministry of Communications and Digital]]''.
* [[Ministry of Domestic Trade and Consumer Affairs (Malaysia)|Ministry of Domestic Trade and Consumer Affairs]] was renamed to ''Ministry of Domestic Trade and Living Costs''.
* [[Ministry of Energy and Natural Resources (Malaysia)|Ministry of Energy and Natural Resources]] and [[Minister of Environment and Water (Malaysia)|Ministry of Environment and Water]] were merged into a single ministry named ''Ministry of Natural Resources, Environment and Climate Change''.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news |last=Parzi |first=Mohd Nasaruddin |date=2022-12-03 |title=Dua portfolio kementerian digabung, satu lagi dimansuh |language=ms |work=Berita Harian |url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2022/12/1034550/dua-portfolio-kementerian-digabung-satu-lagi-dimansuh |access-date=2022-12-05}}</ref>
* [[Ministry of Federal Territories (Malaysia)|Ministry of Federal Territories]] was transferred the Prime Minister's Department as a portfolio and a department under the Prime Minister.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Mat Ruzki |first=Rafidah |date=2022-12-16 |title=Kedudukan pegawai kanan kementerian distruktur semula akan diumum |language=ms |work=Berita Harian |url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2022/12/1040083/kedudukan-pegawai-kanan-kementerian-distruktur-semula-akan-diumum |access-date=2022-12-17}}</ref>
* [[Ministry of Housing and Local Government (Malaysia)|Ministry of Housing and Local Government]] was renamed to ''Ministry of Local Government Development''. Housing portfolio remains in this ministry for now.
* Ministry of Rural Development was renamed to ''Ministry of Rural and Regional Development''.
* Economic affairs portfolio under the [[Prime Minister's Department (Malaysia)|Prime Minister's Department]] was transferred to the returning ''Ministry of Economy''. It was previously the Ministry of Economic Affairs under the [[seventh Mahathir cabinet]].
* [[Ministry of International Trade and Industry (Malaysia)|Ministry of International Trade and Industry]] was renamed to ''Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry''.

=== Changes in result of the reshuffling on 12 December 2023 ===
Since 12 December 2023:
* Deputy Ministers for Ministry of National Unity and for the Minister in the Prime Minister' Department (Religious Affairs) have been appointed.
* [[Ministry of Communications and Digital]] was split to two different ministries, i.e. ''[[Ministry of Communications]]'' and ''Ministry of Digital''.
* Ministry of Natural Resources, Environment and Climate Change was split to two different ministries, i.e. ''[[Ministry of Natural Resources and Environmental Sustainability]]'' and ''Ministry of Energy Transition and Water Transformation.''
* Federal Territories Department under the Prime Minister's Department is given a specific minister.
* [[Ministry of Housing and Local Government (Malaysia)|Ministry of Local Government Development]] was renamed to ''[[Ministry of Housing and Local Government]]''.

=== Changes in result of the reshuffling on 17 December 2025 ===
Since 17 December 2025:

* Deputy Minister for the Minister in the Prime Minister' Department (Federal Territories) have been appointed.

==Unity Government Secretariat==

The Unity Government formed a secretariat to coordinate the activities of its component coalitions and parties in February 2023. [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|Deputy Prime Minister]], Chairman of the [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and President of the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] added that the inaugural meeting of the secretariat would pay attention to finding common ground between the manifestos of its different components and strengthening the government.<ref>{{cite news |title=Finding common ground aim of unity govt's secretariat meeting, says Zahid |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2023/02/07/finding-common-ground-aim-of-unity-govts-secretariat-meeting-says-zahid/ |access-date=8 February 2023 |work=Free Malaysia Today |date=7 February 2023}}</ref> The inaugural secretariat meeting was held at the headquarters of UMNO and attended by [[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Prime Minister]], Chairman of the [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) and President of the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) [[Anwar Ibrahim]], [[Minister of Transport (Malaysia)|Minister of Transport]], Deputy President of PH and Secretary-General of the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) [[Anthony Loke Siew Fook]] and among others. This marked the first time Anwar had revisited the headquarters of UMNO in over 20 years after his sacking from UMNO and removal from government positions in September 1998 that sparked the [[Reformasi (Malaysia)|Reformasi]] movement and the first time a DAP leader had visited the headquarters of UMNO.<ref name="cna_unity">{{cite news |title=In historic return to UMNO headquarters, Anwar says Malaysia unity government will face upcoming state polls together |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/malaysia-anwar-ibrahim-state-polls-unity-government-secretariat-ph-bn-umno-3262321 |access-date=8 February 2023 |work=CNA |date=8 February 2023 |language=en}}</ref> Anwar subsequently announced the formation of three secretariat committees namely the elections committee, the strategy committee and the government and political agenda monitoring committee.<ref name="cna_unity" />
