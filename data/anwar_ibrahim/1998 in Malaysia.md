==Events==
*February &ndash; [[El Nino]] phenomenon begins.
*February–April &ndash; The [[1998 Klang Valley water crisis|Klang Valley water crisis]] caused by [[El Niño]].
*17–26 April &ndash; The [[1998 Sukma Games]] held in [[Selangor]].
*17–19 April &ndash; [[1998 Malaysian motorcycle Grand Prix]]
*19 April &ndash; The [[Malaysia–Singapore Second Link|Malaysia–Singapore Second Crossing]] was officially opened.
*21 April &ndash; Malaysian [[parachute]] expedition team with [[Proton Wira]] cars lands at the [[North Pole]] for the first time.
*14 May &ndash; [[Sultan Mahmud Al-Muktafi Billah Shah]] of [[Terengganu]] died in [[Singapore]], age 68. On 15 May, his son, the Yang di-Pertuan Muda of Terengganu, Tengku Mizan Zainal Abidin was elected as a new Sultan of Terengganu with the title [[Sultan Mizan Zainal Abidin]].
*27 June &ndash; [[Kuala Lumpur International Airport]] (KLIA) in [[Sepang]] was officially opened as the main international airport in [[Malaysia]], replacing [[Subang International Airport]] in [[Subang, Selangor|Subang]].
*1 July &ndash; The [[Kuala Lumpur Mini-Bus Service|Kuala Lumpur mini-bus]] (Bas Mini) service was officially terminated.
*11 July &ndash; The [[National Stadium, Bukit Jalil|National Stadium]] at [[National Sports Complex, Malaysia|National Sports Complex]] in [[Bukit Jalil]] was officially opened.
*August &ndash; Lawsuit submitted by 73 residents in blocks two and three of the [[Highland Towers collapse|Highland Towers]] apartments, with a trial held at the [[Kuala Lumpur]] High Court in front of High Court Judge, Datuk James Foong. Summons against developers and nine others for losses suffered between RM31 million and RM35 million. The group sued Highland Properties Sdn Bhd, Wong Tin Sang's plan, Wong Yuen Kean engineer, [[Ampang Municipal Council]] (MPAJ), [[AmBank|Arab-Malaysian Finance Berhad]], Tropic Developments Sdn Bhd and Metroplux Sdn Bhd.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.hba.org.my/archive/focus/HT/1998.htm|title = Highland Towers Report 1998}}</ref>
*1 August &ndash; In [[Singapore]], Malaysian CIQ for rail passengers controversially remained at [[Tanjong Pagar railway station]] after Singapore relocated its CIQ to [[Woodlands Train Checkpoint]].
*1 August &ndash; The [[Perodua Kembara]], Malaysian first 4WD vehicle was launched.
*17 August &ndash; The [[Petronas Philharmonic Hall]] (a subsidiary of [[Malaysian Philharmonic Orchestra]] played for the first time), [[Aquaria KLCC]] and [[Suria KLCC]] of the [[Petronas Twin Towers]] at [[Kuala Lumpur City Centre]] (KLCC) is officially opened.
*1 September &ndash; The [[Putra LRT|PUTRA Light Rail Transit system (LRT)]], Malaysia's first automated [[light rail transit]] system commenced operations.
*1 September &ndash; Money banking control was introduced. The [[Kuala Lumpur Stock Exchange]] (KLSE) Composite Index rose from 400 points to 500 points.
*2 September &ndash; [[Anwar Ibrahim|Dato' Seri Anwar Ibrahim]], the deputy prime minister was sacked from [[fifth Mahathir cabinet]] and parliament.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=2001&dt=0415&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Rencana&pg=re_04.htm|title=Sejarah ganas reformasi - Politik cara militan perlu dilawan dengan ketegasan|website=Utusan Online|language=ms|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170802134041/http://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=2001&dt=0415&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Rencana&pg=re_04.htm|archive-date=2017-08-02|access-date=2017-08-02|url-status=dead}}</ref>
*2 September &ndash; [[Anwar Ibrahim]] was expelled from [[Umno|UMNO]] and [[Munawar Anees|Dr. Munawar Anees]], Anwar's former speechwriter, and Sukma Darmawan Sasmita Atmadja, Anwar's adoptive brother, were arrested under suspicion of engaging in [[sodomy]] acts.<ref name=":0" />
*11–21 September &mdash; '''[[1998 Commonwealth Games|The Kuala Lumpur 1998, XVI Commonwealth Games]]'''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://library.perdana.org.my/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/1996-1998/1996pz/suka%20dukegara%201996.pdf|title=Suka Duka Sukan Negara 1996|access-date=2017-12-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170331031225/http://library.perdana.org.my/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/1996-1998/1996pz/suka%20dukegara%201996.pdf|archive-date=2017-03-31|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://library.perdana.org.my/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/1996-1998/1996ko/ong%20reason%20in%201996.pdf|title=Sports: Azmi Steals Limelight For Wrong Reason in 1996|access-date=2017-12-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171209204134/http://library.perdana.org.my/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/1996-1998/1996ko/ong%20reason%20in%201996.pdf|archive-date=2017-12-09|url-status=dead}}</ref>
**11 September &ndash; The [[1998 Commonwealth Games]] opening ceremony was held in the [[National Stadium, Bukit Jalil|National Stadium]] at the [[National Sports Complex, Malaysia|National Sports Complex]]. The games were opened by The King of Malaysia, Tuanku Jaafar.
**11–21 September &ndash; This 16th edition of the games in [[Kuala Lumpur]] was the first to be held in [[Asia]]. 69 countries were represented, including [[Cameroon]], [[Kiribati]] and [[Mozambique]] which competed for the first time. For the first time team sports of [[cricket]], [[field hockey]], [[netball]] and [[rugby sevens]] were introduced. [[Malaysia at the 1998 Commonwealth Games|Malaysia]] won ten gold medals, fourteen silver medals and twelve bronze medals and ranked overall in fourth place.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://kl98.thecgf.com/|title=16th Commonwealth Games|website=kl98.thecgf.com|access-date=2017-08-02|archive-date=2020-02-17|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200217162531/http://kl98.thecgf.com/|url-status=dead}}</ref>
**21 September &ndash; The [[1998 Commonwealth Games]] closing ceremony was held in the [[National Stadium, Bukit Jalil|National Stadium]] at the [[National Sports Complex, Malaysia|National Sports Complex]]. The games were closed by [[Queen Elizabeth II]].
*20 September &ndash; [[Anwar Ibrahim]] addressed a protest gathering of nearly 100,000 people in [[Kuala Lumpur]], after which a number of his supporters marched to [[Mahathir Mohamad]]'s then official residence ''[[Sri Perdana]]'' demanding ''[[Reformasi (Malaysia)|reformasi]]'' (economic and political reforms) and Mahathir's resignation.<ref name=":0" />
*29 September &ndash; [[Anwar Ibrahim]] appeared in court and pleaded innocent to charges of [[Political corruption|corruption]] and [[sodomy]].<ref name=":0" />
*October &ndash; The [[Proton Perdana (first generation)|Proton Perdana]] V6 and [[Proton Satria]] GTi were launched at [[Putra Stadium]], [[Bukit Jalil]].
* 1 November &ndash; The [[Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission|Malaysian Communication and Multimedia Commission]] (MCMC) was established.
*17–18 November &ndash; The Tenth [[APEC]] Ministerial Meeting was held in [[Kuala Lumpur]].
*12 December &ndash; The [[Islamic Arts Museum Malaysia]] was officially opened.
