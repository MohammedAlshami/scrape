== Background and key perpetrators ==
{{Further|1Malaysia Development Berhad}}
[[Najib Razak]] became [[Prime Minister of Malaysia]] in 2009. In his first year in office, he established [[1Malaysia Development Berhad]] (1MDB), a [[sovereign wealth fund]], which sought to make strategic investments and alleviate [[Poverty in Malaysia|poverty]], as part of his [[Malaysian economic policy under Prime Minister Najib|economic liberalisation policy]] and trademark [[1Malaysia]] program.<ref name="Chen-2019" /> The fund had a budget of $1&nbsp;billion to invest, with Najib having the sole authority to sign off investments and manage the personnel on its board and management team.<ref name="Ramesh-2016" /> His wife is [[Rosmah Mansor]], whose son is [[Riza Aziz]] from a previous relationship.<ref name="Chen-2019" />

[[Jho Low]] is a Malaysian businessman and financier from [[Penang]]. Despite not having a formal position at 1MDB, Low supported its establishment and was given extensive access to the fund due to high-profile political connections in Malaysia and the Middle East, and entertainment connections in the United States. Low joined high-level meetings and facilitated numerous deals.<ref name="Chen-2019" />

[[Tim Leissner]] is a German banker who was chairman of the American investment bank [[Goldman Sachs]] in its [[Southeast Asia]] ventures. Leissner was credited with making lucrative deals for Goldman in the region after the [[2008 financial crisis]]. The head of Goldman Sachs in Malaysia, Roger Ng, introduced Leissner to Jho Low. Leissner and Ng worked with Low and used his political connections to establish deals and orchestrate the scheme.<ref name="Chen-2019" />

== Scheme ==
The [[United States Department of Justice|US DOJ]] described the scheme as involving three phases. In August 2009, Najib and Low met with representatives of the private oil company PetroSaudi on a yacht in [[Monaco]] to discuss 1MDB's first investment. This included Saudi royal [[Turki bin Abdullah Al Saud (born 1971)|Turki bin Abdullah Al Saud]] and Saudi national Tarek Obaid. During a subsequent visit to [[Saudi Arabia]], Najib signed a $2.5&nbsp;billion joint venture between PetroSaudi and 1MDB. The DOJ later said this was used as a "pretence" to move $1&nbsp;billion into a [[Swiss bank account]].<ref name="Ramesh-2016" />

An additional $1.4&nbsp;billion was then raised by [[Goldman Sachs]] in a [[bond issue]] and misappropriated into a bank account in Switzerland. An additional $1.3&nbsp;billion from Goldman Sachs was also diverted into a bank account in Singapore.<ref name="Ramesh-2016" /> US authorities say that Goldman Sachs, particularly Roger Ng, were central in orchestrating the scheme, particularly the [[Money laundering|laundering of money]] from the fund, some of which was used to pay [[Bribery|bribes]] to officials in Malaysia and the United Arab Emirates.<ref name="BBC News-2022">{{Cite news |date=8 April 2022 |title=1MDB scandal: Ex-Goldman Malaysia boss found guilty |language=en-GB |work=BBC News |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-61043609 |access-date=23 June 2022 |archive-date=17 May 2022 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220517102143/https://www.bbc.com/news/business-61043609 |url-status=live }}</ref>

=== Use of funds ===
More than $4&nbsp;billion was stolen from the 1MDB fund and spent by perpetrators on art, [[Diamond (gemstone)|diamonds]] and property.<ref name="BBC News-2022" />

Low allegedly used 1MDB funds to support a lavish lifestyle in the United States. The DOJ traced $100m from the PetroSaudi deal to the purchase of properties in [[Hollywood, Los Angeles|Hollywood]] and $40m to apartments in [[New York City|New York]].<ref name="Chen-2019" /> He established friendships with musicians [[Swizz Beats]], [[Alicia Keys]] and [[Pras]], was pictured partying with socialite [[Paris Hilton]], and used funds to purchase luxury items for model [[Miranda Kerr]] and singer [[Elva Hsiao]], with whom he was romantically involved.<ref name="Chen-2019" />

Hollywood film production company [[Red Granite Pictures]], co-founded by Riza Aziz and [[Joey McFarland]] in 2010, also reportedly received misappropriated funds. The DOJ suggested these were put towards the production of [[The Wolf of Wall Street (2013 film)|''The Wolf of Wall Street'']], [[Daddy's Home (film)|''Daddy's Home'']] and ''[[Dumb and Dumber To]]'', and by 2017 had seized [[Royalty payment|royalties]] from all three films.<ref name="Latiff-2020">{{Cite news |last=Latiff |first=Rozanna |date=26 July 2020 |editor-last=Birsel |editor-first=Robert |title=EXPLAINER-Malaysia's mega 1MDB scandal that brought down a prime minister |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/malaysia-politics-najib-idUSL3N2EU1O6 |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220620094845/https://www.reuters.com/article/malaysia-politics-najib-idUSL3N2EU1O6 |archive-date=20 June 2022 |access-date=20 June 2022 |work=Reuters |language=en}}</ref><ref name="The Straits Times-2017">{{Cite web |date=19 June 2017 |title=Leonardo DiCaprio turns over Marlon Brando Oscar, other gifts allegedly bought with 1MDB funds |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/world/united-states/leonardo-dicaprio-turns-over-marlon-brando-oscar-other-gifts-allegedly-bought |url-status=live |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180917105357/https://www.straitstimes.com/world/united-states/leonardo-dicaprio-turns-over-marlon-brando-oscar-other-gifts-allegedly-bought |archive-date=17 September 2018 |access-date=23 June 2022 |website=[[The Straits Times]] |language=en |agency=[[Agence France-Presse]]}}</ref> Jho Low was personally thanked in the credits of ''The Wolf of Wall Street''.<ref name="The Straits Times-2017" />

== Malaysian investigations and actions ==

=== Change of auditors and transparency ===
The RM 425&nbsp;million profit declared between 25 September 2009 and 31 March 2010 raised many criticisms and controversies about the lack of transparency in 1MDB's published accounts. [[Tony Pua]], [[Democratic Action Party (Malaysia)|DAP]] Member of Parliament for [[Petaling Jaya]] Utara, questioned Najib, 1MDB's advisory board chairman, as to whether the figures were the result of an asset injection into 1MDB by the government such as the transfer of land rights to the company.<ref>{{Cite news |last=Chooi |first=Clara |date=9 October 2010 |title=Pua says 1MDB profits suspect |work=The Malaysian Insider |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pua-says-1mdb-profits-suspect/ |access-date=26 July 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101012151903/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/pua-says-1mdb-profits-suspect |archive-date=12 October 2010}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=1Malaysia Development Berhad Malaysia Factbook |url=http://malaysiafactbook.com/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140316132016/http://malaysiafactbook.com/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad |archive-date=16 March 2014 |publisher=malaysiafactbook.com}}</ref>

During the October 2010 parliamentary session, 1MDB explained that its accounts had been fully audited and signed off by [[KPMG]], and closed as of 31 March 2010. [[Deloitte]] was involved in the valuation and analysis of the portfolio, while [[Ernst & Young]] provided tax advice for 1MDB.

1MDB eventually rang alarm bells by asking for a six-month extension on the filing of its annual report with the [[Companies Commission of Malaysia]] (CCM) due by 30 September 2013. At the same time, the change of three auditors since its inception in 2009 was considered suspicious.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/has-1mdb-filed-its-accounts |title=Has 1MDB filed its accounts? |date=1 April 2014 |newspaper=The Edge |location=Malaysia |access-date=26 July 2015 |archive-date=25 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210125075149/https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/has-1mdb-filed-its-accounts |url-status=live }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=22 April 2014 |title=Auditors highlight several critical areas in 1MDB's books |work=The Star |url=http://www.thestar.com.my/Business/Business-News/2014/04/22/Auditors-highlight-several-critical-areas-in-its-books-and-reveal-more-funds/ |access-date=26 July 2015 |archive-date=24 April 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140424040457/http://www.thestar.com.my/Business/Business-News/2014/04/22/Auditors-highlight-several-critical-areas-in-its-books-and-reveal-more-funds/ |url-status=live }}</ref> Responding to earlier criticism, CCM said that 1MDB had responded and lodged the necessary information, including registering an address, as required by law.<ref>The business address is also available on the company's website, www.1mdb.com.my</ref>

==1MDB lawsuit against Amicorp Group for alleged fraud==
On 20 December 2024 the Malaysian state fund has initiated legal proceedings against Amicorp Group and its CEO, Toine Knipping. The lawsuit, filed in the [[British Virgin Islands]], seeks over $1 billion in damages for alleged [[fraud]].<ref>{{Cite web |title=Malaysia's scandal-hit 1MDB sues Amicorp, seeks $1bn for alleged fraud |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2024/12/23/malaysias-scandal-hit-1mdb-sues-amicorp-seeks-1bn-for-alleged-fraud |access-date=10 January 2025 |website=Al Jazeera |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=23 December 2024 |title=1MDB Files $1 Billion Claim Against Amicorp to Recover Assets |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-12-23/1mdb-files-1-billion-claim-against-amicorp-to-recover-assets |access-date=10 January 2025 |work=Bloomberg.com |language=en}}</ref> 1MDB claims that Amicorp, a [[Hong Kong]]-based corporate services provider, played a crucial role in facilitating fraudulent transactions exceeding $7 billion between 2009 and 2014. The fund alleges that Amicorp orchestrated a sophisticated scheme involving numerous shell companies, fake transactions, and deceptive financial arrangements that concealed the actual source and flow of misappropriated funds. According to the lawsuit, the misappropriated assets were allegedly funneled through various locations, including Barbados, [[Curaçao]], Hong Kong, and the British Virgin Islands. 1MDB asserts that Amicorp granted access to the international financial system, enabling fund entities and banking services to perpetuate the cycling of assets. This created an illusion that 1MDB's investments were generating returns when, in reality, they had been misappropriated.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Amicorp Group denies alleged fraud of over $7 billion in Malaysia's 1MDB scandal |url=https://www.chinadailyhk.com/hk/article/600910 |access-date=10 January 2025 |website=chinadailyhk |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=23 December 2024 |title='1MDB liquidators file $1B+ BVI lawsuit against Amicorp & CEO Toine Knipping for alleged fraudulent trading' (Plaintiffs' Press Release) – OffshoreAlert |url=https://www.offshorealert.com/1mdb-liquidators-file-1b-bvi-lawsuit-against-amicorp-ceo-toine-knipping-for-alleged-fraudulent-trading-plaintiffs-press-release/ |access-date=10 January 2025 |language=en-US}}</ref>

In response to the allegations, Amicorp Group has denied any wrongdoing and stated its intention to dispute the $1 billion legal claim. The company maintains that the claim is inaccurate and that it will be left to the British Virgin Islands Court of Justice to assess the suit.<ref>{{Cite news |date=24 December 2024 |title=Amicorp Denies 1MDB's $1 Billion Asset Recovery Claim |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2024-12-24/amicorp-denies-1mdb-s-1-billion-asset-recovery-claim |access-date=10 January 2025 |work=Bloomberg.com |language=en}}</ref>

== Recovery of 1MDB assets ==

As of May 2019, Malaysia had recovered US$322&nbsp;million (RM1.3&nbsp;billion) worth of 1MDB assets since its renewed investigations into the 1MDB scandal after the 14th General Election in May 2018.<ref name="Bedi-2019">{{Cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/05/08/rm13bil-in-1mdb-assets-recouped/|title=RM1.3bil in 1MDB assets recouped|last=Bedi|first=Rashvinjeet S.|website=www.thestar.com.my|date=8 May 2019 |access-date=8 May 2019|archive-date=8 May 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190508005548/https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/05/08/rm13bil-in-1mdb-assets-recouped/|url-status=live}}</ref>

Steps have been taken to preserve the value of the assets caught up in the case, including the sale of the [[Park Lane Hotel (Manhattan)|Park Lane Hotel]] in New York in November 2018, a step endorsed by the U.S. DOJ, in accordance with the rule of law and on the basis of no admission of wrongdoing or liability.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.finews.asia/finance/23965-jho-low-trustee-1mdb-asset-seizure-new-zealand|title=1MDB: Emboldened Trusts Wrangle Over Jho Low Assets|date=16 February 2017|website=finews.asia|language=en-gb|access-date=9 July 2019|archive-date=9 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190709153200/https://www.finews.asia/finance/23965-jho-low-trustee-1mdb-asset-seizure-new-zealand|url-status=live}}</ref> In August 2018, Malaysian authorities seized a yacht allegedly purchased by Low, selling it some eight months later to minimize the costs associated with maintaining it. A spokesperson for Low described the seizure as "illegal".<ref>{{Cite news|last1=Harris|first1=Elizabeth A.|last2=Stevenson|first2=Alexandra|date=9 December 2018|title=A Yacht, a Monet, a See-Through Piano: The U.S. Collects on a Fugitive's Shopping Spree|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2018/12/09/arts/jho-low-1mdb-assets-piano.html|url-status=live|access-date=9 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200706072400/https://www.nytimes.com/2018/12/09/arts/jho-low-1mdb-assets-piano.html|archive-date=6 July 2020|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last=Latiff|first=Rozanna|date=24 August 2018|title=Court gives Malaysia nod to sell superyacht seized in 1MDB probe|language=en|work=Reuters|editor-last=Cameron-Moore|editor-first=Simon|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-malaysia-politics-equanimity-court-idUSKCN1L9117|url-status=live|access-date=9 July 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200422230619/https://www.reuters.com/article/us-malaysia-politics-equanimity-court-idUSKCN1L9117|archive-date=22 April 2020}}</ref> [[Leonardo DiCaprio]] returned gifts received from Low to US authorities in 2017, including an Oscar received by [[Marlon Brando]] and paintings by [[Pablo Picasso|Picasso]] and [[Jean-Michel Basquiat]].<ref name="The Straits Times-2017" />
