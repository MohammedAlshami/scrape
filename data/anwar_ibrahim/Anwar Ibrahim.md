{{Anwar Ibrahim sidebar}}'''Anwar bin Ibrahim'''{{Efn|{{Blist|{{langx|ms-Arab|{{Script/Arabic|أنوار بن إبراهيم}}|label=[[Jawi script|Jawi]]}}|{{IPA|ms|anua(r) ibrahem|audio=Anwar Ibrahim Name Pronounciation.ogg}}|Also known as '''DSAI''' and '''PMX''', abbreviations for '''D'''ato' '''S'''eri '''A'''nwar '''I'''brahim and '''P'''rime '''M'''inister '''X''' (as the 10th prime minister), respectively.<ref>{{Cite web |date=9 February 2023 |title=Open Letter To PMX DSAI And YBMK Dr Zaliha: Reform Malaysian Health Care – Dr Tachdjian |url=https://codeblue.galencentre.org/2023/02/open-letter-to-pmx-dsai-and-ybmk-dr-zaliha-reform-malaysian-health-care-dr-tachdjian/ |access-date=9 March 2025 |website=CodeBlue |language=en-US}}</ref>}}}} (born 10 August 1947) is a Malaysian politician who has served as the 10th [[prime minister of Malaysia]] since 2022.<ref name=PRU15/> A member of the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR), he served as [[Parliament of Malaysia|Member of Parliament]] (MP) for [[Tambun (federal constituency)|Tambun]] since 2022. Upon becoming prime minister, he appointed himself [[Minister of Finance (Malaysia)|Minister of Finance]]. Anwar is also the president of PKR since 2018 and the chairman of [[Pakatan Harapan]] coalition since 2020.

<!--Early life and early political career-->Born in the [[Crown Colony of Penang]] during the [[Malayan Union]], Anwar graduated from [[University of Malaya]]. Prior to entering politics, he served as president of the National Union of Malaysian Muslim Students and of the [[Malaysian Islamic Youth Movement]]. He later joined [[UMNO]], then the dominant party in the long-ruling [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition.<ref>{{Cite book |author=Ooi |first=Keat Gin |author-link=Keat Gin Ooi |title=Southeast Asia: a historical encyclopedia, from Angkor Wat to East Timor |date=2004 |publisher=ABC-CLIO |isbn=1-57607-770-5 |location=Santa Barbara, Calif. |oclc=54528945}}</ref> He became the 7th [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|deputy prime minister]] in 1993 and served as Minister of Finance from 1991, playing a key role in Malaysia's response to the [[1997 Asian financial crisis]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Anwar Ibrahim – London Speaker Bureau Asia|work=London Speaker Bureau Asia |url=https://www.londonspeakerbureauasia.com/speakers/anwar-ibrahim/|access-date=20 December 2021|language=en-US|archive-date=20 December 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211220121129/https://www.londonspeakerbureauasia.com/speakers/anwar-ibrahim/}}</ref> In 1998, Anwar was removed from all government posts by then-Prime Minister [[Mahathir Mohamad]] and subsequently led the [[Reformasi (Malaysia)|Reformasi movement]] against the government. He was jailed in April 1999 on charges of corruption and sodomy,<ref>{{Cite web|date=6 August 2008|title=Malaysia: Charges against Anwar politically motivated|url=https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2008/08/malaysia-charges-against-anwar-politically-motivated-20080806/|access-date=20 December 2021|website=Amnesty International|language=en}}</ref> until his release in 2004 after his conviction was overturned. He returned to politics as the 12th [[Leader of the Opposition (Malaysia)|leader of the opposition]] from 2008 to 2015, merging opposition parties into the [[Pakatan Rakyat]] (PR) coalition, which contested the [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008]] and [[2013 Malaysian general election|2013]] general elections. He disputed the 2013 election results and led subsequent protests.

<!--Rise to prominence-->In 2014, Anwar attempted to become [[Menteri Besar of Selangor]] as part of the [[Kajang Move]], sparking a nine-month political crisis that ended with a five-year prison sentence following a [[Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials#2008 allegation|second sodomy conviction]] in 2015.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Kumar |first=Kamles |title=Is it time for Anwar Ibrahim to step aside? |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/12/2/is-it-time-for-anwar-ibrahim-to-step-aside |access-date=1 September 2024 |website=Aljazeera |language=en |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211202055124/https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2021/12/2/is-it-time-for-anwar-ibrahim-to-step-aside |archive-date=2 December 2021}}</ref> While in prison, he rejoined Mahathir Mohamad in the new [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition ''in absentia'', which won the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]]. Mahathir planned for Anwar to succeed him as prime minister after an interim period.<ref>{{Cite web|date=25 February 2020|title=Malaysia's political turmoil: everything you need to know|url=http://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/feb/25/malaysias-political-turmoil-everything-you-need-to-know|access-date=18 June 2021|website=The Guardian|language=en}}</ref> He was granted a [[Royal prerogative of mercy|royal pardon]] by [[Muhammad V of Kelantan|King Muhammad V]] and released in May 2018.<ref>{{cite web|first1=Hannah|last1=Ellis-Petersen|access-date=11 May 2018|title=Malaysia: Mahathir Mohamad says Anwar Ibrahim to be given royal pardon|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2018/may/11/malaysia-mahathir-ibrahim-will-be-given-royal-pardon|date=11 May 2018|website=The Guardian}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cnbc.com/2018/05/15/malaysia-election-anwar-ibrahim-to-be-released-may-lead-country.html |title=Jailed opposition icon seen as Malaysia's future leader is released from custody |last=Chandran |first=Nyshka |date=16 May 2018 |website=CNBC |access-date=1 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180516054354/https://www.cnbc.com/2018/05/15/malaysia-election-anwar-ibrahim-to-be-released-may-lead-country.html |archive-date=16 May 2018}}</ref> He returned to parliament in the [[2018 Port Dickson by-election]] while his wife, [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]], served as deputy prime minister in the PH administration. During the [[2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis]], the PH coalition collapsed, leading to the [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) government under [[Muhyiddin Yassin]], with Anwar becoming the opposition leader for a second term from 2020 to 2022.

<!--Premiership-->Following the [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 general election]], where [[Pakatan Harapan]] won a [[Plurality (voting)|plurality]] of seats, Anwar was sworn in as prime minister on 24 November 2022.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/politics/article/3200790/malaysia-election-2022-decision-anwar-or-pm-nears-sultans-meet|title=Malaysia election 2022: Anwar Ibrahim named PM, swearing in at 5pm|date=24 November 2022|access-date=24 November 2022|work=[[South China Morning Post]]}}{{Dead link|date=January 2026 |bot=InternetArchiveBot }}</ref> On 2 December 2022, he appointed MPs from PH, [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS), and UMNO as ministers in a unity government cabinet, retaining the finance portfolio for himself.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 December 2022 |title=Zahid returns as number 2, Anwar keeps finance as he unveils Cabinet |url=https://www.malaysianow.com/news/2022/12/02/zahid-returns-as-number-2-anwar-keeps-finance-as-he-unveils-cabinet |access-date=2 December 2022 |website=MalaysiaNow |language=en}}</ref> His government has faced criticism for conservative policies and controversies including the discharge not amounting to acquittal of Deputy Prime Minister [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] and the reduction of former prime minister [[Najib Razak]]'s prison sentence. As the ASEAN chairman from 2025 until 2026, Anwar [[Kuala Lumpur Peace Accord|facilitated a ceasefire]] between [[Thailand]] and [[Cambodia]], addressing the [[2025 Cambodia–Thailand border conflict|border dispute]] through regional diplomatic channels.<ref>{{Cite web |date=25 July 2025 |title=Thailand and Cambodia exchange heavy artillery fire as border battle expands |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/thailand-cambodia-exchange-heavy-artillery-fire-border-battle-expands-2025-07-25/ |access-date=25 July 2025 |website=Reuters |language=en-US}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Anwar bin Ibrahim was born on 10 August 1947 in [[Cherok Tok Kun]], [[Bukit Mertajam]], [[Crown Colony of Penang]], [[Malayan Union]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://klpos.com/v2/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=2171&Itemid=75 |date=7 August 2008 |title=Mampukah Anwar membuat 'come-back'? |author=Oleh Hafizah Kamarudin |website=Klpos |access-date=1 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080807160129/http://klpos.com/v2/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=2171&Itemid=75 |archive-date=7 August 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cnn.com/2022/11/25/asia/anwar-ibrahim-malaysia-new-prime-minister-explainer-intl-hnk/index.html |title=From prisoner to prim minister: the remarkable rise of Malaysia's Anwar Ibrahim |last=Chen |first=Heather |date=25 November 2022 |website=CNN |access-date=1 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221127061935/https://www.cnn.com/2022/11/25/asia/anwar-ibrahim-malaysia-new-prime-minister-explainer-intl-hnk/index.html |archive-date=27 November 2022}}</ref> However, during the [[2023 Malaysian state elections|2023 state elections]], he also stated that he was born in [[Sungai Bakap]], [[Seberang Perai Selatan]], [[Crown Colony of Penang]], [[Malayan Union]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/microsite/2023/08/08/sungai-bakap-a-town-of-greats-touts-pm/ |title=Sungai Bakap a town of greats, touts PM |last=Nambiar |first=Predeep |date=8 August 2023 |website=Free Malaysia Today|access-date=7 February 2025 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/08/08/anwar-i-was-born-in-sungai-bakap-pakatan-bn-better-win-this-penang-seat/84192 |title=Anwar: I was born in Sungai Bakap, Pakatan-BN better win this Penang seat |last=Mok |first=Opalyn |date=8 August 2023 |website=MalayMail |access-date=7 February 2025 }}</ref> His father, Ibrahim bin Abdul Rahman, started his career as a hospital porter, and later joined politics as a member of  UMNO.
Ibrahim won election as [[Seberang Tengah]] MP in 1959 and 1964, serving as the parliamentary secretary to the ministry of health from 1964 until his defeat in the [[1969 Malaysian general election|1969 general election]] by [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia]] candidate Mustapha Hussain. Anwar's mother, Che Yan binti Hussein, was a housewife active in UMNO grassroots politics in Penang who served as head of the UMNO Women for Bukit Mertajam division in Penang.<ref>Alias Muhammad (1994), p. 177</ref>

Anwar attended three primary schools, Sekolah Melayu Sungai Bakap (1955), Sekolah Melayu Cherok Tok Kun (1957) and Sekolah Rendah Stowell, [[Bukit Mertajam]] (1959) and undertook his secondary education at [[Bukit Mertajam High School]] ([[Malay language|Malay]]: [[:ms:Sekolah Menengah Kebangsaan Tinggi Bukit Mertajam|SMK Tinggi Bukit Mertajam]]) before continuing at [[Malay College Kuala Kangsar]] (MCKK). He attended Bukit Mertajam High School for three years, where he excelled academically. Anwar was selected to take a special examination for entry into Malay College Kuala Kangsar, one of only three students from [[Penang]] to be chosen for this prestigious institution.<ref>{{Cite web |date=30 August 2024 |title=安華抵大山腳 撥50萬回饋母校 |url=https://guangming.com.my/%E5%AE%89%E8%8F%AF%E6%8A%B5%E5%A4%A7%E5%B1%B1%E8%85%B3-%E6%92%A550%E8%90%AC%E5%9B%9E%E9%A5%8B%E6%AF%8D%E6%A0%A1 |access-date=30 August 2024 |website=光明日报 |language=zh-TW}}</ref> While at MCKK, he has been involved in various organised activities and represented MCKK in the debate competition between schools. He was also active in the Malay Language Association and his school oratory representative. While studying there, he got to know many future political leaders, like [[Sanusi Junid]], [[Kamaruddin Jaafar]] and [[Yahaya Ahmad]]. He was the [[St John Ambulance]]'s youth cadet. He also served as the Secretary of the ''Badan Revolusi Agama'' Association, where the Chairman was [[Sanusi Junid]], his senior.

Anwar later pursued higher education at the [[University of Malaya]], earning a bachelor's degree of arts (BA) in Malay Studies.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Time Chooses Anwar Ibrahim Among The Top 100 |url=http://www.iiit.org/news/time-chooses-anwar-ibrahim-among-the-top-100 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171001045434/http://www.iiit.org/news/time-chooses-anwar-ibrahim-among-the-top-100 |archive-date=1 October 2017 |access-date=25 December 2016}}</ref> Anwar also worked on his Master of Arts (MA) in Literature through the [[National University of Malaysia]] while imprisoned from 1974 to 1975.<ref>Allers, Charles, ''The Evolution of a Muslim Democrat: The Life of Malaysia's Anwar Ibrahim'' (New York: Peter Lang, 2013), 57.</ref>

==Early years and activism (1968–1982)==
From 1968 to 1971, as a student, Anwar was the president of the National Union of Malaysian Muslim Students. Around the same time, he was also the president of the University of Malaya Malay Language Society Persatuan Bahasa Melayu Universiti Malaya (PBMUM)). In 1971, he was a member of the pro tem committee of [[Malaysian Islamic Youth Movement|Angkatan Belia Islam Malaysia]] (ABIM) or Muslim Youth Movement of Malaysia, which he co-founded. At the same time, he was elected as the 2nd President of the Malaysian Youth Council or Majlis Belia Malaysia (MBM).

In 1974, Anwar was arrested during student protests against rural poverty and hunger. This came as a report surfaced stating that a family died from starvation in a village in [[Baling District|Baling]], in the state of [[Kedah]], which was later demonstrated to be false. However, the rubber tappers in Baling were experiencing severe hardship as the price of rubber dropped in 1974. He was imprisoned under the [[Internal Security Act 1960|Internal Security Act]] (ISA), which allowed for detention without trial and spent 20 months in the [[Kamunting Detention Centre]].

From 1975 until 1982, he served as a representative for Asia Pacific of the [[World Assembly of Muslim Youth]] (WAMY).<ref name="oxfordislamicstudies.com">{{cite web |url=http://www.oxfordislamicstudies.com/article/opr/t125/e964 |title=Ibrahim, Anwar |website=Oxford Islamic Studies Online |access-date=1 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160802033832/http://www.oxfordislamicstudies.com/article/opr/t125/e964 |archive-date=2 August 2016}}</ref>

Anwar Ibrahim is also the co-founder of the [[International Institute of Islamic Thought]] (IIIT) in the USA (founded in 1981).<ref>{{cite book |last1=Esposito |first1=John L. |title=The Oxford Dictionary of Islam |date=21 October 2004 |publisher=Oxford University Press |isbn=978-0-19-975726-8 |page=139 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=E324pQEEQQcC&pg=PA139 |access-date=27 November 2022 |language=en}}</ref> Anwar has been one of four acting directors, a board member of IIIT<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iiit.org/news/iiit-vice-president-visits-iium-and-attends-world-conference|title=IIIT Vice President Visits IIUM and Attends World Conference|website=International Institute of Islamic Thought (IIIT)|access-date=16 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161226061057/http://www.iiit.org/news/iiit-vice-president-visits-iium-and-attends-world-conference|archive-date=26 December 2016}}</ref> and a trustee.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iiit.org/news/anwar-ibrahim-reflects-on-the-aftermath-of-the-arab-spring|title=Anwar Ibrahim Reflects on the Aftermath of the Arab Spring|website=International Institute of Islamic Thought (IIIT)|access-date=16 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171001044638/http://www.iiit.org/news/anwar-ibrahim-reflects-on-the-aftermath-of-the-arab-spring|archive-date=1 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iiit.org/news/time-chooses-anwar-ibrahim-among-the-top-100|title=TIME Chooses Anwar Ibrahim Among the Top 100|website=International Institute of Islamic Thought (IIIT)|access-date=16 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171001045434/http://www.iiit.org/news/time-chooses-anwar-ibrahim-among-the-top-100|archive-date=1 October 2017}}</ref> He was also a chancellor of [[International Islamic University Malaysia]] (IIUM) between 1988 and 1998.<ref name="oxfordislamicstudies.com"/>

==Early political career (1982–1993)==
{{Conservatism in Malaysia|Politician}}
In 1982, Anwar, who was the founding leader and second president of ABIM, joined the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO), which was led by [[Mahathir Mohamad]], who had become prime minister in 1981. Anwar's decision was influenced by the advice of Palestinian-American scholar [[Ismail al-Faruqi]], who recognised the Mahathir administration's commitment to its Islamization agenda.<ref>{{cite book |last=Noor |first=Farish A. |title=Islam Embedded: The Historical Development of the Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party PAS (1951-2003), Volume 1 |url=http://hdl.handle.net/1828/8128| publisher=MSRI |year=2004 |isbn=9839986678 |page=307 |hdl=1828/8128 |language=en}}</ref>

In the 1982 general election, he was elected as the MP for [[Permatang Pauh (federal constituency)|Permatang Pauh]], which was created in the 1974 redistribution from parts of the [[Seberang Tengah]] constituency that his father had represented from 1959 to 1969. He defeated a candidate from PAS, even though the seat was regarded as a PAS stronghold.

Anwar rapidly rose to high-ranking positions; he first entered cabinet in 1982 as [[Deputy Minister in the Prime Minister's Department]]; his first ministerial office was that of [[Minister of Youth and Sports (Malaysia)|Minister of Culture, Youth and Sports]] in 1983; after that, he headed the [[Minister of Agriculture and Food Security (Malaysia)|Agriculture Ministry]] in 1984 before becoming [[Minister of Education (Malaysia)|Minister of Education]] in 1986.

During his tenure as education minister, Anwar introduced numerous policies in the national school curriculum. One of his major changes was to rename the national language from ''Bahasa Malaysia'' to ''[[Bahasa Melayu]]'', a decision later reverted in 2007 under the premiership of [[Abdullah Ahmad Badawi]].<ref>{{Cite web |author1=AUDREY EDWARDS |author2=WONG CHUN WAI |title=Back to Bahasa Malaysia |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2007/06/04/back-to-bahasa-malaysia |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=The Star |date=4 June 2007 |language=en}}</ref> Analysts and politicians have also attributed the rise in [[social conservatism]] and [[Islamism]] among Malays to the reforms and Islamisation of the education system done under Anwar, with former law minister [[Zaid Ibrahim]] labelling Anwar an "Islamist".<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 April 2015 |title=Growing popularity of conservative Islam in Malaysia linked to overseas students |url=https://www.scmp.com/news/asia/article/1753788/growing-popularity-conservative-islam-malaysia-can-be-linked-experiences |access-date=26 May 2024 |website=South China Morning Post |language=en}}</ref>

As education minister, Anwar was elected as the 25th President of [[UNESCO#General Conference|UNESCO's General Conference]]. In 1988, Anwar Ibrahim became the second President of IIUM.<ref>{{cite web |url = http://www.iium.edu.my/rector/resource-centre-2 |title = International Islamic University Malaysia |access-date = 10 February 2015 |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20141012125533/http://www.iium.edu.my/rector/resource-centre-2 |archive-date = 12 October 2014 }}</ref>

In 1991, Anwar was appointed as [[Minister of Finance (Malaysia)|Minister of Finance]]. During his tenure as finance minister, his impact was immediate; Malaysia enjoyed unprecedented prosperity and economic growth. Shortly after becoming Finance Minister, [[Euromoney]] named him as a top-four finance minister and in 1996 [[Asiamoney]] named him ''Finance Minister of the Year''. In the midst of the [[1997 Asian financial crisis]], Anwar, as a deputy prime minister and finance minister, was hailed for guiding Malaysia through the period of instability. Anwar backed free-market principles and highlighted the proximity of business and politics in Malaysia. He advocated greater accountability, refused to offer government bail-outs and instituted widespread spending cuts. In March 1998, Anwar was selected as the chairman of the Development Committee of [[World Bank]] and [[International Monetary Fund]] (IMF) from March 1998 until September 1998.<ref>{{cite web|url= http://web.worldbank.org/WBSITE/EXTERNAL/DEVCOMMEXT/0,,contentMDK:23158522~menuPK:7347977~pagePK:7347738~piPK:7347796~theSitePK:277473,00.html |title=Development Committee – Former Chairmen of the Development Committee|publisher= The World bank | access-date =10 February 2015}}</ref>

==Deputy premiership (1993–1998)==
In 1993, he became Mahathir's [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|deputy prime minister]] after beating [[Abdul Ghafar Baba]] for the position of Deputy President in UMNO. Anwar's allusions in public to his "son-father" relationship with Mahathir contributed to view that he was to be Mahathir's successor.

In May 1997, Mahathir appointed Anwar as acting prime minister while he embarked on a two-month working holiday.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lib.perdana.org.my/PLF/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/1996-1998/1997ko/mahathir%20returing%20holiday.pdf |title=Mahathir Returns After Two Months Holiday|publisher= [[Bernama]] |date =22 July 1997|via=[[Perdana Leadership Foundation#Library|Perdana Leadership Foundation Library]]}}</ref> In Mahathir's absence, Anwar had independently taken radical steps, which directly conflicted with Mahathir's policies, to change the country's governing mechanisms. Issues such as how Malaysia would respond to a financial crisis were often at the forefront of this conflict. Towards the end of the 1990s, his relationship with Mahathir began to deteriorate, triggered by their conflicting views on governance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://edition.cnn.com/ASIANOW/asiaweek/97/0801/eye1.html |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20220701093505/http://edition.cnn.com/ASIANOW/asiaweek/97/0801/eye1.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=1 July 2022 |title=A Day in the Life|author=Roger Mitton|publisher=[[Asiaweek]] |date =30 November 2000}}</ref><ref name=split>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysianbar.org.my/article/news/legal-and-general-news/general-news/splits-in-umno-and-opposition-unity|title=Splits in Umno and Opposition unity|author=Wong Chin Huat|work=[[The Sun (Malaysia)|The Sun]]|date=17 August 2007|access-date=29 September 2021|via=[[Malaysian Bar]]}}</ref>

===Financial crisis===
During the [[1997 Asian financial crisis|1997 Asian Financial Crisis]], Anwar supported the IMF plan as a finance minister. He also instituted an [[austerity]] package that cut government spending by 18%, cut ministerial salaries and deferred major projects. "Mega projects", despite being a cornerstone of Mahathir's development strategy, were greatly curtailed.<ref name=split/>

Although many Malaysian companies faced bankruptcy, Anwar declared: "There is no question of any bailout. The banks will be allowed to protect themselves and the government will not interfere." Anwar advocated a free-market approach to the crisis, including foreign investment and trade liberalisation. Mahathir blamed currency speculators like [[George Soros]] for the crisis and supported currency controls and tighter regulation of foreign investment.<ref>{{cite news |last=O'Brien |first=Timothy L. |date=6 December 1998 |title=He's Seen The Enemy. It Looks Like Him |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1998/12/06/business/he-s-seen-the-enemy-it-looks-like-him.html?scp=5&sq=george |access-date=9 January 2012 |work=The New York Times}}</ref>

===Fall from power and first sodomy conviction===
{{main|Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials}}

In 1998 the ''[[Newsweek]]'' magazine named Anwar the "Asian of the Year" in spite of being beaten by the then Inspector General of Police.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Kennedy |first1=Fiona |title=An ode to democracy delivered by Anwar Ibrahim |url= https://www.uq.edu.au/news/article/2006/07/ode-democracy-delivered-anwar-ibrahim |access-date=27 November 2022 |work=UQ News |publisher=The University of Queensland |date=27 July 2006 |location=Brisbane }}</ref> However, in that year, matters between Anwar and Mahathir came to a head around the quadrennial UMNO General Assembly. The [[UMNO Youth|Youth wing of UMNO]], headed by Anwar's associate [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]], gave notice that it would initiate a debate on "cronyism and nepotism".  At the General Assembly, a book by Khalid Jafri, ''"[[50 Dalil Mengapa Anwar Tidak Boleh Jadi PM]]"'' ("50 Arguments Why Anwar Shouldn't Be Prime Minister") was circulated containing graphic allegations of homosexuality, as well as accusations of corruption against Anwar. Khalid Jafri was an ex-editor of the government-controlled newspaper ''[[Utusan Malaysia]]'' and former editor-in-chief of a failed magazine, ''Harian National''. Anwar obtained a court injunction to prevent further distribution of the book and filed a lawsuit against the author for defamation. Police charged the author of the book with malicious publishing of false news.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Judge gives reasons for ruling in favour of Anwar |url= http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news.cfm?NewsID=32237 |work=Daily Express |location= Kota Kinabalu |date=26 January 2005 |access-date=3 February 2010 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20110621232024/http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news.cfm?NewsID=32237 |archive-date=21 June 2011 }}</ref> The police were instructed to investigate the veracity of the claims.

On 2 September 1998, Anwar was fired from the Cabinet, and many reports state that he was under investigation for [[sodomy]], an act that is illegal in Malaysia. The following day, he was expelled from UMNO. In what the ''[[Sydney Morning Herald]]'' termed a blatantly political fix-up,<ref>{{Cite news| url= https://www.smh.com.au/opinion/politics/outdated-political-thuggery-embarrasses-malaysia-20100222-ornl.html |work=The Sydney Morning Herald | title=Outdated political thuggery embarrasses Malaysia | first=Peter |last=Hartcher | date=23 February 2010}}</ref> Anwar was arrested on 20 September 1998 and detained without trial under the country's controversial Internal Security Act (ISA). Weeks later, Anwar was charged with corruption for allegedly interfering with his own police investigation. While he was in police custody in 1998, Anwar was beaten by the [[Inspector-General of Police (Malaysia)|Inspector General of Police]], [[Abdul Rahim Mohd Noor]]. The public and the media only witnessed his black eye after being brought to Court for the first time. Rahim was subsequently found guilty of assault and jailed for two months in 2000. He made a public apology to Anwar and paid undisclosed damages.<ref>{{cite news |title=Apology ends Anwar's suit over beating |url= https://www.nytimes.com/2005/08/03/world/asia/03iht-malaysia.html |access-date=11 January 2012 |newspaper=The New York Times |date=4 August 2005}}</ref>

During the trial, a mattress supposedly stained with Anwar's semen was presented to the court as [[DNA evidence]] of Anwar's sexual acts. Anwar denied having anything to do with the mattress, although the DNA tests came out positive. Lim Kong Boon, a DNA chemist, testified during the trial that DNA taken from 10 of 13 semen stains on the mattress matched Anwar's DNA.<ref>{{Cite news |url= http://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=1998&dt=1231&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Muka_Hadapan&pg=fp_03.htm |work=Utusan Malaysia |location=Kuala Lumpur |title=Saksi: Kesan di tilam milik Anwar, Shamsidar |access-date=20 August 2018 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20180820075412/http://ww1.utusan.com.my/utusan/info.asp?y=1998&dt=1231&pub=Utusan_Malaysia&sec=Muka_Hadapan&pg=fp_03.htm |archive-date=20 August 2018 }}</ref><ref name="CBS89">Alvin Ung [https://www.cbsnews.com/news/a-stain-upon-anwars-defense/ CBS-489A Stain Upon Anwar's Defense], ''The Associated Press'' (via ''CBS News''). 30 December 1998</ref><ref name="Anwar trial hears DNA evidence">[https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/244570.stm Anwar trial hears DNA evidence] 30 December 1998, ''BBC News''</ref> The defence team implied that DNA samples may have been taken from Anwar, while unconscious, after his beating in police custody, to create false forensics evidence to frame Anwar; however, High Court Judge Augustine Paul accepted that DNA evidence.<ref name=CBS89/><ref name="Anwar trial hears DNA evidence"/>

The High Court eventually handed down its decision in April 1999, sentencing Anwar to six years' imprisonment for corruption and sodomy. Two months later, he was sentenced to nine years' imprisonment for sodomy, which he was ordered to serve after completing his six-year sentence for corruption.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www7.nationalacademies.org/humanrights/Cases/CHR_068423.htm |title=Case Information: Anwar Ibrahim |date=6 June 2014 |access-date=10 February 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150213034949/http://www7.nationalacademies.org/humanrights/Cases/CHR_068423.htm |archive-date=13 February 2015 |website=Committee on Human Rights of the [[National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine]] }}</ref> His trial and conviction were widely discredited by the international community. [[Amnesty International]] stated that the trial proceedings "''exposed a pattern of political manipulation of key state institutions including the police, public prosecutor's office and the judiciary''"<ref>{{Cite web |last=Avasthy |first=Divya |date=2014-03-08 |title=Malaysia's Anwar Ibrahim Convicted of Sodomy, Court Overturns Earlier Verdict |url=https://www.ibtimes.co.uk/malaysias-anwar-ibrahim-convicted-sodomy-court-overturns-earlier-verdict-1439435 |access-date=2026-01-25 |website=International Business Times UK |language=en}}</ref> and called Anwar a [[prisoner of conscience]], arguing that his arrest was intended to silence political opposition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lrwc.org/canadian-lawyers-malaysia/|title=Canadian Lawyers Defend the Independence of the Bar in Malaysia|date=19 March 2002|website=Lawyers' Rights Watch Canada}}</ref>

The Federal Court overturned his conviction and Anwar was finally released from solitary confinement on 2 September 2004.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.specialistspeakers.com/?p=1271|title=Anwar Ibrahim|author=Specialist Speakers Profile|access-date=10 February 2015}}</ref>

==''Reformasi'' and formation of KEADILAN (1998–1999)==
{{main|Reformasi (Malaysia)}}

Shortly after Anwar was dismissed as deputy prime minister, Anwar and his supporters initiated the [[Reformasi (Malaysia)|Reformasi]] movement. It consisted of several mass demonstrations and rallies against the long-standing Barisan Nasional coalition government.

Reformasi led to the formation of a new multiracial-based party named ''[[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)#Foundation|Parti Keadilan Nasional]]'' or National Justice Party (KEADILAN). In 1999, a general election was held. The new KEADILAN, [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS), and [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) formed a ''[[Barisan Alternatif]]'' or Alternative Front (BA), in a combined initiative to replace the standing Barisan Nasional (BN) coalition government. In August 2003, KEADILAN merged with ''[[Parti Rakyat Malaysia]]'' or Malaysian's People Party (PRM) to form ''[[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|Parti Keadilan Rakyat]]'' or People's Justice Party (PKR) headed by Wan Azizah as president. PKR made huge gains in the 2008 general election, winning 31 seats and becoming the parliament's largest opposition party. In April 2008, PKR, PAS and DAP formed a new alliance named [[Pakatan Rakyat]] (PR).<ref>{{cite news |last=Cheah |first=Royce |date=1 April 2008 |url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2008/4/1/nation/20080401134152&sec=nation |title=PKR, PAS, DAP to form Pakatan Rakyat (updated) |access-date=10 February 2015 |newspaper=[[The Star (Malaysia)]] }}</ref>

==Interim years (1999–2007)==
===First sodomy conviction===
In 1999, Anwar brought suit against Prime Minister Mahathir for [[defamation]] for allegedly uttering accusations of immoral acts and calling Anwar a homosexual at a news conference in Malaysia.<ref>{{Cite news |title=Anwar vs Dr M: Two of three judges recuse themselves (Updated) | url= http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/3/1/nation/20100301133142&sec=nation | work = The Star| date = 1 March 2010 | access-date=1 March 2010}}</ref>

The sodomy verdict was overturned in 2004, resulting in Anwar's release from prison as he had already served his sentence for the corruption offence.<ref>{{cite news|url = https://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/asia-pacific/3633336.stm |title = Court to allow Anwar appeal | date = 7 September 2004|work= [[BBC News]]|access-date= 14 January 2012}}</ref> Anwar successfully sued Khalid Jafri for his ''50 Dalil Kenapa Anwar Tidak Boleh Jadi PM'' book, but Khalid died in 2005 of complications from diabetes before the High Court found that he had committed [[libel]] and awarded Anwar millions of ringgit in compensation.<ref>{{Cite news|title=What is RM100&nbsp;million? |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/opinion/jameschin/27869-what-is-rm100-million |work=Malaysian Insider |date=28 May 2009 |access-date=30 January 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090715084833/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/index.php/opinion/jameschin/27869-what-is-rm100-million |archive-date=15 July 2009 }}</ref> The Federal Court on 8 March 2010 ruled that the 1998 dismissal of Anwar from his Cabinet posts by Mahathir was constitutional and valid, meaning Anwar had failed in his bid to challenge his sacking.<ref>{{Cite news | title = Court: Anwar's sacking from cabinet posts is valid | url = http://www.mmail.com.my/content/29538-court-anwars-sacking-cabinet-posts-valid | work =Malay Mail| date = 8 March 2010 | access-date = 8 March 2010 | archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20100309110250/http://www.mmail.com.my/content/29538-court-anwars-sacking-cabinet-posts-valid | archive-date = 9 March 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news| title = Anwar's Sacking From Cabinet Posts In 1998 Is Valid, Federal Court Rules | url = http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newsindex.php?id=480510 | work = Bernama |date=8 March 2010| access-date=8 March 2010}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|title=Update Anwar's sacking was lawful, rules Federal Court |url=http://www.theedgemalaysia.com/political-news/161024-anwars-sacking-was-lawful-rules-federal-court-.html |work=TheEdge |date=8 March 2010 |access-date=8 March 2010 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100426113552/http://www.theedgemalaysia.com/political-news/161024-anwars-sacking-was-lawful-rules-federal-court-.html |archive-date=26 April 2010 }}</ref>

An appeal on the corruption charges was heard on 6 September 2004. Under Malaysian law, a person is banned from political activities for five years after the end of his sentence. Success in this appeal would have allowed him to return to politics immediately. On 7 September, the court agreed to hear Anwar's appeal. However, on 15 September, the Court of Appeal ruled unanimously that its previous decision to uphold a High Court ruling that found Anwar guilty was in order, relegating Anwar to the sidelines of Malaysian politics until 14 April 2008. The only way for Anwar to have been freed from this structure would have been for him to receive a pardon from the [[King of Malaysia]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Malaysia's Anwar declares 'new dawn' after his release |url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2018/5/16/malaysia-anwar-ibrahim-released-after-getting-full-pardon |access-date=27 November 2022 |work=AL Jazeera website |publisher=Al Jazeera |date=16 May 2018 |language=en}}</ref>

===Teaching and non-profit work===
After his release from prison, Anwar held teaching positions at [[St Antony's College, Oxford]], where he was a visiting fellow and senior associate member, at Johns Hopkins University's [[Paul H. Nitze School of Advanced International Studies|School of Advanced International Studies]] in Washington, D.C., as a Distinguished Senior Visiting Fellow, and in 2005–2006 as a visiting professor at the [[Prince Alwaleed Center for Muslim–Christian Understanding]] in the [[Edmund A. Walsh School of Foreign Service|School of Foreign Service]] at [[Georgetown University]].<ref>Anwar Ibrahim {{cite web|url=http://acmcu.georgetown.edu/119089.html |title=Anwar Ibrahim – ACMCU – Georgetown University |access-date=29 July 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111129011811/http://acmcu.georgetown.edu/119089.html |archive-date=29 November 2011}}</ref> In March 2006 he was appointed as Honorary President of the London-based organisation [[AccountAbility (Institute of Social and Ethical AccountAbility)]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.accountability.org/ |title=AccountAbility |access-date=16 July 2008}}</ref>

In July 2006, Anwar was elected Chair of the Washington-based [[Foundation For the Future]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.foundationforfuture.org/press_releases/july15.html |title=Foundation for the Future Holds its First Board Meeting in Doha, Qatar |access-date=10 October 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070616074130/http://www.foundationforfuture.org/press_releases/july15.html |archive-date=16 June 2007}}. Foundation for the Future. 15 July 2006</ref> In this capacity, he signed 1 October 2006 letter to Robin Cleveland of the [[World Bank]], requesting the transfer of [[Shaha Riza]] from the [[US Department of State]] to the Foundation for the Future. This transaction led to Paul Wolfowitz's resignation as president of the organisation.<ref>[http://web.worldbank.org/WBSITE/EXTERNAL/NEWS/0,,contentMDK:21297772~menuPK:34463~pagePK:34370~piPK:34424~theSitePK:4607,00.html Ethics Committee Case No2 and President's Paper, p.102], 2 June 2005, ''World Bank''</ref> He was one of the signatories of "[[A Common Word Between Us and You]]" in 2007, an open letter by Islamic scholars to Christian leaders, calling for peace and understanding.

===Return to politics===
In November 2006, Anwar announced he planned to run for Parliament in 2008 after his disqualification expired. Anwar was critical of government policies after his release from prison, most notably the [[Malaysian New Economic Policy|New Economic Policy]] (NEP), which provided [[affirmative action]] for the Bumiputras. The policy set a number of quotas, such as for units of housing and [[initial public offering]]s, that must be met.<ref>[https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=aAjtL0wmitME&refer=home Malaysia's Anwar Says He Plans to Run for Parliament]. 30 November 2006, ''Bloomberg''.</ref>

Before he became re-entitled to run for Parliament in 2008, he acted as an "advisor" of [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]], the party of which his wife Dr Wan Azizah was president. He was at the forefront in organising a November 2007 mass rally, called the [[2007 Bersih Rally]], which took place at [[Dataran Merdeka]], Kuala Lumpur, to demand clean and fair elections. The gathering was organised by [[Bersih]], a coalition comprising political parties and civil society groups, and drew supporters from all over the country.<ref>{{cite news |title=Malaysia police use water cannon at Anwar rally |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/latestCrisis/idUSSP165982 |access-date=27 November 2022 |work=Reuters |agency=Reuters |date=10 November 2007 |language=en}}</ref>

The [[2008 Malaysian general election|2008 general election]] date was set for 8 March 2008, before Anwar's disqualification from politics expired, sparking criticisms that Barisan Nasional called for early elections in a bid to deny Anwar's plans for a return to Parliament. In response, Anwar's wife, [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]], declared that she would step down should she retain her Permatang Pauh parliamentary seat to force a by-election in which Anwar himself would contest.<ref>[http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/specialreport/view/1457/1/.html Wan Azizah declares she is Anwar proxy] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121018224326/http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/specialreport/view/1457/1/.html |date=18 October 2012 }}. 25 February 2008. ''Channel NewsAsia''</ref>

When asked about the possibility of Anwar becoming the next Prime Minister, former leader Tun Dr Mahathir reacted by saying, "He would make a good Prime Minister of Israel".<ref>[http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/mni/when-mahathir-lashes-at-anwar-who-is-he-really-after.html "When Mahathir lashes out at Anwar, will it catch fire?"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080525104747/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/mni/when-mahathir-lashes-at-anwar-who-is-he-really-after.html |date=25 May 2008 }} 31 March 2008, ''The Malaysian Insider.''</ref>

On 14 April 2008, Anwar celebrated his official return to the political stage, as his ban from public office expired a decade after he was fired as deputy prime minister. The opposition seized a third of parliamentary seats and five states in the worst-ever showing for the Barisan Nasional coalition that has ruled for half a century, with Anwar at the helm.<ref>[http://www.inquirer.net/specialreports/inquirerpolitics/view.php?db=1&article=20080413-130108 "Malaysian opposition leader Anwar marks end of political ban"] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140525213520/http://www.inquirer.net/specialreports/inquirerpolitics/view.php?db=1&article=20080413-130108 |date=25 May 2014 }}, AFP, 14 April 2008.</ref> A gathering of more than 40,000 supporters greeted Anwar in a rally welcoming his return to politics. Police interrupted Anwar after he had addressed the rally for nearly two hours and called for him to stop the gathering since there was no legal permission for the rally.<ref>[http://www.foxnews.com/wires/2008Apr14/0,4670,MalaysiaAnwar,00.html Malaysia Police Halt Anwar Speech], AFP (via FoxNews), 14 April 2008.</ref>

On 29 April 2008, after 10 years of absence, he returned to the Parliament, albeit upon invitation as a spouse guest of Wan Azizah Wan Ismail, People's Justice Party and the first female opposition leader in Malaysian Parliament's history.<ref>[http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5jl2uOGMOucxq_mE5rD3BAogvtmmQ Malaysia's Anwar returns to Parliament] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110520164327/http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5jl2uOGMOucxq_mE5rD3BAogvtmmQ |date=20 May 2011 }}, Associated Press, 29 April 2008.</ref>

===Permatang Pauh by-election===
{{Main|2008 Permatang Pauh by-election}}

Anwar Ibrahim was victorious in the Permatang Pauh by-election held on 26 August 2008. [[Muhammad Muhammad Taib]], information chief of the UMNO, stated: "Yes, of course we have lost ... we were the underdogs going into this race."<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thestandard.com.hk/breaking_news_detail.asp?id=4470&icid=4&d_str=20080826 |title=Malaysian government declares by-election defeat to Anwar |work=Thestandard.com.hk |access-date=9 January 2012 |date=26 March 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111220200743/http://www.thestandard.com.hk/breaking_news_detail.asp?id=4470&icid=4&d_str=20080826 |archive-date=20 December 2011 }}</ref> Final results announced by the Election Commission revealed that Anwar Ibrahim won 31,195 of the estimated 47,000 votes cast in the district, while Arif Shah Omar Shah received 15,524 votes and a third candidate had 92 votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pr-inside.com/malaysia-s-anwar-wins-parliament-seat-r773685.htm |title=Malaysia's Anwar wins Parliament seat |work=Pr-inside.com |access-date=9 January 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120217035221/http://www.pr-inside.com/malaysia-s-anwar-wins-parliament-seat-r773685.htm |archive-date=17 February 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5gvk18e1IzUJ9V-VNzo1_wg-4mkWgD92Q0O300 |title=Malaysia's Anwar wins seat in parliament |access-date=26 August 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080829072524/http://ap.google.com/article/ALeqM5gvk18e1IzUJ9V-VNzo1_wg-4mkWgD92Q0O300 |archive-date=29 August 2008}}. ap.google.com. 26 August 2008</ref>

On 28 August 2008, Anwar, dressed in a dark blue traditional Malay outfit and black [[songkok]] hat, took the oath at the main chamber of Parliament house in [[Kuala Lumpur]], as MP for [[Permatang Pauh (federal constituency)|Permatang Pauh]] at 10.03&nbsp;am before Speaker Tan Sri Pandikar Amin Mulia. ==First term as Leader of the Opposition (2008–2015)==

On 26 August 2008, Anwar won re-election in the [[2008 Permatang Pauh by-election|Permatang Pauh by-election]] and returned to Parliament as [[Leader of the Opposition (Malaysia)|Leader of the Opposition]]. ===Parliamentary censure over APCO and 1Malaysia===
Anwar has made numerous remarks about a supposed conspiracy among the Malaysian government, [[APCO Worldwide|APCO]] (a public relations firm retained by the Malaysian government), Israel, and the United States. Anwar condemned the Malaysian government for seeking advice from APCO. He claims that the firm is linked to the "murder of Muslims in Palestine." He further claims to have given proof to a disciplinary committee of the Malaysian parliament, investigating his comments, that APCO is a front for the Israeli government. On another occasion, Anwar, speaking from the parliament, claimed that the firm was controlled by Jews and working on behalf of the American government to influence Malaysian foreign policy. He further implied that changes in Malaysian foreign policy could only be explained if Jews were manipulating Malaysia for the United States.<ref>[http://newledger.com/2010/04/the-reversal-of-anwar-ibrahim The reversal of Anwar Ibrahim] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100506061816/http://newledger.com/2010/04/the-reversal-of-anwar-ibrahim|date=6 May 2010}}, The New Ledger, 10 April 2010</ref>

On 22 April 2010 Anwar was [[censure]]d by Malaysia's parliament for remarks he made during a press conference in parliament on 30 March 2010. During the press conference, Anwar claimed to have documents linking [[1Malaysia]] with [[One Israel]] and the public relations firm APCO but refused to allow access to the documents when challenged.<ref name="thestar.com.my">{{cite web |title=Parliament: Anwar claims Apco was in M'sia before 1Malaysia concept was announced, The Star, 30 March 2010 | work=The Star Online |url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/3/30/nation/20100330150110&sec=nation |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100402222745/http://www.thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F3%2F30%2Fnation%2F20100330150110&sec=nation |archive-date=2 April 2010 |access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref> The Malaysian government and APCO have both strongly denied Anwar's allegations. The censure motion passed by the House of Representatives referred Anwar's case to the Rights and Privileges Committee which will recommend a punishment for approval by the full chamber. Such punishment could include being banned from parliament.<ref name="in.reuters.com">{{cite news |last=Ahmad |first=Razak |date=22 April 2010 |title=Malaysia parliament votes to censure Anwar |publisher=In.reuters.com |url=http://in.reuters.com/article/idINIndia-47905320100422 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160125063053/http://in.reuters.com/article/idINIndia-47905320100422 |archive-date=25 January 2016 |access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref><ref name="malaysianmirror1">[http://en.harakahdaily.net/index.php/berita-utama/247-apco-anwar-produces-documents-as-proof.html Apco: Anwar produces documents, exposes shady clients] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120822061942/http://en.harakahdaily.net/index.php/berita-utama/247-apco-anwar-produces-documents-as-proof.html|date=22 August 2012}}. ===2013 general election===
[[File:Anwar Ibrahim, May 2013.jpg|thumb|right|Anwar during GE13]]
{{Main|2013 Malaysian general election|Malaysia's Post General Election Rally 2013}}

In the [[2013 Malaysian general election|2013 general election]] (GE13), Anwar Ibrahim, as the Leader of the Opposition, led his [[Pakatan Rakyat]] coalition (comprising the three parties [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]], [[Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]], and [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]]) to contest in the election. On 25 February 2013, Pakatan Rakyat launched their [[2013 Malaysian general election#PR manifesto|manifesto]] titled ''The People's Manifesto: Pakatan the hope of the people'', pledging to reduce their financial burden, among other promises.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/222236|title=Manifesto to curb monopolies, spur economy|date=25 February 2013|access-date=10 February 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://dapmalaysia.org/repository/ENG-Manifesto-LEAFLET.pdf|title=Manifesto Pakatan Harapan Rakyat 2013 Summary|access-date=16 May 2018}}</ref> Anwar, who has taught at [[University of Oxford|Oxford]] and [[Georgetown University]], said he would return to academic life if he lost the next election to incumbent prime minister [[Najib Razak]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.abc.net.au/news/2012-08-18/an-anwar/4207706|title=Malaysia's Anwar to retire if he loses next polls|newspaper=ABC News|date=18 August 2012|access-date=16 May 2018}}</ref>

[[File:Anwar Ibrahim (8722796274).jpg|thumb|Anwar speaking at a rally denouncing the May 2013 election results.]]

Pakatan Rakyat did not achieve the regime change target in the election despite obtaining 50.9% of the popular vote compared to the 47.4% gained by Barisan Nasional. This was attributed to the heavy malapportionment of the electoral districts.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.malaysia-today.net/mtcolumns/guest-columnists/56609-pakatan-lost-but-not-defeated|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130510213028/http://www.malaysia-today.net/mtcolumns/guest-columnists/56609-pakatan-lost-but-not-defeated|title=Pakatan lost, but not defeated|archive-date=10 May 2013}}</ref> Pakatan Rakyat also gained 7 more parliamentary seats compared to the [[2008 Malaysian general election|12th general election]].

Anwar did not concede defeat, alleging widespread electoral fraud. ''[[Al Jazeera English|Al Jazeera]]'' reported that Anwar Ibrahim came close to winning the election on 5 May 2013 but refused to admit defeat, and therefore also did not step down.<ref>[https://www.aljazeera.com/indepth/spotlight/malaysiaelections/2013/05/201351125934750972.html "Profile: Anwar Ibrahim"], Kuala Lumpur, 6 May 2013. Retrieved on 10 May 2013.</ref> In an interview for [[ABC News (Australia)|''ABC News'']], interviewer Jim Middleton suggested that Anwar Ibrahim was going to be the [[Lee Kuan Yew]] of the Malaysian opposition.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-05-07/extended-interview-anwar-ibrahim/4673518 |title=Extended Interview: Anwar Ibrahim |website=[[ABC News (Australia)]] |last=Middleton |first=Jim |date=8 May 2013 |access-date=1 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130512104150/http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-05-07/extended-interview-anwar-ibrahim/4673518 |archive-date=12 May 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H59DTFzzxuA?t=7m53s |title=Malaysian opposition leader refuses to concede |author=Australian Network News |date=6 May 2013 |website=YouTube |access-date=1 September 2024 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130508223336/http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H59DTFzzxuA&gl=US&hl=en |archive-date=8 May 2013}}</ref> Anwar answered that he was not at that stage yet.

On 7 May 2013, Anwar Ibrahim vowed to lead a "fierce movement" to reform the country's electoral system and challenge the results of an election he lost.<ref>Siva Sithraputhran.[https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/229379 "Pakatan to hold rally tomorrow against 'polls fraud'"], ''[[Malaysiakini]]'', 7 May 2013. Retrieved on 10 May 2013.</ref> On 8 May 2013, about 120,000 people wearing black gathered at [[MBPJ Stadium|Petaling Jaya Stadium]], [[Kelana Jaya]], Selangor, to attend a rally organised by Anwar to protest against the election results and demand a free and fair election.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/229564 | title=120,000 show of force at post-GE13 rally | publisher=Malaysiakini | date=8 May 2013 | access-date=10 May 2013}}</ref> The #Black505 movement continued for another 2 months with touring all over the country. A successful fundraising #Black505 dinner was held in [[Thean Hou Temple]] hall on 22 June 2013, hosted by Wangsa Maju MP Datuk Dr [[Tan Kee Kwong]] on behalf of PKR. Anwar Ibrahim immediately launched [[election petition|electoral petitions]] for over 30 disputed parliamentary seats in July 2013. However, the cases were thrown off election court for technical reasons. ===2008–2014 sodomy trials===
{{Main|Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials}}

On 29 June 2008, online news portal [[Malaysiakini]] reported that a male aide of Anwar, [[Saiful Bukhari Azlan]], had lodged a police report claiming that he had been sodomised by Anwar.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Aide alleges sodomy: Report lodged |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/85246 |work=Malaysiakini |publisher=Mkini Dotcom Sdn Bhd |date=28 June 2008 |access-date=28 June 2008 }}</ref> Anwar said that the possibility of a prison sentence as a result of the allegations could be seen as an attempt to remove him from the leadership of the opposition following his growing support and by-election victories. He also reaffirmed his innocence and cited evidence in the form of medical reports.<ref name=the-diplomat.com/> In July 2008, he was arrested over allegations of sodomy again, but was acquitted of the charge in January 2012 by [[High Courts of Malaysia|The High Court]].<ref name="aljazeera.com">*{{cite news|title=Anwar Ibrahim found not guilty of sodomy|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/news/asia-pacific/2012/01/201219295952572.html|access-date=9 January 2012|work=Aljazeera|date=9 January 2012}}</ref> The presiding judge ruled that DNA evidence used in the case had been compromised and was unreliable.<ref>{{cite news|title=Malaysia court finds Anwar Ibrahim not guilty of sodomy|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-16463989|publisher=BBC News Asia|date=9 January 2011|access-date=9 January 2012}}</ref> The prosecution filed an appeal against the acquittal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/prosecution-appeals-anwar-sodomy-acquittal/ |title=Prosecution appeals Anwar sodomy acquittal |author=Shazwan Mustafa Kamal |date=20 January 2012 |work=[[The Malaysian Insider]] |access-date=20 January 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120122223637/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/prosecution-appeals-anwar-sodomy-acquittal |archive-date=22 January 2012 }}</ref>

On 7 March 2014, the [[Malaysian Court of Appeal|Court of Appeal]] overturned the acquittal by unanimously deciding that the High Court failed to "critically evaluate" the evidence submitted by government chemist Dr Seah Lay Hong.<ref>{{cite web |title=Anwar given 5 years' jail after appellate court reverses sodomy acquittal |date=7 March 2014 |url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/anwar-acquittal-reversed#sthash.AjQ9kE5q.dpuf |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140310201934/http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/anwar-acquittal-reversed |archive-date=10 March 2014 |access-date=8 November 2022}}</ref> The Court of Appeal rushed through a unanimous decision, signed by all three judges, and sentenced Anwar to five years imprisonment, disqualifying him from nomination in the Kajang by-election scheduled on 11 March. The conviction was viewed by some as a politically motivated attempt to prevent Anwar from contesting in the [[2014 Kajang by-election]], which he was expected to win and becoming Selangor's chief minister.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://perseus-strategies.com/wp-content/uploads/2016/05/Anwar-White-Paper-5.23.16.pdf|title=White Paper On the Case of Anwar Ibrahim - Citizen of Malaysia v Government of Malaysia}}</ref> Human Rights Watch and the [[International Commission of Jurists]] have accused the Malaysian government of meddling in this particular judicial matter and said the verdict was politically motivated.<ref name="AnwarVerdictReversed">{{cite news|title=Court rules Malaysian opposition leader guilty of sodomizing aide|url=http://www.malaysianews.net/index.php/sid/220372179/scat/48cba686fe041718/ht/Court-rules-Malaysian-opposition-leader-guilty-of-sodomizing-aide|access-date=8 March 2014|publisher=Malaysia News.Net|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140308074906/http://www.malaysianews.net/index.php/sid/220372179/scat/48cba686fe041718/ht/Court-rules-Malaysian-opposition-leader-guilty-of-sodomizing-aide|archive-date=8 March 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.voanews.com/a/malaysia-judiciary-criticized-for-anwar-ibrahim-verdict-/1867850.html|title=Malaysia Judiciary Criticized Over Anwar Ibrahim Verdict|work=VOA|date=10 March 2014 |access-date=10 February 2015}}</ref> On 10 February 2015, the [[Federal Court of Malaysia]] upheld the decision of the Court of Appeal and affirmed the five-year prison sentence. He was sent immediately to [[Sungai Buloh Prison]], Selangor, to serve the sentence.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/anwar-guilty |title=ANWAR GUILTY |access-date=10 February 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150210065326/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/anwar-guilty |archive-date=10 February 2015 }}</ref>

==Pakatan Harapan backbencher (2018–2020)==
===Royal pardon and release===
After the Pakatan Rakyat's dissolution during his imprisonment, a new opposition coalition named the [[Pakatan Harapan]] was formed with Anwar as the de facto leader [[Election in absentia|in absentia]]. The coalition had come into power by overthrowing Barisan Nasional in the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] (GE14). Following the formation of the new ruling government, Anwar was given a full [[royal pardon]] and was released from prison on 16 May 2018. He was designated to take over the reins from interim prime minister [[Mahathir Mohamad]] as planned and agreed by the coalition before GE14.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/anwar-ibrahim-freed-from-prison-royal-pardon-malaysia-10237840|title=Anwar Ibrahim freed from prison after royal pardon from Malaysian king|work=CNA |access-date=16 May 2018|archive-date=16 May 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180516074329/https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/anwar-ibrahim-freed-from-prison-royal-pardon-malaysia-10237840}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Anwar hails 'new dawn for Malaysia' following release from prison|url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/new-dawn-malaysia-anwar-ibrahim-no-malice-mahathir-10238874|archive-url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/anwar-ibrahim-release-prison-malaysia-mahathir-mohamad-10238874|archive-date=17 May 2018|access-date=16 May 2018|website=Channel NewsAsia}}</ref> In an interview, he justified his reluctance to immediately step into power on the basis that Mahathir appeared committed to the reform agenda, and was doing a good job tackling a "disintegrating" political system. He also added that he was keen to travel and honour speaking engagements.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/anwar-ibrahim-mahathir-najib-interview-10241488|title=Mahathir 'compassionate', Najib's fall from grace 'karma', says Anwar|work=Channel NewsAsia|access-date=5 June 2018|language=en-US|archive-date=12 June 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180612141205/https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/anwar-ibrahim-mahathir-najib-interview-10241488|url-status=dead}}</ref> Anwar also indicated that he initially had reservations about Mahathir's "reconciliation efforts", but he eventually forgave his former enemy after Mahathir showed "''compassion and concern which (Anwar) thought was absent in the past''".

===Return to parliament===
{{main|2018 Port Dickson by-election}}

Anwar returned to Parliament through the Port Dickson by-election on 13 October 2018,<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/port-dickson-by-election-voters-to-pick-new-lawmaker-in-7-cornered-fight-featuring |last=Hassan |first=Hazlin |date=13 October 2018 |title=Port Dickson by-election: Anwar Ibrahim storms to victory |access-date=1 September 2024 |website=The Straits Times |language=en}}</ref> after receiving a royal pardon for a sodomy conviction he maintained was politically motivated. He won the by-election with an increased majority, returning to Parliament for the first time in three years.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/anwar-vows-to-make-parliament-truly-effective-if-he-wins-port-dickson-by-election |date=21 September 2018 |title=Anwar vows to make Parliament 'truly effective' if he wins Port Dickson by-election |last=Arshad |first=Arlina |website=The Straits Times |access-date=1 September 2024 |language=en |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180922014419/https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/anwar-vows-to-make-parliament-truly-effective-if-he-wins-port-dickson-by-election |archive-date=22 September 2018}}</ref> The seat had been vacated by army veteran [[Danyal Balagopal Abdullah]], a member of parliament for Anwar's PKR.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Port Dickson MP announces resignation, makes way for Anwar |url=https://www.thestartv.com/v/port-dickson-mp-announces-resignation-makes-way-for-anwar |access-date=25 May 2024 |website=The Star TV |language=en}}</ref>

===Sheraton Move and return to opposition===
{{main|2020–21 Malaysian political crisis}}

In late February 2020, the Pakatan Harapan government [[2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis|collapsed]] following defections by Anwar's deputy, [[Azmin Ali]], and several MPs aligned to him, along with the withdrawal of [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party|BERSATU]] by [[Muhyiddin Yassin]] from the coalition. Mahathir promptly resigned from government, as well as from BERSATU. The political manoeuvring has been labelled the "Sheraton Move" for the hotel in [[Petaling Jaya]] at which defecting MPs gathered with the opposition to show support for a new government.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ratcliffe |first1=Rebecca |title=Malaysia's political turmoil: everything you need to know |url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/feb/25/malaysias-political-turmoil-everything-you-need-to-know |access-date=24 September 2020 |agency=Guardian |date=25 February 2020}}</ref> Following the resignation of Mahathir, Anwar attempted to gather support for his appointment as prime minister. However, BERSATU president, [[Muhyiddin Yassin]], was eventually appointed prime minister, with the support of a slender majority in the Dewan Rakyat.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Ong |first1=Justin |title=Ex-PMO officer breaks down the 'Sheraton Move' |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/03/02/ex-pmo-officer-breaks-down-the-sheraton-move/1842542 |access-date=24 September 2020 |agency=Malay Mail |date=2 March 2020}}</ref> Thus, Pakatan Harapan returned to the opposition, with Anwar being named leader of the opposition.<ref>{{cite news |title=Pakatan officially names Anwar opposition leader |url=https://www.todayonline.com/world/anwar-appointed-opposition-leader-msia-says-pakatan-presidential-council |agency=Today Online |date=7 May 2020}}</ref>

==Second term as Leader of the Opposition (2020–2022)==
While Muhyiddin formed a government under the auspices of a new coalition known as [[Perikatan Nasional]], Mahathir attempted to regain his post with his Pakatan allies. However, under Anwar's leadership, KEADILAN refused to endorse Mahathir's bid for a third term as premier, claiming the nonagenarian had reneged on his repeated pledge to hand over the office to Anwar.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Razak |first1=Radzi |title=Anwar: Pakatan won't make same 'mistake' when choosing next PM |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/05/29/anwar-pakatan-wont-make-same-mistake-when-choosing-next-pm/1870494 |access-date=24 September 2020 |agency=Malay Mail |date=29 May 2020}}</ref> Mahathir, in turn, refused to lend support to Anwar's candidacy, and nominated [[Sabah]] Chief Minister, [[Shafie Apdal]], for the position of prime minister instead. Despite the support of allies DAP and Amanah for this compromise, KEADILAN again rejected the proposal and continued to propose Anwar as the coalition's candidate.<ref>{{cite news |title=Reject deceitful politics, Anwar posts after Shafie for PM announcement |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/532071 |access-date=24 September 2020 |agency=Malaysiakini |date=27 June 2020}}</ref>

=== Cabinet ===
{{Further|Anwar Ibrahim cabinet}}Anwar announced his cabinet on 2 December 2022, appointing himself as the [[Minister of Finance (Malaysia)|Minister of Finance]]. He also appointed [[President of the United Malays National Organisation|President of UMNO]] [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] and the Parliamentary Whip of [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS) [[Fadillah Yusof]] as the [[Deputy Prime Minister of Malaysia|Deputy Prime Ministers]]. This marked the first time Malaysia had two deputy prime ministers in office concurrently.

Zahid's appointment was controversial due to his ongoing trials for [[money laundering]], [[bribery]] and criminal breach of trust.<ref>{{Cite news |last1=Latiff |first1=Rozanna |last2=Ananthalakshmi |first2=A. |date=2 December 2022 |title=Malaysia PM Anwar to helm finance ministry |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/malaysia-pm-anwar-says-cabinet-line-up-be-announced-friday-report-2022-12-02/ }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2 December 2022 |title=Malaysia's Anwar names corruption-haunted Ahmad Zahid Hamidi as deputy PM |url=https://www.scmp.com/week-asia/politics/article/3201893/malaysias-anwar-ibrahim-names-graft-haunted-ex-rival-ahmad-zahid |website=South China Morning Post |language=en}}</ref> It has been referred to as a "[[kleptocrat]]" cabinet as a result.<ref>{{Cite news |first=Hazlin |last=Hassan |date=3 December 2022 |title=Malaysia's new Cabinet sworn in as criticism swirls over controversial appointments |language=en |work=The Straits Times |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysia-s-new-cabinet-sworn-as-criticism-swirls-over-controversial-appointments |access-date=1 September 2024 |issn=0585-3923 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20221203231943/https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysia-s-new-cabinet-sworn-as-criticism-swirls-over-controversial-appointments |archive-date=3 December 2022}}</ref>

Criticism and allegations of nepotism were directed at Anwar following his decision to appoint [[Nurul Izzah Anwar]] as the chief economic and financial advisor to the prime minister on 3 January 2023. Despite reports indicating that Nurul Izzah was not getting paid for her position, she stepped down from her role as advisor in February 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Nurul Izzah steps down as adviser to her father Malaysian PM Anwar |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/nurul-izzah-steps-down-adviser-malaysia-prime-minister-anwar-ibrahim-3272721 |access-date=25 May 2024 |website=CNA |language=en}}</ref>

Anwar has publicly stated that he would be open to the inclusion of the Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party, a major component of the opposition [[Perikatan Nasional]] coalition, if they accepted his conditions; including ensuring every citizen regardless of religious identity a place in the country.<ref>{{Cite web |date=12 October 2023 |title=Anwar offers olive branch to Pas, open to unity govt partnership |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2023/10/966229/anwar-offers-olive-branch-pas-open-unity-govt-partnership |website=New Straits Times}}</ref>

==== Criminal justice ====
Anwar's government oversaw the passing of the [[Abolition of Mandatory Death Penalty Act 2023]], allowing judges to choose between the death penalty and a 30 to 40 year prison sentence, with no less than 12 whippings, for 11 offences that previously mandated the death penalty. The act also abolished life imprisonment, replacing them with 30 to 40 year sentences. The act was applied retrospectively.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-04-11 |title=Malaysia Repeals Mandatory Death Penalty {{!}} Human Rights Watch |url=https://www.hrw.org/news/2023/04/11/malaysia-repeals-mandatory-death-penalty |access-date=2026-03-06 |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":02">{{Cite web |date=29 March 2023|title=Reforms of Mandatory Death Penalty in Malaysia: What Do The Bills Say?|url=https://www.amnesty.my/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Amnesty-International_Analysis-of-the-Bills-to-Abolish-The-Mandatory-Death-Penalty-in-Malaysia_March-2023-3.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230330102505/https://www.amnesty.my/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/Amnesty-International_Analysis-of-the-Bills-to-Abolish-The-Mandatory-Death-Penalty-in-Malaysia_March-2023-3.pdf|archive-date=30 March 2023|access-date=6 March 2026|website=Amnesty Malaysia}}</ref>

In response to the [[2023 Quran burnings in Sweden]], Anwar announced that Malaysia would print one million copies of the [[Quran]] to be distributed worldwide.<ref>{{Cite web |date=28 January 2023 |title=Govt to print 1mil copies of Al-Quran, in objection to burning act in Sweden |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2023/01/874321/govt-print-1mil-copies-al-quran-objection-burning-act-sweden |website=New Straits Times}}</ref>

Anwar hosted negotiations in Kuala Lumpur between Thailand and Cambodia to end the [[2025 Cambodia–Thailand border crisis]] on 28 July, where a ceasefire was achieved.<ref>{{Cite web |date=28 July 2025 |title=Thailand and Cambodia agree to 'immediate ceasefire' |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c5yl9l60e3no |website=BBC |language=en}}</ref> The city later served as the location where a [[Kuala Lumpur Peace Accord|peace agreement]] was signed on 26 October 2025.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 October 2025 |title=Joint Declaration by the Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Cambodia and the Prime Minister of the Kingdom of Thailand on the outcomes of their meeting in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia |url=https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefings-statements/2025/10/joint-declaration-by-the-prime-minister-of-the-kingdom-of-cambodia-and-the-prime-minister-of-the-kingdom-of-thailand-on-the-outcomes-of-their-meeting-in-kuala-lampur-malaysia/ |access-date=27 October 2025 |website=The White House |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Krishnan |first=Dhesegaan Bala |date=26 October 2025 |title=KL Peace Accord brings breakthrough, as Anwar and Trump witness Thailand–Cambodia deal to end border conflict |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2025/10/26/kl-peace-accord-brings-breakthrough-as-anwar-and-trump-witness-thailandcambodia-deal-to-end-border-conflict/195974 |access-date=27 October 2025 |website=Malay Mail |language=en}}</ref>

=== North Borneo dispute and the Malaysia-Sulu case ===
{{See also|North Borneo dispute|Malaysia Sulu case}}
Litigation around the [[North Borneo dispute]] and the battle against the self-proclaimed heirs of the [[Sultanate of Sulu|Sultan of Sulu]] has featured prominently in Anwar's premiership. His cabinet's response to the claims made by the purported Sulu descendants has been led by [[Azalina Othman Said]], Minister for Law and Institutional Reforms in the prime minister's department.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Beattie |first=Elizabeth |date=7 July 2023 |title=Fresh from 'Sulu case' win, Malaysia's law minister turns to domestic reforms |url=https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2023/07/07/asia-pacific/crime-legal-asia-pacific/malaysia-law-minister-sulu-case/ |access-date=30 January 2024 |website=The Japan Times |language=en}}</ref>

He has also been vocal about his criticism of the claimants as well as the arbitration award announced by Spanish lawyer and arbitrator [[Gonzalo Stampa]], describing the decision as a "sham and abusive".<ref>{{Cite web |title=Sulu claims: M'sia closer to nullifying 'sham' final award, says Anwar |date=27 June 2023 |url=https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/95427/sulu-claims-msia-closer-to-nullifying-sham-final-award-says-anwar}}</ref> Stampa had ruled in favour of the Sulu claimants in France, and announced a settlement of US$14.9 billion payable by Malaysia, the largest arbitration award in history.<ref>{{Cite web |agency=The Star/Asia News Network |date=2 March 2022 |title=French court rules Malaysia owes $14.92 billion to sultan of Sulu's heirs |url=https://globalnation.inquirer.net/202757/french-court-rules-malaysia-owes-14-92-billion-to-sultan-of-sulus-heirs |access-date=30 January 2024 |website=INQUIRER.net |language=en}}</ref> This ruling was annulled by the French Court of Cassation in November 2024.<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 November 2024 |title=Un Jugement historique inaugure une nouvelle ère de progrès pour les tribunaux européens |url=https://www.latribune.fr/supplement/la-tribune-now/un-jugement-historique-inaugure-une-nouvelle-ere-de-progres-pour-les-tribunaux-europeens-1011299.html |access-date=22 May 2025 |website=www.latribune.fr |language=fr}}</ref>

Also in November 2024, Philippine president [[Bongbong Marcos]] signed the ''Philippine Maritime Zones Act'' and the ''Philippine Archipelagic Sea Lanes Act''.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web |date=15 November 2024 |title=Malaysia protests new Philippine maritime laws that it says infringe on its territory |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/malaysia-protests-philippine-maritime-laws-115829893.html?guccounter=1 |access-date=19 December 2024 |website=Yahoo News |language=en-US}}</ref> These laws reaffirmed the Philippines' maritime territories and rights to natural resources across the South China Sea, including Sabah, drawing strong criticism from Malaysia.<ref name="auto" /> On 15 November 2024, Kuala Lumpur lodged a diplomatic protest against the two maritime laws, arguing that they infringed upon Malaysia's territorial boundaries in the South China Sea.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Punongbayan |first=Michael |title=Malaysia protests new Philippines maritime laws |url=https://www.philstar.com/headlines/2024/11/17/2400754/malaysia-protests-new-philippines-maritime-laws |access-date=19 December 2024 |website=Philstar.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=15 November 2024 |title=Malaysia protests new Philippine maritime laws that it says infringe on its territory |url=https://www.yahoo.com/news/malaysia-protests-philippine-maritime-laws-115829893.html |access-date=19 December 2024 |website=Yahoo News |language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Popularity ===
Public approval for Anwar has fallen since he first came to power, with a study conducted by [[Merdeka Center]] in October 2023 indicating that support had dropped from 68% to 50% among voters, while 60% believed the country was heading in the wrong direction.<ref>{{Cite web |date=22 November 2023 |title=One Year After GE15 Survey Report |url=https://merdeka.org/one-year-after-ge15-survey-report/ |access-date=28 February 2026 |website=[[Merdeka Center]] |language=en-GB}}</ref> In 2023, [[The Economist]] opined that Anwar was wasting his opportunity in power.<ref name=":9" />

In May 2025, a study by [[Merdeka Center]] showed an increase in approval for Anwar at 55% despite slower growth.<ref>{{Cite news |date=2025-06-23 |title=Malaysian PM Anwar’s popularity rises even as growth slows |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysian-pm-anwars-popularity-rises-even-as-growth-slows |access-date=2026-06-01 |work=The Straits Times |language=en |issn=0585-3923}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-06-23 |title=PM Anwar approval rating rises to 55% on the back of economic, political factors, says Merdeka Center |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2025/06/23/pm-anwar-approval-rating-rises-to-55-on-the-back-of-economic-political-factors-says-merdeka-center |access-date=2026-06-01 |website=The Star |language=en}}</ref>

In July 2025, a [[2025 Oust Anwar rally|protest]] was organised by the opposition calling for Anwar's resignation. According to [[Malaysiakini]], it had an attendance of some 25,000 people.<ref>{{Cite web |date=26 July 2025 |title=LANGSUNG {{!}} Kira-kira 25,000 hadir himpunan Turun Anwar |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/750224 |access-date=6 March 2026 |website=[[Malaysiakini]]}}</ref>

===Israel and Palestine===
In a press conference at the [[London School of Economics]] in March 2010, Anwar stated that Jews should not be condemned, but rather only the state ideology of [[Zionism]] and the aggression and injustice perpetrated against the [[Palestinians]]. He also believes that there are good and bad Jews, just as there are good and bad Muslims.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2YYqnEn2bAE | archive-url=https://ghostarchive.org/varchive/youtube/20211118/2YYqnEn2bAE| archive-date=18 November 2021 | url-status=live|title=Press Conference at the London School of Economics (LSE) 18 March 2010 |work=Youtube | date=5 April 2010|access-date=9 January 2012}}{{cbignore}}</ref> In May 2010, [[B'nai B'rith International]], a prominent Jewish human rights organisation, argued that Anwar has used his position in Malaysian parliament to spread anti-Semitic propaganda, such as his claim that Israeli spies are "directly involved in the running of the government". In a 2012 interview with ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'', Anwar Ibrahim stated that he believes the policy towards Israel and Palestine should be clear – protecting the security of Israel while also being firm in protecting the legitimate interests of the Palestinians.<ref>{{Citation |title=War of words between Malaysian opposition leader and ministers over Israel |url=http://www.malaysia-today.net/archives/archives-2012/46946-war-of-words-between-malaysian-opposition-leader-and-ministers-over-israel |newspaper=Malaysia today |year=2012 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130703063718/http://www.malaysia-today.net/archives/archives-2012/46946-war-of-words-between-malaysian-opposition-leader-and-ministers-over-israel |archive-date=3 July 2013 }}.</ref> Anwar later clarified his stance, saying that the remarks were consistent with the [[two-state solution]] adopted by the United Nations and Malaysia itself.<ref name="WSJ 2012">{{cite news |last=Mahtani |first=Shibani |date=31 January 2012 |title=Israel Dominates the Debate in Malaysia – Again |work=The Wall Street Journal |url=https://blogs.wsj.com/searealtime/2012/01/31/israel-dominates-the-debate-in-malaysia-%E2%80%93-again/ |access-date=1 February 2012 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120202003909/https://blogs.wsj.com/searealtime/2012/01/31/israel-dominates-the-debate-in-malaysia-%E2%80%93-again/ |archive-date=2 February 2012}}</ref>

===LGBTQ rights===
{{See also|LGBT rights in Malaysia}}Anwar has expressed changing views on [[LGBTQ rights by country or territory|LGBTQ rights]] throughout his political career. In a 2012 interview, he suggested that Malaysia's sodomy laws, under which he had been prosecuted, were "archaic" and could be amended. He clarified that while he believed in the sanctity of marriage between men and women and did not want to legalise homosexuality or [[same-sex marriage]], he did not perceive it as "[his] business" to attack or arrest people based on their sexual orientation.<ref>Mahtani, Shibani. [https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424052970203363504577184631682335306 "Malaysia's Anwar Presses On Despite Appeal"], "[[The Wall Street Journal]]", New York, 26 January 2013, Retrieved on 29 January 2013.</ref><ref>{{cite web |author=Shibani Mahtani |title=Anwar Clarifies His Views on Malaysia's Sodomy Laws |url=https://blogs.wsj.com/searealtime/2012/01/28/anwar-clarifies-his-views-on-malaysia%E2%80%99s-sodomy-laws/ |access-date=10 February 2015 |work=The Wall Street Journal}}</ref>

In 2018, Anwar urged religious people to counter the demand by "super liberals" to recognise the "LGBTQ lifestyle", saying that they did not have the right to "force" society to accept their views on LGBTQ rights.<ref>{{Cite web |first=Su-Lyn |last=Boo |date=26 September 2018 |title=Anwar: Fight 'arrogant super liberals', oppose LGBT |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2018/09/26/anwar-fight-arrogant-super-liberals-oppose-lgbt/1676754 |access-date=15 April 2022 |website=Malay Mail |language=en}}</ref> In 2022, he filed a defamation suit against Perak PAS Commissioner [[Razman Zakaria]], who had claimed that Anwar would push the "[[LGBTQ agenda]]" and support [[communism]]. Anwar said that he had always denounced the "agenda" and advocated Islamic teachings and values.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Yatim |first=Hafiz |date=6 December 2022 |title=Anwar files defamation suit against PAS' Razman over LGBT claim |url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/anwar-sues-perak-pas-commissioner-over-defamatory-statement |access-date=9 December 2022 |website=The Edge Markets}}</ref>

As prime minister in 2023, Anwar asserted that LGBTQ rights, [[secularism]], and communism would never be recognised under his government.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 January 2023 |title=PM Anwar: LGBT, secularism, communism will never be recognised in unity govt |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/01/07/pm-anwar-lgbt-secularism-communism-will-never-be-recognised-in-unity-govt/48847 |access-date=20 January 2023 |website=Malay Mail |language=en}}</ref> In a 2023 [[CNN]] interview, he stated that while excessive harassment of the LGBTQ community would not be condoned, Malaysia would not officially recognise LGBTQ rights due to the consensus among the population. He also suggested that the sodomy laws should be reviewed to prevent abuse and political persecution.<ref>{{Cite news |date=24 September 2023 |title=No harassment of LGBT community will be allowed, says Malaysian PM Anwar |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/no-harassment-of-lgbt-community-will-be-allowed-says-malaysian-pm-anwar |access-date=10 December 2023 |work=The Straits Times |language=en |issn=0585-3923}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Anwar is an [[Malays (ethnic group)|ethnic Malay]] and a [[Islam in Malaysia|Muslim]].<ref name=":5">{{Cite news |last=Ananthalakshmi |first=A. |date=25 November 2022 |title=Analysis: Gains for Malaysia's hardline Islamist party a challenge for new PM Anwar |language=en |work=Reuters |url=https://www.reuters.com/world/asia-pacific/gains-malaysias-hardline-islamist-party-challenge-new-pm-anwar-2022-11-25/ |access-date=2 December 2022}}</ref> He married [[Wan Azizah Wan Ismail]] on 26 February 1980.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/02/26/anwar-and-wan-azizah-39-years-of-marriage|title=Anwar and Wan Azizah – 39 years of marriage and still going strong|date=26 February 2019|work=The Star}}</ref> They have five daughters and a son. His eldest daughter, [[Nurul Izzah Anwar]], is the former MP for [[Permatang Pauh (federal constituency)|Permatang Pauh]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Nambiar |first=Predeep |date=2022-11-19 |title=Nurul Izzah loses Permatang Pauh family seat |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2022/11/20/nurul-izzah-loses-permatang-pauh-family-seat |access-date=2026-05-17 |website=Free Malaysia Today {{!}} FMT |language=en}}</ref>

During his trials and time in prison in the 2000s, Anwar spent time in [[solitary confinement]], and experienced frequent beatings which required him to undertake surgery in Germany upon his release.<ref name=":2" /><ref name=":4">{{Cite news |date=24 November 2022 |title=Anwar Ibrahim's hard-to-believe journey from prisoner to new Malaysian prime minister |work=[[CBC News]] |url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/world/malaysia-anwar-ibrahim-profile-1.6662722}}</ref> He once appeared in court with a [[black eye]].<ref name=":4" /> Anwar has professed an interest in the works of [[William Shakespeare]], and said that he read a copy of [[Complete Works of Shakespeare|his complete works]] four and a half times during his imprisonment.<ref name=":3" /><ref name=":2" /> He presented a paper to the World Shakespeare Congress in 2006.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web |title=How Shakespeare freed a jailed leader's imagination |url=https://www.uq.edu.au/news/article/2006/06/how-shakespeare-freed-jailed-leader%E2%80%99s-imagination |access-date=2 December 2022 |website=UQ News |language=en}}</ref> In prison, he also said he survived by singing songs and reading.<ref name=":3" />
