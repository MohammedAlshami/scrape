'''Parti Bumiputera Perkasa Malaysia''' ([[Malay language|Malay]] for "Malaysia Mighty Bumiputera Party", [[Jawi script|Jawi]]: ڤرتي بوميڤوترا ڤركاس مليسيا, [[Abbreviation|Abbr.]]: '''PUTRA''') is a [[nationalist]] political party in [[Malaysia]]. The party was founded and led by [[Ibrahim Ali (politician)|Ibrahim Ali]].<ref name="foundation">{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/05/09/ibrahim-ali-unveils-new-party-putra-to-fight-for-race-and-religion/|title=Ibrahim Ali unveils new party, Putra, to fight for race and religion |author= Hanis Zainal|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=9 May 2019|access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref>

==History==
Ibrahim Ali who is also the founding president of [[Pertubuhan Pribumi Perkasa]] (PERKASA), a [[Non-governmental Organisation]] (NGO) for [[Ketuanan Melayu|Malay supremacy]], had announced a new party would be set-up to serve as an alternative to the Malays and would champion the rights of the Malays and [[Islam]] after the [[Malaysian general election, 2018|2018 general election]] which saw the downfall of [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) government; echoing his earlier projection in 2013 of turning PERKASA into a full-fledged political party.<ref>{{cite news | title=We can replace you as Malay champions Perkasa warns UMNO |url=http://www.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/we-can-replace-you-as-malay-champions-perkasa-warns-umno |publisher=[[The Malay Mail]]|author=Syed Jaymal Zahiid |date=22 December 2013 |access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref>

PUTRA formation was first unveiled and announced by Ibrahim Ali as the party pro-tem president on 17 August 2018.<ref>{{Cite news|url= http://www2.nst.com.my/news/politics/2018/08/402454/putra-hopes-join-malaysian-political-scene |title=Putra hopes to join Malaysian political scene|author=Rizanizam Abdul Hamid |date=17 August 2018 |publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref> following its online registration application on 13 August, delivery of its establishment documents on 16 August; and subsequently a few meetings then with the [[Societies Act 1966|Registrar of Societies]] (RoS).<ref name="foundation"/>

In March 2019, Ibrahim had filed a court application for leave and [[judicial review]] to challenge the action by RoS to not approve PUTRA and sought a [[Mandamus|mandamus order]] to compel the respondent to approve the application for registration by PUTRA.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/468104|title=Ibrahim Ali wants ROS to approve Putra's registration|date=15 March 2019|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref> Finally PUTRA registration was approved and recognised by the RoS on 8 May.<ref name="foundation"/><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/475369|title=ROS greenlights Ibrahim Ali's Putra|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=8 May 2019|access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref> PUTRA was officially launched by Ibrahim in Kampung Baru, Kuala Lumpur on 28 September.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/09/28/ibrahim-ali-putra-to-contest-all-malay-majority-seats|title=Ibrahim Ali: Putra to contest all Malay majority seats|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=28 September 2019|access-date=28 September 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2019/09/28/lensa-mg-ibrahim-ali-pada-majlis-pelancaran-parti-putra/|title=Ibrahim Ali pada Majlis Pelancaran Parti PUTRA|work=LENSA MG|publisher=Malaysia Gazette|language=ms|date=28 September 2019|access-date=28 September 2019}}</ref>

Almost all of party constitution of PUTRA is based on the constitution of the "original" UMNO founded in 1946, to match the pure goals of the party in the beginning.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/475512|title=Constitution of Ibrahim Ali's Putra mirrors that of 'old Umno'|date=9 May 2019|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref> The party's membership is open to all Malay, including [[Bumiputera (Malaysia)|Bumiputera]] Malaysians but also non-Bumiputera who are Muslims as associate members.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/05/09/join-putra-and-get-limited-rights-ibrahim-ali-tells-non-bumi-muslims/1751307|title=New Bumi party welcomes non-Bumi Muslims as 'associate members'|author=Yiswaree Palansamy|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=9 May 2019|access-date=10 May 2019}}</ref>

On 10 August 2022, PUTRA vice president and former Member of the [[Perak State Legislative Assembly]] (MLA) for [[Sungai Rapat (state constituency)|Sungai Rapat]] [[Hamidah Osman]] left the party with immediate effect upon her disappointment and sadness over the party joining [[Gerakan Tanah Air]] (GTA), a new political coalition consisting of [[Homeland Fighters Party]] (PEJUANG) led by former Prime Minister [[Mahathir Mohamad]] and other Malay-based political parties five days prior on 5 August 2022 as well as her loss of trust in party president [[Ibrahim Ali (politician)|Ibrahim Ali]] and other party leaders. She also noted that she would carry on championing the issue of Malay reserve land through other platforms like the [[non-governmental organisation]] (NGO).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2022/08/10/disappointed-with-putra-joining-gta-hamidah-quits-party|title=Disappointed with Putra joining GTA, Hamidah quits party|website=The Star|date=10 August 2022|access-date=10 August 2022}}</ref>

On 25 February 2023, Mahathir joined PUTRA along with 12 other former PEJUANG members, including Mohd Tahir Kassim (Pahang) and Liza Meman (Perak).<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/former-malaysian-pm-mahathir-joins-little-known-party-putra | title=Former Malaysian PM Mahathir joins little-known party Putra &#124; the Straits Times | newspaper=The Straits Times | date=25 February 2023 }}</ref>

==State election results==
PUTRA had made a debut contesting for 5 seats in the [[2021 Malacca state election]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/melaka-polls-putra-makes-debut-fields-five-candidates|title=Melaka polls: Putra makes debut, fields five candidates|work=[[Bernama]]|date=6 November 2021|publisher=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Markets]]|access-date=7 November 2021|archive-date=8 November 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20211108111825/https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/melaka-polls-putra-makes-debut-fields-five-candidates|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2021/11/21/melaka-polls-all-independent-putra-iman-candidates-lose-their-deposit/2022474|title=Melaka polls: All independent, Putra, Iman candidates lose their deposit|author=Danial Dzulkifly|date=21 November 2021|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=23 November 2021}}</ref> In [[2022 Johor state election]], the only seat that PUTRA had contesting is [[Semerah (state constituency)|Semerah]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Hadi |first1=Mohd Zaini Samsu |title=PRN Johor: Putra bertanding di DUN Semerah sahaja |url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2022/02/19/prn-johor-putra-bertanding-di-dun-semerah-sahaja/ |website=MalaysiaGazette |date=19 February 2022 |access-date=7 October 2022}}</ref> However, it didn't win any single seats.
