A '''leadership election''' was held by the [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) party on 30 June 2018.

==Background==
The election was initially scheduled to be held in 2019. However, [[Barisan Nasional]] lost the [[2018 Malaysian general election]], a first since [[Independence Day (Malaysia)|independence in 1957]]. Then-president [[Najib Razak]] stood down and was replaced by acting President [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/former-malaysia-pm-najib-razak-steps-down-as-umno-president
|title=Former Malaysia PM Najib Razak steps down as Umno president and Barisan Nasional chairman|website=The Straits Times|accessdate=12 May 2018}}</ref>

The nomination process started on 1 June 2018. The registration closed on 17 June 2018 at 5 pm.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2018/05/375241/nomination-registration-umno-elections-start-june-1|title=Nomination registration for Umno elections to start on June 1|website=New Straits Times|accessdate=31 May 2018}}</ref>

==Debate==

The Presidential election was held on 29 June 2018. It was telecast on [[Astro Awani]] at 9.30 pm. It featured the three prominent candidates, which are [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]], [[Tengku Razaleigh Hamzah]] and [[Khairy Jamaluddin]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2018/06/382975/now-its-three-zahid-agrees-debate-kj-and-ku-li|title=Now it's three: Zahid agrees to debate KJ and Ku Li|website=New Straits Times|accessdate=22 June 2018}}</ref>

==Supreme Council election results==

The result will mostly out on 1.00 am on 1 July 2018.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2018/06/385787/official-results-umno-election-expected-early-1am|title=Official results for Umno election expected as early as 1am|website=The New Straits Times|accessdate=30 June 2018}}</ref>
