The '''Heritage Party''' ({{langx|ms|Parti Warisan}}, '''WARISAN''') is a [[Multiracialism|multiracial]] political party in [[Malaysia]] which was rebranded and renamed from the '''Sabah Heritage Party''' ({{langx|ms|Parti Warisan Sabah|links=no}}), a Sabah-based party led by [[Shafie Apdal]] formed earlier on 17 October 2016 after its expansion into national level politics at the end of 2020.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://malaysiandigest.com/news/637519-shafie-s-party-name-logo-approved-to-be-called-parti-warisan-sabah.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161018215613/http://malaysiandigest.com/news/637519-shafie-s-party-name-logo-approved-to-be-called-parti-warisan-sabah.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=18 October 2016|title=Shafie's Party Name, Logo Approved, To Be Called 'Parti Warisan Sabah'|work=New Straits Times|publisher=Malaysian Digest|date=17 October 2016|access-date=17 October 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://m.thesundaily.my/node/401778|title=Parti Warisan Sabah is new name of Shafie-led party|work=Bernama|publisher=The Sun|date=17 October 2016|access-date=17 October 2016}}{{Dead link|date=August 2024 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref><ref name="r1">{{Cite news|date=2021-11-29|title=Warisan to spread wings in peninsula on Dec 17|url=https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/48403/warisan-to-spread-wings-in-peninsula-on-dec-17|access-date=2021-11-30|website=The Vibes|language=en}}</ref>

==History==
Warisan formed an electoral alliance with [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) in the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] (GE14). Party president [[Shafie Apdal]] promised that the party would be represented in the federal cabinet should PH be elected to power,<ref name="slogan">{{cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/warisan-and-pakatan-harapan-team-up-in-sabah-for-malaysia-elections|title=Warisan and Pakatan Harapan team up in Sabah for Malaysia elections|publisher=The Straits Times|date=2 April 2018|access-date=24 May 2018|quote=Mr Shafie said that Warisan was not contesting in Peninsular Malaysia, but is ready to work with Pakatan Harapan (PH), without a pact, at the national level. We are a multi-racial party, we don't want to follow the PH coalition style in Peninsular Malaysia. When they form the government in Putrajaya, we will have ministers in the federal cabinet from Warisan and the Warisan (Sabah state) government will also have ministers from PH. Speaking to more than 5,000 party members at the assembly with the theme "In God We Trust, Change We Must", he urged all of them to prepare for the election.}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/418074|title=Warisan seals electoral pact with DAP and PKR|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=2 April 2018|access-date=2 April 2018}} {{subscription required}}</ref> adding that through the electoral pact, the party would only cooperate with instead of joining the coalition.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2018/04/352101/warisan-will-work-only-pakatan-harapan-ge14|title=Warisan will work only with Pakatan Harapan for GE14|author=Suzianah Jiffar|work=[[New Straits Times]]|date=2 April 2018|access-date=2 April 2018}}</ref> The party soon became part of the ruling government following Pakatan Harapan's victory in the general election. However, Warisan returned to the opposition in April 2021 following the fall of the PH government.<ref>{{cite news |title=Warisan 'unhooks' from Pakatan, keeps options open on political alignment for GE15  |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2021/04/05/warisan-unhooks-from-pakatan-keeps-options-open-on-political-alignment-for-ge15 |work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]] |access-date=27 March 2022 |date=5 April 2021}}</ref>

During the [[2020 Sabah state election]], the state ruling party was defeated by the informal [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] (GRS) state opposition coalition consisting of the federal ruling [[Perikatan Nasional]], [[Barisan Nasional]], and the [[United Sabah Party]]. Warisan failed to secure a simple majority to retain the state power, allowing the GRS coalition to form a new state government.<ref name="CNS Sabah election">{{cite news |last1=Yusof |first1=Amir |title=Muhyiddin-led Gabungan Rakyat Sabah clinches simple majority in state polls |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/malaysia-sabah-state-election-muhyiddin-gabungan-rakyat-majority-13152014 |access-date=11 February 2021 |work=[[Channel News Asia]] |date=27 September 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201101012126/https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/malaysia-sabah-state-election-muhyiddin-gabungan-rakyat-majority-13152014 |archive-date=1 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> Following the 2020 election, Warisan became the largest opposition party within the [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly|Sabah Legislative Assembly]], and the outgoing Chief Minister, party president [[Shafie Apdal]], became the new [[Opposition (Malaysia)#State Legislative Assemblies|Leader of the Opposition]] in Sabah. 

At the party's [[annual general meeting]] on 12 December 2020, members voted in favour of elevating the party from a state-based party into a national party, aiming to represent [[East Malaysia]] within the national decision-making. Following this announcement, the party was renamed into the '''Heritage Party''' ('''''Parti Warisan''''').<ref>{{cite news |title=Warisan spreads its wings to Peninsular Malaysia |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2020/12/13/warisan-spreads-its-wings-to-peninsular-malaysia |access-date=19 May 2021 |work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]] |date=13 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210102134946/https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2020/12/13/warisan-spreads-its-wings-to-peninsular-malaysia |archive-date=2 January 2021|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Geraldine |first1=Avila |title=Warisan should remain as Sabah based party |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/12/649343/warisan-should-remain-sabah-based-party |access-date=19 May 2021 |work=[[New Straits Times]] |date=14 December 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201214081158/https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/12/649343/warisan-should-remain-sabah-based-party |archive-date=14 December 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

Warisan officially began its expansion into Peninsula Malaysia on 17 December 2021 with the launch of its peninsular chapter, which would be collaborating with [[Malaysian United Democratic Alliance|MUDA]]. At the same event, Shafie also hinted that a Peninsular Malaysian assemblyman would be joining the party.<ref name="r1" /> On 22 January 2022, [[Bryan Lai Wai Chong]], the Selangor assemblyman for [[Teratai (state constituency)|Teratai]] joined the party to become its first peninsular assemblyman,<ref>{{cite news |title=Selangor's Teratai rep becomes Warisan's first assemblyman in the peninsula, |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2022/01/22/selangors-teratai-rep-becomes-warisans-first-assemblyman-in-the-peninsula |work=[[The Star (Malaysia)]] |access-date=22 February 2022 |date=22 January 2022}}</ref> followed two days later by [[Law Heng Kiang]], a former Penang assemblyman for [[Batu Lancang]], and former Jelutong MP [[Jeff Ooi]].<ref>{{cite news |title=No antagonism, sloganeering from us, unlike DAP: Warisan's Jeff Ooi|url=https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/52805/no-antagonism-sloganeering-from-us-unlike-dap-warisans-jeff-ooi |website=thevibes.com |access-date=26 March 2022 |date=24 January 2022}}</ref> In February 2022, [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO]]'s former [[Kukup (state constituency)|Kukup]] assemblyman, [[:ms:Suhaimi Salleh|Suhaimi Salleh]], joined the party to become its Johor elections coordinator in the 12 March state election.<ref>{{cite news |title=Mohd Shafie confirms Warisan will contest in Johor polls |url=https://www.thesundaily.my/local/mohd-shafie-confirms-warisan-will-contest-in-johor-polls-EI8864237 |publisher=[[The Sun (Malaysia)]] |access-date=26 March 2022 |date=16 February 2022}}</ref>

On 15 February 2022, Warisan announced that they would contest in the [[2022 Johor state election]] as a test of the party's support in the Peninsula.<ref>{{Cite news| title=Report: Warisan set to test waters for peninsula expansion in Johor poll |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2022/02/15/report-warisan-set-to-test-waters-for-peninsular-expansion-in-johor-poll/2041688|date=15 February 2022 |access-date=23 February 2022 |publisher=[[The Malay Mail]]|language=en}}</ref> Besides Suhaimi Salleh, Warisan also recruited former [[Malaysian Indian Congress]] (MIC) Central Working Committee (CWC) member Datuk Seri S. Sunther, son of ex-MIC Vice President Datuk Seri S. Subramaniam, as well as former [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia|GERAKAN]] Johor Women's Chief Wong Siew Poh to assist the party in the election.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Former Johor Bersatu state chief accidentally revealed as Warisan state coordinator in technical glitch |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2022/02/15/former-johor-bersatu-state-chief-accidentally-revealed-as-warisan-state-coo/2041835 |date=15 February 2022 |access-date=28 March 2022 |website=[[The Malay Mail]]|language=en}}</ref>  Eventually, candidates from Warisan contested in 6 of the 56 state assembly seats on offer, but failed to win any of the seats.<ref>{{cite news |title=Warisan to re-strategise after failing to win any seat in Johor |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2022/03/13/warisan-to-re-strategise-after-failing-to-win-any-seat-in-johor/?amp |publisher=[[The Borneo Post]] |date=13 March 2022 |access-date=28 March 2022}}</ref>

In the [[2025 Sabah state election]], Warisan won 25 seats, coming close behind GRS both in seat count and vote share. Part of its support came from ethnic Chinese voters shifting from the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP), with Warisan winning all six seats previously held by the DAP.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/malaysia-sabah-election-grs-anwar-ph-dap-5498131 |title=5 insights from Sabah election results, including implications for Malaysia politics and PM Anwar |author1=Aqil Haziq Mahmud |author2=Izzah Aqilah Norman |work=Channel News Asia |date=30 November 2025 |access-date=12 April 2026}}</ref>
