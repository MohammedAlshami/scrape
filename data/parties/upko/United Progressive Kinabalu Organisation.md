The '''United Progressive Kinabalu Organisation''' ({{langx|ms|Pertubuhan Kinabalu Progresif Bersatu}}; [[abbreviation|abbrev]]: '''UPKO''') is a multiracial [[politics of Malaysia|political party]] based in [[Sabah]], [[Malaysia]].<ref name="multiracial">{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2019/11/25/cm-welcomes-rebranding-of-upko-into-party-for-all/|title=CM welcomes rebranding of Upko into party for all|date=25 November 2019|work=Borneo Post|access-date=26 November 2019}}</ref> The party was rebranded from its previous party's name, '''United Pasokmomogun Kadazandusun Murut Organisation''' using the same UPKO acronym; which was a widely known [[Kadazan-Dusun]]-[[Murut people|Murut]] based party in 2019.<ref name="rebrand">{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news/143832/upko-opens-doors-to-other-races-in-rebrand-bid/|title=Upko opens doors to other races in rebrand bid|date=24 November 2019|work=Daily Express|access-date=26 November 2019}}</ref> The party had earlier been renamed once before in 1999 from its initial 1994 formation name of [[Sabah Democratic Party]].<ref name="upko">{{cite news|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/501000|title=UPKO rebranded to United Progressive People of Kinabalu Organisation|date=23 November 2019|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|access-date=26 November 2019}}</ref> In 2021, UPKO joined the [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition,<ref name="PH">{{cite web|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2021/08/26/upko-is-now-part-of-ph/|title=Upko is now part of PH|author=|publisher=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|date=26 August 2021|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref> but left in 2025 and applied to join the [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] (GRS) in 2026.<ref>https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2026/05/upko-kemuka-permohonan-sertai-grs/ UPKO Kemuka Permohonan Sertai GRS (in English: "UPKO applied to joined GRS" - Utusan Malaysia)</ref>

== History ==
===Sabah Democratic Party===
The party started as [[Sabah Democratic Party]] (''Parti Demokratik Sabah'' or PDS) which was founded by [[Bernard Giluk Dompok]] and other detractors who split from [[United Sabah Party]] (''Parti Bersatu Sabah'' or PBS) soon after the [[Sabah state election, 1994|Sabah state election]] in March 1994 to join the [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition.<ref name=upko2>{{cite web|url=http://www.singapore-elections.com/malaysia-political-parties/upko.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190125122816/http://www.singapore-elections.com/malaysia-political-parties/upko.html|url-status=usurped|archive-date=25 January 2019|title=UPKO/PDS|access-date=28 November 2019}}</ref> PBS had earlier won a majority in the [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly]] then but with the two breakaway factions setting their own spliter new parties of PDS by Dompok and another [[United Sabah People's Party]] (''Parti Bersatu Rakyat Sabah'' or PBRS) by [[Joseph Kurup]] had caused the crumble of PBS new Sabah government and allowed BN to form the government instead.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theborneopost.com/2013/02/19/pbs-upko-pbrs-urged-to-regroup-or-merge-under-one-political-party-new/|title=PBS, UPKO, PBRS urged to regroup or merge under one political party|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=19 February 2013|access-date=4 November 2014}}</ref> Part of the enticement offered by BN to the defectors was the promise of a rotating [[List of Chief Ministers of Sabah|Chief Ministers of Sabah]] post, which Dompok held from 1998 to 1999.<ref>{{cite book|title=Federal-state Relations in Sabah, Malaysia: The Berjaya Administration, 1976-85|author=Regina Lim|publisher=Institute of Southeast Asian Studies|year=2008|page=129}}</ref> The defection from PBS however damaged the party at the [[Malaysian general election, 1995|1995 federal election]], in which it won no seats.

===United Pasokmomogun Kadazandusun Murut Organisation===
PDS was renamed as United Pasokmomogun Kadazandusun Murut Organisation (UPKO) or ''Pertubuhan Pasokmomogun Kadazandusun Murut Bersatu'' on 8 August 1999, taking the same acronym of the now defunct original [[United Pasokmomogun Kadazan Organisation]], which was formed and dissolved in the 1960s by [[Fuad Stephens]].<ref name=upko/> The name of the new UPKO includes the words "Kadazandusun" and "Murut", with a new logo of a [[silhouette]] of [[Mount Kinabalu]] and was not a [[domestic buffalo|water buffalo]] as in the old UPKO logo.<ref name=upko2/> It was redefined as an ethnically based party striving to voice the rights and advance the development of KDM populations of Sabah and the [[Orang Asli]] of [[Peninsular Malaysia]].<ref name="UPKO OA">{{cite web|url=http://nst.com.my/Current_News/NST/articles/19upko/Article/index_html|title=Sabah-based Upko targets Orang Asli|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]|date=12 July 2009|access-date=24 July 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090713093307/http://www.nst.com.my/Current_News/NST/articles/19upko/Article/index_html|archive-date=13 July 2009|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theborneopost.com/2014/09/18/upko-pledges-to-help-achieve-ideal-malaysia/|title=Upko pledges to help achieve 'ideal Malaysia'|author=Nancy Lai|publisher=The Borneo Post|date=18 September 2014|access-date=4 November 2014}}</ref>

The party won three federal seats at the [[Malaysian general election, 1999|1999 election]], and four at the [[Malaysian general election, 2004|2004]] as well as [[Malaysian general election, 2008|2008]] polls. In 2009, UPKO opened four divisions in [[Perak]], seeking a foothold among local [[Orang Asli]] indigenous people.<ref name="UPKO OA"/> In the [[Malaysian general election, 2013]], the party was reduced from four to three federal seats and from six to four state assembly seats. Dompok lost his federal seat to the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR). The following year he resigned as the party's president, a position he had held for 20 years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysiantimes.com.my/bernard-dompok-steps-down-as-upko-president/|title=Bernard Dompok quits as Upko president|publisher=The Malaysian Times|date=5 March 2014|access-date=6 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www2.nst.com.my/nation/general/dompok-resigns-as-upko-president-1.499092|title=Dompok resigns as Upko president|author1=Kristy Inus|author2=Laili Ismail|publisher=New Straits Times|date=6 March 2014|access-date=6 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140306015248/http://www2.nst.com.my/nation/general/dompok-resigns-as-upko-president-1.499092|archive-date=6 March 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

UPKO was one of the component parties in the [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition which ruled Malaysia until 2018. The party's core Sabahan indigenous constituency includes many [[Christians]], while BN is, on a national scale, dominated by the [[United Malays National Organisation]], an overtly [[Muslim]]-[[Malaysian Malay|Malay]] party. While a member of the BN federal government, UPKO often spoke out about government policies affecting Christians. In 2013, the party's president [[Bernard Dompok]] distanced himself from Prime Minister [[Najib Razak]] on the contentious question of the use by Malaysian Christians of the word "[[Allah]]" to describe God. Najib had supported a government appeal to the High Court seeking to outlaw the word's use by a Christian newspaper; Dompok criticised the appeal and defended the right of Christians, especially indigenous Malaysians, to use the word.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/christians-to-continue-using-allah-despite-government-appeal-says-dompok/|title=Christians to continue using 'Allah' despite government appeal, says Dompok|author=Boo Su-Lyn|publisher=[[The Malaysian Insider]]|date=27 April 2013|access-date=6 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130428233226/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/christians-to-continue-using-allah-despite-government-appeal-says-dompok/|archive-date=28 April 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref>

UPKO also agitated, often against the national government of which it was a part, for tougher measures against illegal immigration in Sabah. In February 2012, UPKO succeeded in forcing the establishment of the [[Royal Commission of Inquiry on illegal immigrants in Sabah]]. In the same year one of the party's federal parliamentarians, [[Wilfred Bumburing]], quit UPKO and joined the opposition PKR in protest at what he considered to be government inaction on illegal immigration.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dr-m-insensitive-for-defending-sabah-illegals-says-upko/|title=Dr M 'insensitive' for defending Sabah illegals, says UPKO|author=Clara Chooi|publisher=The Malaysian Insider|date=9 August 2012|access-date=6 November 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141105101737/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dr-m-insensitive-for-defending-sabah-illegals-says-upko/|archive-date=5 November 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref>

UPKO was an advocate for the repeal of the [[Internal Security Act (Malaysia)|Internal Security Act]], which for over 50 years permitted detention without charge in certain circumstances. The law was repealed in 2011.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news.cfm?NewsID=60010|title=Upko also wants ISA reviewed|publisher=[[Daily Express (Malaysia)|Daily Express]]|date=23 September 2008|access-date=23 September 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080924152038/http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news.cfm?NewsID=60010|archive-date=24 September 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In the [[Malaysian general election, 2018|2018 general election]], the party won only one federal seat and five state assembly seats. Following this, the party's acting president Madius Tangau announced that UPKO with five of their party state assembly seats members had left BN to form a coalition government with the [[Sabah Heritage Party]], alongside PKR, DAP and Amanah became a partner party for the [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition instead.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://says.com/my/news/sabah-musical-chairs-to-end-with-shafie-swearing-in-as-15th-chief-minister-tonight|title=Sabah Musical Chairs To End With Shafie Swearing In As Chief Minister Tonight|author=Sadho Ram|publisher=Says.com|date=12 May 2018|access-date=12 May 2018}}</ref><ref name="switched allegiance">{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/424352|title=Six switch sides, Warisan has majority with 35 seats|author=Alyaa Azhar |publisher=Malaysiakini|date=11 May 2018|access-date=12 May 2018}}</ref>

===United Progressive People of Kinabalu Organisation===
The party was re-branded again as United Progressive People of Kinabalu Organisation or ''Pertubuhan Kinabalu Progresif Bersatu'' while retaining its existing UPKO acronym on 23 November 2019.<ref name=rebrand/><ref name=upko/><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.theborneopost.com/2019/11/24/upko-undergoes-rebranding/|title=Upko undergoes rebranding |date=24 November 2019|work=Borneo Post|access-date=26 November 2019}}</ref> There is also a slight change in UPKO's logo with the inclusion of a new colour, red while the Mount Kinabalu image remains.<ref name=multiracial/><ref name=upko2/> The party re-branding process was aimed to migrate from the communal politics to a universal and inclusive politics by opening the party membership to other races than KDM communities.<ref name=multiracial/><ref name=upko/> On 26 August 2021, it officially joined opposition [[Pakatan Harapan]] coalition. It left on 10 November 2025.<ref name=PH/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2021/08/26/upko-officially-joins-pakatan|title=Upko officially joins Pakatan|author=|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=26 August 2021|access-date=26 August 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=BP 6 |date=2025-11-10 |title=UPKO officially exits Pakatan Harapan to pursue 'Sabah First' agenda |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2025/11/10/upko-officially-exits-pakatan-harapan-to-pursue-sabah-first-agenda/ |access-date=2026-02-21 |website=Borneo Post Online |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2025-11-26 |title=Upko demands apology from Sabah Info |url=https://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news/271342/upko-demands-apology-from-sabah-info/ |access-date=2026-02-21 |website=www.dailyexpress.com.my |language=en}}</ref>
