The '''United Sarawak Party''' ({{langx|ms|Parti Sarawak Bersatu}}, abbreviated '''PSB''') was a multi-racial political party based in the state of [[Sarawak]].<ref name=rename>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2019/02/26/its-now-psb|title=Its now PSB|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=26 February 2019|access-date=7 March 2019}}</ref>

==History==

=== 2014–18: Formation as United People's Party (UPP) ===
[[File:Wong Soon Koh.jpg|left|thumb|200px|Wong Soon Koh, founder of PSB]]
[[File:United People's Party (Malaysia) Logo.svg|thumb|centre|Logo of initial United People's Party (UPP), 2014–2019.]]
The party was initially founded as United People's Party and officially launched on 17 August 2014 as a new symbol of change and unity for the Chinese community of Sarawak with its headquarters based at [[Kuching]].<ref name="founded">{{cite web|url=https://www.utusanborneo.com.my/2014/10/21/parti-rakyat-bersatu-lancar-ibu-pejabat-di-kuching|title=Parti Rakyat Bersatu lancar ibu pejabat di Kuching|author=Jessica Jawing|language=ms|publisher=Utusan Borneo|date=21 October 2014|access-date=22 October 2014}}</ref><ref name="foundation">{{cite web|url=http://www2.bharian.com.my/bharian/articles/BekasahliSUPPbentukpartibaharudikenaliPartiRakyatBersatu/Article/index_html |title=Bekas ahli SUPP bentuk parti baharu dikenali Parti Rakyat Bersatu|language=ms|publisher=[[Berita Harian]]|date=17 July 2014|access-date=22 October 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140726175406/http://www.bharian.com.my/bharian/articles/BekasahliSUPPbentukpartibaharudikenaliPartiRakyatBersatu/Article/index_html|archive-date=26 July 2014|url-status=dead}}</ref> The UPP was actually a splinter party of [[Sarawak United People's Party]] (SUPP) set-up by the Second Finance Minister for Sarawak, [[Wong Soon Koh]] along with his supporters following a leadership tussle of the party then and it started as a pro-[[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) political pact.<ref name="founded"/><ref name="foundation"/> However the hopes that the UPP would join BN were objected by the coalition leadership.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bharian.com.my/node/21820|title=Harapan UPP dan Teras untuk sertai gabungan BN Sarawak berkecai|language=ms|work=[[Bernama]]|publisher=Berita Harian|date=6 December 2014|access-date=22 October 2014}}</ref> UPP rejected [[Parti Pesaka Bumiputera Bersatu]] (PBB)'s suggestion that UPP should dissolve to pave the way for its members to join other Sarawak BN component parties arguing that they have over 26 full-fledged branches with 30,000 members and that UPP was able to raise RM38 million (US$9.3 million) for an education fund in less than a fortnight.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2016/07/08/lo-upp-will-not-be-dissolved-to-remain-bn-friendly|title=Lo UPP Will Not Be Dissolved to Remain BN Friendly|work=Bernama |publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=8 July 2016|access-date=7 March 2019}}</ref> UPP's ability to raise significant funds is widely attributed to the support they enjoy from Sarawak's powerful timber tycoons.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysia-today.net/2016/05/01/tycoon-politics-in-sarawak|title=Tycoon Politics in Sarawak|language=en|work=Bernama|publisher=[[Malaysia Today]]|date=1 May 2016|access-date=7 March 2019}}</ref>

A Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) was made between UPP and SUPP of BN for the division of seats to be contested in the [[Sarawak state election, 2016|2016 Sarawak state election]] which saw UPP win five out of seven seats it was allocated.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/327252|title=Adenan: Masalah BN S'wak selesai sebelum penamaan calon|language=ms|work=Bernama|publisher=[[Malaysiakini]]|date=18 January 2016|access-date=9 April 2016}}</ref> UPP however cancelled the MoU with SUPP after the 2016 state election.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2018/07/09/decision-to-terminate-mou-made-during-upp-tdc-over-the-weekend-lo/|title=Decision to terminate MOU made during UPP TDC over the weekend|language=|work=Bernama|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=9 July 2018|access-date=9 April 2016}}</ref>

Both rival parties again signed MoU for collaboration in the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]] (GE14).<ref>{{cite news|url=https://dayakdaily.com/ge14-supp-upp-to-collaborate-under-supp-flag/|title=GE14: SUPP, UPP to collaborate under SUPP flag |publisher=Dayak Daily|date=23 March 2016|access-date=9 April 2016}}</ref> After the GE14 which saw the downfall of BN federal government, the state BN components quit and formed themselves as the new [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS) coalition without UPP.

=== 2019: Re-branding as Parti Sarawak Bersatu (PSB) ===
UPP was re-branded to United Sarawak Party or Parti Sarawak Bersatu (PSB), after an Extraordinary Delegates Conference (EDC) on 8 December 2018. The party's name change and new logo was approved by the Registrar of Societies (RoS) in 2019.<ref name=rename/> PSB, amidst of being left out of the newly formed GPS coalition, choose to maintain its status quo, but shifted from being a BN-friendly to GPS-friendly party by providing [[supply and confidence]] to the new GPS coalition of the [[Government of Sarawak|Sarawak state government]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2018/12/08/united-peoples-party-renamed-parti-sarawak-bersatu-pledges-support-for-aban/1701341|title=United People's Party renamed Parti Sarawak Bersatu, pledges support for Abang Johari|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=8 December 2018|access-date=7 March 2019}}</ref> Somehow the party status changed when PSB opted to be an independent instead when its president, Wong, tendered his resignation from the [[Cabinet of Sarawak|state Cabinet]] as the International Trade and e-Commerce Minister and second Minister of Finance in July 2019 .<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2019/07/20/sarawak-cm-wong-soon-kohs-resignation-as-cabinet-minister-accepted/1773297|title=Sarawak CM: Wong Soon Koh's resignation as Cabinet minister accepted|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date= 20 July 2019|access-date= 20 December 2019}}</ref>

=== 2020–21: Malaysian political crisis ===
During the [[2020–21 Malaysian political crisis#February: Sheraton Move, fall of Pakatan Harapan (PH) government|2020 Malaysian political crisis]], the support inclination of PSB Sri Aman MP; [[Masir Kujat]], was in much speculation and attention for the tussling parties of the political scene.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/03/01/sri-aman-mp-now-in-dr-ms-camp/1842457|title=Sri Aman MP now in Dr M's camp|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date= 1 March 2020|access-date= 1 March 2020}}</ref> Despite being arranged to be seated alongside the new governing [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) backbencher MPs in the [[Dewan Rakyat]] during the historic the one-day Parliament sitting on 17 May, Masir has clarified that he and the PSB party is still in the opposition bloc in the federal and state levels.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/05/18/perikatan-govt-majority-falls-to-two-after-sarawak-mp-masir-kujat-insists-s/1867340|title=Perikatan govt majority falls to two after Sarawak MP Masir Kujat insists still part of Opposition bloc |publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|date=18 March 2020|access-date= 19 March 2020}}</ref>

Impact of the prolonged political crisis had also witnessed two former Sarawak [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) legislature representatives; Selangau MP incumbent Ba Kelalan assemblyman [[Baru Bian]] and Batu Lintang assemblyman [[See Chee How]], together with their supporters have joined PSB on 30 May 2020.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2020/05/30/its-official-baru-see-join-psb-with-over-20-former-pkr-leaders/|title=It's official – Baru, See join PSB with over 20 former PKR leaders|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=30 May 2020|access-date= 30 May 2020}}</ref> In August 2021, PM [[Muhyiddin Yassin]] of PN resigned, PSB pledge to support PH's [[Anwar Ibrahim]] as prime ministerial candidate but will back [[Shafie Apdal]] of [[Parti Warisan Sabah]] as an alternative if Anwar not chosen.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2021/08/18/psb-to-back-shafie-as-alternative-pm-candidate-should-anwar-fail-to-be-chosen/|title=PSB to back Shafie as alternative PM candidate, should Anwar fail to be chosen|publisher=[[The Borneo Post]]|date=18 August 2021|access-date=21 August 2021}}</ref> PSB though stated it wish to remain an independent party without affiliation to any political alliance including PH.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2021/08/25/parti-sarawak-bersatu-to-stay-independent-won039t-join-pakatan|title=Parti Sarawak Bersatu to stay independent, won't join Pakatan|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=25 August 2021|access-date=25 August 2021}}</ref>

=== 2021–23: State and Federal elections ===

In the [[2021 Sarawak state election]], PSB managed to retain the four seats of [[Batu Lintang (state constituency)|Batu Lintang]], [[Engkilili]], [[Ba'kelalan]] and [[Bawang Assan]], making it the largest opposition party in Sarawak, replacing [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) which only retained two seats of [[Pending (state constituency)|Pending]] and [[Padungan (state constituency)|Padungan]] through the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP). However on 12 August 2022, [[See Chee How]], the MLA for Batu Lintang resigned from PSB and becoming an independent MLA, leaving PSB with 3 MLAs and the Opposition with 5 MLAs in the Legislative Assembly.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Sulaiman |first1=Fadhilah |title=See Chee How keluar PSB, pembangkang DUN Sarawak kini tinggal lima orang |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-malaysia/see-chee-how-keluar-psb-pembangkang-dun-sarawak-kini-tinggal-lima-orang-376035 |website=www.astroawani.com |access-date=17 June 2024 |language=ms |date=14 August 2022}}</ref>

After elections for Sarawak assembly in 2021 and [[2022 Malaysian general election|for Dewan Rakyat in 2022]], the party lost its representation in the Dewan Rakyat but became the largest opposition party in Sarawak. The party reacted positively to the formation of unity government led by Anwar Ibrahim.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2022-12-17 |title=Soon Koh: Anwar's unity govt is Malaysia's new hope |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2022/12/17/soon-koh-anwars-unity-govt-is-malaysias-new-hope/ |access-date=2023-07-13 |website=Borneo Post Online |language=en-US}}</ref> On 13 July 2023, PSB president Wong Soon Koh, on behalf of the party, signed a [[Memorandum of Understanding]] (MoU) with the [[Progressive Democratic Party (Malaysia)|Progressive Democratic Party]] (PDP) to show its support for the GPS coalition. However, Wong remained the State [[Leader of the Opposition]] of Sarawak and PSB stayed in the opposition in the [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly]].<ref name=mou>{{cite news |last=Tee |first=Kenneth |date=2023-07-13 |title=Sarawak's PDP inks MoU with local Opposition party PSB for collaboration |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/07/13/sarawaks-pdp-inks-mou-with-local-opposition-party-psb-for-collaboration/79582 |work=The Malay Mail |access-date=2024-01-24|ref=none}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Once foes, now friends: PSB to work with PDP, backs state and federal govts |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/07/13/once-foes-now-friends-psb-to-work-with-pdp-backs-state-and-federal-govts |access-date=2023-07-13 |website=The Star |language=en}}</ref> It pledged to support Abang Johari's state government again as well as supporting Anwar Ibrahim's government at federal level.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-13 |title=Sarawak's PSB pledges support for Abang Jo |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/671854 |access-date=2023-07-13 |website=Malaysiakini}}</ref>

=== 2024: PSB dissolves, joining PDP ===
According to PSB Deputy President Johnical Rayong Ngipa, the party was set to be dissolved before [[Chinese New Year]] in 2024 and all existing PSB members were expected to join the PDP, a component party of the GPS coalition.<ref name=dissolve>{{cite news |last=Boon |first=Peter |date=2023-12-24 |title=Rayong: PSB to be dissolved, members likely to join PDP en bloc before Chinese New Year |url=https://www.theborneopost.com/2023/12/24/rayong-psb-to-be-dissolved-members-likely-to-join-pdp-en-bloc-before-chinese-new-year/ |work=The Borneo Post |access-date=2024-01-24|ref=none}}</ref> On 6 April 2024, Wong confirmed that the Registrar of Societies had approved the application of PSB to be dissolved on 19 March 2024 and all 80,000 PSB members had joined the PDP upon the dissolution. PDP President [[Tiong King Sing]] also appointed Wong as the Senior Vice President, Johnical Rayong Ngipa and Baru Bian as Vice Presidents of PDP. Upon the dissolution of PSB and the joining of its members in GPS, which was the state ruling coalition, [[Chong Chieng Jen]] took office as the Sarawak State Opposition Leader for the second term after the Sarawak opposition was left with only PH and DAP who were represented by only two MLAs Chong himself who was the Padungan MLA and Pending MLA [[Violet Yong Wui Wui]]. GPS also held 79 of the 82 seats in the State Assembly, ruling the state with more than two-thirds [[supermajority]] support of the assembly.
