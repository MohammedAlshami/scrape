'''Gabungan Rakyat Sabah'''<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2022/03/13/ros-approves-registration-of-gabungan-rakyat-sabah-says-hajiji/2047212|title=RoS approves registration of Gabungan Rakyat Sabah as political party and known in general as Gabungan Rakyat Sabah Party, says Hajiji|agency=Bernama|date=11 March 2022|newspaper=malaymail|access-date=13 March 2022}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |agency=Bernama |date=11 March 2022 |title=this coalition now officially named as Gabungan Rakyat Sabah Party or GRS PARTY in general |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2022/03/13/ros-approves-registration-of-gabungan-rakyat-sabah-says-hajiji/2047212 |access-date=13 March 2022 |newspaper=malaymail}}</ref> ('''GRS'''; {{langx|en|Sabah People's Coalition}}) is a [[Malaysia]]n [[Parliamentary group|coalition]] of [[Sabah]]-based parties.<ref>{{cite news |date=12 September 2020 |title=Gabungan Rakyat Sabah Parti Lokal Sabah |work=[[Sinar Harian]] |url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/192617/BERITA/Politik/GRS-sah-jadi-entiti-politik-Sabah-Hajiji |access-date=11 February 2021 }}</ref><ref name="Express"/> It was established in 2020 and then registered in 2022 by former [[United Alliance of Sabah]] (GBS) and [[United Borneo Alliance]] (UBA) component parties operating solely in Sabah inspired by the formula of [[Sarawak]]-based coalition, [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS). Gabungan Rakyat Sabah gained significant popularity among Sabahans with a [[Regionalism (politics)|regionalist]] political outlook.

==History==

===Foundation===

On 12 September 2020, [[Hajiji Noor]] established an informal alliance named Gabungan Rakyat Sabah (GRS) to compete against [[Shafie Apdal]]'s ruling Warisan Plus coalition consisting of [[Sabah Heritage Party]] (WARISAN), the [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP), [[Parti Keadilan Rakyat]] (PKR), and the [[United Progressive Kinabalu Organisation]] (UPKO) during the [[2020 Sabah state election]]. The GRS alliance consisted of Hajiji's [[Perikatan Nasional]] Sabah (PN) coalition, the [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) coalition, and [[United Sabah Party|Parti Bersatu Sabah]] (PBS).<!--<ref name="ST 12 Sep 2020" />--><ref name="Bernama"/><ref name="Express"/>

During the Sabah state election held on 26 September, the alliance won a simple majority in the [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly]], total of 38 seats: 17 from Perikatan Nasional, 14 from Barisan Nasional, and seven from PBS.<ref name="CNS Sabah election">{{cite news |last1=Yusof |first1=Amir |title=Muhyiddin-led Gabungan Rakyat Sabah clinches simple majority in state polls |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/malaysia-sabah-state-election-muhyiddin-gabungan-rakyat-majority-13152014 |access-date=11 February 2021 |work=[[Channel News Asia]] |date=27 September 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201101012126/https://www.channelnewsasia.com/news/asia/malaysia-sabah-state-election-muhyiddin-gabungan-rakyat-majority-13152014 |archive-date=1 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref> Sabah Perikatan Nasional chairman [[Hajiji Noor]] became the new [[Chief Minister of Sabah]] on 29 September.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Anand |first1=Ram |title=Malaysian PM Muhyiddin's pick Hajiji Mohd Noor sworn in as new Sabah Chief Minister |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysian-pm-muhyiddins-pick-sworn-in-as-new-sabah-chief-minister |access-date=11 February 2021 |work=[[The Straits Times]] |date=29 September 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201118161456/https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysian-pm-muhyiddins-pick-sworn-in-as-new-sabah-chief-minister |archive-date=18 November 2020|url-status=live}}</ref>

Under the new GRS administration, projects that had been stopped by the previous Sabah government were restarted and continued, including the [[Sabah]] [[Pan-Borneo Highway]] Project,<ref name="ReferenceA">[https://www.astroawani.com/berita-malaysia/projek-pan-borneo-sabah-disambung-beri-manfaat-besar-kepada-rakyat-267488 Projek Pan Borneo Sabah disambung, beri manfaat besar kepada rakyat - Ketua-ketua GRS (Interview from Astro Awani)]</ref> Recognition project of [[Anjung Kinabalu|Anjung Kinabalu KK Night Market]]<ref name="nst.com.my">[https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2024/06/1060525/kk-rebrands-markets-anjung-kinabalu-incorporate-local-elements NST News: KK Rebrands Markets, Anjung Kinabalu incorporate local elements]</ref> and the development project of [[The Skybridge City Centre Kota Kinabalu]].<ref>[https://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news/196422/skybridge-access-to-centre-point-sabah-year-end/ Skybridge access to Centre Point Sabah year end]</ref><ref name="ReferenceA"/><ref name="nst.com.my"/>

On 9 January 2021, the various leaders of Gabungan Rakyat Sabah signed a memorandum of understanding affirming each party's commitment to the alliance, with chief minister Hajiji Noor maintaining that the alliance would remain intact regardless of the political situation on the peninsula.<ref>{{cite news |title=Gabungan Rakyat Sabah signs MoU to continue cooperation, reject outside interference |url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/gabungan-rakyat-sabah-signs-mou-continue-cooperation-reject-outside-interference |access-date=11 February 2021 |work=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge]] |agency=[[Bernama]] |date=9 January 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210116153307/https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/gabungan-rakyat-sabah-signs-mou-continue-cooperation-reject-outside-interference |archive-date=16 January 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.borneotoday.net/grs-signs-mou-to-continue-cooperation-reject-outside-interference/|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210109063532/https://www.borneotoday.net/grs-signs-mou-to-continue-cooperation-reject-outside-interference/|url-status=usurped|archive-date=9 January 2021|title=GRS Signs MOU To Continue Cooperation Reject Outside Interference|publisher=The Borneo Post|date=6 January 2017|access-date=30 April 2021}}</ref>

=== Registration and expansion ===

On 24 November 2021, the alliance's backbenchers club chairperson [[Salleh Said Keruak]] floated the idea of registering Gabungan Rakyat Sabah as an official political coalition, arguing it was necessary as a means of strengthening the alliance.<ref>{{Cite news|date=24 November 2021|title=GRS should be registered as official political coalition - Salleh|work=MalaysiaKini|url=https://m.malaysiakini.com/news/600667|access-date=26 November 2021}}</ref> The proposal received the support of various party leaders, including [[Jeffrey G. Kitingan]], [[Masidi Manjun]], [[Yong Teck Lee]], [[Bung Mokhtar Radin]], [[Joniston Bangkuai]] and [[Joseph Pairin Kitingan]].<ref>{{Cite news|date=25 November 2021|title=GRS registration facing stumbling blocks, says Jeffrey|work=FreeMalaysiaToday (FMT)|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2021/11/25/grs-registration-facing-stumbling-blocks-says-jeffrey/|access-date=26 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=25 November 2021|title=GRS should be registered to avoid clashes between partners in elections, says Salleh Said|work=TheStar News|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2021/11/25/grs-should-be-registered-to-avoid-clashes-between-partners-in-elections-says-salleh-said|access-date=26 November 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|date=25 November 2021|title=Pendaftaran GRS jamin kestabilan politik Sabah|work=Utusan Malaysia|url=https://www.utusan.com.my/terkini/2021/11/pendaftaran-grs-jamin-kestabilan-politik-sabah/|access-date=26 November 2021}}</ref>

The alliance was officially registered and legalised by the [[Societies Act 1966|Registrar of Societies]] on 11 March 2022,<ref name="Bernama" /> with Sabah BERSATU, PBS, [[Homeland Solidarity Party]] (STAR), and the [[Sabah Progressive Party]] (SAPP) as its members.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2022/03/935697/gabungan-rakyat-sabah-didaftar-secara-rasmi-tanpa-umno-pas-bn|title=Gabungan Rakyat Sabah didaftar secara rasmi tanpa UMNO, PAS, BN &#124; Berita Harian|date=18 March 2022 }}</ref>

On 9 May 2022, the [[United Sabah National Organisation (New)|United Sabah National Organisation (Baru)]] (USNO Baru) was admitted as a member of the coalition.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2022/05/09/usno-becomes-newest-member-of-sabah-coalition/|title=Usno becomes newest member of Sabah coalition|date=May 9, 2022|website=FMT News|access-date=May 9, 2022}}</ref>

On 25 September 2022, GRS chairman Hajiji Noor announced that the coalition would support Barisan Nasional candidates in the upcoming [[2022 Malaysian general election]], contrary to Perikatan Nasional, of which both Sabah BERSATU and STAR was a member, which had declared Barisan as its main enemy.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/637003 | title=GRS mahu terus kerjasama dengan BN pada PRU15 | date=25 September 2022 }}</ref>

===Mass defections from Sabah BERSATU===

{{see also|Parti Pribumi Bersatu Malaysia Sabah}}
[[Malaysian United Indigenous Party of Sabah]], the regional branch of the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]], otherwise known as BERSATU, effectively collapsed when all eleven state legislative assemblymen including the party's state chairman Hajiji Noor, and four members of parliament left the party on 10 December 2022.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2022/12/1037660/pemimpin-bersatu-sabah-keluar-parti-kekal-bawah-grs-hajiji|title=Pemimpin BERSATU Sabah keluar parti, kekal bawah GRS - Hajiji|last= Abdullah|first=Izwan|date=2022-12-10|website=BHarian Online|access-date=2022-12-11}}</ref> Rumours surfaced that the former members of Sabah BERSATU would take over [[Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah]] (GAGASAN),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2022/12/1038132/bekas-pemimpin-bersatu-sabah-sertai-pgrs|title=Bekas pemimpin BERSATU Sabah sertai PGRS?|last= Abdullah|first=Izwan|date=2022-12-11|website=BHarian Online|access-date=2022-12-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2022/12/859747/ex-sabah-bersatu-reps-join-gprs-source|title=Ex-Sabah Bersatu reps to join PGRS: Source|date=2022-12-11|website=New Straits Times|access-date=2022-12-11}}</ref> which came to pass as Hajiji Noor took over the party on 29 January 2023, with the party itself having been admitted into the coalition earlier on 9 December.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Miwil |first=Olivia |date=2023-01-29 |title=Hajiji heads Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/01/874515/hajiji-heads-parti-gagasan-rakyat-sabah-nsttv |access-date=2023-12-09 |website=NST Online |language=en}}</ref><ref name="Mirlen Axtius">{{Cite news|url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/hajiji-ketuai-parti-gagasan-rakyat-sabah-404103|title=Hajiji ketuai Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah|author=Mirlen Axtius|date=29 January 2023|newspaper=Astro Awani|access-date=29 January 2023}}</ref>

Political analysts regarded Hajiji Noor's decision to leave Sabah BERSATU as a shrewd,<ref name="Express" /> with Romzi Ationg commenting that the transformation of the Gabungan Rakyat Sabah coalition into a purely Sabah-based entity had been long-awaited by the local population.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Miwil |first=Olivia |date=2023-01-30 |title=Hajiji's Gagasan Rakyat long awaited by people of Sabah |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2023/01/874733/hajijis-gagasan-rakyat-long-awaited-people-sabah |access-date=2023-12-09 |website=NST Online |language=en}}</ref>

However, as a result of the parliamentary anti-hopping law, the four former Sabah BERSATU members of parliament could not join Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah. They instead became direct members of the coalition.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2023/01/bekas-ahli-parlimen-bersatu-tidak-akan-sertai-grs/ | title=Bekas Ahli Parlimen Bersatu tidak akan sertai GRS | date=29 January 2023 }}</ref>

=== 2025 Sabah state election ===
{{see also|2025 Sabah state election}}
In the lead up to the [[2025 Sabah state election]], GRS entered into a seat-sharing pact with [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH). In opposition to this move, STAR and SAPP announced that they would contest under their own symbols rather than be bound by the agreed-upon seat allocation, resulting in the termination of their memberships in the coalition.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Chan |first=Julia |date=2 October 2025 |title=STAR, SAPP signal exit from GRS after insisting on contesting Sabah election under own logos |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2025/10/02/star-sapp-signal-exit-from-grs-after-insisting-on-contesting-sabah-election-under-own-logos/193184 |access-date=12 November 2025 |website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=en}}</ref> Five state assemblymen from STAR left the party and joined GRS as direct members prior to the assembly's dissolution.<ref>{{Cite web |date=6 October 2025 |title=Five Star assemblymen break ranks to stay with GRS after party’s exit |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2025/10/06/five-star-assemblymen-break-ranks-to-stay-with-grs-after-partys-exit/193630 |access-date=12 November 2025 |website=[[Malay Mail]] |language=en}}</ref>
