The '''Homeland Fighter's Party''' ({{langx|ms|Parti Pejuang Tanah Air}}, '''PEJUANG''') is a [[Right-wing politics|right-wing]], nationalist political party of [[Malaysia]], formed in August 2020 by former [[Prime Minister of Malaysia|prime minister]] [[Mahathir Mohamad]] in opposition to then ruling [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) government led by prime minister, chairman of PN and president of the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) [[Muhyiddin Yassin]].

The logo of PEJUANG party consists of a stylised [[Jawi script|Jawi]] letter, '''[[ڤ]]''' (P), which is the first letter in the word "Pejuang".

== History ==
The party was formed by [[Mahathir Mohamad]] at the beginning of the [[2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis]], which was triggered by his resignation as prime minister and the exit of his [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) from the governing [[Pakatan Harapan]] coalition.<ref>{{cite news |date= 24 February 2020|title= Bersatu umum keluar Pakatan Harapan|url= http://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/bersatu-umum-keluar-pakatan-harapan-231535|access-date= 14 August 2020}}</ref> Mahathir and four MPs from BERSATU later rejected the leadership of [[Muhyiddin Yassin]], BERSATU's president, when he was appointed prime minister with the backing of a new coalition, and were subsequently expelled from the party in May.<ref>{{cite news |date= 28 May 2020|title= Tun M dipecat dari Bersatu|url= https://www.hmetro.com.my/utama/2020/05/583424/tun-m-dipecat-dari-bersatu|access-date= 14 August 2020}}</ref> They were later joined by [[Shahruddin Md Salleh]], who resigned as deputy minister of works only three months after his appointment.

Mahathir announced his intentions to form a new Malay-based party ahead of the [[2020 Slim by-election]],<ref>{{cite news |date= 7 August 2020|title= Tun Mahathir umum akan tubuh parti Melayu baharu|url= http://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/tun-mahathir-umum-akan-tubuh-parti-melayu-baharu-254290|access-date= 14 August 2020}}</ref> but was not joined in his endeavour by former youth minister [[Syed Saddiq]], a fellow BERSATU expellee.

The party made its electoral debut at the Slim by-election held in August, where it finished a distant second. Fewer than three months later, former education minister [[Maszlee Malik]] announced his departure from the party, at this point had yet to be formally registered, to sit as an independent MP.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2 November 2020 |title=Maszlee Malik quits Pejuang |url=https://thesun.my/malaysia-news/maszlee-malik-quits-pejuang-EG4932798 |access-date=3 October 2025 |website=thesun.my |language=en-MY}}</ref>

The party was formally registered in July 2021 after judicial review against the state authorities' decision to reject its application.<ref>{{Cite web |date=7 January 2021 |title=RoS rejects applications from Pejuang to be registered as political party |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2021/01/07/ros-rejects-pejuang039s-application-to-be-registered-as-political-party |access-date=7 January 2021 |website=www.thestar.com.my}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2021/07/08/amid-political-tumult-pejuang-says-now-officially-registered-as-a-party/1988346|date=8 July 2021|title=Amid political tumult, Pejuang says now officially registered as a party|publisher=Malay Mail|access-date= 8 July 2021}}</ref>

While stating that the party was open to the possibility of co-operating with other parties, it contested the [[2022 Johor state election]] outside of any coalition and fared poorly. It was defeated in the 42 (out of 56 total seats available) seats it contested. Party president Mukhriz Mahathir blamed the lack of exposure of the party's logo as one of the reasons for their defeat.<ref>{{Cite web |date=13 March 2022 |title=Lepas hilang semua deposit, Pejuang kata logo kurang dikenali |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/614185 |website=[[Malaysiakini]] |language=ms}}</ref>

In the [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 general election]], the party contested 158 out of 222 seats available as part of the [[Gerakan Tanah Air]] coalition, but failed to be elected in any of them. The party's three sitting MPs that sought re-election, Mahathir in [[Langkawi (federal constituency)|Langkawi]], Mukhriz in [[Jerlun (federal constituency)|Jerlun]], and [[Amiruddin Hamzah]] in [[Kubang Pasu (federal constituency)|Kubang Pasu]], fared poorly and lost their deposits.<ref name="deposit">{{cite news |date=20 November 2022 |title=All 158 GTA-Pejuang Candidates Collectively Lose RM1.3 Million Of Deposit Money In #GE15 |url=https://says.com/my/news/gta-pejuang-candidates-lose-rm1-3-million-deposit-money |access-date=21 November 2022 |work=says.com |pages=}}</ref>

A number of party leaders subsequently resigned from their leadership roles, including Mahathir as party chairman.<ref name="Utusan Malaysia 2022 p752">{{cite web |date=16 December 2022 |title=Tun M letak jawatan Pengerusi Pejuang |url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2022/12/tun-m-letak-jawatan-pengerusi-pejuang/ |access-date=4 August 2023 |website=[[Utusan Malaysia]] |language=ms}}</ref>

In January 2023, the party left Gerakan Tanah Air,<ref>{{cite web |date=14 January 2023 |title=Pejuang keluar GTA - Mukhriz |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/651628 |website=[[Malaysiakini]] |language=ms}}</ref> which triggered the departure of a number of high-profile members, including Mahathir and deputy president [[Marzuki Yahya]].<ref>{{cite web |date=10 February 2023 |title=Mahathir keluar Pejuang |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/654686 |website=[[Malaysiakini]] |language=ms}}</ref>

In February 2023, the party's number of elected representatives was reduced to four after its Selangor state chairman was ordered to vacate his [[Batang Kali (state constituency)|Batang Kali]] in the [[Selangor State Legislative Assembly|Selangor legislative assembly]] as a result of non-attendance.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/02/16/batang-kali-seat-now-vacant-assemblyman-evicted-for-absenteeism|title=Batang Kali seat now vacant, assemblyman evicted for absenteeism|website=The Star|date=16 February 2023|accessdate=17 February 2023}}</ref>

The party lost the remainder of its representation after opting not to contest in the [[2023 Malaysian state elections]], which followed [[Perikatan Nasional]]'s decision not to admit it as a component party in March 2023.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-22 |title=PEJUANG TIDAK BERTANDING DALAM PRN DI ENAM NEGERI - MUKHRIZ |url=https://www.bernama.com/bm/politik/news.php?id=2208894 |access-date=2023-07-22 |website=BERNAMA |language=ms}}</ref>
