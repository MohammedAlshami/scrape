'''Sabah People's Ideas Party''' ([[Malay language|Malay]]: ''Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah''; [[Abbreviation|abbrev]]: '''GAGASAN''' or '''PGRS''') is a [[Regionalism (politics)|regionalist]] political party based in [[Sabah]], [[East Malaysia|East]] [[Malaysia]]. Founded in August 2013 by the late [[Ationg Tituh]], continued by [[:ms:Stephen Jacob Jimbangan|Stephen Jacob Jimbangan]] in 2021, the party was then taken over by [[Hajiji Noor]] in 2023 and is currently the largest party in [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] (GRS), the ruling coalition in Sabah.<ref name="Ketuai">{{Cite web| title=Hajiji bakal ketuai Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah | date=29 January 2023 | language=ms | trans-title=Hajiji to lead Sabah People's Idea Party | url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/hajiji-ketuai-parti-gagasan-rakyat-sabah-404103 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230129044047/https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/hajiji-ketuai-parti-gagasan-rakyat-sabah-404103 | archive-date=2023-01-29}}</ref>

== History ==

The party was founded on 28 August 2013 by the late [[Ationg Tituh]] as a successor to the dissolved [[People's Justice Front|United People's Justice Front Party]] (AKAR)<ref>[https://www.sabahgazette.com/pgrs-tandingi-semua-73-kerusi/ PGRS dibentuk pada tahun 2009 tetapi hanya diluluskan secara rasmi oleh Pendaftar Pertubuhan tiga tahun kemudian.]</ref> but remained dormant until 2016 due to a lack of funds and manpower.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Fong |first=Durie Rainer |date=2019-08-27 |title=Sabah's small opposition party has big aims |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/08/27/sabahs-small-opposition-party-has-big-aims/ |access-date=2024-07-17 |website=Free Malaysia Today |language=en}}</ref>

GAGASAN was a member to a failed merger involving [[Love Sabah Party]], [[Sabah Native Cooperation Party]], and a yet-to-be-registered Parti Hak Sabah with [[Anifah Aman]] as president.<ref>{{Cite news|url= http://www.dailyexpress.com.my/news/148398/new-sabah-opposition-grouping-is-formed/|title=New Sabah opposition grouping is formed |publisher= [[Daily Express (Malaysia)|Daily Express]]|author=Hayati Dzulkifli and Sherell Ann Jeffrey |date= March 8, 2020|access-date=March 8, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2020/07/28/anifahs-planned-sabah-opposition-front-fizzles-out|title=Anifah's planned Sabah opposition front fizzles out |work=[[The Star Online]] |author=Muguntan Vanar |date=28 July 2020 |access-date=28 July 2020}}</ref> Prior to the party's takeover by Hajiji in 2023, it was dominated by the [[Kadazan-Dusun]]-[[Murut people|Murut]]-[[Rungus people|Rungus]] (KDMR), an amalgamation of related indigenous ethnic groups.<ref>{{Cite news|url= https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/03/573109/gagasan-led-anifah-aman-not-likely-make-big-impact|title=Gagasan led by Anifah Aman not likely to make a big impact|publisher= [[New Straits Times]]|author=Avila Geraldine |date=March 9, 2020 |access-date= March 9, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |last=Fong |first=Durie Rainer |date=2019-08-27 |title=Sabah's small opposition party has big aims |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2019/08/27/sabahs-small-opposition-party-has-big-aims/ |access-date=2024-07-17 |website=Free Malaysia Today |language=en}}</ref>

Sabah Chief Minister [[Hajiji Noor]] announced his takeover of the party on 29 January 2023, with the party having been accepted into the [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] coalition, which Hajiji also led, on 9 December 2022.<ref>{{Cite web |title=Hajiji bakal ketuai Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/hajiji-bakal-ketuai-parti-gagasan-rakyat-sabah-404103 |access-date=2024-07-17 |website=www.astroawani.com |date=29 January 2023 }}</ref> The four members of parliament who left the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party of Sabah]] in December 2022 were not allowed to join GAGASAN as a result of the federal anti-party hopping law.<ref>{{cite web | url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2023/01/bekas-ahli-parlimen-bersatu-tidak-akan-sertai-grs/ | title=Bekas Ahli Parlimen Bersatu tidak akan sertai GRS | date=29 January 2023 }}</ref>

The party gained 26 members of the [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly]] over the course of the [[2023 Sabah political crisis]].

== Party symbols==
===Flag and colours===
GAGASAN’s flag consists of a red field defaced with a central light blue simplified silhouette of [[Mount Kinabalu]], above the party name “GAGASAN RAKYAT” in white uppercase, set in two right-aligned lines beneath the silhouette, each line approximately less than a half of the silhouette. The main colors used in the party's logo and flag are [[red]], white and [[blue]].<ref name="Ketuai" />
