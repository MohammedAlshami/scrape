'''Muafakat Nasional''' ('''MN''', {{langx|en|'''National Concord'''}}) was a Malaysian [[political coalition]] that was formed between the [[Malaysian Islamic Party]] (PAS) and [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) against the then-ruling [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) coalition which had come into power after the [[2018 Malaysian general election]] (GE14).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.themalaysianinsight.com/g/183399|title= The Umno-PAS 'new deal'|date=14 September 2019 |work=The Malaysian Insight|author=Hasnoor Hussain}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bernama.com/bm/news.php?id=1767460|title=UMNO-PAS meterai Piagam Muafakat Nasiona|date=14 September 2019 |work=Bernama}}</ref> The political cooperation between the two largest [[Malaysian Malay|Malay]]/[[Malaysian Muslims|Muslim]]-based parties was officialised with the signing of the ''Piagam Muafakat Nasional'' (National Cooperation Charter).<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/491923|title=Umno, PAS ink charter formalising political alliance|date=14 September 2019 |work=Malaysiakini|author=Haspaizi Zain}}</ref> The five-point charter was signed by PAS president [[Abdul Hadi Awang]] and UMNO president [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] in the ''Himpunan Penyatuan Ummah'' (Ummah Unity Rally) held at [[Putra World Trade Centre]] (PWTC), Kuala Lumpur on 14 September 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/09/14/political-foes-turned-friends-umno-pas-sign-historic-pact|title=Political foes-turned-friends Umno, PAS sign historic pact|date=14 September 2019 |work=The Star|author= Tarrence Tan, Hanis Zainal and Fatimah Zainal}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/491915|title=Umno, PAS akhirnya meterai Piagam Muafakat Nasional|date=14 September 2019 |work=Malaysiakini|author=Haspaizi Zain|language=ms}}</ref> Its main aim is to unite the Malay/[[Islam in Malaysia|Muslim]] community or [[Ummah]] for electoral purposes.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.1media.my/2019/09/piagam-muafakat-nasional.html|title=Piagam muafakat Nasional Diisytiharkan|date=14 September 2019 |work=1media.my}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://themalaysianreserve.com/2019/09/18/whats-next-for-piagam-muafakat-nasional/ |title= What's next for Piagam Muafakat Nasional? |date=18 September 2019 |work=The Malaysian Reserve }}</ref>

== History ==

Despite calls for Muafakat Nasional to be institutionalised and become more inclusive towards [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and [[Gagasan Sejahtera]] (GS) where UMNO and PAS are major component parties respectively, there has been no formal agreement with the other parties in both coalitions to migrate to Muafakat Nasional.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/nation/2019/12/545078/call-muafakat-nasional-be-institutionalised |title=Call for Muafakat Nasional to be institutionalised|date=5 December 2019|work=New Straits Times|author=Teh Athira Yusof and Teoh Pei Ying }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/calls-in-umno-for-barisan-nasional-to-migrate-to-muafakat-nasional |title=Calls in Umno for Barisan Nasional to 'migrate' to Muafakat Nasional |date=5 December 2019 |work=New Straits Times |author=Reme Ahmad}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/politics-and-policy-choices-mic-mca-and-muafakat-nasional |title=Politics and Policy: Choices for MIC, MCA and Muafakat Nasional|date=19 December 2019|work=The Edge Malaysia|author=Mohsin Abdullah }}</ref> The new Muafakat Nasional coalition had planned to be formalised at its inaugural convention to be held by May 2020.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2019/12/545725/muafakat-nasional-be-formalised-may |title=Muafakat Nasional be formalised by May|date=7 December 2019 |work=New Straits Times }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2019/12/545723/umno-pas-set-muafakat-nasional-secretariat-pwtc |title=Umno, Pas to set-up Muafakat Nasional secretariat at PWTC |date=7 December 2019 |work=New Straits Times }}</ref> A permanent secretariat of the pact was set-up at UMNO's headquarters located at PWTC in May 2020.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://umno-online.my/2020/05/14/zahid-hadi-jejak-masuk-pertama-kali-di-pejabat-muafakat-nasional/ |title=Zahid, Hadi Jejak Masuk Pertama Kali Di Pejabat Muafakat Nasional |date=14 May 2020 |work=UMNO ONLINE |language=ms}}</ref>

Muafakat Nasional had extended an invitation to the [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party]] (BERSATU) led by its president and then-prime minister [[Muhyiddin Yassin]] to join the alliance in July 2020.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/muafakat-nasional-has-extended-official-invitation-bersatu-annuar-253422 |title=Muafakat Nasional has extended official invitation to Bersatu - Annuar|work=[[Bernama]]|date=30 July 2020|publisher=[[Astro Awani]] }}</ref> On 15 August 2020, Muhyiddin reaffirmed that the party will join the alliance.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.nst.com.my/news/politics/2020/07/612938/bersatu-officially-invited-join-muafakat-nasional |title=Bersatu officially invited to join Muafakat Nasional|work=[[Bernama]]|date=31 July 2020|publisher=[[New Straits Times]]}}</ref> However, BERSATU had also formed another coalition called [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) in the same period.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2020/09/14/ros-confirms-perikatan-as-registered-entity-says-approved-on-aug-7/1903174 |title=RoS confirms Perikatan as registered entity, says approved on Aug 7|author=Radzi Razak|work=[[Malay Mail]] |date=14 September 2020 |access-date=17 September 2020 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thediplomat.com/2021/07/umno-withdraws-its-support-for-malaysian-ruling-coalition|title=UMNO Withdraws its support|author=Sebastian Strangio|date=8 July 2021|publisher=[[The Diplomat (magazine)|The Diplomat]] |access-date=12 February 2021}}</ref> On 3 April 2021, [[Wan Saiful Wan Jan]] the information chief of BERSATU revealed that the party had agreed to join the alliance but received no official response from either UMNO or PAS.<ref>{{cite news |url= https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2021/04/kita-setuju-jemputan-masuk-mn-tapi-tidak-ada-respons/ |title=Kita setuju jemputan masuk MN, tapi tidak ada respons|date=3 April 2021|work=Utusan Malaysia |language=ms}}</ref>

On 30 January 2022, ahead of the [[2022 Johor state election]], former prime minister [[Mohd Najib Abdul Razak]] disclosed that BERSATU was not included in the MN alliance as it had decided to contest against UMNO during the [[2020 Sabah state election]], in which BERSATU's newly formed PN coalition fielded candidates in all 17 seats.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/53246/bersatu-not-part-of-muafakat-as-it-betrayed-umno-during-sabah-polls-najib|title='Bersatu not part of Muafakat as it betrayed Umno during Sabah polls'|author=|publisher=The Vibes|date=30 January 2022|access-date=31 January 2022}}</ref>

On 15 December 2022, former Minister of Communication and Multimedia Tan Sri [[Annuar Musa]] declared that Muafakat Nasional was no longer a political coalition but a non-governmental organisation.<ref>{{Cite web |title=[VIDEO] Muafakat Nasional bukan gerakan politik, kata Annuar Musa - Agenda Daily |url=https://www.agendadaily.com/video/video-muafakat-nasional-bukan-gerakan-politik-kata-annuar-musa/ |access-date=2022-12-15 |website=www.agendadaily.com}}</ref>   He is also announced the list of the council members of the Muafakat Nasional (MN), including former Selangor UMNO chairman [[Noh Omar]], former minister of home affairs [[Zuraida Kamaruddin]], Federal Territory President of the Homeland Fighters' Party Khairuddin Abu Hassan, Chinese Muslim scholar Mohd Ridhuan Tee Abdullah, Chairman of the Joint Committee of MCA Kelantan Chua Hock Kuan, and former Minister in the Prime Minister's Department [[Mohd Redzuan Md Yusof]].
