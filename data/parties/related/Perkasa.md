'''Pertubuhan Pribumi Perkasa''' ([[Malay language|Malay]] for "Mighty Native Organisation" or better known by its [[acronym]]: '''Perkasa'''), is a [[Ketuanan Melayu|Malay supremacist]]<ref>{{cite news |title=Perkasa winning support for attacks on demands by minority groups |url=http://www.malaysia-today.net/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=30138:perkasa-winning-support-for-attacks-on-demands-by-minority-groups&catid=19:newscommentaries&Itemid=100131 |publisher=Malaysia Today |date=11 February 2010 |accessdate=27 July 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100325222127/http://www.malaysia-today.net/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=30138%3Aperkasa-winning-support-for-attacks-on-demands-by-minority-groups&catid=19%3Anewscommentaries&Itemid=100131 |archive-date=25 March 2010 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> [[non-governmental organisation]] (NGO) that was formed by [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] in the aftermath of the [[Malaysian general election, 2008|2008 general election]] (GE12). This conservative, [[extreme-right]], [[ethnic Malay]] organisation is led by its president Ibrahim Ali to influence the unity of Malaysian by refusing to accept any decoration of other ethnic group except Malay  decoration. The group is reported to have a membership of 700,000.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Ebrahim Harris|last2=Yuddy Cahya|last3=Rozanna Latiff|last4=Robert Birsel|last5=Paul Tait|title=Malaysian Muslim group joins Indonesian call for Starbucks boycott over LGBT stand|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-starbucks-lgbt-indonesia-malaysia-idUSKBN19R0NM|accessdate=11 July 2017|work=Reuters|quote=Perkasa, a group with about 700,000 members that campaigns for the rights of ethnic Malay Muslims}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Perkasa poised for a new role |url=http://www.thestar.com.my/Opinion/Columnists/Analysis/Profile/Articles/2014/01/05/Perkasa-poised-for-a-new-role/ |work=The Star Online |date=5 January 2014 |accessdate=25 January 2014 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140123100136/http://www.thestar.com.my/Opinion/Columnists/Analysis/Profile/Articles/2014/01/05/Perkasa-poised-for-a-new-role/ |archive-date=23 January 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

The major objectives for establishing Perkasa, according to [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]], are to act as "protectors of [[Article 153 of the Constitution of Malaysia]]" and to defend the rights of [[Bumiputera (Malaysia)|Bumiputera]] from being eroded by certain quarters.<ref>{{cite news |title=The Real Deal with Perkasa |url=http://www.thenutgraph.com/real-deal-perkasa/ |publisher=The Nut Graph |date=16 March 2010 |accessdate=29 July 2010}}</ref> Perkasa is said to be fighting and defending the rights of the [[Malaysian Malays|Malays]] which they feel are being challenged by non-Malays in [[Malaysia]].

Perkasa has recently become infamous for its racial and religious provocations against non-Malays, especially Chinese and Indians; and non-Muslims, especially Christians through vocal, physical and violent means.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2012/05/10/perkasa-antics-will-cost-bn/ |title=Perkasa antics will cost BN |access-date=23 January 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160418232550/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2012/05/10/perkasa-antics-will-cost-bn/ |archive-date=18 April 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Despite the continuous offensive and seditious remarks by its chief Ibrahim Ali and its members that have threatened social harmony, no legal action has been taken by the Malaysian authorities so far.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.mmail.com.my/story/call-action-against-perkasa-chief-45041 |title=Call for action against Perkasa chief |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://archive.today/20130221033309/http://www.mmail.com.my/story/call-action-against-perkasa-chief-45041 |archive-date=21 February 2013 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> It has been branded a [[Fascism|fascist]] organisation by MPs from the Malaysian federal opposition coalition [[Pakatan Rakyat]] (PR) in the past.<ref>{{cite news |title=Curb "fascist" Perkasa, Rahim Noor, Pakatan MPs tell Najib |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/curb-fascist-perkasa-rahim-noor-pakatan-mps-tell-najib/ |publisher=The Malaysian Insider |date=28 October 2011 |accessdate=28 October 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111028195139/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/curb-fascist-perkasa-rahim-noor-pakatan-mps-tell-najib/ |archive-date=28 October 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

In September 2010, Ibrahim Ali claimed that 60% of Perkasa members were [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO) members.<ref name=UMNOties>{{cite news|title=Perkasa has nothing to do with Umno: Ibrahim Ali|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/story/?file=%2f2010%2f9%2f9%2fnation%2f20100909174045&sec=nation|accessdate=15 October 2015|publisher=The Star (Malaysia)|date=9 September 2010|quote=Ibrahim claimed that 60% of its members were Umno members while its younger members were not politically-affiliated. Despite Perkasa's close association with UMNO, Ibrahim Ali during Perkasa's 2013 annual general assembly, had alleged that UMNO had shown weakness and become "toothless" in their efforts to safeguard the rights of the Malays and had implied the possibility of Perkasa becoming a full-fledged political party to participate in [[Malaysian general election, 2018|2018 general election]] (GE14), going head-to-head against UMNO.<ref>{{cite news |title=We can replace you as Malay champions Perkasa warns UMNO |url=http://m.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/we-can-replace-you-as-malay-champions-perkasa-warns-umno |publisher=The Malay Mail Online |date=22 December 2013 |accessdate=25 January 2014 |archive-date=4 March 2016 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304210849/http://m.themalaymailonline.com/malaysia/article/we-can-replace-you-as-malay-champions-perkasa-warns-umno |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2018, after the GE14 which saw the downfall of [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and UMNO government, Ibrahim Ali finally founded [[Parti Bumiputera Perkasa Malaysia]] (PUTRA); a new party that based on the UMNO original constitution to serve as an alternative to the Malays and the new party registration was approved in 2019.<ref name="putra">{{cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2019/05/09/ibrahim-ali-unveils-new-party-putra-to-fight-for-race-and-religion/|title=Ibrahim Ali unveils new party, Putra, to fight for race and religion |author= Hanis Zainal|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=9 May 2019|accessdate=12 May 2019}}</ref>

== Leadership ==
Ibrahim Ali, the former [[Pasir Mas (federal constituency)|Pasir Mas]] [[Dewan Rakyat|Member of Parliament]], is the founder and first president of Perkasa. The other leader of Perkasa is its Deputy President Dato' Fuad Hassan (2008–2014).<ref>{{cite news|title=New platform for old mission|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/2/14/focus/5668392&sec=focus|publisher=TheStar Online|date=14 February 2010|accessdate=6 March 2010|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110622062250/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F2%2F14%2Ffocus%2F5668392&sec=focus|archive-date=22 June 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Ibrahim has attempted twice to resign as Perkasa president in 2013<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2013/05/10/perkasa-rejects-ibrahim-alis-resignation-as-president/|title=Perkasa rejects Ibrahim Ali's resignation as president|publisher=[[The Star Online]]|work=[[Bernama]]|date=10 May 2013|access-date=12 May 2019}}</ref> and again in 2018,<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2018/06/10/ibrahim-ali-my-resignation-can-be-discussed-during-perkasa-agm/1640521|title=Ibrahim Ali: My resignation can be discussed during Perkasa AGM|author=Ida Nadirah Ibrahim|date=10 June 2018|publisher=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=12 May 2019}}</ref> but both were rejected by Perkasa's supreme council.

== Controversies ==
===Publication of seditious articles===
On 1 July 2010, the official organ of Perkasa, ''Suara Perkasa'' published an article on its front page asking [[Wee Ka Siong|Dr. Wee Ka Siong]] to be detained under [[Internal Security Act (Malaysia)|Internal Security Act]] (ISA) for questioning the special position of [[Bumiputera (Malaysia)|Bumiputera]] which is under the [[Article 153 of the Constitution of Malaysia]]. However, Wee dismissed Perkasa's allegation.<ref>{{cite news|title= Perkasa: Arrest Ka Siong under ISA|url= http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/7/1/nation/6585279&sec=nation|publisher= The Star (Malaysia)|date= 1 July 2010|accessdate= 29 July 2010|url-status= dead|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100704032933/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F7%2F1%2Fnation%2F6585279&sec=nation|archive-date= 4 July 2010|df= dmy-all}}</ref> ''Suara Perkasa'' was called by the Malaysian Home Ministry the following day for an explanation. The article received a backlash by the component parties in [[Barisan Nasional]], including [[United Malays National Organisation]] (UMNO), [[Malaysian Chinese Association]] (MCA) and [[Malaysian Indian Congress]] (MIC).<ref>{{cite news|title= Suara Perkasa asked to explain 'ISA' article|url= http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/7/2/nation/20100702203845&sec=nation|publisher= The Star (Malaysia)|date= 2 July 2010|accessdate= 29 July 2010|url-status= dead|archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20100705095913/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2010%2F7%2F2%2Fnation%2F20100702203845&sec=nation|archive-date= 5 July 2010|df= dmy-all}}</ref> On 5 July 2010, the controversial ''Suara Perkasa'' was let off with a warning by the Home Ministry for publishing an article that could have jeopardised national unity. However some members of UMNO, the leading component of the ruling Barisan Nasional government in Malaysia are supporters of its founder, who was also previously a member of the political party. Datuk Ibrahim Ali was an independent MP in his home state of Kelantan and was known to have strong grassroots support in his constituency. He is also close to the top leadership of UMNO (United Malay National Organisation) and at one time was a member of the Supreme Council of that party. He is still an influential politician and was also close to the previous Deputy Minister of Malaysia, Datuk Seri Anwar Ibrahim, as they were previously members of ABIM ([[Angkatan Belia Islam Malaysia]] - Malaysian Islamic Youth Assembly) which the former deputy minister headed. Datuk Seri Ibrahim Ali was the Member of Parliament for Pasir Mas (2008–2013), a constituency in his home state of Kelantan. He is now rallying support from Malaysians to reject Datuk Seri [[Anwar Ibrahim]] as the next Malaysian leader on account of the alleged personality flaws of the latter. This movement seems to gather much momentum among the rural Malays and Malaysians in general as can be evidenced by massive turnout especially among his Malay supporters. He is aided in his campaign by Datuk Hassan Ali, a former member of [[Malaysian Islamic Party|Parti Islam Se-Malaysia]] who had left the Pakatan Rakyat coalition top leadership because of differences of opinion on multiple issues, one of which relating to the controversial use of the word Allah in the Bible.<ref>{{cite news |title= 'Suara Perkasa' let off with slap on wrist|url= http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/136415|publisher=Malaysia Kini|date= 5 July 2010 |accessdate=27 July 2010 }}</ref>

===Counter protest against Bersih 2.0===
During the Perkasa Anti-Bersih launch at Kelab Sultan Sulaiman on 19 June 2011 which is a counter protest of [[Bersih 2.0 rally]], president [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] was reported stoking racial tension in his speech, warning the Chinese to "stock up food" as "anything can happen", because he claimed that if [[Bersih 2.0 rally]] was not cancelled, racial riots similar to [[13 May incident]] may happen. In other word, he implied that the Chinese should be staying indoors to avoid any trouble on the day of the [[Bersih 2.0 rally]] on 9 July 2011. Following his speech, lawmakers from both sides of the political divide have accused him of turning the rally, which was supposed to be demanding for free and fair election, into a racial issue.  [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO]] Youth Chief [[Khairy Jamaluddin]] suggested that Ibrahim Ali should be probed under the Sedition Act.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-claims-chinese-support-in-anti-bersih-drive|title=Perkasa Claims Chinese Support in anti-Bersih drive|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107082833/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-claims-chinese-support-in-anti-bersih-drive|archive-date=7 January 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Distribution of white angpows===
During the Chinese New Year celebration organised by Perkasa in February 2012, Perkasa president [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] was reported distributing RM10,000 packed in "white-coloured [[red envelope|angpows]]" to ethnic-Chinese senior citizens. This has angered many ethnic-Chinese in Malaysia of touching the sensitivities of the Chinese. In Chinese culture, money given out in white packets is traditionally-associated with the 'pak kam' (white gold), which are donations given out at funerals. His actions has received heavy criticisms from [[Malaysian Chinese]] leaders such as MCA Young Professionals Bureau chief Datuk [[Chua Tee Yong]], MCA Youth secretary-general Datuk [[Chai Kim Sen]] and MCA Youth chief Datuk Dr [[Wee Ka Siong]], with many described the president's actions as "disrespectful" and "insensitive". [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] then eventually expressed regret for not being aware of the cultural taboo of the Malaysian Chinese and added that such mistakes could be avoided, giving an excuse that they have run out of red-coloured ang pows.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2012/1/31/nation/10566887&sec=nation|title=Young MCA leaders see red over Perkasa's white packets|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120502071722/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2012%2F1%2F31%2Fnation%2F10566887&sec=nation|archive-date=2 May 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2012/2/1/nation/20120201201314&sec=nation|title=Perkasa expresses regret over white angpow incident|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120503035338/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2012%2F2%2F1%2Fnation%2F20120201201314&sec=nation|archive-date=3 May 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Chinese as a national security threat===
On 12 December 2012, Perkasa chief Datuk [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] commented that of late the [[Malaysian Chinese]] have been playing up political sentiments and accused the country's second-largest ethnic group of forgotten how living in Malaysia is "like heaven". He also commented that the Chinese community will become a national security threat which would result in racial riots similar to the [[13 May incident]] if it grows more powerful economically and politically.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/growing-chinese-clout-may-cause-new-may-13-says-ibrahim-ali|title=Growing Chinese clout may cause new May 13, says Ibrahim Ali|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121219105711/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/growing-chinese-clout-may-cause-new-may-13-says-ibrahim-ali/|archive-date=19 December 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Crusade threat against Christians===
On 12 May 2011, its president [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] launched a crusade against Christians if the community proceeds with its agenda to replace Islam as Malaysia's official religion, criticising them as being ungrateful people, during a speech organised by the Kommuniti 1 Malaysia in Kampung Changkat, Gombak, Selangor, which was attended by several UMNO leaders. His called for a holy war against Christians after a news report from [[Utusan Malaysia]] claiming that the Christians and [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]] had a conspiracy of turning Malaysia into a Christian country. In response, the DAP had criticised the Malaysian government for its refusal to apprehend Ibrahim Ali for his seditious remark, declaring this as a proof that UMNO is backing the Perkasa leader, with DAP Secretary-General [[Lim Guan Eng]] describing Perkasa as an "alter ego" of UMNO. Meanwhile, [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia|Gerakan]] president Tan Sri Dr [[Koh Tsu Koon]] called for a full investigation into all of Ibrahim's speeches made, commented that the leader was "uncouth" and in clear violation of the concept of "[[1Malaysia]]".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dap-claims-umno-backing-perkasas-crusade-threat-against-christians|title=DAP Claims UMNO backing Perkasa's Crusade Threat Against Christians|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107082851/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dap-claims-umno-backing-perkasas-crusade-threat-against-christians|archive-date=7 January 2014|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Call to burn Malay language bibles===
On 21 January 2013, Perkasa again courted intense public outrage when [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] called on Muslims to burn Malay-language bibles which use the term "[[Allah]]" in their texts.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/01/19/burn-allah-bibles-perkasa-chief-tells-muslims/ |title=Burn "Allah" bibles, Perkasa chief tells Muslims |access-date=22 January 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160328032918/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/01/19/burn-allah-bibles-perkasa-chief-tells-muslims/ |archive-date=28 March 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Perkasa president Ibrahim Ali had made a call during the Perkasa convention in [[Permatang Pauh]], [[Penang]], urging Muslims to burn the Malay language version of the Christian Holy Bibles that contain the words "Allah" and other religious Arabic words written in Jawi. He then threatened that he would quit supporting the [[Barisan Nasional]] after receiving criticisms from leaders of [[Malaysian Chinese Association|MCA]] and [[Malaysian Indian Congress|MIC]]. He then added that he did not intend to offend the Christians and instigating racial and religious tension but as a solution to prevent an alleged conversion of Muslims by evangelists. Responding to Ibrahim's remarks, MIC leader S. Vell Paari urged the government to punish the right-wing Perkasa president or face the possibility of losing its non-Muslim votes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-threatens-to-pull-support-for-mca-mic-over-allah-bible-burning-row|title=Perkasa threatens to pull support for MCA, MIC over Allah bible-burning row|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130123221412/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-threatens-to-pull-support-for-mca-mic-over-allah-bible-burning-row|archive-date=23 January 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref> This has resulted in [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]]'s alleged statement being investigated under the Sedition Act by the Malaysian police after several police reports lodged.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2013/1/25/nation/12623561&sec=nation|title=Perkasa chief being probed over Bible-burning remark|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130127072709/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2013%2F1%2F25%2Fnation%2F12623561&sec=nation|archive-date=27 January 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Religious insensitivity towards Hindus===
On 29 March 2013, Perkasa vice-president and [[Kulim]]-[[Bandar Baharu]] [[Member of Parliament|MP]] [[Zulkifli Noordin]] has been heavily criticised by several Hindu politicians after giving a religious sermon belittling [[Hinduism]]. A [[YouTube]] video of the speech has been circulating on social media. In his speech, he explained how he laughed at the Indian traders on why the [[Hindu deities|Hindu gods]] could not prevent the trader's shop from being flooded. He also questioned the purity and holiness of the [[Ganges River]], [[India]], which is considered sacred by the Hindus, claiming that the Ganges River is filled with chicken carcasses and twigs floating. Besides that, Zulkifli also mocked the Hindu god [[Ganesha|Lord Ganesha]], by questioning why the Hindus are fighting over buying the deity's statue with the trunk broken. He also questioned the holiness of the Hindu gods when he said when the broken parts of the statue can just be mended by plaster.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/zul-noordin-asks-why-hindu-gods-did-not-stop-flood/|title=Zul Noordin asks why Hindu gods did not stop flood|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130331002238/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/zul-noordin-asks-why-hindu-gods-did-not-stop-flood/|archive-date=31 March 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

The video clip has sparked outrage among many people from the [[Hinduism in Malaysia|Malaysian Hindu]] community. [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]] vice-president and lawyer [[N. Surendran]] wants Zulkifli to be charged with Section 298A of the Penal Code for uttering words which causes disharmony, feelings of enmity, hatred or ill-will on grounds of religion. He added that Zulkifli is a close ally with [[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Malaysian Prime Minister]] [[Malay styles and titles|Datuk Seri]] [[Najib Razak|Najib Tun Razak]], in which he described that [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO]] and [[Barisan Nasional]] must take responsibility for the sacrilegious remarks and mockery of Hinduism.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/03/29/charge-insolent-zul-noordin/|title=Charge insolent Zul Noordin|access-date=29 March 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303225057/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/03/29/charge-insolent-zul-noordin/|archive-date=3 March 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

[[Malaysian Indian Congress|MIC]] central working committee member Datuk T. Rajagopalu told Zulkifli to "shut up" and apologise for his insensitive remarks against the Hindus. He added that Zulkifli is unfit to be an MP, describing the lawyer as a half-baked lawyer and not a true Muslim man. MIC vice-president Datuk S.K. Devamany also demanded that Zulkifli apologise and withdraw his remarks.  Aside from them, MIC central working committee member P. Kamalanathan condemned Zulkifli's remarks "in the harshest manner possible." Human rights group [[SUARAM]] also rebuked Zulkifli and urged voters to reject him in the upcoming [[Malaysian general election, 2013|Malaysian 13th general elections]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/mic-leaders-tell-zul-noordin-to-shut-up-and-apologise/|title=MIC leaders tell Zul Noordin to 'shut up' and apologise|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130331002157/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/mic-leaders-tell-zul-noordin-to-shut-up-and-apologise/|archive-date=31 March 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Hindu Sangam chief said that no legal action were taken against Zulkifli because he is a Muslim and vice-president of Perkasa.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/no-action-so-far-against-zul-noordin-because-he-is-muslim-says-hindu-sangam-chief/|title=No action so far against Zul Noordin 'because he is Muslim', says Hindu Sangam chief|access-date=29 March 2013|archive-date=1 April 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130401084448/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/no-action-so-far-against-zul-noordin-because-he-is-muslim-says-hindu-sangam-chief|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Zulkifli later apologised on 1 April 2013 for hurting the feelings of the Indian community,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/zulkifli-noordin-apologises-to-indian-community-over-remarks-in-video-clip|title=Zulkifli Noordin apologises to Indian community over remarks in video clip|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130406104340/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/zulkifli-noordin-apologises-to-indian-community-over-remarks-in-video-clip|archive-date=6 April 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref> but only days later another video clip emerged in which Zulkifli was caught uttering the word "[[Keling]]" during a speech, a word deemed to be extremely offensive towards ethnic Indians.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/after-hindu-insult-zulkifli-nordin-says-keling/|title=After Hindu insult, Zulkifli Noordin says "Keling"|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130408011908/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/after-hindu-insult-zulkifli-nordin-says-keling|archive-date=8 April 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Death of Chin Peng===
Perkasa expressed protest and opposed any attempts to bring the remains of the former secretary-general of the [[Malayan Communist Party]] [[Chin Peng]] back to Malaysia. Ibrahim Ali said that he will never allow Chin Peng to return to Malaysian regardless whether he is alive or dead. He also said any record of Chin Peng should be erased from the annals of the country's history and kept from the eyes of the younger generation, adding that Chin Peng's remains should be "tossed into the air".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-opposes-any-attempt-to-bring-back-chin-pengs-remains|title=Perkasa opposes any attempt to bring back Chin Peng's remains|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130918234150/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-opposes-any-attempt-to-bring-back-chin-pengs-remains|archive-date=18 September 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Perkasa secretary-general Syed Hassan Syed Ali warned [[Malaysian Chinese Association|MCA]] not to upset the Malays by insisting that the Malaysian government should allow Chin Peng's remains to be brought back to Malaysia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dont-upset-malays-with-chin-peng-issue-perkasa-warns-mca|title=Don't upset Malays with Chin Peng issue, Perkasa warns MCA|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130923141149/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/dont-upset-malays-with-chin-peng-issue-perkasa-warns-mca|archive-date=23 September 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Racial riot Threats===
Perkasa president Datuk [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] has warned of another race riot if the majority of the Malays remained in poverty, praising Prime Minister of Malaysia Datuk Seri [[Najib Razak]] of making the right decision by implementing the new RM31bil Bumiputera Economic Empowerment Plan (BEEP) intended to increase the economic status of the Malays. He said that the [[13 May Incident|13 May riots]] occurred because the Malays were not satisfied. Many critics say that the programme has further entrenched race-based policies and inhibit Malaysia's economic competitiveness which then results in a huge brain drain of many non-Malays.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-chief-warns-of-fresh-race-riot-if-malays-not-helped-economically|title=Perkasa chief warns of fresh race riot if Malays not helped economically|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131014173540/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/perkasa-chief-warns-of-fresh-race-riot-if-malays-not-helped-economically|archive-date=14 October 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

===Call on ban of Malay bibles===
On 17 October 2013, Perkasa president Datuk [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] urged Prime Minister of Malaysia Datuk Seri [[Najib Razak|Najib Tun Razak]] to ban the Malay language bible in Malaysia, commenting that the government had been too compromising and give in to the demands of the Christians in Malaysia as they are not satisfied enough because the Christians insisted on using the word "Allah" in the bible, which he claims that "Allah" can only be used by Muslims in Malaysia. He added that the Malays had been "trodden and spat on" by "ungrateful Christians" and demanded the government to ban the Malay language bible as a retaliation. He resorted to name-calling by describing [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]] Secretary General [[Lim Guan Eng]] as a "pig" for defending the Christians right to use the word "Allah" in the bible. He also mocked the Christians saying that the Christians insisted that they use the word "Allah" because they had no name for their own God.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/10/18/teach-christians-a-lesson-ban-malay-bibles/|title=Teach Christians a lesson, ban al-Kitab|access-date=18 October 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160817075126/http://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2013/10/18/teach-christians-a-lesson-ban-malay-bibles/|archive-date=17 August 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Criticism ==
On 9 July 2010, the group was labelled 'militant' by former US Ambassador to Malaysia [[John Malott]].<ref>{{cite news |title=What Anwar's Trial Means for Malaysia |url=https://online.wsj.com/article/NA_WSJ_PUB:SB10001424052748704913304575371001840553096.html |publisher=Wall Street Journal|first=John R. |last=Malott |date=19 July 2010}}</ref>

The group has been criticised by former parliamentary leader of the opposition [[Lim Kit Siang]] from [[Democratic Action Party]] (DAP) of [[Pakatan Rakyat]], for re-using the religion, race and nationalism card when it's been "proven obsolete and irrelevant in bringing progress and change to Malay social and economic development". It had the tacit support of the former Prime Minister of Malaysia Datuk Seri Mahathir Mohamed when the latter attended its inauguration ceremony.<ref>{{cite news |title=Rebirth of a Demon |url=http://blog.limkitsiang.com/2010/03/10/perkasa-rebirth-of-a-demon/ |publisher=Lim Kit Siang |date=10 March 2010}}</ref>

State [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia]] (Gerakan) official and lawyer Baljit Singh has condemned Perkasa as the Malaysian version of the white American supremacist movement [[Ku Klux Klan]], which openly advocates racism, religious extremism and fascism. He added that Perkasa incites racial hatred and social disharmony by threatening to wage a [[Persecution of Christians|holy war against Christians]] and other ethnic and religious minorities in Malaysia.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.malaysia-today.net/mtcolumns/newscommentaries/40502-is-dr-m-supporting-perkasas-holy-war |title=Is Dr M supporting Perkasa's holy war? |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110829095015/http://www.malaysia-today.net/mtcolumns/newscommentaries/40502-is-dr-m-supporting-perkasas-holy-war |archive-date=29 August 2011 |df=dmy-all }}</ref>

Perkasa had also received backlash from two ethnic-Chinese [[Barisan Nasional]] leaders, namely [[Penang]] [[Parti Gerakan Rakyat Malaysia|Gerakan]] chief Datuk Teng Hock Nan and [[Malaysian Chinese Association|MCA]] deputy secretary Loh Chew June, whom they labelled the right-wing Malay group as "racists". Perkasa Youth Chief Arman Azha Abu Hanifah then demanded apology from the duo. Loh responded that he did not need apology as Perkasa's actions were to segregate everyone and  hampering the concept of '[[1Malaysia]]' mooted by [[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Malaysian Prime Minister]] [[Najib Razak]]. Loh also lambasted its president [[Ibrahim Ali (Malaysia)|Ibrahim Ali]] for flashing the 'keris' (Malay dagger) during the group's congress which may stoke racial tension in Malaysia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/litee/print/malaysia/No-sorry-for-Perkasa-BN-Chinese-leaders-say/|title=No sorry for Perkasa, BN Chinese leaders say|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130216221233/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/litee/print/malaysia/No-sorry-for-Perkasa-BN-Chinese-leaders-say/|archive-date=16 February 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref>
