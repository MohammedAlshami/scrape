# Frog (Malaysian politics)

*Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frog (Malaysian politics)*

---

{{Short description|Term for switching parties in Malaysia}}
In [[Malaysian politics]], a '''frog''' ([[Malaysian language|Malay]]: {{Lang|ms|katak politik}}'','' {{Lang|ms-arab|کاتق ڤوليتيک}}; [[Dusun language|Kadazandusun]]: {{Lang|dtp|buhangkut politik}}) refers to an act where a politician [[Party switching|crosses the bench from one party to another]] ''(changing support)''.<ref>{{Cite web |last=partyforumseasia |date=2020-09-17 |title=Malaysia's "Katak" Parliament |url=https://partyforumseasia.org/2020/09/17/malaysias-katak-parliament/ |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=Political Party Forum Southeast Asia |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Party hopping in Malaysia - Google Search |url=https://www.google.com/search?q=Party+hopping+in+Malaysia&tbm=nws |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=www.google.com}}</ref> This term was first coined in during the [[1994 Sabah state election]]s after [[United Sabah Party]] losing its majority even the party won the state elections. Despite its usage nationwide, it is more familiar within the state of [[Sabah]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2020-08-05 |title=Political Turmoil in Sabah: Attack of the Kataks |url=https://www.iseas.edu.sg/media/commentaries/political-turmoil-in-sabah-attack-of-the-kataks/ |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=ISEAS-Yusof Ishak Institute |language=en-US}}</ref> Since May 25, 2023, nine (9) states of [[Malaysia]] has approved the ''Parties hopping prevention law'' also known as the "''Anti-Switching Parties Law''" or "''Anti-Hopping Parties Law''" for both Parliament and State legislative assembly including [[Sabah]] and [[Sarawak]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-25 |title=Sabah's history of party hopping has ended, says Hajiji|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/05/25/sabah039s-history-of-party-hopping-has-ended-says-hajiji|access-date=2023-05-25 |website=The Star News|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Origins ==
Even prior to the 1994 Sabah state elections, a series of defections made by several local politicians has brought Sabahan politics into instability. For instance, the [[1985 Sabah state election]]s produced a [[Parti Bersatu Sabah|PBS]]-led [[Government of Sabah|government]] with [[Joseph Pairin Kitingan]] being the new [[Chief Minister of Sabah|Chief Minister]]. However, some of the new government lawmakers defected to either [[United Sabah National Organisation|USNO]] or [[Sabah People's United Front|BERJAYA]], resulting in the government losing its majority.

Pairin called a [[1986 Sabah state election|new election]] amidst a [[1986 Sabah riots|conflict]] across the state. His party later won the polls with a landslide victory, wiping out BERJAYA from the state political landscape. The government continued to rule for another eight years.

During the [[1994 Sabah state election|1994 elections]], PBS was able to retained its position as the government with a narrow majority, going on to form a new government with a majority of just two seats. In just only a few months, defections brought PBS left with only five MLAs, resulting an [[UMNO Sabah|UMNO]]-led [[Barisan Nasional]] state government being formed. [[Sakaran Dandai]] was sworn in as the next CM until his appointment as the [[Yang di-Pertua Negeri of Sabah]] later that year.

The term ''katak'' derives from how the frog moves, which is hopping. It is widely becoming popular as the politicians jump from one party to another.

== Examples of the usage ==

=== 2018 Sabah state election ===
The [[2018 Sabah state election|2018 state elections]] produced almost a similar outcome as the 1994 elections except it was ended with a [[Hung parliament|hung assembly]]. Neither BN nor newly formed [[Heritage Party (Malaysia)|Parti Warisan Sabah]] and [[Pakatan Harapan]] was able to win the 31 seats of majority as both won 29 seats, leaving the [[Homeland Solidarity Party]] as the [[kingmaker]] with two seats. At last, STAR decided to teamed up with BN, allowing [[Musa Aman]] to be reelected as the Chief Minister for a fifth consecutive term, being the only Chief Minister of Sabah to do so. Despite that, the government immediately fell apart when [[United Progressive Kinabalu Organisation|UPKO]] decided to gave its support to WARISAN-PH, bringing the support to [[Shafie Apdal]] to 35 compared with Musa which was left behind with 25 seats. Only two days later, Shafie became the new CM with support from PH and UPKO.

A series of serious defections occurred after that. Not only several component parties of BN left the coalition, most of Sabah UMNO politicians decided to cross the floor. Musa Aman was the sole UMNO MLA throughout the term, while the rest either joining the newly formed [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party of Sabah|BERSATU]], WARISAN or remained independent.

=== 2018 Malaysian general election ===
After the election defeat, several of [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO]] leaders decided to jump to [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party|BERSATU]] with MP for [[Jeli (federal constituency)|Jeli]], [[Mustapa Mohamed]] became the first MP to do so. From 54 UMNO MPs elected, the party only had 39 MPs when the Parliament dissolved.

=== 2020 Malaysian political crisis ===
During the [[2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis]], several MPs from PKR and Bersatu from the then-ruling Pakatan Harapan coalition switch allegiance and sided with BN and PAS to topple the PH coalition. MPs [[Azmin Ali]], [[Rina Harun]], and [[Zuraida Kamaruddin]], who were prominent in this crisis, later lost their elections during the [[2022 Malaysian general election]].<ref>{{Cite web |last=Parkaran |first=K. |date=20 November 2022 |title=Netizens hail fall of 'political frogs' in GE15 |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2022/11/20/netizens-hail-fall-of-political-frogs-in-ge15/ |access-date=22 July 2023 |website=Free Malaysia Today}}</ref>

=== 2020 Sabah state election ===
The term 'katak' was once again coined in after Musa Aman claimed that BN has a majority to form a government, resulting into some speculations that several WARISAN-PH MLA may defect to the opposition. To prove the legitimacy of the government, Shafie asked the Yang di-Pertua Negeri to dissolve the [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly]], in which his request was accepted. However, WARISAN lost the [[2020 Sabah state election|election]] to another newly formed [[Gabungan Rakyat Sabah]] coalition with a majority of three seats (32 (WARISAN)-38 (GRS)) (later six as all the independent MLAs joined GRS).<ref>{{Cite web |last=Team |first=Malaysiakini |date=2020-09-26 |title=LIVE: Battle for Sabah - GRS 38, Warisan Plus 32, Independent 3 |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/541008 |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=Malaysiakini}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=Commentary: Sabah's surprise results – and how Warisan lost big in state elections |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/commentary/sabah-election-results-how-warisan-lost-big-grs-won-huge-605926 |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=CNA |language=en}}</ref> [[Hajiji Noor]], leader of the GRS was sworn in as the Chief Minister.

Across the three years prior to a state political crisis, 10 MLAs defected. Most of them joining the government coalition, while only two remained in opposition.

=== 2023 Sabah political crisis ===
Following the [[2022 Malaysian general election|2022 general elections]], the Sabah BERSATU within GRS decided to gave its support to Anwar Ibrahim as the Prime Minister. However, BERSATU at the federal level rejected any 'grand coalition' proposal and became the opposition. This caused a dispute and eventually led to a [[Malaysian United Indigenous Party of Sabah|BERSATU Sabah]] breakaway. All the BERSATU Sabah MPs except [[Ronald Kiandee]], MP for [[Beluran (federal constituency)|Beluran]] (who contested under [[Perikatan Nasional]] ticket instead of GRS) left the party.

All the state legislators joined the rebranded local party, The [[Parti Gagasan Rakyat Sabah]] (GAGASAN), while the members of the [[Dewan Rakyat]] remained direct members of GRS due to a law that forbade any lawmakers to defect.

Owing to Hajiji's decision to defect, then-Deputy Chief Minister I of Sabah, [[Bung Moktar Radin]] pulled his support for Hajiji, along with all UMNO MLAs except of five UMNO MLAs and one KDM Limbahau MLAs ''(The movement also known as '''[[2023 Sabah political crisis|Langkah Kinabalu]]''' or '''[[2023 Sabah political crisis|Kinabalu Move]]''')''. This situation resulted a crisis which led to expulsion of Bung from his position. Later on, [[Shahelmey Yahya|Shahelmy Yahya]] (one of five UMNO MLAs who supported Hajiji) was appointed as Deputy Chief Minister III. Hajiji also reshuffled [[First Hajiji cabinet|his cabinet]], in which Pakatan Harapan rejoined the government.<ref>{{Cite web |last=Chan |first=Julia |date=2023-01-11 |title=Pakatan duo in, Sabah Umno top three out in new state Cabinet line-up |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/01/11/pakatan-duo-in-sabah-umno-top-three-out-in-new-state-cabinet-line-up/49580 |access-date=2023-04-03 |website=Malay Mail |language=en}}</ref> With Pakatan Harapan and partial support from UMNO lawmakers, Hajiji resisted the crisis.

As a result, eleven MLA crossed the floor, in which the composition is as follows:

* One [[Parti Bangsa Malaysia|PBM]] MLA joined Barisan Nasional (UMNO)
* Five WARISAN MLA joined GAGASAN
* Five BN (UMNO) MLA joined GAGASAN,

giving Hajiji a supermajority control over the assembly. After that, UMNO and [[Social Democratic Harmony Party|KDM]] decided to support the government once again except [[Bung Moktar Radin]]-sect.

On 25 May 2023, the [[Sabah]] law of anti-hopping parties finally approved in the state legislative assembly and successfully gazzeted on 8 June 2023, the end of party hopping history in Sabah.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-25 |title=Sabah's history of party hopping has ended, says Hajiji|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/05/25/sabah039s-history-of-party-hopping-has-ended-says-hajiji|access-date=2023-05-25 |website=The Star News|language=en-US}}</ref>

=== Rumors about political instability in Perak ===
On May 6, 2023, [[Perak]] [[First minister|MB]], Datuk Seri [[Saarani Mohamad]], insisted that the state government will remain intact until the end of the term, thus advising State Assembly Members (ADUN) not to be deceived by offers and jump (hopping) parties to the detriment of their respective political futures. He commented on rumors about four state assemblymen resigning, thus causing the Perak State Unity Government between [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) and [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) to lose its majority in the state assembly and be forced to re-hold the state election (PRN). Commenting further, he who is also the Chairman of BN [[Perak]] rejected the possibility that the leadership of the Perak state government will change and the desire to form a new government by the opposition in Perak cannot be achieved if the BN-PH coalition still wins at least one of the vacant state assembly seats. [[Saarani Mohamad]], who is also a Kota Tampan State Assembly Member, admitted that he knew about the rumors of an attempt to overthrow the Perak government three weeks ago, but denied that it would happen.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-06 |title=Kerajaan Perak utuh, ADUN jangan terpedaya|url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2023/05/1097743/kerajaan-perak-utuh-adun-jangan-terpedaya|access-date=2023-09-13 |website=BHOnline|language=en-MS}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-05-06 |title=Saarani Tepis Khabar Angin ADUN Lompat Parti|url=https://harapanmadani.com/saarani-tepis-khabar-angin-adun-lompat-parti/|access-date=2023-09-13 |website=Harapan Madani|language=en-MS}}</ref>
