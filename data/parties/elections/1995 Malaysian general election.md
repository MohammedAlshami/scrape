General elections were held in [[Malaysia]] on 24 and 25 April 1995. Voting took place in all 192 parliamentary constituencies of Malaysia, each electing one [[Member of parliament#Malaysia|Member of Parliament]] to the [[Dewan Rakyat]], the dominant house of Parliament. [[1995 Malaysian state elections|State elections]] also took place in 394 state constituencies in 11 out of 13 states of Malaysia (except [[Sabah]] and [[Sarawak]]) on the same day.

The result was a victory for the [[UMNO]]-led [[Barisan Nasional]], which won 162 of the 192 seats. Voter turnout was 68%.<ref>[[Dieter Nohlen]], Florian Grotz & Christof Hartmann (2001) ''Elections in Asia: A data handbook, Volume II'', p157 {{ISBN|0-19-924959-8}}</ref>

==Aftermath==
The 1995 general elections were the second and last elections involving [[Parti Melayu Semangat 46|Semangat 46]]. Having lost around a third of its vote share and facing desertions from party members, its leader [[Tengku Razaleigh Hamzah]] disbanded the party in 1996 and rejoined UMNO along with some other former Semangat 46 members.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://lib.perdana.org.my/PLF/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/1996-1998/1996pz/parti%20melayu%20semangat%2046%20t.pdf |title=S46-Dissolve: Parti Melayu Semangat '46 To Be Dissolved On Oct 6|website=[[Bernama]]|date=18 August 1996|via=[[Perdana Leadership Foundation#Library|Perdana Leadership Foundation Library]]}}</ref>
