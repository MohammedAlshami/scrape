General elections were held in [[Malaysia]] on 2 and 3 August 1986.<ref>[[Dieter Nohlen]], Florian Grotz & Christof Hartmann (2001) ''Elections in Asia: A data handbook, Volume II'', p152 {{ISBN|0-19-924959-8}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |last=Ramanathan |first=Sankaran |title=Malaysia's 1986 General Election: The Urban-Rural Dichotomy |last2=Adnan |first2=Mohd H. |date=1988 |publisher=ISEAS Publishing |isbn=978-981-4376-82-2 |location=Singapore}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |last=Rachagan |first=S. Sothi |date=1987 |title=The 1986 Parliamentary Elections in Peninsular Malaysia |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/27908577 |journal=Southeast Asian Affairs |pages=217–235 |issn=0377-5437}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal |last=Hanafiah |first=A. Malek |date=1987-12-01 |title=The Malaysian general election of 1986 |url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/0261379487900400 |journal=Electoral Studies |volume=6 |issue=3 |pages=279–285 |doi=10.1016/0261-3794(87)90040-0 |issn=0261-3794|url-access=subscription }}</ref> Voting took place in all 177 parliamentary constituencies of Malaysia, each electing one Member of Parliament to the Dewan Rakyat, the dominant house of Parliament. [[1986 Malaysian state elections|State elections]] also took place in eleven of the thirteen states.
