{{Politics of Malaysia}}

State elections in 2023 were held in [[Selangor]], [[Kelantan]], [[Terengganu]], [[Negeri Sembilan]], [[Kedah]] and [[Penang]] on 12 August 2023, following the dissolution of their state assemblies between 22 June and 1 July 2023.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysia-to-hold-six-state-elections-on-aug-12-nomination-day-is-on-july-29 |date=2023-07-05 |access-date=2023-08-13 |title=Malaysia to hold six state elections on Aug 12, nomination day on July 29 |last=Zunaira Saieed |work=[[The Straits Times]] |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230809211444/https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysia-to-hold-six-state-elections-on-aug-12-nomination-day-is-on-july-29 |archive-date=2023-08-09 |url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/spotlight-on-selangor-as-vote-count-begins-for-malaysia-s-state-polls |date=2023-08-12 |access-date=2023-08-13 |title=Malaysia state polls: PH and PN retain three states each in status quo result |work=[[The Straits Times]] |first=Shannon |last=Teoh |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20230813005300/https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/spotlight-on-selangor-as-vote-count-begins-for-malaysia-s-state-polls |archive-date=2023-08-13 |url-status=live}}</ref>

The state elections were held concurrently with the [[2023 Kuala Terengganu by-election]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-18 |title=KUALA TERENGGANU BY-ELECTION TO BE HELD SIMULTANEOUSLY WITH SIX STATE POLLS ON AUG 12 |url=https://www.bernama.com/en/news.php?id=2207556 |access-date=2023-07-18 |website=BERNAMA |language=en}}</ref>

== Background ==
[[File:6 states general election results map by state constituency, 2022.svg|thumb|500px|center|The results of the 2022 general election, visualised with the borders of state constituencies. Gray areas represent constituencies with [[election petition]]s filed.]]
State elections in Malaysia were traditionally held alongside [[Elections in Malaysia|general elections]]. However, states can independently dissolve their own assemblies. Therefore, during [[2022 Malaysian general election|the previous general election in 2022]], only three states, [[Perlis]], [[Perak]] and [[Pahang]] dissolved their state assemblies and held their state elections concurrently, while another four states, [[Sabah]], [[Malacca]], [[Sarawak]] and [[Johor]] held their state elections earlier before the 2022 general election, due to [[2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis|the ongoing political crisis]] at that time, leaving only the remaining six states which have chosen not to dissolve their assemblies at the time of the general election.<ref>{{cite web |date=6 June 2022 |title=No early elections for PAS-held states, says party vice-president |url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2022/06/06/no-early-elections-for-pas-held-states-says-party-vice-president/ |access-date=6 June 2022 |work=[[Free Malaysia Today]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |title=GE15: Penang, Selangor and Negri not dissolving state assemblies this year, says Anwar |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2022/10/05/ge15-penang-selangor-and-negri-not-dissolving-state-assemblies-this-year-says-anwar |access-date=24 October 2022 |website=The Star |language=en}}</ref><ref name=":7">{{cite news |date=15 October 2022 |title=GE15: Penang, S'gor, Negri assemblies will not dissolve, says Pakatan presidential council |newspaper=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]] |department=Nation |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2022/10/15/ge15-penang-s039gor-negri-assemblies-will-not-dissolve-pakatan-presidential-council-decides |access-date=15 October 2022}}</ref><ref name=":8">{{cite news |last=Hassan |first=Hazlin |date=14 October 2022 |title=Malaysia's PAS says its three state assemblies won't be dissolved for elections |newspaper=[[The Straits Times]] |url=https://www.straitstimes.com/asia/se-asia/malaysias-pas-says-its-three-state-assemblies-wont-be-dissolved-for-elections |access-date=16 October 2022}}</ref>

== Participating parties ==
The elections are the first to be held under [[Anwar Ibrahim cabinet|the unity government]] led by [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) chairman and prime minister [[Anwar Ibrahim]]. All main component parties of PH, [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]], [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]] and [[AMANAH]], as well as the opposition coalition, [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN), are participating in the elections. [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN), the second largest coalition in the unity government, saw only [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO]] members taking part, with the other two main component parties, [[Malaysian Chinese Association|MCA]] and [[Malaysian Indian Congress|MIC]] sat out the elections.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-05 |title=MCA, MIC to skip state elections |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/670952 |access-date=2023-07-07 |website=Malaysiakini}}</ref> Among the parties in the unity government not participating in the state elections include the Sabah-based [[WARISAN]]<ref>{{Cite web |title=Warisan will not contest in upcoming state polls, says Shafie |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/05/11/warisan-will-not-contest-in-upcoming-state-polls-says-shafie |access-date=2023-07-07 |website=The Star |language=en}}</ref> and [[Parti Bangsa Malaysia|PBM]].<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 18, 2023 |title=PBM tidak bertanding enam pilihan raya negeri |url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2023/03/pbm-tidak-bertanding-enam-pilihan-raya-negeri/ |website=Utusan |language=ms}}</ref>

Similar decisions were taken by several smaller parties such as [[Homeland Fighter's Party|Pejuang]]<ref>{{Cite web |last=Amin |first=Manzaidi Mohd |date=2023-07-26 |title=Putra tidak akan bertanding PRN secara solo - Tok Him |url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2023/07/26/putra-tidak-akan-bertanding-prn-secara-solo-tok-him/ |access-date=2023-07-27 |website=MalaysiaGazette |language=en-US}}</ref> and [[Parti Bumiputera Perkasa Malaysia|Putra]],<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-07-22 |title=Pejuang to skip state polls, says Mukhriz |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2023/07/22/pejuang-to-skip-state-polls-says-mukhriz/81159 |access-date=2023-07-27 |website=Malay Mail |language=en}}</ref> which decided not to participate. Their leaders, together with some Perikatan Nasional leaders, previously agreed informally not to contest against each other.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-06-06 |title=Sekutu Proklamasi Melayu setuju bergerak bersama |url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/667683 |access-date=2023-07-27 |website=Malaysiakini}}</ref> 
   
Despite being part of the unity government, [[Malaysian United Democratic Alliance|MUDA]] announced its decision to contest against unity government coalitions in the elections, after its president [[Syed Saddiq Syed Abdul Rahman]] claimed that the party was sidelined by PH.<ref>{{Cite web |title=MUDA to contest state polls alone after 'left hanging' by Pakatan Harapan: Syed Saddiq |url=https://www.channelnewsasia.com/asia/malaysia-syed-saddiq-state-election-muda-pakatan-harapan-pact-3592011 |access-date=2023-07-07 |website=CNA |language=en}}</ref> Following this, MUDA and the [[Socialist Party of Malaysia]] (PSM) signed an electoral pact after the latter announced candidates to contest in the state elections too.<ref>{{Cite web |title=State polls: Muda, PSM ink electoral understanding to work together to bring new brand of politics |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2023/07/15/state-polls-muda-psm-ink-electoral-pact-to-work-together-to-bring-new-brand-of-politics |access-date=2023-07-18 |website=The Star |language=en}}</ref> Another party without any elected representatives in Parliament taking part in the state elections is [[Parti Rakyat Malaysia|PRM]] contesting 15 seats in Penang and Selangor.<ref>{{Cite web |date=2023-03-13 |title=PRM TO CONTEST 15 SEATS IN PENANG STATE ELECTION |url=https://www.bernama.com/en/politics/news.php?id=2172669 |access-date=2023-07-18 |website=BERNAMA |language=en}}</ref>

==Selangor==
[[File:DUN Kota Damansara during 2023 Selangor State Election 20230812 084214.jpg|thumb|Polling day of 2023 Selangor state election]]
{{Main|2023 Selangor state election}}
{{For|complete results|Results of the 2023 Malaysian state elections by constituency#Selangor}}

Pakatan Harapan won the Selangor state election after they reached 34 of 56 seats to form state government while Perikatan Nasional won 22 seats. On 21 August 2023, incumbent Amirudin Shari sworn in as Selangor Menteri Besar for the second term.

Earlier, Pakatan Harapan (previously Barisan Alternatif, Pakatan Rakyat) maintained their stronghold in Selangor after they took over control of the state government from Barisan Nasional in the 2008 election and continued to do so for three consecutive terms in 2008, 2013, and 2018. The unity government, now represented by Pakatan Harapan and Barisan Nasional, faced Perikatan Nasional which consists of Bersatu, PAS and Gerakan in the election.

==Kelantan==
{{Main|2023 Kelantan state election}}
{{For|complete results|Results of the 2023 Malaysian state elections by constituency#Kelantan}}

Perikatan Nasional (PN) won the Kelantan state election for the eighth time after they reached 43 of 45 seats to form state government. Meanwhile, BN and PH won one seat each. On 15 August 2023, [[Mohd Nassuruddin Daud]], [[Meranti (state constituency)|Meranti]] Assemblyman, appointed as a new Kelantan Menteri Besar, replaced [[Ahmad Yakob]].

Earlier, [[Malaysian Islamic Party|PAS]], which is now part of the [[Perikatan Nasional]] coalition, ruled Kelantan from Barisan Nasional since the 1990 election and held seven consecutive terms in 1990, 1995, 1999, 2004, 2008, 2013, 2018.

==Terengganu==
{{Main|2023 Terengganu state election}}
{{For|complete results|Results of the 2023 Malaysian state elections by constituency#Terengganu}}

Perikatan Nasional (PN) won the Terengganu state election for the second time after they won all 32 seats to form the state government. On 15 August 2023, incumbent Ahmad Samsuri Mokhtar retained as Terengganu Menteri Besar for a second term.
 
Earlier, PAS, which is under Perikatan Nasional, ruled the Terengganu state government from Barisan Nasional since the 2018 election.

==Negeri Sembilan==
{{Main|2023 Negeri Sembilan state election}}
{{For|complete results|Results of the 2023 Malaysian state elections by constituency#Negeri Sembilan}}

The unity government (PH + BN) won the Negeri Sembilan state election after they won 31 of 36 seats to form state government, while PN won 5 seats. On 14 August 2023, incumbent [[Aminuddin Harun]] retained his position as Negeri Sembilan Menteri Besar for the second term.

Earlier, Pakatan Harapan ruled the Negeri Sembilan state government since the 2018 election. Now, the unity government, which represents Pakatan Harapan and Barisan Nasional, will face Perikatan Nasional which consists of Bersatu and PAS in next state election.

==Kedah==
{{Main|2023 Kedah state election}}
{{For|complete results|Results of the 2023 Malaysian state elections by constituency#Kedah}}

Perikatan Nasional (PN) has won Kedah state election after won 33 of 36 seats to form state government while unity government (PH + BN) has won 3 seats. On 14 August 2023, incumbent Sanusi Mohd Nor sworn in as Kedah Menteri Besar for second term.

Earlier, PAS which is under Perikatan Nasional has ruled the Kedah state government after took over from Pakatan Harapan due to political crisis in 2020.

==Penang==
{{Main|2023 Penang state election}}
{{For|complete results|Results of the 2023 Malaysian state elections by constituency#Penang}}

Pakatan Harapan (PH) won the Penang state election for the fourth time with cooperation from BN after they reached 29 of 40 seats to form the state government, while PN won 11 seats. On 13 August, incumbent Chow Kon Yeow sworn in as Penang Chief Minister for a second term.

Earlier, Pakatan Harapan (previously Barisan Alternatif, Pakatan Rakyat) remained a stronghold in Penang after it took over state government from Barisan Nasional in the 2008 election, holding three consecutive terms in 2008, 2013, 2018. It would be challenged by former governing party of Penang, Gerakan, which now joins Perikatan Nasional together with Bersatu and PAS.
