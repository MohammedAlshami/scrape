General elections were held in [[Malaysia]] between Thursday, 22 April and Monday, 26 April 1982.<ref>[[Dieter Nohlen]], Florian Grotz & Christof Hartmann (2001) ''Elections in Asia: A data handbook, Volume II'', p152 {{ISBN|0-19-924959-8}}</ref> Voting took place in all 154 parliamentary constituencies of Malaysia, each electing one Member of Parliament to the Dewan Rakyat, the dominant house of Parliament. [[1982 Malaysian state elections|State elections]] also took place in 293 state constituencies (except [[Sabah State Legislative Assembly|Sabah]] and [[Sarawak State Legislative Assembly|Sarawak]]) on the same day. It was the first election for [[Mahathir Mohamad]] as Prime Minister since his appointment to this position in 1981.

The result was a victory for [[Barisan Nasional]], which won 132 of the 154 seats. Voter turnout was 74%.
