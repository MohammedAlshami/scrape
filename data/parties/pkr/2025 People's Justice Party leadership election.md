A '''leadership election''' is scheduled to be held by the [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|People's Justice Party]] (PKR) in Malaysia from 14 March to 23 May 2025 to elect new leadership at the central and branch levels.<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/693995/berita/politik/pemilihan-pkr-selepas-mei-2025---fuziah | title=Pemilihan PKR selepas Mei 2025 - Fuziah | website=Sinar Harian |date=30 October 2024 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/YBFuziahSalleh/posts/pfbid026AJZAyE7cGgTpJRoC9eYvq6dkUQvaC3RxUtBFMqpBoPDbH8vgzwWGsQSEDLxoMfjl|title=Pengumuman Tarikh-Tarikh Penting Pemilihan KEADILAN 2025|website=Facebook|date=26 January 2025|accessdate=3 February 2025|language=ms}}</ref>

This leadership election will be the first since party president [[Anwar Ibrahim]] became the [[Prime Minister of Malaysia]] and the second since [[Seventh Mahathir cabinet|PKR became a ruling party]], after the [[2018 People's Justice Party leadership election|2018 PKR leadership election]]. This leadership election is considered very important for the party's direction for the [[Next Malaysian general election|next general election that will expected in 2027]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/728341/berita/politik/saya-ditawar-jutaan-ringgit-supaya-tinggalkan-pkr---abdullah-sani|title=Saya ditawar jutaan ringgit supaya tinggalkan PKR - Abdullah Sani|website=Sinar Harian|date=14 May 2025|accessdate=14 May 2025}}</ref>

== Background and events ==
The voting of this leadership election is only opened to some delegates, instead of all party members. The number of delegates eligible to vote is expected to be more than 20,000, and each party division has 12 delegates. According to Vice President of PKR, [[Chang Lih Kang]], this was to save time and effort while maintaining [[democracy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.chinapress.com.my/20250202/%E5%85%AC%E6%AD%A3%E5%85%9A5%E6%9C%88%E5%85%9A%E9%80%89-%E6%8A%95%E7%A5%A8%E8%80%852000%E5%A2%9E%E8%87%B32%E4%B8%87/|title=公正党5月党选 投票者2000增至2万|website=China Press|date=2 February 2025|accessdate=3 February 2025|language=zh}}</ref>

Party chairman of the international bureau of PKR, [[Shamsul Iskandar Md Akin]] revealed the possibility that the top two party positions, the presidency and deputy presidency may not be involved in this leadership election, similar to the [[2023 United Malays National Organisation leadership election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2025/02/01/pkrs-top-2-posts-not-likely-to-be-contested-says-anwars-aide/|title=PKR's top 2 posts not likely to be contested, says Anwar's aide|website=Free Malaysia Today|date=1 February 2025|accessdate=3 February 2025}}</ref>

The current president of PKR, [[Anwar Ibrahim]] won unopposed after no other candidate submitted a form to contest the position on 9 May 2025. Anwar also managed to retain his position for the third consecutive times, after the [[2018 People's Justice Party leadership election|PKR leadership election held in 2018]] and [[2022 People's Justice Party leadership election|2022]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/pemilihan-pkr-pencalonan-tamat-anwar-tidak-dicabar-520120|title=Pemilihan PKR: Pencalonan tamat, Anwar tidak dicabar|date=10 May 2025|accessdate=12 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2025/05/10/zaleha-sahkan-anwar-kamil-menang-tanpa-bertanding-mgflash/|title=Zaleha Sahkan Anwar, Kamil Menang Tanpa Bertanding #MGFlash|date=10 May 2025|accessdate=12 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref>

This leadership election focuses on the position of Deputy President of PKR, where the daughter of the [[Prime Minister of Malaysia]] and President of PKR, [[Nurul Izzah Anwar]], will contest the position with the incumbent, [[Rafizi Ramli|Mohd Rafizi Ramli]], who is also the [[Minister of Economy (Malaysia)|Minister of Economy]]. [[Minister of Home Affairs (Malaysia)|Minister of Home Affairs]] and Secretary-General of PKR, [[Saifuddin Nasution Ismail]] make the decision to withdraw from contesting the position of Deputy President of PKR in this leadership election. This decision was announced by the Special Officer to the Minister of Home Affairs, Omar Mokhtar A Manap on 9 May 2025 in a brief statement to the media. He added that the decision was taken as a result of discussions with many parties, including the top leadership of PKR.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/2025/05/presiden-pkr-tidak-dicabar-saifuddin-tidak-hantar-pencalonan/?utm_source=lumi&utm_medium=lumi_web&utm_campaign=article|title=Presiden PKR tidak dicabar, Saifuddin tidak hantar pencalonan|last=Abu Bakar|first=Juani Munir|date=9 May 2025|website=Utusan Malaysia|accessdate=12 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref>

== Campaign ==
=== HIRUK vs DAMAI ===
The leadership election saw two major factions vying for key positions within the party.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/729067/berita/cetusan/mencari-hiruk-atau-damai|title=Mencari hiruk atau damai?|date=18 May 2025|website=Sinar Harian|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.utusan.com.my/nasional/politik/2025/05/hiruk-rafizi-vs-damai-nurul-izzah/|title=‘Hiruk’ Rafizi vs ‘Damai’ Nurul Izzah|date=18 May 2025|website=Utusan Malaysia|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/opinion/columnists/its-just-politics/2025/05/18/which-will-win-hiruk-or-damai|title=Which will win, Hiruk or Damai?|date=18 May 2025|website=The Star|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=en}}</ref>

The HIRUK ({{literal translation|agitate}}) faction  (''Hidupkan Idealisme Reformasi dalam Ujian Kuasa'') is led by [[Rafizi Ramli]], [[Minister of Economy (Malaysia)|Minister of Economy]] and Member of Parliament for [[Pandan (federal constituency)|Pandan]]. Among the key party figures aligned with the faction are [[Aminuddin Harun]], [[Chang Lih Kang]], [[Nik Nazmi Nik Ahmad]] and [[Rodziah Ismail]]. It advocates for a more confrontational approach that embodied PKR's early reformist zeal, opposing a big tent and cooperation with parties like [[United Malays National Organisation|UMNO]]. The campaign was themed around eliminating the alleged "cartel" surrounding PKR President [[Anwar Ibrahim]] along with alleged [[Political corruption|money politics]] and [[nepotism]] within the party. It has a base of support amongst party grassroots, urban liberals, and progressives.

The DAMAI ({{literal translation|peace}}) faction (''Dari Akar, Membina Aspirasi Inklusif'') is led by [[Nurul Izzah Anwar]], co-chairperson of the Secretariat of the Special Advisory Body to the [[Minister of Finance (Malaysia)|Minister of Finance]]. It is joined by key party figures such as [[Amirudin Shari]], [[Ramanan Ramakrishnan]], [[Adam Adli|Adam Adli Abd Halim]], [[Fadhlina Sidek]] and [[Muhammad Kamil Abdul Munim|Muhammad Kamil Munim]]. It represents the party establishment that supports coalition-building and forging closer ties with UMNO. The campaign manifesto pledged to strengthen and focus on the future of the party along with helping the [[sandwich generation]] escape the cocoon of poverty. Its support is derived from local leaders, with over 200 of 222 divisions pledging support, along with party veterans and conservatives who wish to emulate PKR into a 'reformed' UMNO.

== Controversies and issues ==
=== Alleged weaknesses in the voting system ===
The leadership election was marred with allegations of weaknesses in the implementation of the online voting system (e-voting). [[Rafizi Ramli]],  the party's deputy president, claimed that the results at the divisional level  were like "[[Headless men|a body without a head]]",<ref>{{cite web|url=https://malaysiagazette.com/2025/04/22/pemilihan-pkr-keputusan-badan-tanpa-kepala-risaukan-banyak-pihak/#google_vignette|title=Pemilihan PKR: Keputusan `badan tanpa kepala’ risaukan banyak pihak|date=11 May 2025|website=Astro Awani|accessdate=22 April 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.astroawani.com/videos/video-terkini-x7sio1/fenomena-kepala-tanpa-badan-cetus-perbincangan-x9ja7i2|title=Fenomena 'kepala tanpa badan' cetus perbincangan|date=11 May 2025|website=Astro Awani|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref> owing to many division leaders opposed to Rafizi winning despite the rest of their 'team' losing. There have been protests by losing candidates to re-examine the results at the divisional level, such as the defeat of [[Nik Nazmi Nik Ahmad]] to [[Afdlin Shauki]] in [[Setiawangsa (federal constituency)|Setiawangsa]] along with [[Akmal Nasir]]'s defeat to Taufiq Ismail in [[Johor Bahru (federal constituency)|Johor Bahru]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/725214/berita/politik/pemilihan-pkr-nik-nazmi-hantar-surat-bantahan-kepada-jpp|title=Pemilihan PKR: Nik Nazmi hantar surat bantahan kepada JPP|date=26 April 2025|website=Sinar Harian|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref> However, the PKR Central Leadership Council (MPP) confirmed and finalized the election results for all 220 branches nationwide on 5 May 2025 after an external audit found no irregularities.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-politik/keputusan-pemilihan-cabang-pkr-2025-disahkan-dimuktamadkan-mpp-fuziah-519407|title=Keputusan pemilihan cabang PKR 2025 disahkan, dimuktamadkan MPP - Fuziah|date=5 May 2025|website=Astro Awani|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref> 

=== Allegations of money politics ===
A video went viral on [[Twitter]] showing [[Ramanan Ramakrishnan]], a vice-presidential candidate aligned with DAMAI, [[Bribery|handing out money]] to several people at [[Sungai Buloh (federal constituency)|Sungai Buloh]]'s ''Pusat Khidmat Rakyat''. However, Ramanan denied the accusation that they were bribes to supporters, instead claiming it was an old clip of hand-outs to his staff personnel after the [[2023 Selangor state election]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/743496|title='Itu pekerja saya sendiri, apa yang salah?'|date=17 May 2025|website=Malaysia Kini|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.sinarharian.com.my/article/728971/berita/politik/ramann-nafi-politik-wang-quotitu-sumbangan-kepada-pekerja-sayaquot#google_vignette|title=Ramanan nafi politik wang: “Itu sumbangan kepada pekerja saya”|date=17 May 2025|website=Sinar Harian|accessdate=19 May 2025|language=ms-MY}}</ref>
