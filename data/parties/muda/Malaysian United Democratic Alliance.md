The '''Malaysian United Democratic Alliance''' ([[Malay language|Malay]]: ''Ikatan Demokratik Malaysia''), abbreviated as '''MUDA''' (meaning "young" in [[Malay language|Malay]]), is a multi-racial, youth-centric [[political party]] in [[Malaysia]]. Founded in September 2020 by [[Syed Saddiq Syed Abdul Rahman]], MUDA advocates for [[social democracy]], [[multiracialism]], and [[youth politics]]. The party is known for its focus on inclusivity, innovation, and reform.

== History ==
=== Formation ===
The Malaysian United Democratic Alliance was formed by [[Syed Saddiq Syed Abdul Rahman]] in September 2020. Previously, Syed Saddiq served as [[Minister of Youth and Sports (Malaysia)|Minister of Youth and Sports]] in the [[Pakatan Harapan]] (PH) administration. === Rejected registration and court case ===
MUDA's registration was rejected by the RoS on 6 January 2021 via a RoS email, in which no reason was given for the rejection.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.freemalaysiatoday.com/category/nation/2021/01/07/syed-saddiqs-muda-also-turned-down-by-ros/|title=Syed Saddiq's Muda also turned down by RoS|date=2021-01-07|access-date=2021-01-07|website=[[Free Malaysia Today]]|language=en-GB}}</ref> On 12 January, MUDA through its 13 pro-tem committee, including Syed Saddiq, sought a court order to reverse the decision of [[Minister of Home Affairs (Malaysia)|Minister of Home Affairs]] and for the RoS to register it.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2021/01/24/syed-saddiqs-muda-sues-home-minister-asks-court-to-order-ros-to-approve-par/1943548|title=Syed Saddiq's Muda sues home minister, asks court to order RoS to approve party's registration|author=Ida Lim|date=24 January 2021 |website=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref> Judge Mariana Yahya had set the date 4 February 2021 after hearing arguments from MUDA's lawyers' team the [[High court (Malaysia)|High Court of Malaysia]] to decide whether to allow MUDA a judicial review against the government decision. The lawyers representing MUDA are former attorney-general [[Tommy Thomas (barrister)|Tommy Thomas]], [[Ambiga Sreenevasan]] and Lim Wei Jiet.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-25|title=Court to decide next Thursday if Muda can challenge ROS' rejection of party|url=http://www.theedgemarkets.com/article/court-decide-next-thursday-if-muda-can-challenge-ros-rejection-party|access-date=2021-01-30|website=[[The Edge (Malaysia)|The Edge Markets]]}}</ref>

Syed Saddiq claims the Home Affairs Minister [[Hamzah Zainudin]] had told him that his party would be registered and approved if he pledged to support the ruling [[Perikatan Nasional]] (PN) coalition. He also claims that Hamzah had asked him to refrain from voting on government bills he opposed as part of the deal. MUDA also alleged that the rejection was PN government's agenda to prevent it from participating in the next general election.<ref name=report>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2021/03/25/still-not-approved-as-a-party-muda-questions-ros-six-month-background-check/1960831|title=Still not approved as a party, Muda questions RoS' six-month background check |author=Sharin Aizat Noorsharizam|date=25 March 2021 |website=[[Malay Mail]]|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/585984|title=Wan Fayhsal: I asked Syed Saddiq to put aside political rivalry|author=|date=2 August 2021 |website=[[Malaysiakini]]|access-date=23 September 2021}}</ref>

=== 2022 Johor state election ===
On 29 January 2022, MUDA confirmed its intention to participate in the Johor state election.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/53138/syed-saddiq-confirms-muda-to-contest-in-johor-polls | title=Syed Saddiq confirms Muda to contest in Johor polls | newspaper=The Vibes | date=29 January 2022 }}</ref>

On 9 February 2022, [[Democratic Action Party|DAP]], [[National Trust Party (Malaysia)|AMANAH]] and MUDA signed an agreement unveiling the outcomes of their cooperation on seat negotiations by declaring that they will not contest against each other, with MUDA getting 6 seats previously allocated to DAP and AMANAH ranging from [[Tenang (state constituency)|Tenang]], [[Bukit Kepong (state constituency)|Bukit Kepong]], [[Parit Raja (state constituency)|Parit Raja]], [[Machap (state constituency)|Machap]], [[Puteri Wangsa (state constituency)|Puteri Wangsa]] and [[Bukit Permai (state constituency)|Bukit Permai]]. MUDA also clarified that the seat negotiations with [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]] are still ongoing to "achieve unity in facing this election"<ref>{{Cite web|date=2022-02-09|title=DAP dan Amanah setuju Muda bertanding 6 kerusi di Johor|url=https://www.malaysiakini.com/news/610061|access-date=2022-02-09|website=Malaysiakini}}</ref> The three parties also said they would combine their election machinery to support all of their candidates as they were about to move on as election strategic partners although MUDA is not part of PH and this is in line with the "big camp" principle to unite all Opposition parties to take on the ruling BN coalition. They also promised to form the Johor state government together if they win in the election. Johor PKR Chairman [[:ms:Syed Ibrahim Syed Noh|Syed Ibrahim Syed Noh]] said PKR had offered 3 seats to MUDA and was awaiting a response to the offer.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Razak|first=Radzi|title=Johor polls: Muda says seat talks not done yet, chides state PKR for jumping the gun |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2022/02/09/johor-polls-muda-says-seat-talks-not-done-yet-chides-state-pkr-for-jumping/2040589|access-date=2022-02-13|website=Malay Mail|date=9 February 2022 |language=en}}</ref>

On 13 March 2022, MUDA won one seat in the first election it contested. MUDA Secretary General [[Amira Aisya Abdul Aziz|Amira Aisya]] won the [[Puteri Wangsa (state constituency)|Puteri Wangsa]] seat. President of MUDA Syed Saddiq Syed Abdul Rahman described the victory in the seat out of the seven seats contested as the party's first step to continue to gain a foothold in the country's political landscape.<ref>{{Cite web |date=March 13, 2022 |title=MUDA menangi satu kerusi dalam pilihan raya pertamanya |url=https://www.bernama.com/bm/news.php?id=2061344 |website=[[Bernama]]}}</ref>

As of 2024, MUDA holds one seat in the [[Dewan Rakyat]] and one seat in the [[Johor State Legislative Assembly]]. The party has faced challenges in recent elections, failing to win any seats in the [[2023 Malaysian state elections]]. Following the conviction of its founder, [[Syed Saddiq Syed Abdul Rahman]], on corruption charges in November 2023, [[Amira Aisya Abdul Aziz]] was appointed as acting president. MUDA continues to position itself as a progressive, youth-oriented party, though its influence remains limited.

== Ideology ==
MUDA's ideology is centered on [[social democracy]], [[youth politics]], [[multiracialism]], and [[secularism]]. The party's charter outlines five key pillars:

=== Five pillars ===
* 1. Economic Progress: Promoting sustainable economic growth and job creation.
* 2. Education & Health: Ensuring access to quality education and healthcare.
* 3. Excellence in Governance: Advocating for transparency, accountability, and anti-corruption measures.
* 4. Environmental Sustainability: Addressing climate change and promoting green policies.
* 5. Youth Representation: Empowering young people in leadership and decision-making roles.

=== Key principles ===
* Diversity and Inclusion: MUDA emphasizes representation for women, indigenous peoples, people with disabilities, and other marginalized groups.
* Term Limits: Leaders can hold office for a maximum of three terms to prevent monopolization of power.
* Transparency in Political Financing: The party advocates for donation limits, digital donations, and annual audits.
* Anti-Warlordism: MUDA seeks to eliminate corruption and power concentration within political structures.

== Criticism and controversies ==
MUDA has faced criticism for its role in vote-splitting during the [[2023 Malaysian state elections]], particularly in the [[Sungai Kandis (state constituency)|Sungai Kandis]] seat, where its candidate allegedly contributed to the defeat of a [[Pakatan Harapan]] candidate. Additionally, the party's credibility has been questioned following the conviction of its founder, [[Syed Saddiq Syed Abdul Rahman]], on corruption charges in November 2023.
