The '''Progressive Democratic Party''' ({{langx|ms|Parti Demokratik Progresif}}, '''PDP'''), formerly known as '''Sarawak Progressive Democratic Party''' ('''SPDP'''), is a [[Sarawak]]-based [[political party]] in [[Malaysia]]. Founded in 2002, the party was formed in the aftermath of an initial deregistration of the [[Sarawak National Party]] (SNAP). The party is a founding and principal component of the [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS) which [[Government of Sarawak|governs]] the state of Sarawak.

== History ==
The party was founded in the wake of the deregistration of the [[Sarawak National Party]] (SNAP) in November 2002. Initially named as the Sarawak Progressive Democratic Party (SPDP), it was governed by a faction aligned to the then [[Chief Minister of Sarawak]], [[Abdul Taib Mahmud]], led by William Mawan Ikom.<ref>{{cite news |title=SPDP holds first meeting headed by Mawan |url=http://lib.perdana.org.my/PLF/Digital_Content/Prominent_Leaders/Mahathir/News_1968-2004/2001-2005/2002pz/spdp%20awan.PDF |access-date=18 April 2025 |work=New Straits Times |date=14 November 2002}}</ref> A leadership crisis between Mawan Ikom and [[Tiong King Sing]] factions led William Mawan Ikom and entire SPDP state assemblymen to leave SPDP in 2013 for newly founded party [[Sarawak People's Energy Party|TERAS]]. Tiong King Sing who was then member of Parliament then took control of SPDP.<ref name="autogenerated2">{{cite web |author=Desmond Davidson |date=15 May 2014 |title=Senior Sarawak leaders quit Barisan affiliates to join new party |url=http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/senior-sarawak-leaders-quit-barisan-affiliates-to-join-new-party |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140517002752/http://www.themalaysianinsider.com/malaysia/article/senior-sarawak-leaders-quit-barisan-affiliates-to-join-new-party |archive-date=17 May 2014 |access-date=15 May 2014 |publisher=The Malaysian Insider}}</ref>

In 2017, the party rebranded as the Progressive Democratic Party, having planned to expand to certain [[West Malaysia]]n states, including [[Selangor]], [[Negeri Sembilan]] and [[Johor]] (where it has established 6 divisions in 2017),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.theborneopost.com/2017/11/14/pdp-plans-to-expand-to-selangor-negri-sembilan-klang-valley/|title=PDP plans to expand to Selangor, Negri Sembilan, Klang Valley|publisher=The Borneo Post|date=14 November 2017|access-date=15 November 2017}}</ref> although the party remains based and mostly active in Sarawak. It is one of the former component parties of the [[Barisan Nasional]] coalition.<ref name="Barisan Nasional coalition" /> Following the fall of BN in the [[2018 Malaysian general election|2018 general election]], the aftermath of a meeting between Sarawak-based BN component parties on 12 June 2018 resulted in all parties, including the PDP, to leave the coalition. The parties, led by [[Parti Pesaka Bumiputera Bersatu]] (PBB), decided to form a Sarawak-based coalition named [[Gabungan Parti Sarawak]] (GPS) approximately one month after the election.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2018/06/12/sarawak-bn-parties-pull-out-of-coalition/|title=Sarawak BN parties pull out of coalition to form independent state-based pact|author1=Sharon Ling|author2=Geryl Ogilvy|work=[[The Star (Malaysia)|The Star]]|date=12 June 2018|access-date=12 June 2018}}</ref>

In April 2024, [[Parti Sarawak Bersatu]] (PSB) was merged into PDP and its members were accepted into PDP, including 3 state assemblymen.<ref>{{cite web |author1=The Borneo Post |title=Former PSB leaders, members officially join PDP |url=https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2024/04/07/former-psb-leaders-members-officially-join-pdp/127724 |website=www.malaymail.com |publisher=Malay Mail |access-date=17 July 2024 |date=6 April 2024}}</ref>

=== Electoral history ===
At the [[Sarawak state election, 2011|2011 Sarawak state election]], PDP won six out of the eight seats it contested.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v5/newspolitic.php?id=579787|title=Exceptionally Tough Fight For SPDP, Says Mawan|publisher=Bernama|access-date=21 April 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120904235847/http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v6/newspolitic.php?id=579787|archive-date=4 September 2012|url-status=dead}} {{subscription required}}</ref> In the [[2013 Malaysian general election|2013 General Election]], all 4 of the party's candidates won their respective seats, including William Mawan himself, where he won in [[Saratok (federal constituency)|Saratok]] against [[Ali Biju]] of [[People's Justice Party (Malaysia)|PKR]].

At the [[Sarawak state election, 2021|2021 Sarawak state election]], PDP won five out of the six seats it contested.
