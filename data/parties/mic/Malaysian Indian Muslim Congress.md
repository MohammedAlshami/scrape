The '''Malaysian Indian Muslim Congress''' ({{langx|ms|Kongres India Muslim Malaysia}}; {{langx|ta|மலேசிய இந்திய முஸ்லிம் காங்கிரஸ்}}; [[abbreviation|abbrev]]: '''KIMMA''') is a [[political party]] in Malaysia which seeks to represent the interests of [[Muslims|Muslim]] people [[Malaysian Indians|of Indian descent]] in the country.

===Formation===
KIMMA was formed on 24 October 1976 to represent the interests of the minority [[Indian Muslim]] community in [[Malaysia]], having split off from the [[Malaysian Indian Congress]] (MIC).<ref>{{Cite web |title=Kimma seeks Malay status for Indian Muslims |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2005/09/19/kimma-seeks-malay-status-for-indian-muslims |access-date=2023-12-09 |website=The Star |language=en}}</ref> The majority of KIMMA  members are Indian Muslim. KIMMA was deregistered in 1978 and 1998 before it was successfully re-registered.<ref name= deregistered>{{Cite web |url=http://www.usj.com.my/bulletin/upload/showpost.php?p=91542&postcount=14 |title=Indian based political parties, Bob Kee |access-date=27 April 2016 |archive-date=13 March 2020 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200313172400/http://www.usj.com.my/bulletin/upload/showthread.php?9886-Malaysian-Politicians-Arrogance-per-se&s=868fadb347a6c47fa35025f971a5db4b&p=91542 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

===Crises===
The party has managed to re-register back again by the [[Societies Act 1966|Registrar of Societies]] (RoS) after its registration was cancelled twice in 1978 and 1998.<ref name= deregistered/>

In 2008, KIMMA went through a leadership crisis when Amir Amsa Alla Pitchay, who had been dismissed by party president Syed Ibrahim B. Kader, claimed the presidency and suggested merging KIMMA with the [[People's Progressive Party (Malaysia)|People's Progressive Party]] (PPP), a [[Barisan Nasional]] (BN) component party.<ref>[http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2008/2/5/nation/20237702&sec=nation No talks, says the real Kimma head, 5 February 2008, The Star] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121101130206/http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=%2F2008%2F2%2F5%2Fnation%2F20237702&sec=nation |date=1 November 2012 }}</ref>

The party faced another crisis in 2009 when the validity of its 33rd Annual General Meeting was put into doubt over allegations of misconduct and the dismissal of the party's Federal Territory deputy secretary-general, Mohd Fazil William Abdullah.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://mt.m2day.org/2008/content/view/22698/84/ |title=Crisis within Malaysian Indian Muslim Congress, 03 June 2009, Bernama |access-date=27 April 2016 |archive-date=6 June 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090606130936/http://mt.m2day.org/2008/content/view/22698/84/ |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>[http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/108228 Kimma: Pecat Mohd Fazil ikut perlembagaan, Abdul Rahim Sabri, 10 Jul 2009, Malaysiakini]</ref><ref>[http://www.malaysiakini.com/news/108155 Kimma jelas kontroversi di WP, Abdul Rahim Sabri & Rahmah Ghazali, 09 Jul 2009, Malaysiakini]</ref>

=== Parliamentary representation ===
Party president Syed Ibrahim Kader was appointed senator in the country's upper house, the [[Dewan Negara]], for two three-year terms, holding that position from 2011 to 2017.<ref>{{Cite web |author=Syed Mu’az Syed Putra |date=20 May 2011 |title=Presiden Kimma, tiga wakil BN angkat sumpah Senator minggu depan |url=https://dialograkyat.blogspot.com/2011/05/presiden-kimma-tiga-wakil-bn-angkat.html?m=1 |access-date=17 August 2021 |work=Elbi Risalah |publisher=Dialog Rakyat |language=ms}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |date=17 May 2011 |title=Presiden Kimma dilantik senator - Bodek Najib & UMNO, Terus Diangkat Senator |url=http://greenboc.blogspot.com/2011/05/bodek-najib-umno-terus-diangkat-senator.html |access-date=17 August 2021 |work=TMI |publisher=Kelab Greenboc |language=ms}}</ref>

===2022 Malaysian general election===
On 14 October 2022, four days after the [[dissolution of parliament|dissolution]] of the [[14th Parliament of Malaysia|14th Parliament]], party president Syed Ibrahim Kader revealed that BN leadership had agreed to nominate a candidate from the party to contest for a "safe" seat in the election, allowing them to make their election debut. He expressed his happiness that the party's loyalty to the coalition was recognised after 13 years as an UMNO associate member party and likened the seat as a "reward".<ref>{{cite web |date=14 October 2022 |title=GE15: Kimma to contest for first time, says loyalty to BN rewarded |url=https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2022/10/14/ge15-kimma-to-contest-for-first-time-says-loyalty-to-bn-rewarded |accessdate=15 October 2022 |website=The Star}}</ref>

KIMMA pledged to carry on supporting and working with UMNO through its Jalinan Rakyat (JR) machinery in the [[2022 Johor state election]].<ref>{{cite news |date=23 January 2022 |title=Johor election: KIMMA to reach out to 68,000 Indian Muslim voters |work=[[Bernama]] |publisher=[[Astro Awani]] |url=https://www.astroawani.com/berita-malaysia/johor-election-kimma-reach-out-68000-indian-muslim-voters-343191 |accessdate=23 January 2022}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |author=Mohd Farid Noh |date=23 January 2022 |title=Pengundi baharu di Johor bertambah 30 peratus |language=ms |work=[[Berita Harian]] |url=https://www.bharian.com.my/berita/nasional/2022/01/914726/pengundi-baharu-di-johor-bertambah-30-peratus/ |accessdate=23 January 2022}}</ref>

On 1 November 2022, BN Chairman and UMNO President [[Ahmad Zahid Hamidi]] announced that Syed Ibrahim Kader would contest the [[Puchong (federal constituency) |Puchong]] federal seat, which he would eventually lose to the [[Pakatan Harapan]] candidate.
